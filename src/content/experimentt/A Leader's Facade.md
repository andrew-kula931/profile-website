The storage garage complex offers a small indicator of what’s to come when they find the front gate completely broken off its hinges. The eight foot tall, steel framed, chain linked gate lies flat on its side next to the fence blocking only a few ants from getting in without a climb. 

Blake and Ryan exchange glances. The former swallows uneasily while the latter just sighs. Both teens had seen plenty of the apocalypse so far—this is unfortunately nothing new at this point.

“I’m not so sure about this,” Blake discloses as he glances between Ryan and the gate.

Ryan looks at him indifferently, “This was your idea.”

Blake nods as he waves his hand towards the complex, “Yeah, because I thought it’d be one zombie,” he gestures towards the ground decoration, “This is not something we’re equipped to handle.”

Ryan shakes his head as he points towards the fence, particularly where the gate would have connected to, “look at the fence,” Blake complains, “see how it’s running along a straight line. Now look at where the gate is.” He gestures to the gate which lies outside the line created by the fence, “The gate’s on the outside of the fence-line, that means something broke out not in.”

Blake looks over the scene briefly but this doesn’t seem to phase him, “I noticed that too, but if something is strong enough to break out, who’s to say something wasn’t left behind.”

Ryan looks around the complex. Rows of storage units create hallway-like paths between three unique structures. White doors line the buildings with only a single empty storage unit having its door open.

“You’re probably right to be skeptical,” Ryan says, deciding that this isn’t really something he needs to push his luck on.

Blake shakes his head, “No you’re right. I just needed a second to wrap my head around it. A little danger will be worth it if it keeps everyone safe,” he starts heading inside, “Let’s go.”

Ryan glances one time at the street beyond the fence-line before following Blake inside.

They start off by checking the building closest to their base. None of the garage doors are in an open position so they begin to carefully knock on every door one at a time hoping to find the origin of the strange noise.

Blake approaches the first garage door and promptly bangs his fist on the side of its surface. They then stand there in silence for a moment.

“Not this one,” Blake decides as he starts heading to the next after a moment of silence.

“Hey Ryan,” Blake adds as he stops in front of the next door, “A lot of people are talking about it, and it's got me thinking. Do you ever worry about your family?”

They both stop and stare at each other. Blake seems mildly insistent on an answer while Ryan deliberately decides to avoid thinking about it.

“Not anymore,” Ryan says blandly, avoiding eye contact.

Blake gives him a concerned look, “What do you mean?”

Ryan shakes his head as he bangs on the garage door, “Don’t worry about it.” They stand in silence for a moment listening for a response to the knocking but nothing arises, “What about you? Do you worry?”

Blake bites his lip as he starts for the next door with Ryan in tow, “...Yeah. I worry a lot actually… but…”

“But you've got responsibility,” Ryan concludes knowingly.

Blake stops in front of the next door and rests his hand on it, unable to knock right away. He looks back to Ryan, “Yes and no. My father used to tell me that responsibility takes precedent over feelings—there are times to feel and there are times to work. With so many people depending on me… I think this is just one of those times to work.”

If Ryan were normal, he’d probably say that it's deep. If he was sensitive, he’d probably remind Blake that he’s here for him. If he were clever, he’d probably rationalize the man’s fears… but Ryan is none of those. So, instead of responding with the perfect words, he just stands there in silence, unable to put any words to the empathy he knows he wants to feel.

Blake shakes his head, “Sorry, I thought you’d understand.”

He draws his hand back and prepares to hit the door.

Ryan looks off into the distance, particularly at a hill off in the distance with a tree at the top, “I get it.”

Ryan doesn’t look back, but he can feel Blake staring at him with his hand hesitating in front of the garage door. By the time he does look back, Blake has already broken through that surprise and strikes the door, awaiting a response. This door, thankfully, offers no reply to their call so they move on to the next one.

“So how’d you do it?” Blake asks changing the subject, “How’d you get out of the school?”

Ryan takes a deep breath recalling the long string of events that led up to his eventual escape from the school. As much as he hates to admit it, he only got out of there thanks to Heimes’ help. So how is he supposed to explain that the enemy saved him while also punishing him. The healing bite on his shoulder and concealed cut on his arm make his entire right side ache at the thought.

“I just ran,” Ryan says as he bangs on the garage door, “I just ran and ran and luckily stumbled across a door that hadn’t barred closed. I had to get a key off a staff member, but I made it out.”

“What door?” Blake wonders, “All of them looked barricaded when we escaped.”

“It was a maintenance door,” Ryan explains as he remembers crawling through that cramped space only to hear the scream of an innocent student. His blood runs cold for a moment.

Suddenly the last door in the line of garage doors begins to shake. Both teens hone in on the disturbance immediately as they exchange glances. Blake nods to him as they creep towards the infrequent banging on the door—the rumbling noise sending chills down their spine as they check their flank constantly.

Ryan is the first to arrive at the door and promptly reaches for a handle at the bottom. He expects it to be locked but after pulling at it slightly, he realizes that there’s no resistance at all.

“It’s unlocked,” Ryan communicates as Blake creeps over to the other side of the door.

“Crack it open,” Blake commands, “I’ll check how many there are.”

Ryan nods as he pulls at the door just enough to open a small gap at the bottom. Blake places his ear to the ground and peers inside. After less than a second of observation, he’s already back on his feet, nodding to Ryan.

“There’s only one,” he draws his pocket knife, “Open it up and grab its attention, I’ll stab it in the back.”

Ryan shoots him a skeptical look as he slowly lowers the garage door down, “You sure?”

Blake nods.

The occasional impact on the door causes Ryan’s arms to shake. Based on the vibration, he can tell that the person on the other side is not very large—maybe a small woman or largish middle schooler. Either way, he decides that he’ll trust Blake and open it on his command.

With one last confirming nod, Ryan pulls the door all the way up revealing a boy in mostly clean clothes. His jeans look as if they were washed the day before and his tennis team T-shirt seems to be in a very similar state except for a bit of saliva dripping from his mouth onto the collar. The boy's bloodshot eyes dart around wildly with his mouth open, barring teeth like a cornered cat. Out of his left eye is a thin stream of blood dripping down his cheek creating a small pool on the floor. The last and saddest thing of note is the boy’s left arm. On it are various scratch marks and the outline of a set of bloody teeth.

Ryan stands firmly as the zombie locks onto him and lunges forward. Instead of fighting the zombie’s momentum, Ryan sidesteps out of the way and trips the zombie up with a swift kick to the ankle. As the zombie stumbles forward and ultimately wipes out on the ground, Blake springs forward and stabs the knife right into the back of the zombie’s neck. Ryan is immediately met with the memory of him doing the same with the zombie woman all that time ago and just as he did then, Blake lifts the knife out and stabs it back in ensuring the kill.

“Nice job,” Ryan admits as he checks himself for any unaccounted for injuries.

Blake pulls away from the corpse and takes a deep breath. His arms avoid any loose gore, but he inspects them as if they were foreign objects. Eventually he takes another breath, crouches down, and rolls the zombie onto its back. Once the boy’s face is in view, Blake goes silent and stares at the zombie boy with an expressionless face.

Ryan doesn’t ask any questions and opts to just stand in silence as Blake reaches forward and closes the zombie’s bloodshot eyes.

"Damn," he says softly.

"You know him?" Ryan presumes.

Blake begins with a morbid tone, his eyes not drifting from the boy for a second, “His name was Daniel. He was a guy from my tennis team... picked him up from a vacant classroom on our way out,” he looks at the boys face intently taking note of a subtle indention in the boy’s hair, “He normally wears glasses and some... stupid nerdy shirt... he didn't even play video games," Blake leans back and takes a seat beside him, "When we found him, he was crying in the corner of the room... you know... it's just... there's something about it, you know," Blake carefully re-sheaths his knife without bothering to wipe the blood off, "Finding him there, in that state... he wouldn't come out, so I… I promised to keep him safe…” Blake strains his eyelids as they waver closed, “The things we had to walk through to get out of the building… he just couldn’t take it. A couple of hours later, he was gone. No one knew where he went… Now I know why.”

Ryan watches silently as Blake lifts the corpse off the ground in a princess carry and transfers it into the storage room. He sets the boy down respectfully on the ground inside the garage before slowly closing the door. His eyes don’t leave the boy’s body as the white door slides closed.

“It’s not much of a grave, but we don’t have any shovels,” Blake admits, “This is the best I can do for him.”

Ryan nods.

“It’s just…” Blake starts lowering his head as he takes a long pause, "These kids shouldn't have to deal with this. This shit ain't fair."

Ryan glances at the struggling teen with a reserved, yet sympathetic look. His gaze doesn’t hold long as he looks back at the white door before them, “Remember that you’re one of those kids too.”

Blake’s fist clenches as he grits his teeth. Neither of them exchange any words as Blake takes a deep breath and loosens his body.

They stare at the door for a few moments more before Ryan speaks, “What are you going to tell the others?”

Blake doesn’t look as he watches the featureless door as if in a trance, “I won’t tell them. Everyone’s endured enough heartache. This one stays here.”

They head back to the camp in absolute silence. Neither teen bothers to decompress what they saw verbally. Maybe it’s just Ryan’s proclivities speaking, but he feels that Blake’s mere presence is enough to lighten the burden—even if only a little.

They return to the base and are strangely not interrogated about what went down. Beth offers a bit of curiosity, but even her remarks lack any meaningful prying. As far as the teens were concerned, their little 'errand' was swept under the rug the second they returned. 

Blake is immediately met with questions from a few girls who heard a dog barking nearby. Ryan stalls to listen for a moment, but once Blake lightly convinces them to give it up, he meanders to the second floor balcony and looks over the base. 

The hand-made wall is hardly pleasing to look at, but the haphazardly placed two-by-fours and sheets of scrap metal seem to be effective enough as he can't imagine he'd navigate over it easily. It's a wonder they got this far given the threats lurking around ever corner, but for some reason he's truly glad they did make it. Watching half the camp encircle Blake with trivial questions they could probably figure out on their own and the other half passing time in the most reserved manner a teenager can handle is truly a sight to behold. Although unnatural, it offers Ryan a certain level of comfort—a comfort he needed.

"Impressive right?" Chloe jokes as she leans on the balcony railing beside him, "You wouldn't believe how hard it was to get the red van on the end in position."

Ryan glances at her and finds himself drawn to a particular object on her face. On the lobe of her ear is a small red circle with black polka dots, it's a signature of a certain bug that offers a certain allure to it. Ryan can't help but stare at it until Chloe looks over.

"I like your earing," Ryan covers as he looks away, not wanting to be awkward.

Chloe touches her ear and smiles, "Thank you. Red's not my preferred color, but it's all I've got."

Ryan looks out beyond the walls and ponders what an adequate response would be. To be honest, he doesn't really know how to give complements, but commenting on an article of clothing was the only thing he could think to talk about. It's not that he's nervous, it's just... he's not used to this kind of interaction.

"Maybe red should be your color," Ryan says immediately biting down on his tongue. The words come out like sandpaper which makes him regret it immediately. Not only that, but red only brings up one thing in his mind right now and that's the opposite of his intention.

He turns to apologize for what he said but finds Chloe's shoulders raises as she bashfully tries to avoid eye contact.

"You really think so?" she comments, still avoiding any attempt to match Ryan's gaze.

"I-" Ryan starts before putting the brakes on his voice and turning back to the balcony. What in the world has gotten in to him. 

As he suspected, the conversation falls on its face after that as Ryan drifts off into eavesdropping on teens' conversations and Chloe just stands in silence. Eventually, Chloe decides the conversation wasn't hopeless, and she looks at him again.

"So... where have you been the last couple of days?"

Ryan slouches down on the railing as he presses his palms together, "Surviving... I guess."

Chloe's eyes dwell on him for a moment before eventually turning towards the base below, "And now you're surviving with me," her words settle but are quickly interrupted by her ears growing bright red and her head snapping back up, "—I mean us. Surviving with us, all of us."

Ryan chuckles to be kind, "I know what you meant."

Chloe folds her fingers together, her shoulders curling inward as if to make herself smaller. Her long blond hair falls forward to cover her face as she looks down.

Ryan looks away casually, but Chloe squeezes her fingertips into her knuckles in any way but casual. She quickly looks back to him with an apologetic expression, "I'm sorry. I mean, I wanted to apologize."

Ryan raises an eyebrow quizzically, "For what?"

Chloe swallows and looks away as her fingers fidget, "Back in the school... I should have tried to stop you from... you know, risking everything like you did." 

Ryan shrugs, "You don't have to apologize, I wanted to do it."

Chloe looks over, locks eyes with him, and immediately turns her head as if eye contact was a no-go, "Yeah, I just... I wanted to... I don't know what I'm saying."

Now that he’s settled into the conversation, his thoughts come easier. Watching Chloe stumble over her words feels strangely comforting. For once, he doesn’t feel like the only person in the world who doesn’t know what to say

Suddenly their comfort is shattered by a shout below them. Both of them look straight down to find a group of teens congregating outside the old film shop with some escalating commotion occurring inside. Blake and Beth both push through the group to get to the source while Ryan and Chloe exchange glances. It doesn't take a psychic to know that they were sharing the same thought—we've got to stop this.

By the time Ryan and Chloe make it through the thick peanut gallery, Blake is already standing in between two girls and a much larger boy. The boy in question is not large in stature or muscle but large as a byproduct of slothful habits which results in a hard-on-the-eyes visage twisted with rage.

"You did it! I know you did!" the boy shouts, disregarding any potential consequence that might occur due to this volume.

"I already told you that I didn't do it," one of the two girls counters as she pins her hands to her hips.

The larger boy's nostrils flair as he takes a step towards them. Blake intercepts and is able to stop the boy in his tracks, but this does not include a suppression of his voice.

"You tried to trip me back in the school!" the boy hollers, "Admit it! You did!"

"She already said she didn't," the second girl argues, "You're only making this up because she rejected you."

The boy fiercely shakes his head, "That's not true!"

"You've got to calm down," Blake asserts while he begins to struggle to hold back the two-hundred plus pounds of the boy.

"Like hell I will," the boy complains as he shoves Blake off of him and gets another step towards the girls who quickly cower in fear. 

Another guy, whom Ryan vaguely recalls being in his cohort, pushes past the crowd and rushes to Blake's aid by pulling the boy back before he can get within arms reach of the girls. Beth also assists by stepping in between the two parties.

"Let me go!" the boy shouts, "They tried to kill me!"

"Stop lying!" the first girl complains as she fails to duck under spit flung at her, "What the fuck was that?!"

The boy glares at her with a malicious grin as he sucks in any excess saliva simply relishing his work. At the sight of this, the guy restraining the larger boy has had enough and pulls his fist back only to launch a punch right into the boy's fat face. He doesn't stand a chance and falls to the ground like a wet rag screaming some profanities. In the meantime, Beth shuttles the two girls out of the room and proceeds to tell everyone outside to back away.

"Jennifer, what'd you do to him," Chloe whispers to herself under her breath.

Ryan looks over at her and finds her eyes starring holes into the boy now lying on the ground.

Beth approaches them as she motions for everyone to back up, "Hey, let Blake and Caid handle this."

Ryan nods and starts backing up, but Chloe doesn't budge an inch.

"Chloe?" Beth questions as she becomes the only person to still be crowded around the film store.

Chloe looks to her friend with a determination that makes Beth freeze, "I might know how to calm him down. Please, let me help."

Beth starts off by shaking her head, but then after a moment of consideration, lets out a begrudging sigh, "Fine."

"Thank you," Chloe says as she rushes past her friend and enters into the film store, now with only three teens inside.

Beth turns to Ryan with a curious look on her face. She mentally debates saying something but ultimately brushes it off and continues pushing the crowd back—excluding Ryan.

Seeing as he's been given a pretty clear green light to head inside, he does so and finds the fat boy curled up into a ball in the corner of the store. Blake and the guy named Caid keep their arms crossed as they stand around him like menacing prison guards containing a volatile prisoner. Between then, Chloe crouches down in front of the boy with her hands on her knees.

"Why won't anyone believe me," the boy whines pathetically to himself.

"What you're name?" she asks softly.

The boy glances up quickly before covering his head with his hands and starring back into his lap, "I'm surprised you don't remember. We've been in the same Spanish class for two semesters."

Chloe ponders the notion for a moment before raising a finger at the realization, "Jeremy, right?"

The boy nods without looking up.

Chloe's voice softens as she offers an empathetic smile in vain, "I understand how you feel."

The boy shakes his head violently in his hands, "No you don't! You're hot and I'm not. People like you will never understand people like me."

In light of the back-handed complement, Chloe struggles to find her next words. She looks over to the guards standing beside her before her eyes settle on Ryan. It's at that point that she decides to keep pressuring the boy.

"You're wrong," Chloe asserts, "I understand how you feel because I believe you."

The boy freezes before slowly lifting his head to reveal a red face on the verge of tears, "What? You're lying, just like they are. You're trying to manipulate me... no one believes me."

"Oh shut up," Caid spits out callously which is immediately met with a disapproving look from Chloe.

"You're no better you fuckin meathead!" Jeremy points out with every ounce of spite he can muster.

"What'd you say pig?!" Caid starts rolling up his sleeve and stepping forward but Blake cuts him off and shakes his head.

The fat boy, finally finding the nerve to stand up, stares at Caid with an unnerving amount of malice, "A zombie broke into our classroom and that bitch tried to trip me so she could escape. You only have to run faster than the person next to you, they say. Trip the fat kid, they say. She's a narcissistic slut who's only going to get other people killed. I'm just doing this to help everyone, honest."

Caid is forced to practice commendable restraint with how furious his face grows. Blake helps too by keeping a hand on him to hold him back. 

Chloe stands up as well and takes a step towards Jeremy, "There's more too it than that, isn't there".

Jeremy pauses and fails to make any meaningful eye contact. He offers a grunt and a disapproving head gesture but doesn't verbalize anything further.

"Did—did you ask her out?" Chloe suspects with a light but forward tone.

Jeremy does a double take, panic flashing on his face, "What? Her? No, never. Why would you..." he trails off as he paces towards the corner of the room. 

Chloe hesitates as she holds one hand to her chest defensively, "I'm just trying to help."

Jeremy waves a hand dismissively, "Well you're not, so... just leave me alone."

Chloe looks around for with uncertainty as she grapples with how to handle the situation. From the outskirts of the conversation, Ryan can't help but admire how far they've come regardless. He wonders if maybe... this is what he should have done back then.

"I think we should give him some space," Blake admits as he waves for everyone to leave.

"Wait," Chloe counters turning to Jeremy, "I don't know what happened. But... I just... What I'm trying to say is that I don't trust Jennifer. Please, Jeremy, tell me what happened, I want to help you."

"She tried to kill me," Jeremy repeats without looking back.

Chloe continues with restraint but not without sincerity, "Is that really it?"

He quickly spins back with a distressed tone, throwing his arms into he air, "Okay, fine. I did... I asked here out... And you know what? She had the gall to call me ugly. Said I didn't deserve to have an entitled brat like her... there I said it... that's probably why she tripped me."

Chloe takes a deep breath allowing Jeremy to have a moment of silence, "I hate to admit it, but that does sound like Jennifer."

"Damn," Caid says rubbing the back of his head, "You? You've got balls of steel, I'll give you that."

Ryan crosses his arms and leans against the wall—he too was wondering what that girl meant about rejection. It seems these things are never simple.

"Even if that's the case," Blake begins, "That doesn't warrant this behavior."

Jeremy looks over at him with a new found passion for throwing fists, "Did you not hear me?! She tried to kill me!"

Chloe steps into the situation which thankfully stops Jeremy in his tracks without anyone needing to hold him back. 

"Jeremy, it's okay. What happened in the school was unacceptable. We all made mistakes back there," Chloe's words, being heartfelt, seem to settle Jeremy's rage, even if momentarily, "But getting worked up about this is only going to prove her right."

Jeremy looks around the room quite a bit more distressed, but also less aggressive, "But... she tried to kill me."

"And no one here will ever let that happen again," Chloe defends.

"But," Jeremy says looking to the ground as he holds back tears.

Blake nods his head a few times in thought, "You're not here because of what she did, you're here in spite of it. Give yourself some credit."

Jeremy falls to his knees and curls up into the corner covering his face. His tears quietly seep into his palms as the four other teenagers silently agree to give the boy his space.

As they depart from the building, Ryan can't shake his gaze from Chloe. Thankfully she doesn't look back, but his thoughts race at what he saw. No one got hurt, no one was okay, yet it seems as though everyone said the right things. So... why couldn't he?
