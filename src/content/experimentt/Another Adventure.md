Three weeks pass and quite a lot happens in that amount of time. Foremost, Drew didn't give him much an option but he started taking him out into the city to "track down Heimes". While this certainly true, it felt like a cover for Drew to take an uncomfortable stance as an unwanted mentor figure for Ryan. He would constantly challenge him to do dangerous and difficult tasks and proceed to critique him for every inefficiency he made. As time went by Drew began stepping back more and more from the little "adventures" and started making Ryan do all the work. Granted, none of them were particularly difficult, especially in the latter part of the three weeks, but it did feel like Drew was just using him. This probably would have pissed former Ryan off quite a bit, but he noticed himself actually taking to the lessens and developing a tactically inclined quick wit amidst chaos. 

Not only that, Drew "forced" him to partake in rigorous workouts while not in the field. The last part was honestly too much for him to handle, but then Chloe catch him doing pushups in his room and... it pissed him off... but that face... he begrudgingly let Drew drag him out the next day.

The next important topic was survivors across the city. Not a day went by without mention of the "Elementary School Camp" or the "Fire Station Group". Ryan never personally met any of these people or their respective representatives but the other survivors couldn't stop thinking about what they could trade and who was still alive. In a set of various exchanges between the camps, many of the other high school students would watch from afar and pray that one of the representatives was a loved one. As luck would have it, one of the teens actually did get to reunite with their long lost father, but the rest weren't so fortunate. At the end of the day, they persevered and put in another days work.

Another notable thing, for Ryan at least, was his relationship with his friends, particularly Chloe. On multiple occasions, Ryan would get asked to join a group of adult women, Chloe, Beth, Alison, and occasionally Caid—though Caid was really only there to talk to Beth. This seemingly arbitrary group wouldn't stray far from the prison but would head over to a community center to scavenge supplies and—what Ryan deems—crafts. He would have refused outright if it weren't for Chloe's pitiful insistence that always had a way of circumventing his excuses and getting him to say yes. Then again, he did find himself enjoying his failed attempts at Origami, Chloe showing him the correct way, and Caid trying to act "cool" around Beth.

On the topic of Caid and Beth, he noticed it explicitly about two days after the mission to save Nana. Caid was sitting outside his room bouncing a ball when Beth passed by. She said that her dad used to have a Mustang and thought his looked really cool. Of course, this compliment touched Caid on multiple fronts and sparked a conversation. They ended up taking a ride in the Mustang around a couple of blocks, but that was only after about two days of Caid trying to convince Michael to let him take a joyride. Ryan doesn't know much more than that, but he can tell there is definitely something between them, or maybe it's been there for a while, but he's only noticed it in recent memory. 

That last thing to note is how sparse the mutant attacks had been the past three months. Allegedly, when the apocalypse started, the prison was attacked almost daily—standard zombies mostly. A moderately dangerous mutant would join the fray here and there but nothing on the magnitude of the behemoth that Ryan and Steve faced off against. Then, from around the time that the teens arrived, the attacks dropped off considerably. A small group of zombies appeared maybe once or twice a week, but they were always manageable enough for the towers alone to maintain security. In all that time, not a single zombie nor mutant stepped foot into the compound.

And that's it. The days went by quickly and smoothly and everything was great, perfect even. Not a thing worried Ryan whatsoever. The days were unremarkably smooth... the days were good... the nights... the nights were something else. Maybe it was his solitary confinement cell or maybe it was just mere coincidence—though he knows that's a rationalization. He would get a good nights rest three or four times a week and then have a great day... the other three, four nights were spent in the same, perpetual nightmare. It always started off the same with him waking up in a younger version of himself, sometimes as a child, sometimes only a few weeks younger. What happened after that varied wildly, but it always ended the same. He always found himself as his current version and Steve always woke him up—with the sound of a bang. Every single time he would wake up in a cold sweat in the middle of the night, and every single time he wouldn't get an ounce of sleep after that. At some point he just learned to deal with the sleep deprivation. Maybe that alone would have drawn him away. Maybe those nightmares would have been enough for him to regress back to escapism, but one thing held him back. Just one thing stopped him from walking out without a word.

---

Ryan finds himself alone in the cafeteria eating cold soup. He and Drew had been out in the city a bit longer than expected and subsequently returned almost an hour after lunch ended. As a result, he gets the honor of drinking his cold soup in solitude as he chews on the near frozen beef and hard potatoes. The taste is fine thanks to Max's touch who's been helping out in the kitchen quite consistently, but the texture and temperature make it an annoyance to eat.

In the midst of his ravenous consumption, he finds a lone, familiar figure standing at the cafeteria door. 

"I thought I'd find you here?" Chloe says with a knowing glance as a playful smile.

Ryan shrugs indifferently as he finishes off the last gulp of soup, "We're you looking long?"

"Careful, you're going to choke on that if you eat to quickly," she comments as she helps herself to seat across from him at the empty cafeteria table.

Setting the bowl down and swallowing the chewy meat, he leans forward and places his elbows against the table, "I'll take my chances. Max's food just hits different sometimes."

Chloe looks at him incredulously, "That potato looked raw."

Ryan shakes his head and responds dryly, "That was the other cooks fault."

Although not particularly meant to be funny, Chloe starts laughing. Ryan just stares which seems to amply the humor of it somehow. Perhaps his impulsive sarcasm could be seen as a clean witticism, but he's still transitioning out of field mission mode. He braces for impact because he knows what's coming next.

Chloe smiles as her laughter dies down, "Lighten up Ryan."

He nods, "Yeah, that's why Drew pushes my workouts so hard—so I feel lighter."

"That's not what I—" Chloe starts.

Ryan sighs finally transitioning into a more casual demeanor, "Yeah, yeah, I was just joking. I think... I'm back to reality now."

Chloe immediately give him "the look". No, not the one most people associate with that phrase, this one is much more specific to Chloe. It's the kind of look she gives him when he says something borderline insensitive or just plain ignorant. The kind of look he receives when he says something that only he would say. The look feels a bit exasperated but he never feels put off by it. Instead he gets the feeling that she's glad it came from him. 

"Okay, good," she says swiftly after "the look" concludes, "there's something I want to show you."

She doesn't wait for his reply as she promptly pushes herself to her feet, grabs his hand, and pulls him towards the cafeteria exit. He doesn't even get a chance to put his bowl away before he's kidnapped and tugged down the hallway. He lead through the prison relentlessly until they finally arrive at their destination: his room.

He's about to ask what this is all about, but his question is answered implicitly as he actually gazes into his formerly bland, undecorated room. What he finds at present is a completely different place. Scotch taped to the ceiling are multiple strings with white paper snowflakes. Each dangles from the ceiling haphazardly with different lengths of string yet the imperfection makes it feel like so much more like a real first snow. On the walls are additional cutouts of snowmen, elves, and various other winter themed creations—each hand crafted by someone still learning the basics of the scissors. Beyond the cutouts are a few full sheets of paper with marker drawn pictures of a multitude of things, some of which, he can't even identify. The final cherry on top is a single paper crane resting on the chair in the corner of his room. The same chair that Chloe brought in early on to sit and read books in his room. That chair has now become the humble nest of the familiar origami creation.

"Did you do all this?" Ryan wonders as he inspects the childish drawings.

Chloe smiles, "Do you know how some kids started going with us to the community center?"

Ryan nods.

"One of the girls made you a snowflake. Then another did and well," she looks around, "it sort of cascaded from there. I hung everything up while you were gone."

Looking up at the ceiling once more, Ryan searches for a familiar water stain that he's been starring at for weeks. He eventually does find it, but only by looking through one of the hanging snowflakes. It seems his nights of personifying that stained ghost will now be replaced with personifying snow.

"I know it's kind of childish," Chloe admits with a bit of embarrassment, "...but do you like it?"

Ryan tries to verbalize something but it never makes it past his throat as he looks around in awe. He never decorated his room as a kid because his room changed so often he never saw a point in it. Having something in her now feels... he's not sure how he feels, but he knows it means something.

He looks back to Chloe and finds her waiting patiently with a considerate grin as if taking pride in the entire endeavor. His mouth forms into a consonant but stops as something appears in the doorway.

Appearing from nowhere is the merry smile of a young woman: Stephanie Grail, a former high school history teacher. She looks around at the decorations with awe, "This looks amazing! Is this what those kids were working on yesterday?" she asks with her usual thoughtful yet sweet voice.

Chloe nods, her pulled back shoulders flaunting her pride, "It is."

Stephanie can't help but laugh a little, "I was wondering why little Penelope was carrying an armful of paper snowflakes," she covers her mouth as she giggles again, "the poor little girl dropped them all twice in one hallway."

Chloe laughs along, "I told her to get some help when carrying them all."

The two women share a moment of laughter while Ryan reaches out and touches one of the dangling snowflakes. It's rough surface feels closer to cardboard than notebook paper which means it holds well when he pushes it and it starts swaying from side to side. He wonders how long it must have taken to make so many creations, and that's not even considering the multitude of drawing that fill up an entire wall. Granted many of them seem like they were scribbled out in ten seconds flat, but it's probably the thought that counts for toddlers. He's about to move on when his eyes land on a particular picture that captures his attention. Within the small, rectangular space is a scribbled, blue background with a green foreground. Atop the grass-like green is a round, brown animal, a darker shade of brown surrounding it's head. The poor imitation of a lion catches him off guard, not because of the youthful imagination, but because of it's striking resemblance of Ryan's own memories. It perfectly matches an illustration Ryan created fifteen years ago to give to his mother. Maybe this whole thing is like that... maybe.

Stephanie eyes him carefully, "Ryan, you seem enamored with the drawings. You feel that youthful spark again?"

Snapping out of his trance, he glances back at the two women staring at him with smiles on their faces. Once again, him zoning out seems to be humorous to the people around him.

"—uh, no... I was just caught up in thought," he responds looking away.

"Yeah, right," Stephanie says knowingly, "Anyways, I'm sure you're wondering why I stopped by on your alone time."

"It's not like that," Chloe says defensively, thought Ryan gets the impression she wasn't hurt by it, "you can stop by whenever you want."

Stephanie places her hands on her hips, "I know you feel that way, but Mr. Solitary Confinement over there probably doesn't feel the same."

Startled, Ryan realizes he's staring at the lion again and promptly rejoins the conversation, "Yeah—I mean... what were you saying?"

Both women laugh again, this time truly at Ryan's expense. His thoughts just don't seem to stay with him right now.

"So..." Ryan starts, trying to get the conversation back on track and away from his aloof personality, "Why'd you stop by again."

Stephanie rolls her eyes but gives in to his question, "I stopped by to make sure you were doing your homework..." she stops which evokes another laugh from Chloe, but Ryan isn't in the right headspace to laugh at something like that, "Tough crowd. Anyways, me and some other people want to go into the city to get a few things. I was hoping you'd come with."

Ryan looks her over incredulously, "Why do you need me?"

She sighs exasperatedly, "I figured you'd ask that. Well, Drew left as soon as he got back, Xavier is currently taking care of a sick old lady, and Charles unfortunately declines. That means you're next on the list."

Ryan doesn't quite buy it yet, "It's not illegal to go out on your own. Besides, don't you have a husband for something like this?"

Stephanie shakes her head, "Normally yes, but Michael's kept me on a tight leash recently—I know that sounds bad, but I think it's perfectly understandable after what happened a few years ago."

“A few years ago?” Chloe wonders.

“Yes, Michael's been the warden here for a while and as such he's seen his fair share of rough things. Anyways, a few years ago there was one particular prison riot. The guards all did the protocol procedures and attempted to subdue the riot with some tear gas and riot shields, but then things got worse. A few prisoners managed to overpower some of the guards and took them hostage. After that, the prisoners barricaded themselves into their hallway and separated their hostages into two groups. To those that were friendly and treated the inmates like humans, they tied them up, and let them be. The other group however was beaten savagely—Michael never told me how bad but... it was bad. By the time the rest of the security force broke through the barricade, two of the guards had succumbed to their wounds and died. One of those guards was Michaels best friend. Ever since then, he's lost a lot of trust in people and, as such, doesn't want me going out alone."

Stephanie finishes the story and looks out to the opposite wall just pondering what it must have been like to be at the very same location that something so terrible occurred. 

"That doesn't explain why you can't go to Michael directly," Ryan points out.

“Okay fine," Stephanie says reluctantly, throwing her hands up in the air, "To tell you the truth, it’s Michael and I’s anniversary tomorrow and I want to get him something special... and... that requires going a bit further in town than the community center. Rest assured that it's not just me, a couple people want to come along.”

Ryan looks at her a bit skeptical, "How far are you talking?"

Stephanie does some mental math to chart out a route, "Just to like... fourteenth street, so maybe two or three miles away."

"Does that mean I can come?" Chloe chimes in, "I haven't been beyond the community center in a while."

“Of course you can, I'd appreciate your input on a few gift ideas,” Stephanie smiles as she looks back to Ryan, "So what do you say?"

Looking between the two women, Ryan can't help but feel some sort of peer pressure on him. It's the same experience he had when being coerced into going to the grocery story with Max and Caid. Being the fourth option, there's not really any chance he can say no.

"Fine," Ryan says, "When do we leave?"

“We’ll leave in one hour and plan on being back an hour before dark. It gets dark at 6 so let's plan to be back around 5,” she smiles, "Does that work?"

Ryan rubs his shoulder, he's not quite at one hundred percent after his rigorous morning of training with Drew. He'd prefer to take it easy for the rest of the day and start out first thing in the morning. It would also give him some time to plan out what to bring if a whole group is going pretty deep into the city.

“Awesome, let’s meet behind the parking lot around back,” Chloe decides to Stephanie's delight.

They both smile and nod. Before he can even respond to the plans, both women are already walking out the door to gather their belonging for their little excursion. He's left alone with his thoughts and the lion sketch. Slowly, he takes a seat on his bed and glances over at the paper crane perched on the chair. His eyelids soon grow heavy.

Before he knows it, he's shaken awake as Chloe appears overtop of him thrashing his shoulders back and forth.

“What is going on?” Ryan asks, sitting up quickly.

"I'm gone for less than an hour just to find you napping. Did you even sleep last night?" she smiles as she asks her question playfully.

Ryan rolls his eyes. It's probably be awkward if he told the truth, “Just about as much as you know what mind your own business means.”

She punches him lightly on the shoulder, “You take that back.”

Ryan shakes his head, finding his shallow comment to quite funny to him, "I don't think I will."

Chloe's mouth drops open, "That's so mean."

He leans forward, the paper crane in the corner of the room occupying his attention for a brief moment, "You're right, that was kinda mean."

She sighs as she takes a seat right next to him on the bed—a little too close. She pulls her bangs back a little with a smirk, "You're probably right though, I do bug you a lot... Does it bother you?"

Despite the playful banter, Ryan really takes that final comment to heart. Does it bother him? Chloe ends up in his room practically ever day. Sometimes she just wants someplace quite to read after a loud argument in the women's room and other times she just wants to come into his space and yap about who knows what. It's not like he ever has alternative engagements at those moments so he sort of just let it happened. This still begs the question of whether he ever thought Chloe a bother. Does he?

He looks straight ahead, "No... it doesn't."

Chloe smiles, "Then don't complain when I start leaving books in here."

Ryan looks at her—confused.

She shrugs, "Alright, let's get going. Everyone is probably ready to go by now."

When Ryan and Chloe arrive to the parking lot, they are quickly greeted by the chilly autumn air. The early November day is still young yet the orange leaves in the forest, clear sky, and fluffy white clouds set a tone for an adventurous day. Ryan instinctively finds himself looks at the clouds perfect for pattern matching. A small group approaching them snaps him out of it. 

In addition to Stephanie, a forty-something year old woman with a welcoming smile and jovial disposition, a more timid woman closer to Stephanie's age, and most surprisingly, Jeremy. His eyes hang on the disgruntled boy as he rolls his eyes at the sight of Ryan.

“Hello there Ryan,” the happy-go-lucky sounding woman says before they even get into conversational range, “My name is Amber Gray. Stephanie was telling me about how you guys were taking a secret outing to go shopping and I just had to come.” The woman claps her hands together and radiates her warm smile even more.

The more timid girl nods her head, "I'm Janelle Hemmingsworth," she backs out of the conversation quickly as Amber takes back over.

"I'm sure you already know my son Jeremy," she says gesturing over to the begrudging boy who refuses to make eye contact with any of them, especially Ryan, "He always refuses to come with me to the community center so I thought this might be a terrific opportunity for him to get some fresh air. I'm sure you three kids will get along just swimmingly."

"No one says swimmingly anymore, mom," Jeremy nags as he crosses his arms and finds a sudden fascination at some teenage girls standing near the basketball court. Incidentally they also happen to be the girls he got into a heated argument with, but they don't even notice him as they are all preoccupied with Caid, Blake, and some other boys playing four-on-four basketball.

Amber crosses her arms and gives her son a lightly defensive look, "Everyone was saying it in the Victorian novel I just finished."

"Sure, whatever," Jeremy accepts not paying attention to the conversation in the slightest.

Ryan looks around for any indication of a mode of transportation but nothing stands out as claimed, "How are going to get there?"

Stephanie nods as she points to a large SUV on the other side of the parking lot, "I already cleared it with Xavier and he's relaying it to everyone else—except Michael that is. We'll take that car there and back."

The vehicle in question is modern enough for Ryan's standards. The car body is clean and any wear-and-tear is rather minimal. Despite not knowing anything about cars, he's confident that it'll get them where they need to be and won't break down if they need to gun it out of a situation.

"Doesn't that car just have..." Amber hesitates, "Character."

Jeremy rolls his eyes as he starts walking towards it without any further prompting. Janelle and Amber follow suit as Stephanie dangles some keys in front of Ryan's face.

"You've been in town more than me," she says, "Wanna drive?"

The simple task of driving, for some reason, completely petrifies Ryan. He remembers how difficult it was to drive a motorcycle with only one person on it and here he's being offered the chance to drive some giant SUV with five other people in it. If he wasn't embarrassed enough last time, this would definitely be the killing blow. How is an eighteen year old supposed to justify the fact that they have never learned to drive. Maybe Stephanie could be a good teacher, like she was in history, but... with Chloe there?

"Uh..." Ryan draws out, his mind still racing.

Stephanie laughs, "Hey, no pressure. I was planning on driving anyways. Now that I think about it, you'll probably be better at navigating anyway, after all, half the reason you're coming is because you're the only one of us allowed to carry a gun."

"R-right," Ryan accepts trying to dodge any further suspicion.

"Alright then," Chloe adds, "Let's get going, I haven't been able to shop in almost a month. My hands are shaking I'm so excited."
