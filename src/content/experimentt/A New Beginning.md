Finding his way out of the forest takes about an hour, give or take as he wasn't sure where he was to begin with. Once he finally breaks through the last patch of bushes and shrubs he arrives to the familiar gray of a concrete road. As he looks around, he finds himself in the parking lot of an old hunting store and a gas station. 

The lot is barren with only a single flat tired car waiting for him at the pumps. Both the hunting store and the gas station have broken windows, and an ajar door, but no sign of blood or a struggle of any sort. A few scattered pieces of metal litter a particular parking spot, but Ryan assumes it couldn't have been more than a fender-bender to cause a bumper and scraps to be lying around.

Ryan elects to enter the hunting store first in hopes of finding some sort of weapon: his fists were great but he's killed one too many zombies with his bare hands. He carefully climbs through the broken window and scans the room for any threats. He finds the store completely empty and unsettlingly still which is both a good and concerning thing. Everything from guns to pocket knives has been looted with only worthless trash like niche tools and baseball caps remaining. It almost looks like the store is closed for renovations, but a poster in the front promoting a new deal claims otherwise. 

He quietly walks down the dark isles of the small shop in search of anything he could use to defend himself. With every passing empty shelf his spirits drop. He arrives at the back of the store empty handed and pounds his fist on the back counter in annoyance more than frustration. Upon hitting the wooden counter, he hears something drop from the other side. He hesitates at first expecting something to jump out, but after nothing happens for a solid ten seconds he elects to vault over the counter and look behind. Lying on the floor, likely shaken loose from its tucked away position under the counter, is a solitary pistol.

He slowly reaches for the weapon with a shaky hand. Almost begrudgingly, he rotates the gun in hand to check for the magazine and thankfully finds it right where it should be. Then, as if the obvious next thought, he checks the safety and finds it on. He quickly switches it off and stares at the weapon carefully. To think a pistol has been the only constant factor in all of this.

“What? Didn’t care for the gun I allotted to you?”

Ryan snaps his head up to look for the familiar voice only to find a very peculiar sight. Before him stands a person dressed in a cryptic attire. He wears black boots, neatly ironed black pants, a black tunic and a black bolero hat. To top it all off, his face is covered with a raven colored mask, but the eyes look too sinister to be simple vision holes. For a second, he almost thinks that the mask is the man’s actual face, but then he realizes that the mouth isn’t moving which would lead him to conclude it's only a mask.

“Who are you?” Ryan questions walking around the counter to get a better view of him.

“Oh, you probably don't recognize me…” he says with the voice of a high class man from a foreign nation. 

Ryan shakes his head despite recognizing the sound of the voice—very distinctly.

The man crosses his arms behind his back, “I wanted to check up on you, after all, preserving you was more... tedious than I anticipated.”

Ryan recognizes what he’s trying to say and steps forward instinctively, "You were there."

The man stares at him curiosity, "Fascinating".

Ryan looks at him blankly, "What do you mean?"

The enigmatic man leans his head from side to side studying Ryan like a doctor inspecting a patient, "To think mere moments ago, you desired nothing more than death. What perplexing phenomenon."

Ryan stares at the floor, unable to produce a proper response. If this man knew that, then just how much does he know?

The man waves his hand, "None the matter, I only wanted to ensure there were no aftereffects. Reproducibility is... sparse these days. I trust your resolve has solidified in light of recent events?"

Ryan studies the man before looking around, “I'm... not sure," he looks back at the man, "But... why me?"

The man chuckles to himself quite amused, “Who’s to say, perhaps it is those that see the darkest of depths that lust for the brightest of lights.”

The man then begins to walk towards the back door of the store. An employee only sign hangs from the door indicating some sort of storage room.

“Wait!” Ryan calls out, “Tell me why you did it.”

The man chuckles again softly as he opens the door and walks through, “That's the wrong question Ryan. A reason to live is not given—it’s found.”

With that the man departs from the building leaving Ryan alone in the store. He still had so many questions to ask, so many things he needed answered, yet he has a sinking suspicion that a mere explanation will not quench his curiosities.

His mind continues to wander through question after question until his vision falls upon the front doors of the store and locks eyes with someone. Their expression is sheer shock as they stand frozen at the door. Their blond hair is pulled back to reveal subtle bags under their eyes and their clothes harbor dirt stains won through tangible effort. Despite that, their bright blue jacket stands out as stylish for an apocalyptic setting. Their skin isn’t dirty, but it’s evident that they have been through their fair share of grime over the past few days. Their soft eyes and round cheeks remind him of a life before this one—one he forgot that he lived. Standing there, looking back at him is Chloe.

“Ryan?!” she says as her jaw drops.

Ryan gives a half smile and nods his head, unable to respond—he couldn't begin to guess what she's thinking right now. After all, it may have only been two or three days, but it feels like it's been an eternity. The fact that she is alive is nothing short of a miracle. He doesn't know what, but something stirs inside of him.

“We all thought you were dead,” she explains, almost ashamed of the comment.

Ryan studies her quizzically, “We?”

She takes a few steps closer to him, “The whole class made it out. We even saved people from other classes. All of us banded together and fought our way out. We've constructed a base and survived so much… all thanks to you for breaking the front doors open.”

“I…” he stops himself knowing he's about to divert blame. He can’t ruin this mood by more self-destructive attitudes. Isn’t this supposed to be a joyous reunion? “Where’s everyone else?”

Chloe smiles and looks out the window, “My friends are over in the gas station right now. The rest of us are all camped out in the food pantry a few blocks from here.”

“So you all stayed together?” Ryan surmises.

Chloe nods, “A bunch of people wanted to go home, but most of them returned when they found nothing to go home to. The ones who didn’t return either found their families or…”

Ryan sneaks the pistol underneath his shirt and approaches Chloe saying, “May I… may I join you?”

Chloe’s face lights up as she excitedly nods her head—something about the notion makes Ryan pause. His heart nearly skips a beat at the thought of someone accepting him rather than criticizing him. He takes a deep breath perishing the thought as he follows Chloe out.

Heading over to the gas station, they meet the rest of Chloe’s scavenging group. Just like before, the group consists of her, Alison, and Beth. None of them have any traditional weapons, but Beth keenly wields a metal pipe in one of her hands, though she doesn’t look like she has ever had to use it.

“Ryan?!” Beth exclaims as she and Alison exit through the front door with a nostalgic bell clinging, “You’re still alive!”

He nods his head as they all form a circle in front of a gas station pump. Each of the girls wear a backpack which look to be rather void of contents.

“Chloe told me that you guys have a base nearby,” Ryan explains, suddenly finding it harder to speak.

“We do… but everyone is going to be so surprised when you show up,” Beth says.

“Why?” Ryan glances between them.

Alison jumps in, “You’re a hero! After drawing the attention of the entire school all of the zombies just disappeared. After finding the doors wide open, we just assumed it was you.”

Ryan nods his head but doesn’t respond, in the corner of his eye he spots some motion. Upon looking over he finds a zombie beginning its charge at the group. None of the other girls spot it as it flails its twisted arms around silently. The skin on its face sags down blocking out one eye, but the other is wide open filled with rage and pain. The zombie’s ragged business suit creates a juxtaposed sight which causes Ryan to stutter at the thought of the former humanity.

He forces his body to move as he reaches out and swipes the metal bar out of Beth's hands. She's surprised by his action at first, but when her eyes meets the real danger, the true horror sets it. Alison stumbles backwards onto the ground and Chloe immediately attempts to get her friend back up.

Ryan steps away from the group as the zombie silently charges them, growing closer by the millisecond. He notices it's tie flailing around like a flag in the wind as it runs haphazardly. The sad possibility that he might still be in there dawns on him, and it makes him squeeze the metal bar much, much harder. It that really were the case, and he couldn't save him on his own, the best thing he could offer this man is a swift end as to relieve him of the horrors he now has to bear witness to silently. 

Ryan draws the bar back and right as the zombie is about to grapple him, he slams the blunt edge right into the undead's temple. It drops immediately as it slaps around on the ground trying to regain its footing and continue its attack. Ryan shoves a kick into it's side as it rises to its knees and proceeds to slam the bar right into zombie's head. The first blow hardly stuns the creature in which case he kicks the zombie down once more and goes in for another lethal blow. This time the connecting impact penetrates the skull and the zombie goes limp. 

In all of that, Ryan can't help but feel sorry for the man. He must have been screaming inside to stop—to prevent another catastrophe from unfolding. But it's all over now, he can finally rest in peace. And through all of that, the only thing Ryan feels is a bit of adrenaline.

“Let’s go back to your base,” Ryan commands, as he offers the bloody metal bar back to Beth.

Alison stares at the corpse in shock, trembling from the violence of it all. Beth looks no better, her eyes fixed on Ryan with open disbelief.

Chloe watches him too, though her expression is different. Her breathing is uneven, but there’s no fear in her eyes—only a quiet familiarity, as if some part of her had expected Ryan to move when nobody else could.

Beth hesitantly accepts the weapon being sure not to touch any of the blood. Her eyes don't leave Ryan as she takes a step down the street, “O-okay then, let’s hurry.”

They start a light jog down the road, being careful not to turn any corners without looking both ways before crossing the street. The last thing they would want to happen is to rush into a group of zombies without knowing.

“For some reason, there's not that many zombies around here. There should be a lot more based on the town’s population,” Chloe says as they jog past a laundry mat.

“They probably tried to evacuate,” Ryan responds knowing what these streets could look like.

“Really?” Alison says with a heavier breath than the rest of them, “Do you think a lot of them got away? Maybe a family of four but missing one.”

Ryan shakes his head at the lack of subtly, yet can't help but feel his heart ache as he thinks about his own mistakes. The charred scene of the camp makes him bounce out of his thoughts.

“Yeah,” he lies, “Plenty of people did.”

Alison smiles with contentment; she could rest easy knowing that the people she was referring to are most likely still alive. Everyone will find out about the military camp eventually, but he doesn’t need to be the bearer of that news—not yet.

The group rounds the last corner, the corner with a cozy cafe, and arrives at the food pantry. Said food pantry is a chain of two-story buildings all connected to each other. On the ground floor is a small movie rental store, a barber shop, an Italian restaurant, and a food pantry. On the top floor are four apartment-style homes. The parking lot is surrounded by cars parked end to end enclosing a space in between. On top of each car are pieces of plywood screwed into the cars to hold them together creating a makeshift barrier. To a teenager, it's probably a full-on DIY medieval fortress. In the front of the wall closest to the street is some kind of billboard on wheels; he assumes it must be the entrance.

“Home sweet home… or at least, all we’ve got for now,” Beth introduces as the door opens for them.

“Welcome back you guys, find anything?” a boy, probably a freshman, says from the other side of the billboard.

Inside the walls of the makeshift fortress are around twenty former high school students working on fortifying the walls or simply killing time. In the center of the fort is a brand new white corvette. Three people stand around the hood of the car talking and pointing at a map.

Beth smiles up to the door man as she pulls off her backpack, “A few snacks, a few water bottles, and… oh yeah, this guy,” she points to Ryan.

The freshman shoots Ryan an incredulous look, “Who’s he?”

“Ryan!” someone yells from deeper inside the base. Their voice carries a particular authority that the other teens don't. Ryan can't quite put why he feels that way, but it all becomes clear as Blake, the same guy from the classroom, reveals himself. He smiles broadly as he steps past a curious cliché of teens and arrives before the scouting party.

“I can’t believe you’re here!” he exclaims, gaining attention from anyone who wasn't already invested. “After we escaped the school and found so many people missing, we naturally assumed we would never see again. Yet here you are.”

“Yeah,” Ryan says awkwardly, rubbing the back of his head, “Mind catching me up?”

By this point, enough eyes have settled on them that Ryan's heart begins to race. He finds it odd to think he killed a zombie with a lower heart rate than this, yet here he is. If only they knew the things that he'd seen, their eyes wouldn't be as curious as they are now. He makes a quick attempt at perishing the thought by crossing his arms and taking a deep breath.

“Right,” Blake begins, “I guess you probably don't know anything since the school incident,” the comment, particularly the 'incident' part, enacts a visceral reaction for many of the former students who turn away in disdain. Blake, however, doesn't seem as bothered by it, "After you made that spectacle through the hallways, the school became frighteningly quiet. There were a few stragglers, no doubt, but the school was basically empty. We tried to round up any survivors and escape together. Afterwards, most people went their own way, but everyone here agreed to stick together... some people did try and go home, but... well, let's just say they're here for a reason."

"I see," Ryan comments knowing too well what that situation might look like.

Blake looks around at all the eyes and makes a quick decision as he moves towards a barber shop on the first floor, "Let's talk more inside."

Ryan follows breaking away from the mass of teenagers outside and into the much quieter, much more secure, barber shop. While Alison and Beth disperse into the crowd, Chloe follows directly behind him and closes the door behind.

"Strangely enough, zombies haven't really been much of a problem," Blake explains as he takes a seat on raised counter, "We get a few wandering by here and there, but it's... too quiet."

“Have you seen the mutants?” Ryan asks with a griming expression as he recalls the monster on the highway.

Blake’s smile fades as he realizes the gravity of his statement.

“Chloe, could you please wait outside for a minute,” Blake asks with a very kind voice.

She glances at Ryan before nodding and making a silent departure.

Blake waits to speak until the door closes, “She talked about you more than anyone else,” he explains, “...sorry not important. But back to the point–mutants,” Blake hops off the counter and leans against the back of a barber chair. “Me and two other guys were looting a convenience store when we saw it. Some kind of mutant with four legs, a shark-like head, more teeth than you can counter, and some sort of sprouting wings. I've never seen anything like it... I couldn't even move," he latches onto his pinky finger as his gaze drops, "I couldn't do anything. I just... I let them..." he takes a deep breath as he seemingly shoves the boil emotions far enough down that they disappear from his face, "No one else knows... and I haven't seen on since." 

“I'm confident they're rare” Ryan explains, "I'm not sure the first one counts, but the second one... I fought the second one."

“Fought one?” Blake wonders incredulously.

Ryan looks away, “Yeah... I'd prefer not to talk about it... not even guns could bring it down."

“Guns? What exactly have you been up to the last few days?” Blake's eyes narrow as he studies him.

Ryan sighs as past visions cement themselves in his forefront of his memory. He clenches his fist as he relives the pain, sorrow, and despair. No matter how he feels about it, he can't let it get to him. He just—he has to keep pushing forward, one day at a time.

“I don’t really want to get into it, but my stepdad had some guns that we used to try and leave the city.”

“Leave?” Blake asks seemingly more invested in the conversation with each dodge of the question.

Ryan nods, “Yeah, leave.”

“So why are you here?” Blake questions.

Ryan takes a deep breath as he looks around the dim barber shop interior, ”...I don’t want to talk about it.”

“...okay,” Blake comments, trying to seem empathetic and understanding, “I do want to know, Ryan, did you make that hole in the front doors? Back at school I mean,” Blake asks standing back up straight.

Ryan snaps out of his spontaneous thought paralysis and shakes his head, “We'll, kinda. I tore down a piece... and they did the rest.”

Blake nods his head in thought as he considers everything he’s heard, “I guess you’ve really been through the thick of it, haven’t you?” he says rhetorically as he begins to pace around the room. “You know, having you show up is the first good thing that's happened. It's almost sad how our lucks been. We got here with thirty five... and... no one's supposed to count, but we're at twenty three right now... scouting missions mostly."

Ryan looks down at his feet for a moment, “I'm sorry.”

Blake shakes his head, "It's fine. All my friends are still in the school," he says strikingly callous, as if he the deaths meant little to him. This interpretation is immediately followed by Blake pressing his fist against the chair and biting his lip. When his eyes match Ryan's, he knocks it off and returns to a professional demeanor, "Regardless, out of everyone here, you're the first person to show up that I think I can trust."

Ryan avoids eye contact, "I'm not so sure about that."

Blake inspects him thoughtfully as he continues, “Most everyone here is an underclassman. As it stands now, we have you, me, Caid, Chloe, Beth, Alison, Max, and Jeremy for seniors and everyone else is young enough that I don't know them. Of the seniors, I suspect only Caid, you and I would actually win in a struggle against a zombie one-on-one. There are a few promising juniors but, again, I don’t know them well enough to make any assumptions... besides, if they were truly fearless, they wouldn't have went on a scouting mission already. At the end of the day, if we get attacked, we don’t really stand much of a chance, especially since most of the people here are terrified of leaving the safety of this base. The three girls who brought you here are some of the few who are still willing to leave. It’s a shame all the football players wanted to tough it out on their own,” Blakes takes a deep breath as he pinches the bridge of his nose, “And to top it all off, I have to put up with everyone grieving for their lost friends and family. Believe me," he says frustratedly, "I get it... I get it, but we don’t have time to grieve right now. Not with zombies and mutants running around out there. I don't want to be the leader right now... but I will if I have to.”

“Why are you telling me all of this?” Ryan wonders, finding the guy’s words to be strangely heartfelt and sincere. 

Blake doesn’t shy away from his conviction, “Honestly, it’s because of what you've done. While everyone else was freaking out, about to make matters worse, you built up the courage to lead everyone to survival. To top it all off, you made it back here all on your own through the crap going on out there. Half the people here, I've never met in my life, and now I'm supposed to keep them alive... this is not what I wanted out of high school."

Blake’s last words echo through the room or maybe just inside his head. His outlook strikes Ryan somewhere deeply, somewhere Ryan didn't know could be touched. For some reason, Blake feels like the first guys he's meet that wasn't trying to hide something from him. That list includes himself.

“Will you help me?” Blake asks, holding up his hand.

Maybe what Ryan was searching for all along wasn’t a place he wanted to be at, but a place he belonged in. It’s too early to tell, but at least he can regain grasp of that sliver of light he once called hope. Perhaps that masked man was right, purpose is not given, it's found.

“I will,” Ryan says as he accepts Blake’s handshake.

After their hands return to their sides, Blake crosses his arms and starts transitioning towards the door, “I’m glad to have you, but there’s one thing that needs to be done before anything else.”

“Hm?” Ryan responds.

Blake nods his head towards the door as he opens it, “Let me show you.”

They leave the barber shop with a multitude of eyes immediately locking in on them. Most of the bashful gazes are just bored teens dangling their legs off the second floor balcony, but a few people seemed genuinely scared of the circumstances. He honestly can’t imagine what they’ve all been through the last few days, but at least he’s been through the thick of it as well so they’re all in the same boat.

Blake leads him into one of the apartment rooms on the second floor. He notices that the door has very conspicuous dents right around the handle as if it was pried open with shear force. Once inside, they head straight past a table of kids playing poker at the kitchen table and through the living room to the back window. From there, Blake points at something behind the building.

“That garage over there,” he says gesturing towards a gated storage lot across a small field, “Some people have been saying that they hear banging from one of those storage garages at night. Normally I’d say it’s not worth the risk of investigating, but it's terrifying people to the point that there’s talk of relocating the base.”

Ryan looks back at the oblivious teens throwing cards on the table and laughing. Despite their easy going facades, Ryan can hear the unease in their tone as no one makes a sex joke or directs their comments at one another. It’s subtle, but hormonal guys don’t act like that at their age.

“I think we should check it out,” Blake continues, “It’s probably just one zombie stuck in a garage, we should be able to handle it.”

Ryan nods, “Yeah, you’re probably right… but why do we both need to go? Shouldn't you stay here?”

Blake tilts his head back with offense, “And let you do this by yourself. Who do you think I am?”

“But what about the camp?” Ryan clarifies.

Blake shakes his head, “This won’t take long, besides, everyone here isn’t useless, they just prefer to stay put rather than venture out. As long as no one does anything reckless, they don’t need someone to give them constant orders.”

Ryan nods his head in understanding as they turn to leave.

Blake glances at the guys playing cards, “Besides, I know these kids will stay in line."

One of the underclassmen lifts his head up with some sort of instinctual resentment towards the comment, “Who are you calling a kid?”

Two of the other boys around the table motion as if to make an underhanded comment, but both of them pull back just before vocalizing their thoughts. One of the boys even looks down ashamed of the thought. Blake simply shrugs his shoulders as he and Ryan exit the apartment and head back to the open space underneath.

As Blake sorts through some supplies in the back of the barber shop and finds a pocket knife, Chloe approaches them with Beth and Alison. 

“What are you guys doing?” she asks innocently.

Blake shakes his head as he fastens the sheath of the knife to the belt on his jeans, “We’re just going to check out that noise behind the building.”

Chloe perks up a bit but Beth’s the one who raises an eyebrow and says, “Do you need some help?”

“It’s fine,” Ryan immediately counters. He knows this could easily become a difficult situation and having too many people would lead to an unorganized mess that could get people injured or worse. Although the injury was on its way towards healing, he could still feel the bite mark in his shoulder and scabbed over cut on his arm.

“Why?” Beth argues, “The more people the better.”

Blake looks like he’s about to argue against the idea further but he chokes on his words and ends up biting his lip instead. Beth studies the guy before nodding her head knowingly, “I see how it is.”

Blake lowers his head in thought, taking the comment personally—and silently. Ryan tries to say something to clear things up but he can’t find the words either.

“I don’t think we should go,” Alison voices a bit timidly.

Beth nods suppressing poorly administered spite, “Right, that’s fine, we’ll just stay here.”

“Thanks,” Blake expresses with relief in his voice despite the conspicuous subtext, “It’s probably better this way.”

The three girls depart out the front door with Chloe giving a prolonged stare before following. The room remains motionless until the door completely swings shut.

Blake looks over to Ryan, “Beth’s always been a big help so I didn’t want to say no to her. She's gotten more supplies than anyone else, so I owe her that much.”

Ryan shrugs his shoulders, “At least she seems understanding.”

Blake rolls his eyes, "If you give a mouse a cookie. Beth can handle herself, but..." he looks around to ensure no one in the empty room is looking, "Alison can't."

Ryan purses his lip with understanding, "Sure."

Blake stands up as he tightens the straps on a small backpack, “Let’s get going.”
