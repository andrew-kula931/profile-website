The back garage door of the car dealership remains wide open when he returns. Angry voices echo through the building as he climbs over the fallen boxes at the base of the door. One of the voices, a deep one with authority, seems annoyed more than anything else. Another voice, much higher pitched and feminine pierces through the silence, yet her voice feels, slightly different that he remembers. Initially Ryan gauged her as an almost too perfect match of the gang leader's slut, but this time she feels a bit more authoritarian than Ryan remembered. He tries not to dwell on a trivial detail as he sneaks inside.

He creeps into the storage room walks along wall leading to the front. He peers through a mechanic's window and bites his tongue as he witness the scene beyond.

Caid, Max, and Blake are all kneeling next to each other with their hands behind their heads. Members of the gang slowly circle around the teens holding their weapons ready to beat them if they make any sudden moves. The hooded leader stands in front of the boys looking down on them with the slut wrapped around his right arm and his left arm in his pocket. The slut, who Ryan initially brushed off, slides her hand down into the hooded figure's back pocket. At first, Ryan believes this to be a terrible time to be disgustingly affectionate, but then he catches sight of a tint of silver as something slides out and into the woman's hand.

The hooded man spits on the floor in front of Blake, "You prison trash really ought to be more careful. Can't have kids like you running around out in this dangerous world." 

“What are you talking about?” Blake protests receiving a kick to the face knocking him to the ground.

“I never gave you permission to speak!” his voice full of anger before mellowing down to a more civil tone, "We saw the prison accepting any and every survivor they could find into their gates, but the second we showed up, we were turned down. The only explanation they gave was that we looked dangerous. Dangerous!? You wanna know what’s dangerous, go talk to the damn zombies!"

The woman lets go of his arm as he walks over to Caid, “You know, you guys are probably completely ignorant of what we went through, but you know what? I’m feeling merciful today. Jimmy’s not here with us right now so most of you will probably survive our beatings, I can’t say the same for your friend out there.”

Caid’s eyes widen as he realizes who the man is talking about. Max can’t even bring himself to hold his head up as the defeat and despair set in. Little did they know that Ryan isn’t dead and highly motivated to protect his friends. The only question now is what he will do to take down the gang. He isn’t a trained assassin or navy seal so he can’t use some advanced tactics to win the fight. Picking them off one by one seems to be the obvious choice, but he doesn't have the faintest clue how to knock someone out with only a absurdly sharp knife. 

“Alright boys, which one do we want to beat first?” the man with the hoodie begins to pace between the boys trying to select their first target.

Ryan leans to the side a bit more to try to get a better view of one of the guys in the back, but then the woman turns her head towards the window he's looking through. Ryan ducks below the frame as quickly as his body will allow and prays that she didn't see him. He waits for a few moments listening for any footsteps leading in his direction, but thankfully none approach. So, after a few more seconds of waiting, he takes the risk to look back and finds the woman re-engaged in the topic at hand.

“How bout we castrate them all?” one of the guys who looks a bit high says. 

“What is with you and castration?” the leader argues, “Are you recruiting again for your dickless club.”

The rest of the gang laughs at his joke but the high guy looks embarrassed by the comment. Once again, Ryan can't get it out of his mind that each gang member laughs like it's an inheritable trait from the same family tree. 

“Actually, change of plans,” the leader says, “How about we kill two of them and send the last one back with only a mild beating as a message to the prison.”

A few people respond with "yeah" and then go silent.

Then with the new plan decided on, the leader pulls out a pistol and points it at Blakes head. His places his finger on the trigger all the while Ryan can sense that he’s smiling under the hoodie.

Ryan doesn’t waste a moment more as he dashes out of the back room and straight to the leader. The whole distance is only a few yards but his footsteps give him away allowing for the leader to take notice and to turn his head. His gun immediately follows his line of sight as he swings around threatening Ryan with it, but he doesn’t allow this to happen without a fight. In a similar way to Jimmy—though less acrobatic—he kicks the gun out of the leader's hand as it swings around and he places his knife right up to the man’s throat. He holds it still there staring the man right in the eyes. His angry scowl and dragon tattoo around his left eye let Ryan know this man is the real deal.

“Let them go,” Ryan commands very quietly but with more conviction than he’s ever had before.

“Ryan!” Blake explains.

“What?!” the woman shouts, her surprise feeling almost manufactured, “Jimmy was supposed to deal with him.”

The leader puts his hands in the air signaling that he doesn’t want his throat slashed, which is good because Ryan doesn’t want to kill anyone.

“How’d you escape Jimmy?” he asks more calmly than a man should who could die at any moment. He then answers his own question when he looks down at the blade—a small trickle of blood running down his neck. Disturbingly, the man doesn't wince at the pain, he actually smirks.

“All of you leave, or he gets it!” Ryan yells aggressively at the gang, trying to stay focused on his audacious plan. 

Then despite his warnings, the woman reaches down and picks up the gun, pointing it at Ryan.

The leader waves his hand in protest, “Stop!” the woman follows the command and lowers the gun slightly.

“What are you saying?” she protests, “We can kill him right now.”

The man looks back into Ryan’s determined eyes. The eyes of someone who has walked to death's door and returned. Then, as if to make Ryan feel small, the man stares back with eyes so cold, they couldn't possibly be human. Eyes so disassociated from reality that death was no more a threat than existence itself. 

“These cars aren’t worth it,” he explains to the group, "If I know Jimmy, he'll be after this kid in no time. I wouldn't want to spoil his fun."

Ryan is the first one to respond, “You heard him, I’ll let him go AFTER all of you are out the door.”

The leader slides his hands into his pockets as relaxed as a walk on the beach, “Do as he says.”

Reluctantly, each of the gang members file out of the car dealership and back onto the street—each of their departures identical to the last. Then, all that remain are the gang leader and the slut. 

"He told you to leave," Ryan asserts pressing the blade a fourth of an inch into the man's neck.

The woman rolls her eyes, "I don't think I will."

Suddenly the woman raises the pistol in her hands right at Ryan's head. To evade the potential bullet, Ryan steps around the gang leader to use him as an obstacle obstruction a clear shot. Then, to prove that he's serious, he uses his free hand to grab his own pistol and points it the woman. This action causes Ryan's friends to gasp and the gang leader to smile.

The woman's eyes widen in shock, but not until after an unnaturally long delay, "Oh heavens what will I ever do?"

"Leave!" Ryan demands.

The woman raises the pistol into the air with one hand before carefully placing it on the ground, "Now, now we don't have to do anything hasty."

Ryan releases a sigh of relief as the pistol is carefully placed on the ground. The woman takes one step away from the weapon by staying close to the ground. It's suspicious behavior, but it's not like Ryan has a bullet to shoot as a warning.

Then, like a bolt of lightning, the woman whips out a small kunai and skillfully throws it right into Ryan's exposed shoulder. The blade doesn't sink in deep, but it's just enough for him to be caught off guard. The gang leader, maintaining a smile on his face, blocks Ryan's knife wielding hand out of the way and shoves a kick right into his core. All shock aside, the maneuver occurs so quickly it might as well have been the same technique Jimmy used against him—strikingly similar.

Taking a step back and gasping for air, Ryan looks back at the two thugs and finds Caid, Blake, and even Max rushing in to aid him. Caid throws a powerful hook right into the gang leader's face and the man takes it without resistance. Blake kicks the slut over but she rolls out of it and lands on her feet. Max tries to throw an elbow into the gang leader's side but he dodges out of the way and heads for the back door in a hurry. The woman follows him out as the teens don't pursue.

Right before the gang leader exits, he looks back at the boys, but particularly at Ryan with a sinister grin, "Just wait."

With that, Ryan is left with his friends and a bunch of empty cars. Just to be safe, they check on the gang and watch as the precarious bunch stroll back to the street and away from view. 

“Thank you so much!” Max shouts rushing over and patting Ryan on the back only to notice the kunai sticking out of his shoulder, "Oh, fuck!"

Ryan shakes his head, grabs onto the kunai, and tears it out. What he finds below is not a clear cut, instead he finds the jacket completely undamaged. When he pulls it back and looks underneath he finds a small wound creating a miniscule splotch of red in his shirt. Whatever happened, the strange leather jacked seemed to help but somehow protected himself more than anything else.

"You good?" Caid asks completely unprepared to offer first aid.

Ryan nods, "Yeah, it's just a scratch... seriously."

Blake inspects the wound from afar before coming to some personal conclusion on the matter, “By the way, what happened out there with that Jimmy guy?”

Ryan shakes his head. That fight was way more than he ever bargained for and he came out of it with far more questions than a simple victory. So much happened in that one fight that he doesn’t even know how to express it in words. 

“Jimmy was just a skilled fighter, but I was able to chase him off,” Ryan explains, wiping the bit of blood off of his knife. He then sheaths his blade, hopefully for the rest of their trip.

"That's not...", Blake starts but is quickly cut off by Caids more intentional comment.

"Skilled fighter, ay," Caid says knowingly, "Is that all?"

Turning away, Ryan shrugs his shoulders. It's not like him to flaunt his abilities, nor does he want to. That fight was anything but heroic and he only made it out alive because that masked man appeared and gave him a second chance. Now that he thinks about it, he finds it concerning that he always seems to show up when he's most in need, as if he's waiting for the optimal time to arrive. He looks over his shoulder and out the main windows of the car dealership searching for a pair of eyes—his search bares no fruits.

"More importantly," Max starts, "Since when did you have a gun?"

Ryan freezes. Does he say he's always had it? Lie and say he got it somewhere in between? Lie the other way and say his foster father gave it to him? How does he respond to it.

"He's had it a while," Blake defends with an affirming nod. It's not like he read Ryan's mind, but for some reason he seems inclined to support the guy who saved them, "He used it on a mutant yesterday. You got it before meeting us, right?"

All eyes turn to Ryan. He awkwardly shrugs his shoulders, "Yeah, something like that."

Max sighs, "If you don't want to tell us your secrets, that's fine. You can have one secret per life saved, but this is your last one. Now let’s reap some reward already," he holds up a key with the Corvette logo.

The boy's serious faces grow into smiles of joy as they each hop into the luxury Corvette and Max starts it up.

“This car is a beauty,” Caid admires as he looks at the perfectly clean leather seats. 

“Tell me about it,” Max flaunts, “This bad boy is all mine, you guys can go pick out some other ones. The keys are in the room over there, I already picked the lock.”

“How do you know how to pick locks?” Caid asks as he opens the back door to get out.

“Don’t worry about it,” Max says dismissively, “I have my ways.”

“Regardless, I can’t believe we actually went through with this plan,” Blake reiterates as he looks around at the inventory of new cars.

“Yeah, I can’t believe it either. To think our day was going to go like this,” Caid recalls.

“Well I don’t know about you guys,” Max starts additively, “but I’m glad we came out here and did this. I feel so alive doing stupid shit like this," he raises his fist out the window of the Corvette and into the air, "For our friends!"

"For our friends!" Caid joins.

Blake sighs with a subtle smirk before lifting his own hand, "For our friends... all of them."

Max leans back into his seat, “You know, I really wish Seth and Danny could have seen this," he turns to Caid beside him, "Did you know Seth?"

Caid rolls his eyes, "Of course I know Seth, if there was one dumbass that could trump you, it'd be him."

Blake looks into the window of the Corvette with an incredulous look, "Are you talking about Seth Vargo?"

Max nods with a smile, "This is absolutely something he'd do, no?"

Blake sighs, "I hate that you're right about that. I didn't know him personally but he was infamous for stuff like this. Everyone knew about him. Thank goodness I didn't hang out with him when he got caught by the police twice."

“That's valid, you've got me anyways,” Max turns around in the drivers seat to make sure Ryan is still with them, "You guys are pretty chill. Too bad it took us this long to become friends."

Ryan knows he wants to say something but his usual habit of silence makes him choke on his words before even saying a word. 

Max hops out of the car and holds his fist over the roof expectantly, “Cheers to the The Boys.”

Caid and Blake both join the group knuckles before each of them look to Ryan to join. It takes him a moment to break his concentration on the window but eventually he looks at the three hands in the middle of the car. He hesitates, unsure of whether he deserves to be part of something so special, but then he shakes his head. Things won't change unless he makes them, after all, he's down to his last life.

“Cheers!” they collectively announce.

“Alright,” Max says with a playful smile, "Now get out of my damn car."

The three other boys each head to the back room to check out the key situation and find that the door leading to the back room has been opened, just as Max described. It looks almost like a vault door to get inside the small room with useless documents and keys. To their convenience, each key has a small tag with the name of a car model on it. Honestly, Ryan only recognizes a small minority of the cars by name, but Caid and Blake seem significantly more cultured and intrigued by the selection. Caid grabs some fob with twenty something number and Mustang while Blake grabs one to a Ford truck keys. Blake mutters under his breath that it’ll be useful as a work vehicle, though Caid seems to have a specific attachment to his choice. Ryan on the other hand has no idea which car to choose because he doesn’t know how to drive. He considers admitting it to his friends, but know he'll get an earful for never learning despite being eighteen. Of course, he planned on learning at some point, but his sixteenth birthday and beyond have been anything but conducive to driving instruction. The basic concept seems intuitive enough, he just hopes he doesn't shift it into reverse when he wants to go forward—does that happen often? Then again, if he is going to learn to ride something, he might as well pick a good first car. Then the solution jumps out at him. If he doesn't know how to drive, the best way to hide it would be to drive something no one else knows how to drive. There's only one of it's kind on the lot, but he'll try it out: the motorcycle.

As the other two boys grab their keys and go search for their new vehicles, Ryan sneaks the motorcycle key into his pocket. He has a feeling motorcycles aren't as easy as they look, but they likely can weave through a traffic jam much easier than a full vehicle and their fast too—everyone knows that last part. Those two things alone would make up for any necessary embarrassment that would follow sitting on a motorcycle. Then again, anything can sneak up on you when you don't have walls around you. Just to be safe, he grabs a generic set of keys that probably goes to a soccer mom's minivan.

After searching the premises for a good two minutes he finally finds it. The only motorcycle on the lot in between two SUVs. He glances around to ensure no one is nearby and silently hops on, placing the key into the ignition. It starts up with a loud rumble which makes his heart race before the noise quickly fades into a much more low-key hum of a working engine. Looking around the bike, he looks for some sort of pedal to, you know, start moving but, frankly, he finds nothing of the sort. He does find one pedal which has significant resistance and seemingly no effect on the engine so he decides it must be the break. After another glances around the car, he ends up stepping off and inspecting the vehicle at a distance. There's plenty of knobs and switches, but he can't, for the life of him, figure out what makes the vehicle go. Eventually he stumbles upon a small metal protrusion from the bottom of the bike that sticks into the ground, seemingly holding a fair portion of the vehicles weight. He mentally catalogs it as something he'll have to move if he wants to actually move.

Feeling a bit disheartened, he looks over the vehicle one last time, conceding to the fact that he'll probably never figure it out. Then, right as he's about to find the soccer mom car, he spots something interesting. Along the handle bar he finds a long metal lever running parallel with the handle. Thinking he's finally figuring it out, he hops back on, carefully kicks the kickstand up with his food—though it takes more effort and finagling then it probably should—and proceeds to pull the left lever inward slowly with his hands firmly on both handlebars. To his dismay, nothing happens. He pulls the lever all the way in just to be sure and still nothing happens. In defeat, he leans forward, hits a few things with his chest, and rolls his hands forward along across the handlebars, and only then does the bike decide to come to life.

The motorcycle lurches forward and knocks him off as he falls to his back onto the concrete. The bike, with it's sudden, short-lived action, rolls a few feet forward before falling to its side and sliding and additional six inches on the pavement. Ryan rubs his side as he is slow to get to his feet.

Blake appears from the line of cars with a humorous, surprised look, "Of all the things you could struggle with?"

Ryan averts his gaze, "It's not what it looks like."

Blake laughs briefly, "It looks like you just took a mouthful of concrete while your motorcycle got away."

"I've never driven one okay," Ryan defends as he stands back up. It bugs him that this catastrophic miscalculation needed to be witnessed by arguably the most competent teen here. He's bound to get a earful now.

"Did you use the clutch?" Blake asks as he inspects the vehicle, "Probably hit it into gear."

"What?" Ryan wonders, "I just lost control is all."

Blake smiles, "I've never driven one but my uncle did. I'm no expert but I can give you a few pointers."

Ryan studies the boy incredulously, "You're not going to question me?"

Blake shrugs, "It's the most dangerous vehicle on the lot, but for some reason... I feel like you of all people might be able to pull it off. You know, because you're not Max."

Ryan snickers politely finding the witticism to be a little out of character but more than welcome. After that, Blake leans the motorcycle back upright and proceeds to point out a few necessary features on the bike. He, of course, exposes the—not one but two—breaks and the twist-action throttle. He also explains a few other features to varying detail but Ryan is only able to catch the general notion of it all. Essentially, don't lean too far, but expect to lean. Don't go too fast, but it's always a possibility. Don't treat it like a bicycle—something he also had little experience with—because they're not the same thing. Once the explanation concludes, he offers to show Ryan how it's done with a small circle around the lot. Blake's subsequent demonstration is far from perfect as his control shakes a little, starting off, but he gets all the way around the lot without incident, pops the kickstand, and hands it over to Ryan.

"To be honest, that's the first time I've actually ever done that," Blake admits, rubbing the back of his head with a bit of embarrassment.

Ryan finds it odd that Blake of all people is feeling embarrassed by this, but he just shakes his head. At least he's not a complete novice anymore—just a fresh rookie.

Under Blake's direction, Ryan hops on the vehicle and starts pulling forward, being extra careful not to overcompensate on balance—since he was directly advised against it. He finds it to be extraordinarily awkward to move his arms forward and backwards to turn, but driving at a blazing speed of five miles per hour, he finds it rather straightforward to maneuver on. Before too long, he makes a full circle around the lot and returns, not just to Blake, but to Max and Caid too.

"Fantastic work!" Max cheers, "You're like a stunt man now."

Caid shakes his head, "Nah, he's working his way up to being a Spirit of Vengeance."

Max strokes his chin in thought, clearly giving the sarcastic remark far too much speculation. He ends up just laughing which draws Caid in too.

"What it that bad?" Ryan asks, praying that his face doesn't grow red.

Blake shakes his head, "For the first time every riding a motorcycle, I'd say you're a natural."

"But it was your first time too," Ryan points out.

The comment should level the playing field to some degree, at least in Ryan's mind, but Blake doesn't seem to take it that way at all, "I've had a real teacher, or... uh, I've had an uncle how knows a lot. All you got was little ole me."

Caid smacks the guy lightly on the back, "Give yourself some credit. I was ease dropping on your little class and it made perfect sense to me."

Blake rolls his eyes, but he can't hide the sliver of pride.

Max looks straight at Ryan, "Think you got the hang of it?"

Ryan shakes his head.

Completely disregarding it, as if the question was rhetorical to begin with, Max flips around and marches off, "Alright, let's head back to base with our bounty."

---

The journey back to the prison required quite a few detours due to traffic and civil unrest, at one point a few zombies even give chase, but with proper hardware the the chase doesn't last long. Ryan's bike unexpected shuts off a few times, something he vaguely recalls Blake calling "stalling", but after a few failed attempts he starts to pick up on how sliding to a halt is supposed to work. He also loses his balance three times and nearly falls off but he manages to stomp his foot into the ground and wobble to a stop. It's a miracle traffic is slow and stopping in the middle of an intersection doesn't get him pulled over. Max does a lap around him, laughing the whole time, but then he slides in between cars in a traffic jam and ends up with the last laugh.

When they are just shy of the prison, Blake pulls to a stop in the middle of the road and gets out to talk to Max who was trailing right behind him. Caid isn't far behind with Ryan coming in at an abysmal last place. 

“Yeah, we should probably do that,” Max agrees as Ryan approaches.

“What is the hold up?” Caid complains as he steps out of his Mustang.

“We’re trying to figure out what to say when we pull up with a bunch of cars. It'll be pretty obvious just how far into the city we went,” Blake explains.

Max crosses his arms, "I was saying that we should just pull up and say nothing. We're all back, that's what's important."

Blake shakes his head, "I think we should just be honest. There's no sense in hiding things."

Caid shrugs while Ryan decides that he'll likely be the deciding vote. He knows that his input is far from informed, but he's inclined to take Blake's side on this one.

"I think we should just answer any questions they have," Ryan says.

All eyes turn to him. Despite taking a side, no one seems particularly offended, Max namely. They all sort of shrug their shoulders, agree to do just that, and head back to their cars. Unsure why it worked out so well, he heads back to the motorcycle right behind Caid.

Caid opens the Mustang door and looks back at Ryan. He doesn't say anything, he just nods.

With that, they return to the prison.

---

Upon arriving to the metal chain link gates, they are greeted by a multitude of ARs and suspicious gazes. A guy whom Ryan vaguely remembers being called Xavier, exits the watch tower and leans over the railing. He looks between the cars cautiously until he spots Ryan in the back. 

"What the heck?" Xavier calls out, "Is it you kids?"

Ryan waves up to him timidly as Blake and Max stick their heads out their windows. They wave a bit more flamboyantly just to push the narrative that they were not rando's trying to get in. Xavier blinks a few times just to make sure he's not seeing things but eventually comes to his senses as Blake calls out for the door to be opened.

They receive quite a few confused, yet curious looks as they drive their strange assortment of vehicles through the front gates. 

Ryan struggles quite considerably trying to park the darn motorcycle as he can't seem to slow down without the vehicle wobbling. Feeling the pressure of the people around him, he elects to hop off the bike entirely and push it the last few feet to a parking spot next to Blake's truck. Only once he pulls out the kickstand and steps away, does the crushing weight of people's attention subside.

"What's all this?" Michael asks as he appears from inside the prison.

Max rubs the back of his head with a conspicuous grin as he steps away from the Corvette, "You know, we just thought this place could use some more horsepower."

Michael releases an exasperated sigh, "We actually don't, and now I want to know how exactly you even got them. There's no place with a Corvette, Mustang, and Harley Davison anywhere near here."

Blake steps into the conversation with a much more serious disposition, "We went downtown."

Michael hesitates as he does a double take, "Downtown? You mean to tell me that you went all the way to the dealership on fifth?!"

Blake nods.

To say that he was shocked would be a severe understatement. Michael starts off by pacing which causes the four teenagers to grow uneasy. He then starts talking to himself, which makes Max squirm and Caid shove his hands into his pockets to avoid looking as nervous as his friend. One would think, this would be the worst of it, save a justified punishment, but no, Ryan crosses his arms and exposes his pistol just enough that Michael catches wind. 

Michael points at the gun, "What's that?"

Instinctively Ryan pulls his jacket forward to hide the gun better, though he knows it's far too late. 

"Hey calm down," Max butts in trying to cover for Ryan—something he wasn't expecting the talkative teen to do.

Michael shoots Max a very serious glare which makes him back off immediately, the warden then takes a few steps towards Ryan to stand right in front of him. He holds out his hand, "Give it here."

"...it's empty," Ryan tries.

"Don't care," Michael says immediately, "there are no guns inside the camp unless one's been allocated to you."

"But..." Ryan tries his hand holding the gun defensively.

Michael doesn't seem to be taking no for an answer, "No buts, hand it over son."

Reluctantly, Ryan gives in to the demand and unholsters the empty pistol. He looks over the cool metal one last time knowing this is where they must part ways, maybe for quite a while. Then, not wanting to be a problem he offers it up, but a hand suddenly gets in between him and the warden.

"Hold on," Drew says swiftly as he pushes the pistol back into Ryan's chest, "I don't think confiscation is the right thing here."

Michael narrows his eyes at Drew as the—normally aviator wearing—man jumps right in the middle of the conversation. He raises his chin a bit and says, "Care to explain why?"

Drew smiles confidently, not to show off but to assert that he's not stressed by Michaels intimidating presence one bit, "Look at it this way, the kid had a gun before coming, he had the gun while he was here, and he probably used the gun to keep everyone save."

The argument feels a bit flimsy for Ryan's standards, but he is both skeptical and touched for the man's aid. It's not that Ryan needs the pistol, but he does like the security it brings, even without having bullets in the magazine—his interaction with Jimmy proved that.

As Ryan suspected, Michael doesn't buy the "heat of the moment" logic and promptly shakes his head, "No, even if I did make you the temporary head of security, that's not a secure mentality."

Drew glances back at Ryan, his eyes fall to Ryan's chest, namely at the concealed sheath that blends in with his black jacket. He pretends not to see it.

"Okay," Drew admits, "I realize I might sound a bit hasty, but I need him to have the gun when I take him out to the city."

Ryan decides his confusion cannot wait any longer as he puts his pistol back in its holster, "The city?"

Drew nods as he glances between both Michael and him, "I need your help with something in the city. I'm sure you're tired, so we'll leave first thing in the morning."

Michael steps forward, a bit of impatience in his actions, as he pushes Drew back to ensure he's in between him and Ryan, "Even so, I can't allow a kid to walk around here with a gun," he turns to Ryan, "Give it to me, and I'll return it to you before you leave... that reminds me," he turns back to Drew with a disapproving look, "Why do you need him anyway? If you have something important to do in the city, there are plenty of adults that would be willing to go with you."

Drew shakes his head, "No, it's a personal matter..." his gaze lands directly on Ryan, something serious—something dangerous—hiding behind that composed demeanor, "And he's involved."
