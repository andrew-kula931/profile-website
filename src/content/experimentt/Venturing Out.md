_Ryan stands silently in the hallway staring down towards his mothers room. The large wooden door with a smiley face painted in marker sits closed and locked. The door to her room is always open except for one time: when his father is home. At first he didn’t really understand why the door was closed or why his parents would hide back there. He would just go along with his business and set up his army men or whatnot, but this time he knew. His father had the audacity to come home once a month and take away his mother for his own sick pleasure. He was absent and this is all he can think to do.

_Ryan storms down the hallway finding that the door is smaller than it used to be… no he’s grown, he’s not some little helpless kid anymore. He picks up his leg and throws the strongest more rage filled kick he could muster breaking the old door open.

_Inside sits his father, forehead against his mother’s chest with his hands covering his face. Ryan’s mother wraps her arms around her husband and calmly rubs one hand comfortingly on his back. For a brief moment, he could almost make out the sobbing words, “I don’t want to do this anymore.”

_As soon as it began, Ryan’s father snapped around giving his attention to Ryan.

_“Oh hey son, do you want me to come play army men with you?” he asks, so compartmentalized that it sends a chill down Ryan's spine.

_Ryan doesn’t know what to say, his assumption couldn’t be that far off, could it. This has to be a dream, no—a nightmare. His father was a a heartless monster who didn't even show up to his own wife's funeral, who left to him to years of foster nonsense, this could not be a forgotten memory. He refuses to accept this to be the truth, that his father had any feelings for this family. 

_His father gets off the bed and kneels down next to the five year old Ryan. 

_“Let’s go have a bit of fun, what do you say,” his father puts a hand on his shoulder.

_“This can’t be real,” Ryan says.

_“It is real,” his father says more seriously this time.

_“No, it's not. You never let anyone cry around you. You were a heartless bastard who was never home and then came home and acted like you owned the place,” Ryan argues, taking a step back rubbing his hands through his, now 18 year old, hair.

_His father stands up, looking to match Ryan’s grown height, “Is that what really happened or is that just what you wanted to see?”

_“Mom... she waited for you. Every time she waited for you and you never showed up. She couldn’t give you up no matter how much of a burden you forced upon her. I can’t stand you!” Ryan practically yells.

_He closes his eyes and shakes his head trying to escape from this nightmare—from this false reality trying to trick him into giving his father any sympathy. 

_“It’s okay Ryan.”

_Ryan freezes and slowly releases his hands from over his eyes. That voice could only belong to one man: Steve.

_“I may not have been your real father, but you discovered that there is more to me than meets the eye,” Steve says while standing in the burned out military camp, “My life was difficult just like yours. I never had a mom waiting to give me a hug when I scraped my knee or a father to play ball with. Despite that, I gave it my all to try and build a better life.”

_Ryan’s breathing grows uneven and he can feel tears build up inside his eyes, but for some reason they won’t come out. He can’t force himself to cry no matter how badly he desires it.

_“Just hear this:” Steve pauses briefly, his voice descending into that of a particular masked man's, "You must survive." Steve pulls out a pistol from behind him and places it to his temple.

_“NO!” Ryan screams, beginning to dash towards him.

**Bang**

Ryan awakens in a cold sweat and flings himself to a seated position. His heart races as his hands shake nervously for something that only existed in his head. As he looks around the dark, empty room, he finds himself clutching his arms and gritting his teeth. 

The nightmares aren't getting better. Every time he thinks things are moving in the right direction, he finds himself in this downward spiral—a spiral that he isn't sure where it's leading. His priorities are becoming more tangible, so why doesn't he feel like things are getting better? What is missing?

He sits silently in bed until the sun rises a few hours later.

About an hour later after taking a much needed shower, grabbing some breakfast, and playing rock paper scissors with a little kid to open the door to his hallway, he finds himself sneaking into the girl's room to retrieve his gun, at Chloe's request of course. To his surprise, no one is there when he arrives except for Chloe and Alison but Allison quickly leaves as he arrives.

"You can seriously hold onto it," Ryan offers making sure no one is ease dropping.

Chloe shakes her head as she reaches into her bag and hands the contraband over to him, "I'd feel better if you took it with you. Besides, some of the girls started going through each other's stuff so... I didn't want to get you in trouble."

"Me?" Ryan repeats a bit dumbfounded.

Chloe looks away with a nervous nod.

"Alright," Ryan says as he reattaches the pistol to his belt. Upon putting the belt back on a second time, he finds that the part that wraps around his waste can actually sit a bit higher comfortably which means the pistol can be concealed underneath his jacket with moderate success.

Right as he finishes adjusting his jacket, Max appears in the doorway.

"I knew you'd be here," he says with a mischievous grin, "Everyone's waiting for you, let's go."

A few minutes later, Ryan finds himself standing at the gate with Blake, Max, and Caid.

“You boys ready to go get that Corvette?” Max says with a little subtle wink.

“Heck yeah,” Caid agrees, “We can really test out its zero to sixty with no one around.”

Blake nods, “Yeah, but be careful the car can get pretty loud.”

Drew, leaning against the railing of a guard tower and smoking a candy cigarette, notices their approach and laughs to himself briefly, "Why am I not surprised."

"We just want to get back to our old base and collect a few things we left," Blake explains, rather sincerely given what he knew.

Drew looks back into the two and says something inaudible to another man inside. They discuss something quietly for a few moments while the boys look at each other nervously. Eventually, Drew glances back around lowers his aviators, "Can I trust you kids."

Blake waves the radio in the air, “We understand the risk and will be responsible, you can trust me… I don’t know about them.”

Max puts his hand underneath his armpit and flips Blake off, causing Caid to snicker. Ryan isn't sure, but he suspects the boy much do this exact routine quite often for someone like Caid to laugh at the disrespect.

"Alright," Drew says rather easily, "A particular kid about your age once taught me not to hold back ambition. I'll let you guys pass."

"Great, thanks," Blake admits politely, answering for their entire group who nod respectfully.

“Oh one more thing,” Drew says right before opening the gate, his eyes glossing over the four boys but ultimately settling on Ryan, "Blake, if you come across another mutant like you saw yesterday, don't fight it. You got lucky yesterday, but I have reason to believe they're not going to get friendlier."

Ordinarily, this would strike Ryan as reasonable and he'd think nothing of it, but something felt off the way he said it. Maybe it's his suspicion about what Blake and Drew could have been talking about. Did he tell him about his gun? But Drew already knew. The other thing that felt unsettling is that Drew was addressing Blake, yet his gaze never left Ryan.

Max and Caid eagerly exchange glances before rushing out the opening gate. Just as before, Blake and Ryan hustle after.

“I gotta say,” Blake starts, "Drew strikes me as someone who's seen some shit, I think we can trust him."

"Probably," Caid concludes, "He seems like the head of security so I don't see why we wouldn't listen to him."

Max, looking around at the passing buildings, settles on a particular brick building, “So are we allowed to… you know, break into any house we want?”

Blake interjects immediately, “Don’t say it like that, all of these houses are abandoned so it's not breaking in. You do remember what Drew said though, that there are other survivors and we should leave them alone if we go by them.”

"When did he say that?" Max wonders, genuinely confused.

Blake hesitates as he looks between all three of them, "Oh, maybe he only said that to me."

"Teacher's pet", Max comments only for Blake to look thoroughly offended, "I'm only messing with you. One of us has to be careful, I know I won't."

"Don't even joke about that," Blake asserts.

Max just rolls his eyes and Caid simply shrugs. Ryan doesn't get the impression that anyone has forgotten about their circumstances, yet despite what it may seem, Blake seems to be the least stressed at the moment. Blake is cautious, yes, but beyond his constant bickering for security measures, he seems pretty relaxed all things considered. Shoulders back, head held high, and only looking around every once in a while. Ryan almost thinks himself crazy, but despite making continuous jokes, Max and Caid's eyes tell a completely different story. Neither one of them have looked Blake in the eyes once since they left.

They arrive to the gate of the base to find a terrible, horrifying sight. The reserved spot in the center of their base—the same place they played beer pong and planned scouting operations—is completely vacant.

“No!” Max screams, falling to his knees in utter tragedy.

“Shut up, man, you’re going to attract unwanted attention,” Caid criticizes.

Max performatively curls up on the ground like he's sobbing, “But my baby, my beautiful white Corvette is gone… who could have done such a thing?”

Blake sighs, "Let's look around for clues."

Blake, Caid, and eventually Max, stand up and start searching the remnants of the base for any indication of the perpetrator. Nothing of note is left behind under Ryan's perception, but his concentration begins to veer in another direction. 

“You guys can keep looking,” Ryan says, beginning to walk away, “I have to make a stop at the post office, I’ll meet back here in ten.”

“What?” Blake protests, “We’re supposed to stick together!”

“Just let him, he survived out there alone anyway,” Caid says defending Ryan as he jogs down the block towards the post office. 

Ryan hears Blake and Caid say something to each other, but it is too far away by this point to decipher what it means. He simply continues on his journey to check out what Chloe saw.

He passes by a book store and notices a person standing in the display window. At first he thinks it’s a mannequin but then he realizes that the book store doesn’t have mannequins. He doubles back and finds the one figure duplicated to three. Mangled flesh bulges beneath tattered clothes. Five eyes stare him down with the last consisting of a empty eye socket with fresh blood dripping out. Two visages harbor unadulterated hatred and agony, the third holds a sinister smile. 

Ryan freezes, his body unsure of what he's even looking out. He tries to reach for his knife but his hand can't find it as his eyes refuse to leave the grin watching him closely. Then, when a fight seems imminent, his legs start moving and he rushes out of their line of sight. The sound of broken glass never enters the scene as Ryan sprints away, his hand finally finding the knife much too late.

Eventually he arrives in the parking lot of the post office, his breath racing. He leans onto his knees as he takes three deep breaths and looks around. Only two cars sit in front of the building, one being a post office van while the other a gray suburban. Just as Chloe had described, the suburban’s windows are shattered and the driver side door is resting ajar.

Ryan takes out his knife properly and creeps closer to the car ready for any surprise attack that may follow. He attempts to listen for any noise that may leak out from the car, but nothing catches his ear as he gets close enough to the car to grab the handle. With one last deep breath, he swings the door open and jumps back preparing for something to jump out. 

To his surprise, he finds the driver's seat completely empty—no blood, no sign of a struggle. It's as if the initial driver just casually stepped out of the care and failed to close the door properly. That's the moment he hears a faint whimper from deeper in the vehicle.

He immediately grabs the passenger door and swings it open, being very careful to protect himself in the case of an attack. What he finds instead makes his grip on his knife waver.

Laying on the seat behind the driver is a weak, golden lab with glossy eyes. It's legs are thin and it's ribs protrude out of its body as if food was a distant memory. Its frail body, not stronger than a puppies, tries to move but finds itself completely incapable the motion is tragically interrupted by a severe bout of twitching as every muscle in it begins to contract at uncontrollable intervals. Amidst the twitching, a single protrusion rises out if its chest and begins to shift around before descending back into the dog's core. When the fit of squirming eventually subsides, the dog whimpers weakly as it look directly at the human before it. 

Ryan steps back, his hand nearly loosing grip on his knife. He shakes his head, believing it must be another nightmare, or maybe his imagination. Perhaps those zombies in the book store were just his imagination and this is too. This... this can't be real.

The dog whimpers again.

Ryan grabs onto the door preparing to slam it closed and when he opens it again, there will be nothing there. It's all in his mind, he's going crazy—he knows he is.

The dog whimpers again, this time drawn out as if begging Ryan for something—something he doesn't want to give.

Ryan begins pushing the door closed, but isn't able to bring himself to close it outright. The black, sorrowful, glossy eyes of the dog, barely beyond the age of a puppy, eat away at his will. There's no way he can... he can't...

He closes his eyes. His thoughts drain away. He lifts his hand. The puppy's pleas go silent. 

By the time the door closes, his knees give out and he finds his back against the door. His body makes the sincere request for a brief intermission and his mind has no choice but to agree. The knife falls out of his hands as he takes a deep breath and hangs his head. Something inside of him wants to be let out, but he simply closes his eyes and wishes it all away.

He’s about to get up and leave the scene behind, for good, but he stops when he notices a duffle bag beside him that must have fallen out of the shattered window. Sticking out the side pocket is a small book, only six inches by six inches, with a very pronounced and important title: scrapbook.

He slowly reaches over and opens it up. On the first page a picture of Chloe’s family. Her dad looks like a computer guy with his nerdy glasses and a blue polo. His mom is actually really attractive with Chloe's long blond hair and a very beautiful red dress. Chloe’s little brother is probably twelve in this picture and wears a polo to match his father but his is wrinkled and has small hints of dirt on the ends. Lastly, Chloe is wearing a similar dress to her mother but for some reason he can’t take his eyes off her. The way her hair is positioned and the angle of her smile, the joy in her beautiful turquoise eyes. He couldn't help but wonder if she'd ever smile like that again. Then his gaze lowers just enough and what he sees makes the photo fall out of his hands. Sitting between all four family members is a tiny little golden puppy, hardly two months old. It's eyes so full of life, it's tail wagging in the static image. He pulls the photo and out stuff it into his pocket hoping the denim of his jeans, not nearly thick enough to block the memory of what he saw.

The front door to the post office lays a few feet inside the main lobby. Each window is shattered and some torn clothing remains on a shard of glass jutting out of the windowsill. The remainder of the room is void of abnormalities, so he presses forward with his knife drawn and read.

The door to the back room looks like it should normally be locked, but when he pushes it open, he notices that someone put a magnet on the door frame to prevent it from locking. The proceeding room is cluttered with mailboxes and packages. It's almost funny how simple the messy room with boxes labeled 'this side up' can be during a time like this. He feels the photo in his pocket and can't help but laugh at the irony—the normal feels so foreign for some reason.

At the back of the mail room is another door, this one has visible damage to the outside as if zombies attempted to bash it down. It reminds him distinctly of the time when he was trapped in the furniture store office. 

Being careful not to slip on any loose mail on the floor, he creeps up to the door and swings it open finding that the lock is broken.

The other side opens to a hallway but directly across from the door is another broken window, but this one is different. This broken window has a towel neatly placed over its base. He notices that the side of the towel on the inside is ruffled as if someone were pushing against it but the other side is completely flat and untouched. He glances out the window for any sign of where the family could have gone but finds nothing but a generic street beyond. With a sigh of relief he begins to head back to his group. He can't confirm much, but the situation might be the most promising thing he's seen in a while.

Blake scowls at him as he returns to the old base. Caid and Max continue talking casually despite his entrance.

“Why’d you go to the post office?” Blake interrogates, jumping off the hood of the wall car he was sitting on.

Ryan shrugs, “I had a personal matter to attend to.”

Blake gets into his face, Ryan immediately gets the feeling that there's something deeper going on than just insubordination, “I understand that you’ve already survived a bit in this wasteland, but that doesn’t mean you get to fly solo anymore.”

Max and Caid’s conversations ceases as they rush over to prevent a fight.

Ryan looks at the boy incredulously as he waves his hands defensively, “I don’t know why you’re so pissed off, I said I was going to be back in ten and here I am.”

Blake studies him closely, his analysis making all three teens a bit uncomfortable, "Fine, just don't do it again," he eventually says.

"Alright then," Max says as he begins skipping out the front gate, "Let's move on to our next destination."

"I still don't like the idea of going downtown," Blake groans.

Caid shrugs, "Loosen up a bit. Maybe you should have had more to drink at the party."

Ryan watches as the two boys follow Max out the front gate. He wants, quite badly in fact, to side with Blake on this one, but something catches his attention which forces his pacificism. It's such a small detail that Ryan's surprised he even noticed. As Caid made the underhanded comment towards Blake, he hid his hand behind his back. From Blake's perspective, it probably just looked like he was doing a quick stretch, but Ryan saw it. Ryan saw that his hand was shaking—and it wouldn't stop.
