About two minutes down the road, Ryan finds himself staring out the window with his head leaned up against it. Jeremy, who had greedily taken the shotgun seat of the car puts earbuds into his ears and start playing some downloaded music off his phone. The sight of the technical relic startles Ryan at first as he realizes he's completely forgotten that staple of society. He notices plenty of people pulling them out over the last three weeks, but with the cellular towers down, there wasn't nearly as much to do with them.

With Ryan and Jeremy intentionally avoiding eye contact and Stephanie driving, Amber takes it upon herself to start conversation with the only ears left to bug.

"So Janelle," she starts, sounding easy-going and interested, "I hear you were a teacher like Stephanie. Is that true?"

Janelle, who had been staring out the window similarly to Ryan, looks over and habitually smiles kindly, "Yeah that's true. I worked in special education with elementary schoolers."

Amber's eyes widen, "That's fascinating!"

"Wait," Chloe butts in, "Did you work with a student by the name of Tina Smith? She's in, uh, third grade?"

Janelle's mouth drops open, "Oh, little Tina, she was the nicest little girl I've worked with. She would always ask me how _my_ day was."

Chloe beams at the comment, "You're so right, she's my cousin, and she's just the nicest person ever. I can't believe you were one of her teachers."

The two women laugh and recall fond memories of the little girl. The mention of family makes Ryan wonder where the little girl could be now, but he knows that's a dangerous question to ask nowadays. Amber tries to join in on the conversation with some neutral remarks here and there, but it's clear that this is finally a subject area that she's not well versed in. Ryan never gets the impression that Amber doesn't know what to say, but rather that she's well beyond the age that third-grader concepts are familiar terrain. The only thing of not that she can add is a brief story of something Jeremy did when he was around that age—Jeremy doesn't hear a word of it.

Feeling glad that he hasn't been dragged into the conversation, Ryan studies the passing scenery with a careful eye. He's been out and about enough times to know that nothing is likely to jump out at them—assuming they don't drive into a horde lingering on the street—but that doesn't prevent him from keeping a watchful eye on the curtains behind each window. If anything did threaten them, he isn't planning on fighting but at least he'd know which direction Stephanie should turn to outrun the danger. 

"Chloe, right?" Amber asks, transitioning the conversation to a new direction, "Jeremy has been... hesitant to socialize recent, despite my best efforts. I was wondering if you'd be so kind as to invite him to a few things you and Ryan go to. I know I'm asking a lot, but I just want to make sure he doesn't get to isolated from people his age. I love talking to my son, but I know that I can't be the only person he talks to," she looks at Chloe expectantly, "So... I hate to put you on the spot, but would you keep him on your radar."

Ryan looks forward one seat to find Jeremy still ignorantly in his own little world with earbuds in and loud music leaking out. He respectfully keeps his head from bobbing and gives no outward indication that his music tastes are... niche, but he likely doesn't realize just how well Ryan can hear the lyrics. 

"Absolutely," Chloe responds with a confident smile, "I'm always willing to get more people to join our trips to the community center."

Amber lets on a wide grin as she places a hand on Chloe's shoulder with gratitude, "Oh, thank you so much. I knew I could count on you," she switches faces from a thankful one to a rather mischievous one, "And if you kids get into any trouble... well... I'd be willing to turn a blind eye as long as Jeremy comes home safe."

"Mom," Jeremy says catching the attention of the entire care.

"What dear?" Amber asks.

"Please stop talking," immediately following, he picks a button on his phone and the music lyrics becomes a bit more pronounced.

Amber simply purses her lips and shakes her head. She doesn't necessarily look disappointed, but it's clear this is a conversation they have had countless times in the past. 

Chloe shakes her hands and head in sync, "Oh no, we won't be doing anything like that."

Amber laughs, "Oh please, I'm only teasing you. You seem much more responsible than I was when I was your age. It's embarrassing to admit, but I was a bit of a rebel in my youth. This one time, my friends and I—"

As Amber continues to reminisce on her childhood, Ryan notices movement in the gas station down the street. At first, he's about to disregard it as it doesn't seem to coming outside, but then Stephanie pulls over in front of their first destination and immediately exits the car. Everyone else piles out without noticing a thing.

Ryan takes one good look inside the thrifting shop and doesn't find anything threatening so he turns his attention back to the gas station across the street, "Head in without me, I'm going to go check something out."

A few people cast him a suspicious glance—or even a disingenuous one—but no one stops him as he steps away from the group and crosses the empty street. He draws his knife and prepares for anything to happen. He fully expects it to be a single stray zombie in which he will face the danger and dispatch it so that it doesn't come back to be a nuisance later, but as he gets closer, he realizes that his initial suspicion might not be the case. 

Opening the gas station door quietly, he steps inside and hears the ruffling of plastic behind some metal shelving. As he creeps closer he begins to get a better view of the origin of the crunching. What reveals itself behind the non-perishable good section is not a traditional zombie nor mutant, instead it looks to be a cloaked figure with a dwarf-like stature. Their brown cloak covers the majority of their body with one deformed hand reaching out and grabbing a pack of bubblegum off the counter of the store. The hand, while not shaped like a traditional human's, still harbors a striking resemblance to one as the index and middle finger have been fused together creating a four fingered appendage. Dark, scaly skin wraps around their fingers as they pull the gum off the shelf with a quiet chuckle to themselves. He then excitedly rips open three separate sticks of pink gum and tosses them into their mouth behind the hood.

Everything about this guy seems human enough, even the deformed hand can easily be choked up to an unfortunately genetic lottery. Ryan doesn't let his guard down for a second, but he's willing to admit that he doesn't foresee a conflict arising.

“Hello,” they say in unison—the dwarf not turning around the match Ryan's words exactly. 

The man’s voice is raspy and rather high pitched, all things considered. Despite only being one simple word that is easy to understand, it comes out sounding forced as if spoken by an non-native speaker.

Ryan relaxes finding that speech is _usually_ enough to distinguish monster from human. 

“I’m not here to start anything,” they say in unison once again, this time catching Ryan a bit off-guard.

Ryan takes a step back in surprise, “How’d you know…?” They each speak the same line at the same moment once more. Their words line up so perfect that Ryan almost believes he's imagining things, but it feels too vivid to be anything but reality.

“Please stop…” they say together, frighteningly in tune.

Ryan preps a single word that should hopefully clear up any misconceptions, “Unicorns!” they shout simultaneously.

“What is going on?” they say together once more. By this point Ryan decides speaking itself has begun to creep him out, and so he finds himself backing off carefully.

“I’ll explain that,” the man says, this time without Ryan having to say anything. 

The dwarf turns ninety degrees very slowly exposing its face to be one large mouth with three unique rows of teeth like a shark's. In fact, the teeth themselves are only a secondary concern as all facial feature the man could have are completely engulfed by this single mouth wrapping from one side of the head to the other. 

“You only said seventeen words, well really nineteen because two were contractions, but that doesn’t matter,” it shakes his deformed hand dismissively, “I only had to restart the conversation four times to get that perfect. I’m proud of how well I did.”

The man, or creature rather, nods its head a few times appreciating its accomplishment while throwing a few more pieces of gum in his mouth, swallowing them whole, and hobbling over to the isle with potato chips and other snacks in it. It reaches over and grabs the spiciest bag of red-hot chips.

“Were you the one—” Ryan starts, finally able to say something on his own.

“Yes, I did save your life. You're very welcome by the way,” it cuts him off, “I don’t like wasting time. Time is a very valuable commodity you know, just like these delicious snacks.”

It rips open the bag with too much force and the chips fly across the floor beneath it. Then, without delay, it bends over and grabs a few red-hot chips and throws them into his gaping mouth. It proceeds to smile contently which becomes quite disturbing as its entire face is handling said grin.

“So—” Ryan attempts again being cut off immediately.

“Yes, the raven mask guy sent him… That’s such a strange way to describe him. I don’t think his mask looks like a raven at all. You must be seeing something.”

Ryan only needs to think the words: “How often do you go back in time” before the monster answers his question.

“If it’s not overtly obvious by this point, I do it constantly. It’s like lifting a finger for me—a really strange and hard to explain finger. You say something and I save both of us the time by answering before you ask, it works… Oh, my! These things are scrumptious! It’s burning my mouth! Fire! Someone put out the fire!”

The monster hobbles down to the powered off fridges and flings a door open before pulling out a small carton of spoiled milk. It chugs it down quickly.

“This milk has such a new taste. What brand is this, I must drink more.”

Ryan thinks: “So what did the masked man send you here for?”

“What not even going to thank me first before getting down to business?” it complains, "I know I said time is important, but pleasantries are often a worthwhile expenditure of time—especially when there's food involved."

“Right, thank you very much for saving me,” Ryan is almost startled by the sound of his own voice, thinking the creature might copy him again.

The monster’s smiles grows even wider and more sinister, “Oh I just wanted to hear you say it. Now, we can get down to business.”

It promptly swings the fridge door closed and walks over to the isle pulling off the last bag of jerky before tearing that one open too. This time it catches a few pieces midair and tosses them into its mouth munching down on them with a glutenous vigor.

“So,” it begins, “I was sent here to deliver a message.”

Ryan thinks, “What do you mean?” This time reacting normally when the thing continues to talk.

“Now you are starting to get it,” it comments, “Now, before I get to the nitty gritty of my message, do you happen to know where I can find some more food?”

Ryan doesn’t even have time to think about it before the monster already has a response ready.

“Oh, I see… everyone is low on food. That’s a shame. I really wish there was more scrumptious consumables available to satisfy all of our hungry bellies. You humans do have bellies, yes? Oh, pardon me, that was a silly question.”

It pauses and ‘looks’ down at the floor, a sharp, slender tongue sticks out of its head and slams into a loose piece of jerky on the floor. The projectile-like tongue doesn't only skewer the jerky but also pierces through the tile floor before retracting back into the creature's mouth with the skewered jerky.

“Well with that out of the way…” it pauses once more shaking its head quickly and aggressively, “That’s problematic. We’re about to be attacked, and I haven't even given my message yet.”

Ryan takes a step back and draws his knife looking out the window.

"You would like to know what's coming," it says directly before Ryan speaks.

It swings its arms up dramatically as if a grand reveal is in order. A grin arrives on its toothy visage... and then it stops. For a brief moment, it's entire body freezes before its arms slowly lower and its grin fades, "Oh, I guess I'm not supposed to say. It's one of these conversations."

"What do you mean?" Ryan asks being caught off guard yet again that he can talk.

The creature sighs, "I hate when I reach these ridiculous forks. They're so... tiresome. Like real forks—why can't I use my tongue."

Ryan inspects the creature quizzically. Despite his best attempts at comprehension, he simply can't fathom what is going through it's head. 

“Stop, Stop, Stop!” it shouts, shifting its tone into a spontaneous bout of startlement, “fighting here equals death. Please keep your gun tucked, tucked away. Unlike before, I can't show you what happens so you can't be reckless.”

Ryan stares at the creature completely dumbstruck. He hasn't even formulated a plan and here he is being criticized for it. If the situation is really about to collapse so such extremes, wouldn't it be more productive for the creature to tell him what to do rather than what not to do.

“I have to tell you my message now,” it starts picking up a bag of gummy worms behind some empty wrappers and tucking it under its arm, “stay alive... that's all.”

Ryan stares at the anomaly with a growing sense of confusion before glancing out the window to see if the enemy is about to appear. When he turns back, the monster has  completely disappeared without so much as a crunch of snacks underfoot. He'd love to dissect what could have happened, but the current threat takes precedence. 

He ducks underneath one of the nearby windows and peers out looking for whatever this attacker may be. Additionally, he checks on the thrift store across the street and finds no one loitering outside. It eases his worries a bit to know that he can handle this upcoming skirmish without involving others. Looking back down the street once more, he spots three men walking towards the gas station, two with assault rifles and one with a nail spiked bat. They each wear leather jackets with a red symbol of a dragon on the back. He'd think it's some sort of post-apocalyptic biker gang, but he recognizes the dragon symbol to be the same one he saw on that thug a few weeks ago.

The three guys casually approach the gas station as they laugh about something ambiguous. Ryan eyes their movements carefully as they get closer, hoping to find some sort of weakness that he can exploit, even if that must be his words

He turns away from the window and is greeted with a finger pressed up against his lips, hushing him from saying anything. His heart skips a beat as Jimmy Lashing, the infamous serial killer, crouches behind him with a serious stare. Ryan flinches immediately and tries to swing his knife, but Jimmy grabs onto his wrist and prevents him from attacking.

“Don’t say a word,” he whispers. The creepiest part of it all isn't that it's Jimmy, it's that his eyes are calm and calculated—a stark contrast from the insane disposition he possessed last time they met.

Then without giving Ryan time to retaliate, speak, or even think clearly, he lets go of his hand and walks out of the gas station. His eyes immediately take on a wild look as he opens his mouth with a wide and active grin.

“My boys, it's so good to see you all again,” he says, his crazed and overly dramatic voice fully returning, “I missed you all.”

“Jimmy?!” one of the exclaims as all three stop in their tracks.

“Where have you been? The boss ain’t happy with your disappearance,” another explains as they all lower their weapons.

“Come closer, I have a secret,” Jimmy whispers folding his hands up to his chest, leaning forward, and expressing a wide enthusiastic grin. He then reduces his volume to a whisper as the three men circle up in front of him. "I've got booze and sexy woman in there who’s flat out drunk. I'll share my goods if you can keep this hush, hush, right boys?” He cocks his head to the side as they decide.

One of the men looks over to the gas station and ponders the offer. "So this is where you've been the last few days? Fucking some whore?"

Jimmy inspects the man with a smirk, "Are you blaming me for that?"

One of the men sighs with exasperation, "No, I can't. How about you slip us some of your booze, and we'll pretend we didn't see anything."

Jimmy nods as he gestures towards the door, "Right this way."

One of the men smiles smugly as he heads for the door without a second thought. One step is all he gets before a large gash opens across this throat. Blood sprays out as he falls back barely able to get a hand up to cover the lethal opening. By the time the man hits the ground, the other two gang members have already joined him in the afterlife.

Ryan wasn't even able to witness what just happened. He saw Jimmy move and then a second later everyone was dead. Somehow his flashy movements slit all three throats in the time it took Ryan to watch just one of them fall.

Jimmy turns back to the gas station and holds his arms out to the sides, “Ryan!” he shouts excitedly, “Come out here and look at this.”

Ryan cracks open the door and looks around to make sure the coast is clear. Following directions now is less than ideal, but he doesn't really have much of a choice given that Jimmy knows he's here. Given the circumstances, it would have been far simpler if Jimmy had just tried to kill him again. 

Jimmy crouches down next to the bodies and points at the blood pool, “Look at this one, they usually don’t get this big, which means I opened the neck just perfectly to optimize the blood current running out,” he smiles and energetically points at another, “This one’s throat is split perfectly symmetrical across their body. Even for me, that's a rarity.”

Ryan narrows his eyes looking at the horrific scene of corpses and promptly reaches for his knife.

"Oh, calm down," Jimmy says casually as he stands up and starts walking away, "I'm not here to hurt you."

Jimmy proceeds to walk over to locked propane take shelving and squats down in front of it. He inspects the combination lock in front of it rather keenly before reaching for it and looking at the little numbers on the bottom. He rolls his hand over it three times and after the third, he pulls down on the lock, and it miraculously unlocks. After that, he tosses the lock dismissively to the side and pulls two heavy propane tanks out. Ryan simply watches as Jimmy proceeds to create a small wall out of eight propane tanks that wrap around the outside of the corpses. While the bloody scene is still completely visible to Ryan and Jimmy, he assumes anyone on the other side of the street might not be able to see past the white canisters. Why he did this, Ryan hasn't the faintest clue. Finally, when the wall is satisfactorily completed, Jimmy steps over it and starts walking away from the gas station.

Maybe Ryan could have brought it upon himself to drop the issue and let Jimmy leave in peace. Maybe this could have been the end of their conversation, but then he realizes the direction Jimmy is walking. Not in some arbitrary direction, but very intentionally, towards the thrift store.

"Hey!" he shouts towards the store, "Ryan said there are people in there! Is that true?!"

At first, they're left with a tense silence as Ryan starts off after him to ensure Jimmy doesn't try anything. Then, as Ryan is passing Jimmy and getting in between him and the thrift store, Amber appears out of the front doors and prances over. Chloe, Jeremy, and the others follow close behind. Once they approach, Jimmy’s face seems to morph from his usual wide grin to a much more reasonable and civil one. In the blink of an eye, he turns from a maniacal serial killer to a welcoming man on par with Amber.

Ryan knows he's not going to be able to take Jimmy down with ease and he wouldn't want to with his friends so close nearby. Not only that, he knows he doesn't stand a chance in knife combat so his only option would be to draw his gun. But that too, feels a bit precarious after the strange entities' ominous warning. Whether he believes the warning or not it makes no difference that this is the worst case situation.

He opens his mouth to tell his friends to run, but he can’t in fear that Jimmy will rush past him and kill them on the spot. Given what Jimmy just proved he's capable of, can he really do anything?

"What's going on?" Amber asks giving Jimmy a once over, "And who's he?"

Jimmy slides his feet together and promptly does a little bow to the women, “Hello my lovely ladies, my name is Henry Baxter, it's a pleasure to meet you. Ryan and I are old acquaintances, you see. His parents were personal friends of mine back in the day, I can’t believe I stumbled upon him of all people at a time like this.”

“Oh really?!” Amber says excitedly, giving him a look of fascination.

Stephanie, however, tries to figure the strange man out a bit more cautiously, “I feel like I recognize you from somewhere.”

“Oh really, perhaps you are thinking of my brother Harrison Baxter. We look a lot alike but he tends to be on the covers of magazines. I am honestly mistaken with him all the time.” Jimmy’s voice comes off as extremely friendly and feels so genuine it sends a chill down Ryan's spine. So this is what a real serial killer looks like.

Ryan tightens his fist, he knows that he needs to expose his true identity. If he waits any longer, Jimmy might... he will... he's going to...

"Are you staying nearby?" Amber asks Jimmy.

“Who me?” Jimmy says with a little laugh, "oh no, I'm a wandering soul you could say. Just passing through."

Amber seems thoroughly entertained by Jimmy's little theatrics and Stephanie seems to be easing off the suspicion breaks with each passing word. The other three seem rather indifferent to it all which only makes him worry more... or at least he thought they were indifferent until Chloe speaks up.

"Why are you wandering around alone at a time like this?" she asks skeptically which reignites the cloud of suspicious amongst the group.

Jimmy—or Henry Baxter—lowers his head with defeat, "To tell you the truth, I'm traveling with another, but we got separated after a scuffle earlier. Never fear, I'm more than confident that he's alright, I'm actively searching to find him once more. You could call it the tragic tale of two desperate friends."

"Oh, how awful," Amber comments, humoring the man.

“Thank you for your concern, ma’am. It truly means a lot to me. Now, what would a beautiful woman such as yourself be called?” Jimmy looks back to her with a warm smile and a level of charm Ryan wishes he had—the subconscious thought disturbs him after remembering who Jimmy really is.

“My name would be Amber,” she says, her eyes hanging on Henry before turning to the other members of the group, “This is Stephanie, that's Janelle, that's Chloe, and that that lovely, quiet boy in the back is my son Jeremy."

Ryan's had enough, being around this serial killer is dangerous enough, but given him their names? He has to put an end to this. He opens his mouth to break through this man’s facade, but hesitates when Jimmy looks over at him and stares deep into his eyes. His face maintains a warm, charismatic smile, but for some reason, it sends a chill down his spine. The gaze is so oppressively friendly at all courage Ryan may have had immediately sinks away.

Jimmy jumps over and wraps an arm around Ryan’s shoulders giving him a side hug, "Well don't you just have a merry bunch here. Proud of you kid."

Of all the people that could have reacted to that statement, it wasn't Amber or Chloe who got his attention. Instead, Jeremy of all people, rolls his eyes and crosses his arms. The friendly banter seemingly making him upset to even be there.

Ryan shoves Jimmy off of him with enough force to nearly knock him over and prepares to reveal his true identity, but yet again he gets cut off before he can speak.

“Ryan?! Is this about the last time we spoke?” he holds a hand over his heart and looks sympathetically, “I’m so sorry ladies, I hate to catch you all in the middle of this, so would you all be so kind as to give Ryan and I some space to talk. I'm certain we can hash this out in no time... after all, Ryan is such a kind and understanding person."

Amber's smile vanishes as she glares at Ryan in shock. Stephanie looks confused, Janelle seems a bit hesitant to make a stance, and Chloe seems genuinely concerned on Ryan's behalf. Then he notices Jeremy trying—and failing—to suppress a smirk. Ryan's seemingly spontaneous reaction is the only thing that has caught the boys attention thus far.

Eventually, Amber nods with a careful expression directed towards Ryan. She then begins pushing the other group members away from the conversation, “It’s not a problem at all Henry, I know how teenagers can be. Just remember that I’ll be at the prison just down the street if you ever want to talk again. I’d love to see you again.”

Jimmy smiles and waves as the women head back into the thrift store. Then once they are alone, he turns back to Ryan in order to hide his face as it returns to its usual sinister grin and insane eyes, “You’re probably wondering… Why? Why did I act so friendly. Why didn’t you expose me immediately. Why didn’t I kill you when I had the chance.”

Ryan nods his head slowly as his hand reaches underneath his jacket, his fingers sliding into the handle, “Are you going to explain or will this end the same way it did last time. Either way I’m going to reveal your identity even if you try to lie."

Ryan tries to rationalize his actions but neglects the part where Jimmy’s eyes burned a level of fear into him he had never felt before. There is a difference between fearing for your life and looking into the eyes of the pure embodiment of malevolence and insanity. He’ll never be able to outlive that sight from his mind. 

"You're free to do as you like," Jimmy puts his hands on his hips and leans forward with a more playful than sadistic smile, "It won't change anything."

Ryan sighs, getting the feeling that he might not have to resort to violence, “I just don’t understand you at all.”

"And you never will."

Pointing a finger a Jimmy, Ryan demands an answer, “What I really want to know is why you didn’t kill me before and why you killed members of your own gang. Everything about you is contradicting itself?”

Jimmy shakes his head and makes gestures with his hands as he talks, “You realize that the best lies are always woven with the bonds of truth. Anyone who has ever lied can attest to that. I have used the name Henry Baxter before, and I did come here with someone else. None of that was a lie... and yes, I did know your parents—your real parents."

Ryan immediately finds himself shaking at the mere idea of his real parents, “You’re a serial killer, I can’t believe a thing you say.”

Jimmy laughs, "Just because I'm around someone, doesn't mean they're my friend," his words remind Ryan a bit too much of a particular someone he knows, "Those people were not _my_ gang, they just knew my name. Even if they blood was a beautiful red, they were never my friends."

Ryan shifts uncomfortably as his sweaty palm readjusts on his knife. He's still hesitant about starting a conflict as there's no guarantee he'll win. In fact, he's probably going to lose if he tries anything rash. That being said, they've clashed before so he can't shake the feeling that it might happen again.

“So, you might be wondering what my plan is,” Jimmy continues to rant in a whisper, “It’s simple, I'm searching for someone and this gang might lead me to my target. All you have to do is keep moving forward. Danger has a way of finding you and I might be able to use that."

"I still can't trust you," Ryan asserts.

Jimmy narrows his eyes, "And I wouldn't want it any other way."

Ryan swallows hard as Jimmy spins on his heels with a little hop and starts off down the street. The deranged serial killer's strut is anything but inconspicuous as he waves a hand towards Ryan without looking back. Ryan just stares as the threat disappears once more into the distance.
