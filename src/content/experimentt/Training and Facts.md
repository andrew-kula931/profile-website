Ryan follows the aviator wearing man down the desolate street on foot despite having ample vehicles to speed up their operation. As Michael said, he ended up confiscating the pistol from him but allocated it back to him the following morning with a full magazine. Naturally this whole ordeal drew a lot of attention to him both from the other teens and some adults that witnessed the events. Most of them presented their questions in a reserved manner, warded off mostly due to the "personal matters" line that was dropped. Alas, Ryan was forced to politely explain that he didn't really know what was going on. That being said, in order to preserve Drew's honor, he kept his responses vague enough that most people wouldn't think either of them a fool. 

To be honest, Ryan really has no clue why he's here in the first place. Drew seemed like a reasonable enough guy, and he even defended him when the pistol was found, but that doesn't mean Ryan fully trusts him. Coming out here is probably just some pretext for a one-on-one discussion without prying ears, but he can't shake the thought that an ambush could happen. Drew doesn't strike him as the type to do that... though, nowadays, Ryan's not sure he knows what any "type" is.

They walk in silence for nearly thirty minutes: enough time to get well out of view and no chance that anyone follows. This detail feels like an over examination on Ryan's part, but it's truly the only description he can think of for the reason for their silence. Drew was all talkative just the other day.

"You're probably wondering what this personal matter is," Drew surmises as he leans back a bit and places his hands behind his head.

Ryan nods.

Drew gives Ryan a once over before chuckling to himself, "Do you want me to get straight to the point or would you prefer some lip service and small talk?"

Ryan shrugs, he's just here.

Drew smiles as he keeps walking, "Man of few words... you're just like him."

"Just like who?" Ryan glances around at the passing buildings wondering where they could be going. Nothing particularly looks dangerous, precarious, or relevant so he's just at an all-around loss.

Drew waves it off, "Don't worry about it. You just remind me of an old friend. He died a while back, so there's no sense in bringing back the dead—we get enough of that these days... though... you would be about his age, now that I think about it."

Drew's giant slows for brief moment as he goes back on his word—however brief. He's back to his usual disposition two paces later as his hands slide into his pockets and he looks off at the passing buildings with tame inspection.

Seeing as how Ryan doesn't want to lead the conversation, Drew picks back up, "Anyway, I brought you out here for a very specific reason. You see, I have an informant here in the city, and he said you're a particularly enigmatic kid."

Ryan thinks it over briefly and comes to the realization that the only people he's come into contact with are thugs and serial killers. His hand immediately reaches for his loaded gun, "You're with that gang... aren't you."

Drew lifts his hands defensively as he laughs, not a hint of nerve in his tone, "Calm yourself, I'm not with them."

His response isn't good enough for Ryan to take his hand off his pistol, but he elects to keep it holstered for the time being.

Drew nods his head a few times with a smirk growing on his face, "You don't remember me, do you? Then again, I guess I didn't stand out much the first time we met, but you were one of the only teens I saw that day."

Ryan shakes his head, not recalling any such time that they would have met, "I... don't follow."

This response seems to what Drew expected as he waves his hands for a moment and places them against his hips, "I was there at the military base, back when you, Steve, and Bill were there. I was also the one who gave them orders to track down Seth Heimes."

Ryan's blood runs cold. So all this time, the person who gave them false hope and sent them on a wild goose chase was just some soldier with aviators. Now that he thinks about it, there was one—just one—soldier that had aviators back in that military base. 

"Why?" he tires, "You—you know what... what.... why would you order that?"

Drew sighs, both hands disappearing behind his back, "You see, I'm only here for Seth Heimes. Steve Hughes and Bill Valentine just so happened to be two unregistered former soldiers that I could use. Besides, sending you out saved your life. That checkpoint fell soon after you left."

The surrounding air seems to get thinner as Ryan's heart begins to race. His grip on his gun becomes a lot less precautionary and a lot more reactive, "But... you don't know what happened!"

The empty street goes silent for a moment as Ryan's exclamation hangs in the air like a nasty filth. He didn't want to think about what happened because of that order and Drew didn't seem like he felt any remorse for giving it. Ryan grits his teeth as he undoes the strap holding the pistol in place.

Drew lowers his head, "Ryan, I don't know what happened to you, Steve, and Bill, truly I don't. It was never my intension to send you on a death march, if that's what's upsetting you. What I will say is that Seth Heimes is my target and that mission was supposed to be nothing more than reconnaissance."

"But..." Ryan attempts, his thoughts swirling through flashes of horrifying memories that make his heart ache. Bill's rage induced, undead face flashing its teeth at Steve struggling on the ground. Ryan digging a grave for a family he thought he could protect. A monstrous entity that convinced him that he was just a humble principal. His shoulder, now scared by the teeth marks no longer threatening his life. That mission changed his life forever.

"Why am I telling you this? Well, it's because I have a question." Drew guesses as his right hands slides out from behind his back, with it comes a shiny, silver pistol. He points it right at Ryan, "Who are you?"

Blood surges through Ryan's veins. Of all the things he could do right now, freezing feels like the absolute worst thing to do. Maybe giving in is the right call, maybe fighting someone clearly more skilled than him is reckless, but this guy is responsible for Steve's death. He refuses to just stand here and what? Talk?

Ryan draws his own gun, steps to the side, and fires a round before Drew can. Drew, evading to the side, lunges forward before a second shot can be fired. Instead of using his own gun, he drops it. One hand clamps over the barrel while the other strikes Ryan's wrist sideways. The pistol is ripped from his hands and sent skidding across the pavement as Drew pushes him back. 

Stumbling backwards, Ryan draws his knife and prepares for a much more ugly fight. Drew inspects the knife with a hint of awe before picking up his own gun with a smile. As the gun is lifted off the ground, Ryan notices that the magazine is completely vacant. He averts his eyes before unsavory memories can come back to him.

"Huh," Drew says to himself with two pistols in hand, "I was wondering how you pulled that off."

Ryan glares at him, lowering his stance in preparation for an additional assault. Drew has proven himself to be someone not to be taken lightly. Even if he is toying with him, this situation could get ugly if he's not careful.

Drew looks Ryan over with a watchful eye, he analyzes his stance as he nods his head back and forth in thought, "You've got a decent instinct to lower your center of gravity, but you're leaning too far forward."

"I don't know if I can trust you," Ryan takes a step back just as a precautionary measure. He still wants to punch the guy clean in the face, but that approach is likely going to get himself killed.

In an act to clear any suspicious, or perhaps just a simple gesture of good will, Drew tosses Ryan's pistol back to him, "That's good, don't trust anyone," Ryan gives him a confused look as he catches his gun and clutches the handle, "I'm not your ally, but I'm far from your enemy."

"That doesn't change things."

Drew shrugs as he pulls a magazine out from the back of his belt and feeds it into his gun. He then holsters the gun onto his right leg before crossing his arms and taking a deep breath, "Where I come from everyone is a potential asset and enemy—not a friend. You don't come from the same place I did, but I still think you could be an asset to me. I only pulled the gun on you to ensure you're worth my time and not one of those useless high schoolers."

"Useless, huh?" Ryan repeats, not necessarily agreeing with the assessment, but deciding that this "foreign place" that Drew came from probably dignifies such a brutal critique. Ryan would never call his friends useless, in fact, he detests that line of thinking because it reminds him of how he's falsely categorized people in the past. The fact of the matter is that he doesn't know how to refute Drew as useless is ultimately a subjective comparison. The phrasing just bugs him is all.

Drew starts off past Ryan, completely ignoring the fact that Ryan now has both a pistol and knife drawn. The aviator wearing soldier just strolls on by as if those objects were mere toys, and he had somewhere else to be. 

"Walk with me," Drew commands, "We're not quite to our destination."

Not willing to wait for a reply, Drew starts off down the street. Begrudgingly, Ryan follows. The man seems dangerous, he seems unpredictable, but he doesn't strike Ryan as malicious. Even if they started off on the wrong foot, they share a common enemy and that alone warrants a bit of investigation. Drew may have been in charge of sending him and Steve on the suicide mission but getting into a heated argument won't solve anything... and it won't bring Steve back. After all, Drew wasn't really responsible for Steve's death. He knows it to be the truth, yet for some reason is so incredibly hard to accept. That fact alone is probably why he resists the urge to fight.

Their journey wraps around a few residential blocks before stopping in front of a very impressive house. The front door is guarded by two Greek-like, white pillars with a small balcony overtop. The rest of the lightly colored building is constructed using rustic looking aluminum to give it a "blast from the past" feel while also seeming modern in its own right. Ryan notices the absence of a garage door, though there is a driveway which leads around to the back of the property.

"What's this?" Ryan asks.

Drew glances over with a smug grin, "This here's a hive."

Ryan immediately steps back and reaches for his knife, not weary of Drew, but weary of the subtle shift of an inside curtain, "Why are we here?"

Drew pulls out some sort of DIY canister with one hand and his pistol with the other hand, "You're going to help me clear out this hive."

To say this result is unexpected would simply be a lie on Ryan's part. This situation is, more or less, what he planned on happening. The elite solider in camp inviting him—and only him—out to the field was bound to lead to some combat situation. Maybe something inside this building is of value, maybe it's a clue to finding Heimes, but Ryan isn't stupid enough to follow blindly anymore.

"No," he asserts as he takes a few steps away from the presumptuous man, "I'm not risking my life for you."

Drew rolls his eyes, "Come on, help a man out. I swear on my honor that I won't backstab you in there. If I wanted you dead, you'd already be dead."

Seeing as the situation is no longer "civil" but not yet outright hostile, Ryan draws his knife but keeps it close to his body rather than drawing it back to swing, "As far as I'm concerned, I can't trust you."

"Good, the feeling is mutual," Drew comments as he looks back to the building, "So keep your guard up on all fronts in there."

"No!" Ryan repeats with a bit more emphasis this time, "I'm not going in there."

Drew sighs, disappointment causing him to press his index and middle fingers against his forehead, "I brought you out here to test you and in turn, prove to you that I'm who I say I am."

Ryan shakes his head as he creeps closer to the street. If all else fails, he's willing to take his chances alone then going on another suicide mission for this guy, "I don't care. I've been inside a house with zombies—with mutants. There's no reason to go in there."

Drew raises an eyebrow, "So have you..."

Ryan confirms with a nod as he steps off the side of the road and back onto the paved ground. By this point he's probably ten yards away from the man and ready to bolt if the need arises. Drew, however, hasn't moved his feet an inch since the start of the conversation. Whether it be absurd audacity or some unknowable calculation, he doesn't seem to mind the fact that Ryan's practically leaving mid-conversation. This confidence is clarified as Drew points his pistol right at Ryan's head—the magazine inside the gun. Ryan freezes solid.

"Don't even think about running," Drew commands, not with oppressive authority, but a humble warning he intends for Ryan to follow, "You're not leaving my sight. Not until my questions are answered."

Ryan tightens his grip on his knife. He has his pistol but what are the odds he can draw it, aim it, and fire it all before Drew retaliates. The first time may have been a test, but the curiosity behind those aviators has since vanished and has been replaced by a weary mistrust and skepticism. He gets the feeling that Drew isn't playing around this time.

"What?" Ryan asserts, waving his free hand off to the side, "What do you want to know?"

Drew narrows his eyes as his finger tightens on the trigger, "I sent you off to hunt down a cultist and when you're dealing with the Cult or an experiment only two things can happen: either you kill them... or you live long enough to become one... Seth Heimes is still alive."

Ryan hesitates before slowly shaking his head in disbelief. Join Heimes? He would never. He isn't sure what this "Cult" is, but he has nothing to do with it. Sure he's run into Heimes more than a few times, but that's sheer coincidence. He's not with Heimes, if anything, Drew is the suspicious one for making these accusations.

"I—I'm not..." Ryan tries.

"I already asked you who you were, now I want an answer," Drew lowers his head to reveal two cold eyes glaring javelins into Ryan, "Who are you?"

Air becomes stagnant as Ryan's mouth hangs open. His hands trembling beneath the weight of such a simple question. He's Ryan of course—he always has been. Up until this apocalypse started he was just an ordinary kid with a typical amount of hardships to overcome. He never wanted to join into the fray of some "Cult" and fight off monsters for survival—all of that just happened to him. To ask who he is... it makes him feel dehumanized, like the accusation alone strips him of some dignity he didn't know he had. How does he even respond to that? Does he refuse the premise? Try and weasel his way out of suspicion with sheer ignorance?

Then again, he recalls the conversations he's had with the masked man. Those are not normal high schooler conversations, heck, they're not normal apocalyptic survivor conversations. Whether he wants to hide it or not, he's been forced into situations that feel more paranormal than reality should allow. For crying out loud, he somehow had a premonition and talked to the masked man in his fight with Jimmy. To claim he's innocent would be a lie, not just to Drew, but to himself. Ryan's not a cultist, but he can't claim to be normal anymore. Perhaps the only path forward is to start from the beginning.

"The first time I met Heimes—the real Heimes, not my principal—he offered me a knife," Ryan recalls. His words don't arouse any tension relief in Drew's controlled demeanor, but he doesn't seem to be stopping him from talking, "He told me... he told me to end my suffering before it begins. I refused." His last comment seems to get a small, mostly concealed reaction from Drew as his lip twitches, "That's when he handed me a key and told me to go to the library. I later found out that the key opened a secret hallway around the library so I could sneak a peek inside... in the library was some messed up doctor experimenting on teens for something—I don't know what—but I think it may have been a cure."

Drew shakes his head, "That's impossible, there's no cure this disease. It's cause this isn't a disease."

Ryan stops his story, "What? What do you mean?"

Drew deflects the question with a stern glare, "Keep going."

Unable to make any demands in the current situation, Ryan continues, "I swear that's all I know. Not long after that, zombies broke out of the library and massacred most of the school."

Instead of offering Ryan any sympathy, Drew only raises his DIY canister and sticks his thumb into some sort of pin, "How can you say that with such a straight face?"

Straight face. That hit him hard. In pure shock, Ryan can't even formulate a thought as the overwhelming weight of that comment comes crashing down. He's not... he didn't mean it... he was just trying to survive... for the love of God, he suffered too. He's just trying to be objective, nothing more. Those memories... they haunt him. Of countless corpses strewn about the school hallway, of the girl's scream as she dies on her birthday... of the Jacobsons. He's not crazy... he can't be crazy... he's not a monster!

"I—I" Ryan says, his breath accelerating far faster than if he had just finished a sprint. He steps back, nearly stumbling over, "I didn't mean it like that... I... I... I didn't..." each word seems to come out harder than the last. Each word seems to cause him to grow shorter and shorter on breath. Gun pointed at him or not, his is going to suffocate before any lead crossed through his brain. 

Ryan's gaze falls to the ground as he tries to take another weak step backwards and unintentionally collapses to the unforgiving concrete. He tries to get back to his feet but finds his hands trembling to such a degree that he can't muster the strength to get up. To think all of this has to happen right in front of a hive.

Despite his weakness, he musters the courage to fight his body and get to his feet, but before he can, he feels a hand on his back. He flinches thinking he's about to be stabbed, but then he notices the touch isn't rough and hostile but soft and careful. Looking up, he finds Drew crouched down in front of him with his aviators hanging in his hand. The cold, tactical eyes now replaced with mature sympathy: one of a hardened soldier accustomed to grief.

"Just breathe," Drew says slowly.

Adhering to his advice, Ryan forces himself to expand his lungs further than they were before. He can't quite seem to slow his breathing down, but he makes the conscious decision to at least try. His breathing remained uneven for several moments. Every attempt to slow it down seemed to fail halfway through. Eventually the sharp gasps became deeper breaths, then slower ones. He feels his hands go numb as they continue to shake and his head can't seem to raise higher than parallel with the ground. His chest still hurt, but at least he could think again. 

Seeing as the hyperventilation has mostly subsided, Drew releases a sigh of relief, "I take it you're not too prone to those?"

Ryan instinctively shakes his head but then stops himself, "It's... it's happened once before."

"And what triggered that?" Drew inquires rather clinically.

Ryan immediately averts his eyes. He can't bring himself to recount what happened back then and even if he could, he'd just sound like a heartless monster no matter what he did. Perhaps the best solution is to just remain silent for the moment.

Drew sighs, "Never mind, you don't have to answer that," he stands up and checks back in on the occupied house, still silent in it's foreboding presence, "Are you ready to go?"

Ryan looks around, not just at Drew and the house, but at the entire world around him. Everything felt so surreal all of the sudden, as if collapsing had disoriented him enough to uncertainty. He wouldn't call it fear, per say, but he feels as if everything around him is... off. He tries to stand up but his shaky arms buckle underneath him.

Drew sighs as he takes a seat on the curb, "We can give it a second, they're not coming out."

Rolling to his bottom and allowing himself to rest, Ryan leans back on his shaky arms. He feels so pathetic. He's survived giant mutants and hordes of zombies yet a single sentence puts him to the ground. A single harmless body causes him to collapse. What a pathetic person he is.

"Did you like school?" Drew asks casually as he ties the DIY canister to his belt.

Initially, Ryan is flabbergasted by why he would bring up the school so quickly after... after all that. That being said, Drew doesn't seem like he's prying at simple small talk, his approach seems much more calculated—much more experienced.

"No not really," Ryan admits, "But... I guess there were some redeeming qualities."

Drew snickers, "It's that girl isn't? The one you were talking to on the way to the prison. I don't know where your head is but I can spot a woman in love a mile away."

"I—" Ryan stutters completely caught off guard by the comment, "It's... it's not like that."

Drew shrugs, "Sure it's not," he pivots as he rests his pistol on his lap, "You get good grades?"

Taking a deep breath, Ryan leans forward to rest his hands on his knees. He still feels like he needs a second, but that only makes the incoming conversation even more imminent. He'll get the same lecture as always: "You should have taken school seriously while you had the chance" or "It's a shame you didn't do any sports". It's not like he can refute those points of view—they were normal after all—he just gets annoyed because everyone treats high school like the pinnacle of adolescence. To him it was just another stepping stone in the arduous journey called life. 

"Decent I guess?" Ryan says softly, "I... wasn't a smart kid or anything."

The comment seems to fit Drew's suspicious pretty well as he just nods with a knowing look. Whatever impression he might have had or Ryan—outside of the whole cult member interrogation—is probably correct all things considered. Ryan didn't view himself as remarkable back then, heck he still doesn't. Though, verbalizing his experience embarrasses him a bit is all.

Drew leans forward a bit, clearly he wasn't satisfied despite Ryan initial interpretation, "So we talking like an A-B student or were you just scraping by?"

Ryan releases a drawn out sigh, hopefully long enough to tell Drew that he doesn't like where this conversation is going, "I don't really know... I never looked at my report card," he glances back at Drew and finds him waiting for more, "I guess... I got nineties on everything, maybe one or two one-hundreds—not many though. I think that's pretty normal, not sure how that translates to a letter grade."

"You don't really know?" Drew repeats, "To be honest, I never really understood the grading system either. I suppose that's probably normal if you study properly."

"Studying..." Ryan says to himself. Now that he thinks about it, he doesn't really know what that all entails. Sure he heard countless obnoxiously loud conversations about "all-nighters" studying for finals or alleged study groups, but none of those things pertained to him. He listened as school because, what else would he do, but the second the bell rang, he become more interested in walking around town just to get away from his foster homes. He's always seen studying as one of those teacher pet things to do. When it came to the tests, sure he didn't know every question, but almost every single test felt like a recycled assignment. If he did it once, regurgitating the same answer felt monotonous if anything, it was always the questions about readings he didn't do that tripped him up. 

Drew sighs as he starts to stand up, "I can't fault you kid, I didn't even go to a proper school so whatever you did was probably fine. I do envy kids like you though, you know, because homework is the only priority you have after classes get out."

Ryan looks at him quizzically as if the statement was born from sheer ignorance, "Or just do it all during study hall. That's why it was there."

Drew shrugs, "Sure, the homework I had took me days out in the field, but if you claim this 'study hall' was enough, then I'll take your word for it." He looks Ryan over before stepping off the street, "You ready to go now?"

Ryan looks past the man and at the building in question. The dominating presence of those forsaken white walls makes his head start to spin just thinking about what could be contained within them. He remembers the zombie woman at the very start of all this and what she hid in the basement. If anything remotely close to that is in there, he wants nothing to do with it—regardless of what Drew says. 

"I'm not going in there," he reasserts unyieldingly just to make his position blatantly clear.

With an exasperated look, Drew rubs the back of his head and look between Ryan and the house. To both of their reliefs, nothing stares at them through the checkerboard curtains.

"I'm certain there's a significant clue in there for finding Seth Heimes," Drew explains, "If you're not going to help, then just stand out here and make sure nothing escapes..." he narrows his eyes with a very serious tone, "And if you run... let's just say you'll be signing a death warrant."

Ryan swallows hard as Drew doesn't waste anymore time. The man heads straight into the building with his weapons drawn while Ryan returns his his feet at a much slower pace. Almost immediately after the front door swings closed, something inside shatters and a zombie cries out with a twisted groan. The house remains dormant as chaos ensues within. 

Not realizing he had dropped his knife, he retrieves it from the ground and stands back up with a drawn out sigh. Nothing can ever be simple for him. Looking back on it, he feels he's been caught up in the heat of the moment for quite a while now—just taking one step forward in the name of survival. But, that doesn't feel like him. At the start of this he just wanted to escape, so at what point did he give up on that ambition and just exist. When Drew said to stand out here and wait, the first thing he thought wasn't "escape route" it was "okay". Going inside may have been a line in the sand for him, but why does waiting outside feel like a completely acceptable thing to ask. He never questioned it when Drew told him to come out here today, he never questioned it when his friends dragged him out to some locally owned grocery store, and here he is accepting the orders to wait from someone who just pulled an unloaded gun on him. It's not that he feels lost or uncomfortable in his own skin, but he just feels... like he doesn't really remember who he was back then. The present is now and the past was back then, so why does it feel like... oh, never mind.

He kicks a rock down the street and as it lands back on the concrete it impacts with the bone chilling sound of shattering glass. Snapping his head towards the origin of the noise, he finds a zombie squirming on the ground underneath a shattered window, the checkerboard curtain flapping in the wind with stains of blood littering the inside of the fabric. The aggravated zombie wears a torn up hoodie and ragged jeans as they scramble to their feet and attempt to climb up to the window. 

Ryan looks to the sidewalk and finds that the zombie is nowhere near it. Not only that, but the zombie has no intention of escape. If he recalls correctly, Drew told him to wait here and catch anything that tries to escape—this situation doesn't fulfill the criteria. Does he help anyway? Help a man who has pressed into his deepest and darkest moments without remorse? Or maybe, he can just do what he was told and wait here—right here. That line of thought is actually quite enticing to him. He quite prefers the safe route of patience—one of the few virtues he has. But perhaps the rest of his body simply doesn't see it that way. He's rushing forward before his thoughts can catch up.

The zombie never looks back as Ryan's knife slides right through its back like paddle through water. The slash is so easy, in fact, that he cuts the entire zombie in half. The two sides slip with the small, uncut chunk of flesh in the front acting as a pivot point for the face to slam into the legs. The entire body collapses to the ground as a faucet of greenish red liquid springs forth from the opening. Ryan steps back in horror as he looks at his blade—completely untouched by vile mixture.

"What the hell," he whispers to himself.

Suddenly two new zombies appear from the corner of the house, both enraged and deadlocked on him. They charge forward with their arms flailing as he struggles to exit his haze.

The first zombie doesn't lung and instead attempts to grapple him like the muscle memory of a wrestler. Ryan avoids this attempt by shove it back, though it ultimately ends in him stepping back instead of the other way around. The second zombie does lung and Ryan instinctively puts his knife in between him and the assailant only for the knife to slide straight into the zombie's skull without a single push on his part. The blade slides cleanly through the head resulting in two halves of a skull slamming into his side and knocking him over. Terrified by blood and brain matter splattering across his chest, he fumbles around until he break free from the corpses empty hold only for a different—less dead—corpse grabbing him with a professional grapple the pins him to the ground on his back. Looking up he finds the zombie drawing its head back preparing to reopen his shoulder wound. His knife arm, partially pinned under the zombie's knee struggles to break free, so in a desperate attempt to regain control, he shoves his other hand against the leg and rips the knife out. The zombie uses Ryan's opening to swing it's head down, but only as Ryan shoves the back of his elbow into the side of its face and follows up with a knife across its throat. He tried to hit it in the head, but his accuracy missed and ends up slicing the throat halfway clean. The subsequent splattering of blood sprays across his face as the zombie twitches and is physically unable to lean it's head any further forward to bite him. Instead the zombie stands in a static twitching motion for a moment before collapsing on top of him.

Ryan carefully slides free of the zombie and stands back up, being sure to look around in case something else appears. Only when the coast seems clear enough does he do a damage assessment.

The first thing he notices is the shiny metal of his knife. The impossibly sharp weapon shines in the sunlight as it has somehow evaded even the slightest blemish to it's surface. Not even traveling through three zombie's bodies produces a stain of blood as the metal remains as untouched as the day it was forged. To add to his bafflement, Ryan's black, leather-like jacket is also completely unfazed by the struggle. The copious amounts of blood that has sprayed itself over him has clung to his hands, undershirt, and face like glue, but it slides off his jacket as if the foreign material was oil against water. The last thing he notices is his hands—completely covered in the greenish, red mixture which reeks of decay. His undershirt and jeans are also completely soaked in the vile substance as he makes himself out to be a horror prop of a slasher movie. Although he can't see it, he can feel the putrid ooze clinging to his cheeks and forehead which makes him immediately use his clean jacket sleeve to ensure nothing gets in his eyes.

He looks down at his knife with a shaky hand. This piece of steel—or whatever it's made of—cannot be real. The masked man gave it to him in order to protect himself, but this is not some simple knife. This weapon might be more unnatural than the zombies he just killed. He wants nothing more than to drop it and step away, but he knows he can't do that. If he does, a zombie might appear at the very moment. For better or for worse, he's stuck with it for the moment.

Looking up, his attention gravitates towards the front door sliding open. Preparing for a fight, he raises his strange knife and braces for impact. What reveals itself on the other side of the door is not an obvious threat, however, instead it's only Drew.

The highly skilled man has exactly zero percent of the battle scares Ryan caries as his clothes are completely clear of any zombie gore. In fact, he's so untouched that when he notices Ryan, he immediate draws his pistol and prepares to shoot him. Perhaps he would have too if it not for Ryan holds his hands up to protect his face and falling down to a knee.

"Oh," Drew says with relief, "It's just you."

Realizing that he's not about to be shot, Ryan resheaths his knife and lets his arms hang. With an exasperated sigh, he stands back up.

Drew gives him and the corpses a quick glance, "What the hell happened to you?"

Ryan shrugs his shoulders lightly as he avoids eye contact, "They... were strong."

Upon looking back, Ryan notices Drew visually inspecting the corpses with clinical precision. His gaze interrogates the scene as if it were a crime and his eyebrows raise as he reverse engineers the zombie against the window sill, "You don't see that every day," he whispers under his breath.

"So," Ryan says, not curious but itching for an answer, "Did you find what you were looking for?"

Drew nods calmly, "I think I have his trail. He's still got some distance and misdirection but I'm getting closer."

Ryan turns around and starts walking away. To be honest, he doesn't care if Drew tries to threaten him again—he's going back to the prison. Nothing short of shower will clear his head both physically and mentally of everything that's happened today.

"Wait," Drew calls out.

Out of some semblance of respect, Ryan glances back.

"You can have the rest of the day off," Drew begins, "But starting tomorrow, I want to show you a few things."

Ryan rolls his eyes, "And what if I refuse."
