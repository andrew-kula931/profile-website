Upon entering his foster parents' house, he’s greeted with a toy drone ramming into his face. The rotating blade cuts a very shallow cut on his cheek but he withstands it and keeps walking by. The drone, however, takes a long plummet to the tile floor below.

“No, my drone!” Charlie, the biological son of Ryan’s foster parents, yells.

“Oh, Ryan you’re back?!” Karmen, the foster mother, calls out.

Ryan disregards them and goes to the stairs leading to his room above. He knows Karmen is about to turn the corner from the kitchen and talk to him, but putting one foot on the stairs will give the impression that he doesn’t want to speak for long.

As expected, Karmen turns the corner to greet Ryan glancing at Charlie who is now picking up his potentially damaged drone off the floor.

“Did you invite anyone to your birthday party tomorrow?” Karmen asks, “I’ve got a cake, balloons, and even a present I know you’ll like.”

Karmen and her husband Steve are his current and seventh set of foster parents he’s had which will soon be his last. Karmen is plagued with severe social anxiety so she tends to stay home but despite that, she tries her very best to act like a mother figure for Ryan. He doesn’t buy the mother part but can sympathize with her fears and pains so he puts up with her. Charlie can be irritating at times and even make Ryan want to beat him, but in the end, he’s only just a kid and listens when he’s in a good mood… or when Steve comes home. Steve is the bane of this family. Having to cover for Karmen’s unemployment, he tends to work eighty-hour weeks at a low-paying job. His saving grace is alcohol and he’s not afraid to let everyone know just how he feels about his life. The one thing he has going for him is how much of a suck-up he is to people with more power than him. Ryan joined this family because they were the last willing to accept him and because Steve got an ‘under the table’ payment to get Ryan out of his previous family's house. The family remains intact most of the time, but when Steve comes back on a bad day, everyone hides.

“No,” Ryan responds.

Karmen quickly grows outwardly sympathetic by his statement, “Are you doing okay at school? You know you can tell me.”

The drone by the door starts up again and quickly sails through the air racing around the room. Charlie follows behind it eagerly which draws some of the tension away from Ryan.

“I’m fine,” Ryan says apathetically before walking up the stairs without a second thought. He retreats to his room and locks the door behind him. Karmen’s subsequent words are muffled out by the music Ryan puts in his ears from an old mp3 player.

Karmen is a good actor, but Ryan has done nothing but trouble for this family for the past two years. He gets injuries multiple times a month and as well as he can hide them, he slips up from time to time. A few were from school early on, but most were from his frustration and anger he let on trees. Karmen figured out early on that he was being bullied and brought it up to the administrators. They suspended three kids, which ultimately ended the bullying but nothing truly meaningful ever came of it: his life still felt like a joke.

The beat of his music suddenly ramps up to its climate and his head bobs with the beat—it hits the wall every once in a while. He isn’t crazy about music but it works really well when Karmen is trying to talk to him on the other side of the door.

One more day and he’ll be a free man. He will no longer be a burden to anyone.

He pulls his broken phone out of his pocket and sets it on the floor next to him. It broke a while ago and he’s been lying to Karmen about it ever since. A bully didn’t break that one however, this situation was completely unexpected.

About a month or two ago, he was walking to a grocery store to pick up some snacks. It was the middle of the night because he just wanted some fresh air. Suddenly, a guy runs out of a nearby building holding a folder and begins running off in his direction. Shouts follow him and it’s clear that whatever the man is holding, he shouldn’t have it. Instinctively, Ryan took out his phone and snapped a picture. He wasn’t too sure why he did it, but it was the first thought that passed through his head. The man saw Ryan doing this and rushed right up to him. Ryan stepped out of the way trying to avoid any further provocation, but the man stripped his phone from his hands and smashed it on the ground. He quickly took off as soon as the deed was done.

Ryan doesn’t honestly know what happened to the man after that night, but he truly doesn’t care enough to find out. His phone was never important to him anyway. Karmen would try and buy him a new one if she found out what happened, but that would cost money and Steve wouldn’t like that.

His bedroom remains still for the duration of the song, but outside his window, a bit of a commotion occurs. Ryan peers out to see a dog vigorously barking at the window of a neighbor’s house. It continues to bark for a solid thirty seconds before a middle-aged woman walks out of the front door and up to the dog. The ‘crazy’ dog takes a bite out of the woman’s leg and is given no pain response from the woman. Inside, she does something even crazier. She reaches her head down and bites the back of the dog’s neck. It immediately breaks free and whimpers as it runs around the corner of the house. The woman follows in a sprint and catches up right before the dog goes underneath the fence behind the house. She kicks the dog to force it to its back. Before the dog can regain its footing, the woman drops onto it.

The next few seconds would make normal people vomit, but Ryan just watches—analyzing. The dog's barks slowly drown to silence and only once the action simmers to a simple feast does Ryan back off from the window. 

Great. The rumors about mysterious riots around the world must be true; a zombie apocalypse is starting. Although mostly sarcastic, he can't shake the feeling that his initial conjecture might actually be more accurate than he cares to admit. Regardless, he needs to first sort the facts. Whether that woman is a zombie or just some deranged person, it doesn't matter, all he knows is that she's extremely hostile.

He slides up against the wall and peeks back at the scene. To his amazement and horror, the woman and the dog are gone with only a small patch of blood left behind. 

That’s... unexpected. The woman’s actions were not normal, yes, but assuming he knows that it's a zombie is pushing his luck. A very dangerous thought quickly pops into his head and every rational part of him screams no—he entertains the idea anyway. A deep sense of thrill races through his veins as a spark of interest arises out of nowhere and makes his ghostly existence a little bit more tolerable. Intellectually this is the worst possible thing that could be going through his head, but something... inexplainable, makes him surge with energy and excitement.

He gets up and moves to the desk in his room but promptly stops himself. 

What is he thinking? This isn’t his problem so he shouldn’t get involved. Even if it were his problem, why would he put his life at risk when he’s so close to freedom—that’s just pure idiocracy. Even with this adrenaline high, he can't let the momentum of the situation get the better of himself.

Ryan slides open the drawer to his desk and stares at a pocket knife he managed to get his hands on a few weeks ago. The small, metal blade is only about three inches long, but it's the only method of self-defense he has in his arsenal… but is this really the time to use it.

He slams the drawer closed and runs his hands through his hair.

This can’t be real. He’s not actually thinking of doing this. He should just keep listening to music and call it a day… So why then… Why is his heart racing at the idea of something so dangerous? Why?

He looks back out the window at the house across the street. He imagines what it would have been like if he’d been more proactive—actually done something—when his mother was ill. If he didn’t just sit around and watch his life walk by, maybe he wouldn’t be such a screw-up now.

His eyes focus back on the drawer with a newfound conviction. He then pockets the knife and opens his window before hanging off the edge. With him hanging off, the fall to the ground is only a few feet, and he easily lands—it’s an action he’s done plenty of times.

The street is eerily quiet for a Wednesday afternoon which is probably only amplified by the suspense of what is to come. He crosses the street and crouches down next to the blood spot. Several blades of grass are tainted with dark, red, painty blood. For what he saw happen to the dog, it’s surprising there isn’t more. Ryan takes a quick moment to listen for any sound of movement. The slight breeze greets his ear and nothing else. With another quick, deep breath he finds himself pressed up against the fence. The knife in his pocket gets pulled out and put at the ready. This is the moment of truth; if this really is some zombie, he has to learn as much as he can before everything goes to shit. Many would probably call him stupid for doing this, but he sees it as reconnaissance. Why wait for a horde of zombies to learn about them when he can study one and learn all of it before it's too late? At the end of the day, however, this situation is an absolute win, either he gets to learn more about an upcoming threat or he doesn’t have to deal with it ever again. Both options are deemed acceptable in his skewed mental narrative.

He calms his overactive nerves with the thoughts of how terrible his life is and takes the moment to hop the fence. 

The other side presents no abnormal threat. A trampoline sits in the grass of the yard and a frisbee lies nearby leaning against a different wall of the fence. Everything looks normal, that is, until he sees the back door. The door is cracked open and a stream of blood leads into the house. 

He crouches low to the ground and inches forward with his pocket knife raised. If something is going to jump-scare him, now is the time. Step by step he gets closer to the door. Once he’s about five steps away he starts circling the door to give some distance in case something jumps out. The last thing he wants is to be within arm's reach of a cannibalistic monster. One step more and the inside of the house comes into view. His blood runs cold at the sight.

The trail of blood, presumably from the dog, is met with a trail of blood from other sources meeting at various points and all converging around a single door at the back of the kitchen. The door leads to the basement. With another glance, he counts four trails of blood. The connection is immediate, and his body refuses to move after realizing it.

The family who lives here consists of a mom, a dad, two daughters, and a dog. The mom is the aggressor so that leaves four living individuals in the household and four separate trials of blood. The horror sinks in deeper and deeper until he can’t even tell if he’s seeing the correct thing. His body starts to shake, and a scream develops in his throat, but then he catches himself. Dead bodies are nothing new to him. Action is how he will live, not panic.

He’s not dumb enough to investigate that scene by himself so he heads back to his house reaching a sprint by the time he makes it to the street. He plans to take Charlie’s drone and use its camera to see what’s down there. It might give a clue to a weakness or, in worse cases, the location of a hive of this zombie-like woman.

Ryan enters his foster parent’s house and grabs the home phone making sure to stay out of sight of Karmen. He dials 911 and places it to his ear. After a single ring, he’s brought to a recorded message saying, “Emergency services are unavailable at this time. Your calling device has been pinged and police will be sent to your location as soon as available. Press one to document your situation. Press two…” he sets the phone down. He honestly doesn't know a thing about emergency response, but it somehow makes logical sense that the phone line services would be down—the fact that mass panic hasn’t broken out yet is quite terrifying, however.

His next course of action is to go for the drone. After stealthily searching the house, he finds Karmen and Charlie in the kitchen eating a snack while the drone is on the floor in the living room. He gets to his stomach and crawls across the floor behind the couch to stay out of the kitchen’s view. Once he gets a hand on the drone and controller, he reverses and sees himself right back out of the house. 

He runs quickly back to the neighbor’s house and prepares the drone. The controller has a screen on it that displays the camera view strategically positioned on the head of the little flying device. The drone itself consists of a center box with a camera and computer chip and then four arms on each corner with a propeller on top. The battery on the camera view shows about half which is more than enough. 

Ryan pulls out his pocket knife and jumps the fence again scanning the backyard once he clears the top. The yard is still empty so he hustles over to the back door and peers in just like he did the first time. The blood trail has unsurprisingly not vanished but the house itself feels even more cryptic than ever before. The overarching tone radiates not just through him but through everything around him. He knows what’s in the basement, but he needs to confirm. At the end of the day, it's better to know than to think.

Ryan glances around at his surroundings once more and places the drone on the ground. He starts it up and within seconds it’s in the air zooming straight through the back door and into the house. As he pilots the drone he makes sure to check around so nothing sneaks up on him.

The drone turns down into the basement and the trails of blood only get thicker and thicker the closer he gets to the bottom. The stairs to the basement are wooden and the basement itself acts more as a cellar with concrete walls and an unfinished ceiling. The drone clears the bottom doorway and enters the main room of the basement. No windows are available in the basement, but a bathroom light blinks slowly on and off. Every flicker allows Ryan to get a better look at the scene. 

At first, he thinks he sees a pile of clothes in the center of the room, but then he gets close enough to make out what it is. It’s a pile of clothes, just a generic, average pile of clothes that need to be washed. He sighs with relief. Then the bathroom light flickers again, and he sees just how dirty this strange collection of fabric is. His blood runs cold; ice even. Every second that passes the ice only gets colder and colder. His eyes are glued to the screen as the face of the father reveals itself under the pile of blood-soaked jeans. The father’s face is stuck in the horrified expression of pain and agony. One of his eyes is gouged out, and his tongue is missing allowing for blood to flood out. Despite the blood still running, one can still take solace in the fact that the man is at least saved from any further torture. On the same side of the pile, a leg sticks out… or what used to be a leg. The limp only presents itself up to the knee. Everything after that was torn off, bone and all. The leg is too small to be a grown man’s however. What he once believed to be a fur coat now reveals itself to be just that, only the fur was not of some wild animal and it was still warm. This deplorable object sits on top of the pile while the snout resides at the bottom… or rather half of the snout is at the bottom, the rest looks to be completely eaten off. 

Suddenly, the pile shifts. Nothing substantial but just enough for a drop of blood and fling out and splash on the camera lens. He finally allows his shaking eyes the relief of a blink and it happens again. A drop of blood hits the camera lens… only this drop wasn’t on the drone, this drop was on the controller itself.

His head darts up to meet the eyes of a bloodthirsty woman above him hanging out of the second-floor window. Her visage radiates hunger and the blood running down her face only accentuates this desire. The insanity-ridden face quickly reforms into a smile, a long and malicious grin that isn’t happy at all. 

Ryan drops the controller and dives out of the way to dodge the woman who falls right in his previous position. He shuffles to his feet and prepares his knife—shaking. The zombie woman snaps her head up and leaps at Ryan who falls to his back letting her fly over. In the short window of time while he's on his back he manages to catch a better look at the antagonist and it makes his skin craw.. Her teeth are as long as a fang and and no less sharp. Her eyes are a storm of ink and crimson and her arms are frail and claw-like as they narrow off a the ends. Bits and pieces of her hair have even fallen off leaving behind a partially bald scalp with wrinkled, irritated skin. Finally, to make the whole aesthetic even more horrific, he spots a name tag on her torn-up business suit written in another language. Despite the twisted appearance, Ryan recognizes the human features beneath as those belonging to the woman who once lived here and, as such, can only assume that this is not some random imposter but the true resident of this home.

The zombie woman charges at Ryan again this time lowering herself closer to the ground. He pivots on his back foot and charges to the door of the house, closing it just in time to hear a slam on the door behind him. Another slam follows shortly behind, but then... silence. Ryan’s heart races like a bullet train as he is forced to use every bit of self-control to suppress the panic he’s enduring. Then, to make matters worse, the window of the room over shatters and a something hits the floor with a thump. 

Ryan glances around the room frantically and ultimately settles on an empty bowl on the kitchen table. He picks it up and rushes over to the closed door of a small walk-in closet. The kitchen is in the corner of the house with the door to the backyard on one side with a door to the basement and walking closet directly next to it. On the other side of the kitchen is a hallway to the front door and beside that is another door likely leading to the room with the smashed window. The walk-in closet sits right next to the back door flush with the unexplored room containing the deranged woman giving him a clearly break between himself and the zombie's line of sight.

The zombie woman’s footsteps echo through the house as she slowly walks closer and closer to the kitchen from the unexplored room. Every step chills Ryan’s body deep down to the core. Finally, right as the footsteps are about to enter the kitchen, they stop. The silence is deafening as Ryan's racing heart becomes a powerful roar in his ears.Not even a breath escapes Ryan’s body as the tension builds and horror looms over him. 

Why’d he have to do this? He should have just stayed in his room. If there's any truth to the saying, “Curiosity killed the cat”, he's about to discover it first hand and he doesn’t get nine lives. In the heat of the moment, his depressing, lonely life doesn’t seem so bad when faced with a brutal demise.

As the tension climbs to an even higher climax, the zombie woman comes into view as she steps into the kitchen looking around starting with the basement side of the room.

Ryan doesn’t waste time analyzing. He throws the bowl across the room into the hallway making sure the bowl doesn’t cross the line of sight of the woman. Her head snaps to the location as the bowl shatters on the ground. He uses the commotion to leap forward and stab the woman right in the back of the neck—he prays the neck is still a vital area. Without hesitation, he lifts his foot and kicks the woman forward making sure not to lose grip of his knife. The zombie woman takes an unbalanced step forward as her limbs twitch uncontrollably and an inhuman squeaking echoes from her bloody lips. In a sort of staggered fashion, the woman attempts to turn around slowly but Ryan responds by stabbing his knife right into the woman's eye as she turns. The zombie's knees buckle as she collapses to the floor bringing Ryan with her as he pulls the knife out and stabbing her over and over. This thing is not going to reanimate on him no matter what. Only when the head is halfway severed from the rest of the body does he stand up and back off. 

His eyes gloss over the bloody corpse unsure of what he's supposed to be seeing. Then, his eyes lower further, and he finds blood running up his arms turning his skin a color they shouldn't be. His stomach churns as nausea sets in and his knees threaten to buckle under his weight as he stumbles backwards and catches himself on the door. He looks at the bloody knife in his hands and drops it immediately before kicking it away from himself. So what if that was a zombie... did he? Did that?

His hands refuse to quit shaking profusely as he slowly regains his senses and grabs the controller and flies the drone back up to the back door. He takes extra care not to look at his fingers as he operates the controller. Afterwards, he washes his knife off in the kitchen sink and wipes the blood off the controller and his arms. After all of his evidence is gone, he crouches down next to the corpse of the zombie despite his inner monologue, and gut, protesting.

The once hostile, hatred-filled body, now lays still; a stark contrast to its grotesque, mutated appearance. The zombie isn’t as he imagined a zombie would be, though his expectations were vague to begin with. The mutations of the woman around her face and arms all seem to be present. The skin around her claws is clumped and cut as if the claws were forced out from the inside. The claws and teeth themselves are dark and almost moist; he isn’t going to test them and touch them. The mutation couldn’t have been long ago and it must have happened very quickly. 

After analyzing the corpse for another few moments, he stands up and turns to leave. On the door of the walk-in closet hangs a calendar. The top picture of the calendar is one of the two daughters swinging, smiling at the camera. The lighting is so perfect and their smiles seem genuine. The bottom of the calendar consists of the rows of days. Various marks are made on days describing different events the family needs to attend, but one particular day stands out. October 10th, today, is marked with big red letters filling the entire box reading, “Mom comes home.” 

This woman is a carrier of this disease, or whatever it is. After killing it, Ryan is most likely infected but that fact is inevitable. He wanted to learn and everything comes at a cost.

He thinks through all the zombie lore he can remember. The most common way is through saliva or blood, but none of those got on him. The closest thing would be the blood he washed off from his knife but that’s still only on the exterior of his body. Then again, he didn’t know if pop culture references would be applicable to the current situation. He would have to monitor himself very closely for the next few days to be sure to avoid any signs of infection. 

Finally, he takes his exit from the house and never looks back as he walks back home. Karmen never notices the slight smear of blood on his hand that he missed cleaning up.

About an hour later, the authorities arrive at Ryan’s house due to the call. Steve didn’t get home yet and Karmen has a borderline breakdown when seeing the police at her house. Ryan handled the situation and explained to them what happened. He said that he saw a woman across the street kill her dog. He left out the part where he went there. The police checked out the scene and came back to him with a very clear lie. They told him that the mother of the family suffered from severe mental trauma and needed help, but that they'd handled it. They said that the situation was taken care of and that the family would need to be moved temporarily. Ryan saw through the bluff despite what he knew. He also saw the fear.
