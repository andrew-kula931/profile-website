The high schoolers’ base bustles with every kid inside scrambling to gather all his or her belongings. The front gate sits sealed shut as two teens stand behind it with makeshift spears. As he approaches, one of the guys stationed at the front notices him and makes a call to the rest of the camp causing an uproar of motion. When no zombies follow him, he seems to retract his call and the commotion behind them settles dramatically. Thankfully they don't notice his limp as he tries his best to walk normally despite the pain. He finds himself able to stand upright by the time the gate slides open and Chloe is the only one waiting for him.

"What happened!?" she as asks with genuine concern as she steps up to him.

"It was just a chase..." Ryan intentionally misremembers, "I lost them in a building a few blocks from here."

Chloe lifts her hand up to his face and rubs it against his cheek. At first he's completely taken aback by the boldness and forward demeanor of his friend, but then he feels the brittle crunching of dried blood on his cheek. Chloe's eyes tell him all he needs to know. He should have looked in a mirror before coming back.

Ryan promptly runs a hand over his pale visage and feels multiple pieces break away as he swipes his hand downward. There isn't quite as much remnants of the fight as he anticipated but it's more than enough to warrant Chloe's concerned expression.

"I promise, I'm fine," Ryan defends as he looks away.

Chloe's gaze falls only for her eyes to land on something he'd preferred she didn't see. Her eyes widen.

"Wait!" Ryan exclaims with a reserved volume, "It-it's not what you think."

Chloe looks into his eyes trying to discern some level of sincerity from his troubled complexion. Perhaps she saw something redeemable, or maybe she just felt sorry for him, either way she ends up looking back to the camp before making a final decision on what to think.

"Do you want me to put it in my backpack," Chloe offers, "—I mean, of course you want to keep it with you. I just thought... because they might think... because of, you know, the school. I'm sorry."

Ryan reaches over to his hip and feels the weapon that saved his life. For a moment he almost considers telling everyone the truth, explaining that he's had a gun and he'll use it to keep them save. The thought crosses his mind that maybe this weapon saved everyone—maybe he deserves... that's when Drew's words reoccur in his mind and it's empty magazine weigh down on any aspirations. Getting his hopes up now might lead to failed expectations later. He can't let himself get ahead of where he really is—where his weaknesses really lie.

"Would you," Ryan starts, grasping Chloe's attention completely, "Hold on to it for now? It's empty and... I can't imagine the prison will allow an armed teenager."

Chloe hesitates, likely disbelieving Ryan's honestly, but quickly pulls the backpack off her back, unzips the largest compartment, and holds it out to him, "You can take it back, whenever you need."

“Thanks,” he says simply, quietly unclipping it from his belt, holster and all, and placing it into the bag, "I owe you."

She shakes her head, "Don't mention it."

"Where's Blake?" Ryan asks looking around the busy crowd of teens, "Did he make it back?"

Chloe looks back to the crowd, finally drawing some eyes their direction, “He did make it back. I just saw him a little bit ago when he told us all to get packed and prepare for an attack. He must be back in the barber shop.”

"Shut up, they're probably confessing their love," one of the boys jokes a bit too loud on the wall.

He disregards it and locks eyes with Chloe one last time before heading towards the barber shop. As he enters in, he finds Caid and Blake discussing something quietly in the back of the room.

“Yeah, I didn’t really trust it either,” Caid says before they both realize Ryan walked in.

“Ryan! What happened?” Blake exclaims, stepping away from his conversation and rushing across the room.

"I lost them in a building a few blocks away," Ryan reiterates, find this excuse to be the cleanest to explain. Honestly, he feels a bit guilty lying, but now's not the time to explain that he's involved with a mysterious physician and a zombie that can talk. Those details can come later.

Caid does a brief visual inspection of Ryan's new getup and can't help but comment, "What's with the fit?"

Looking down, Ryan suddenly feels self-conscious about how he looks. The leather-like jacket may be a bit overkill but it doesn't seem that crazy to wear, especially because the jeans and jacket definitely provide much better scratch protection than his former t-shirt and athletic pants. He personally approves of its functionality, but the look evidently is conspicuous which, again, unsettles him.

"I... uh," Ryan starts failing to make eye contact, "Saw it in a window... thought it'd work better than a t-shirt."

Caid nods knowingly as he holds back a sly smile, "I see how it is. Looks fine to me."

Blake rolls his eyes, "Give him a break, that's more fitting clothes than what we've got."

Caid glances down at his gym shorts, "touché".

Turning back to Ryan, Blake takes on a more sincere demeanor, the same one he had when Ryan stepped out of the freezer, "But you're okay, right?"

Ryan hesitates, invasive thoughts flooding his mind in ways that demand a visceral reaction, he forces it all back with a last-ditch mental effort and nods, "Yeah."

Blake studies him closely for a moment, not buying the answer, but likely debating how far he wants to push. Eventually, he brushes it off and looks back between the two teens, “Alright, let’s get back to the matter at hand. We already plan on leaving the Corvette here and coming back for it later. The engine is a bit on the louder side so we don’t want to attract unwanted attention—especially since everyone will be on foot.”

“Agreed,” Caid responds, as Ryan glancing at the expensive white vehicle now being used as a glorified table.

“The question is: How are we going to get there without an incident. With just the two of you, you allegedly drew a small gang of zombies after you. What will we do with a whole crowd of people?” Caid asks, taking a seat in the barber chair.

The room goes silent for a moment as they each require a bit of time to contemplate their options. After nearly twenty seconds of silence, it becomes apparent that none of them actually have any idea what they are supposed to do.

“I got an idea,” Beth says leaning into the room from outside, “I overheard the jist of it.”

“Why didn’t you come in sooner?” Blake asks, trying to divert any suspicion from their secrecy.

“I thought it was a boys only club, didn’t want to intrude on your male bonding rituals,” Beth comments sarcastically as she steps inside and takes a position next a mirror.

Blake rolls his eyes, “What’s your idea, Beth?”

“I think we should just trust those guys from the camp. As you said when you got back, they seem competent enough and if they're right, all we need to do to get around is to just be quiet. I don't think there's really much to brainstorm.” she smiles at them waiting for them to get over their egos and accept the truth.

Caid sighs as he shrugs his shoulders, "Maybe she's got a point, we might be overthinking it a little. After all, they do have guns, and we don't."

Beth’s smile only grows as Blake nods too, “I'm still a bit hesitant, but after thinking about it, there really isn't much we can do. Just stay on guard in case anything suspicious happens.”

As soon as he finishes speaking an underclassman runs to the door and peaks his head in, “The guys are almost here, are you all coming?”

Blake nods to the three other seniors and exits the room to go rally up the high school survivors. The remainder of them drift towards the door but don't leave as quickly as the first. After glancing at Caid, Beth follows suit and leaves the room, hopefully for the last time.

Ryan's about to follow but, Caid grabs his shoulder and pulls him back, “Hey man, I have a question to ask?” 

Ryan turns around as the door swings closed, “Sure.”

Caid’s eyes struggle to match his gaze as he nervously throws his hands into his pockets. His shoulders droop a bit forward and he bites the side of his lip all before saying a word.

“Have you… I mean… have you actually killed one of them?” he asks awkwardly as if the question is inherently uncomfortable and degrading.

Ryan studies him suspiciously, not because he's offended, but because it's evident there's something more here, “...yeah. Why?”

Caid takes a moment fighting some sort of shyness Ryan never knew he had, “It’s embarrassing.”

Perhaps Ryan should have lightened the mood after such a statement, but he does nothing, he just listens.

“The zombies... actually scare me man…” he looks out the windows at the teens gathering behind the door as he bites his lip again and rubs a hand across the back of his head, “I thought I could handle it... but then Max and I looked in the freezer.”

Those words cling to Ryan’s mind as he mentally fumbles forward trying to formulate a response. It dawns on him that most of these teens haven’t been through what he has. They haven’t encountered these monsters as intimately as he has. Most of them just escaped the school, watched their friends get slaughtered and reborn, and lose their entire way of life to the bodies of people they once knew. This is something he's always understood intellectually, but hearing Caid actually come out and say it is something far more disturbing. It's as if they all had watched the most horrific movie imaginable, but Ryan didn't have a screen to protect him.

To only make matters worse, he remembers when he too was a zombie. How he could see everything that was happening, feel everything the zombie did, and hear the grotesque noises that zombie made. If that was the fate of every zombie, how could he tell someone that was their future if they failed—that's too heartless.

“It’s... rough,” he finally says as Caid nods his head silently, "Just... do yourself a favor and stay away. I... I don't really have any advice that I could give you."

Caid nods his head a couple of times and looks down to the floor in thought, "That's probably fair."

Ryan starts out the door, "Hang in there though. We're all in this together."

Outside every high schooler has already rounded up their belongings and currently loiters by the front gate. A couple of kids have makeshift weapons in hand, but most just have their school backpack and a look of extreme anxiousness plastered on their face. Despite all the assurances they've been given, many of them haven't left the wall since it was constructed.

"You guys ready?!" a familiar, masculine voice calls out from the other side of the wall.

Shortly after, Drew and five other men arrive through the front gate. Two of the men admire the teenager's handywork as they enter and quickly inspect the backside with just as much curiosity. Besides Drew, none of the men give Ryan the same vibe Steve and Bill did so he makes the careful assumption that they're not actually former military. With that in mind, he feels a bit more at ease being around them. 

Drew gives the group of teens careful instructions to stay behind him and in front of two of his guys. As long as no one strays and everyone keeps their voice down, everyone will make it safe and sound. This comes as a great relief for many of the teens, but Ryan can't quite get comfortable as Drew looks to him specifically for an unnerving amount of time throughout the speech. 

Before too long, whole group is making its way down the street with armed guards stationed on every corner of the group. Most people speak in a whisper or even keep their mouth shut all together, but every now and then someone laughs a little too loud. One or two nearby teens give them a glare to settle their inappropriate outbursts, but strangely enough, the adults completely disregard this behavior as they walk. 

Blake and most of the other seniors take point and start a bit of small talk with Drew and the guy next to him. Ryan, however, finds himself lingering near the back of the pack with only a stray glance from the adult stationed back there. Chloe, initially walking with Beth and Alison, slowly breaks off and approaches Ryan, matching his pace..

“You've been to their base already. How does it look? Blake told us all his opinion, but... I was wondering what you think.” she folds her hands behind her back.

Ryan glances at the armed man a little ways to his right before looking back, “I think it's trustworthy enough. There's also a few familiar faces,” he explains briefly.

"Really!?" Chloe exclaims, "Who?"

Ryan looks ahead, "You'll have to wait and see."

A subtle smile crosses Chloe's face as she fiddles with a small blue beaded bracelet around her wrist. The miniature wooden beads comprising the bracelet rolls easily under her fingers as she pinches the back and forth. On the center of her wrist is a small medallion, hardly larger than the beads surrounding it with a certain figure Ryan has never seen before. He wonders what it's significance could mean as she looks at it with tender eyes.

"I hope," Chloe whispers to herself.

"Hm?" Ryan prompts, thinking that she may have meant to articulate the thought a bit more clearly.

She looks up a bit surprised, "Oh, I wasn't saying anything."

"Okay," Ryan accepts bluntly, "...were you... are you hoping to see your family," Ryan sighs knowing he wouldn't want to asked that question, not right now, "Sorry, it's probably... probably a sore subject."

Chloe’s smile fades and her gaze falls to the old concrete street below, “It’s fine. I don’t know where any of my family is right now, but I hold out hope that I'll see them again. Who knows, maybe they're waiting for me up ahead."

Ryan loosens up not wanting to push too deep, "Yeah, I'm sure you'll meet again."

“Thanks,” Chloe’s smile begins to grow once more even if a bit somber.

“You’re a real romantic, aren’t you,” Max jokes as he butts into the conversation, “Talking about family really gets to a woman’s heart. Amirite?” His smug smile immediately fades as he notices Chloe's demeanor, "Oh shit, did I interrupt something?"

Ryan takes a deep breath. Romance was never his intent—it probably wasn't for Chloe either.

"It's fine," Chloe says quickly lightening her expression and standing up a bit straighter, "What'd you want Max?"

Max chuckles uncomfortably, "Seriously, I didn't mean to intrude."

He's met with a playful push of Chloe's, "Oh lighten up, you look better when you're laughing for real."

Max hesitates as he stares the girl down defensively. Their silent conversation quickly concludes, however, as Max shakes his arms a bit before placing them behind his head and taking a much more relaxed demeanor, “Alright then, let me start off by saying that I'm on board this ship,” Chloe blushes and averts her eyes while Ryan just inspects boy, mostly blind to what the meaning is supposed to be. Max keeps going, "My first girlfriend thought I was the funniest person in the world. She wouldn't stop laughing when I told her stories about all the stupid stuff me and my brothers did."

“And how old were you at the time?” Ryan asks disinterestedly.

Max purses his lips as he looks up into the sky in thought, “I was in like… fourth grade.”

“Then that’s not even a girlfriend then,” Chloe adds finally reengaged into the conversation.

“Maybe, maybe not, either way that was only the first of many,” Max smiles, holding back a laugh.

Neither Ryan nor Chloe has an adequate response to that. Max seemingly expected one as he gives them ample time to react, but unlike Caid, neither of them are quite so quick witted. Instead Max just shrugs his shoulders, “I’m only joking around. I think everyone is a bit too tense for their own good.”

Ryan truly wishes he could accept that claim and believe there were still people who could think like that. But... as Max hurries forward and joins a group of junior girls, Ryan notices the blue wristband still wrapped firmly around his wrist—it's mere presence refuse to allow Ryan a moment of hope.

“Is it really that obvious?” Chloe stresses as she tries her best to conceal her red ears. Her light blond hair shining in the sunlight which only makes her efforts more obvious. He'd laugh, but he finds himself starring a bit to closely—a bit too intrigued.

“Is what obvious?” Ryan asks, using the vague question to force himself to snap out of it.

She looks into Ryan’s ignorant eyes and her cheeks grow red in embarrassment. She then looks away giving Ryan a sort of cold shoulder vibe. He's honestly completely confused about what's going on inside her head, but she clearly doesn't want to address it directly so he'll respect her boundaries.

As they go silent for a moment, Ryan notices Chloe’s friends, Beth and Alison namely, looking at them and talking to each other. It reminds him of just a few days ago when that sharp tongued Beth convinced him to meet Chloe in the gym. Who knew that a simple event like that would lead to a friendship in the apocalypse.

“So what have you been up to since you escaped that school?” Chloe asks, clearly searching for an out to escape the situation. Little does she know that she's already asked him that question, though it has been a whole day so he can't feel too offended.

For a moment, Ryan almost considers trying to mentally summarize the last few days, but that only lasts until the creep in the school and after that... he'd prefer not to think about it. Honestly, so much has happened, that there's really no good answer here without lying or being misleading. What does he even say? Maybe he diverts the conversation entirely and keeps things light, maybe it's better that way.

"Just wound up with my foster family honestly," Ryan shrugs thinking this is the safest route for both of them.

A minor shock strikes Chloe as eyebrows raise just slightly, "You're a foster child?"

Ryan shrugs, of all the things going on in his life, this is the least thing he's secretive about, "Yeah, have been for most of my life."

Chloe, still a bit shocked, offers him a sympathetic expression, one that Ryan doesn't particularly care for, "I had no idea."

He shakes his head and diverts his eyes, it isn't his place to get annoyed at an honest reaction. Heck, everyone seems to do this when he explains that his mom died when he was in elementary school. That being said, it never gets easier to see someone look down on him and offer their pity when they have no idea what it's like. He isn't sad, he just wants people to move on—this is one of the things he thought the apocalypse would have helped with.

"It is what is is," Ryan eventually concludes, "But... that's enough about me... uh... what-what do you plan on doing, you know, now that we've officially graduated?"

Chloe’s empathetic gaze shifts upwards slightly in attitude as the subject returns to something more conversational, “I did plan on picking up some hobbies like gardening, I know it sounds cliche but my mom and I really did enjoy it," she touches her lip momentarily, "Maybe... I'll learn to sew. Yeah. I think that makes since, after all, we can't just go to the mall like we used to."

"The mall?" Ryan wonders, not because he doesn't know what it is, but suddenly realizing that he's never been to one. Sure he's seen plenty of pop culture references and even some videos online of people walking through them, but personally he's never had a desire nor opportunity to enter one. 

"Yeah," she says with a hint of excitement, "I feel like it's been forever since I've been to one. Alison always dragged me out to one whenever there was a book signing or something. Honestly though, Beth was the real spender anyways... For some reason I really want to go to a mall again. Hey, Ryan, you could come too," she catches herself again and immediately backtracks, "I mean, only if you want. I know malls can be kinda girly."

Ryan shrugs thankful of the conversation trajectory, "Yeah, I'd love to come."

Chloe smiles, "Oh, and sorry about this, but we'll have to postpone the scouting adventure we planned last night. It seems, we'll likely be caught up in other stuff for a bit."

He nods as the tall walls of the prison begin to take form amidst the plethora of trees down the road. A few people gasp at the sight while most just study it curiously, after all, this will become their new home for the foreseeable future.

The guard towers sit with someone inside checking the surrounding area with their sniper rifle. After a quick callout from Drew, the gate slides open and the crowd of teens enter into the prison and, more importantly, into safety. The group walks through the first gate and quickly through the second with every head turning to see them as they pass by. Most of the people here are hard at working reinforcing the outer wall or beginning the preliminary stages of a farm. A couple of people sit around eating lunch which makes Ryan’s stomach grumble, perhaps a bit too loudly.

Now that he thinks about it, he hasn't properly eaten in a hot second. He did have an ice cream sandwich the other day and before that was... the rabbit. With how much has happened, he feels like he should have noticed it much sooner, yet here he is seeing a sandwich a little ways away and realizing that he could eat a whole whale and still be hungry.

"Man, I could eat," Ryan says aloud, mostly to himself.

"You and me both," Chloe smiles beside him, "I could eat a whale."

Ryan pauses after the unexpected mind reading, or would it be mind syncing... shared thought? He brushes off the digression and keeps walking with the group.

Michael Grail walks out from the prison followed by his wife and a few others, the entrance causes several students gasp at the sight of their long lost teacher. She just smiles and waves at all of them as Michael addresses the crowd.

“Welcome everyone, my name is Michael Grail and I’m sure many of you recognize my wife Stephanie. I'm in charge of this place, so if you need anything and don't know where to go, I'll be happy to direct you. Now, I'm sure you're all anxious to settle in so I'll spare you a long winded speech. I've got a few people here that you should all know going forward, so I'll allow them to introduce themselves and afterwards, we'll get each every one of you a nice place to rest up." Michael steps to the side and allows the people who came with him to take center stage.

The next twenty minutes go by gruelingly slow as every adult with any notable amount of authority decides to give a bit too much information about what they do and the implications of that role. The information may because important to consider, but as for the exhausted and starving Ryan, he simply doesn't care one bit about any of it. His only priority is food, even as some chick named Kriss explains that there are other survivors around the city which demands a plethora of side conversations. Maybe if all of these introductions weren't cluttered with filler words and obvious notions like, don't leave without telling someone, then he's pay closer attention. As far as he's concerned, he'll do whatever is asked of him, but that's it—at least until he gets a proper meal.

Once a heavier man named Charles finishes up, Michael takes over and addresses the crowd one last time, “Kriss here will escort you all to your new rooms. Feel free to explore the premises, but if you come across any locked doors, then please don’t try and break in. I hope you all work hard to build this place into something special.”

Michael retreats back into the prison followed by Stephanie and Charles. Kriss takes the transition period to raise her hand and waves for the crowd to follow her as she heads a slightly different direction.

Max and Caid approach from behind Ryan causing him to look back before they can say anything but words were never the former's intentions. Max promptly throws an arm around Ryan's shoulders which demands that he raise to his tippy-toes as he reaches up. He smiles jovially as Caid follows close to his side.

"Hey Ryan," Max says drawing out his name for a suspicious amount of time.

"What?" Ryan asks a bit annoyed, but draws back as he knows it's only out of hunger.

Max slides his arm off of him and readjusts the blue wristband before taking on a much more casual demeanor, "We were thinking, you know how cool you are under pressure, how great a team we make, how in sync our vibes are, you know what I'm saying?"

Caid rolls his eyes and pushes the boy back a step so he can see Ryan directly, "What he's trying to say is that we want to go back out there... and we want you to come with."

Ryan does a double take as he glances between them blankly, "Me?"

Max nods vigorously, "Yeah, it has to be you."

Ryan looks at them incredulously.

Caid sighs as he grabs Max's wrist and holds up the blue wristband, "This wrist band was his oldest sisters," Ryan inspects it hesitantly as he narrowly avoids a flashback, "This idiot may have pulled a stunt back there, but I won't play that game. We... we went back there and..."

Max cuts him off, "You don't have to tell him."

Caid shakes his head as he clears his mind of something serious, "Anyways, I've got family that I want to check on too. If you don't want to go, I won't coerce you, but it's clear that you're the most trustworthy one here."

Ryan averts his eyes. They're only able to say that because of their ignorance, if they really knew... they'd... trust is far from the word he'd use.

"Why don't you ask Blake?" Ryan wonders, trying once again to avoid their ambitions.

Max shakes his head as he looks around for Blake, "He's just going to tell us to back off if anything dangerous happens. We know this is dangerous, but... this is a matter of our own flesh and blood."

"Then why concern me?" Ryan tries one last time.

Caid takes a deep breath as he restrains something tangible enough it nearly slipped his tongue, "Will you come or not?"

Ryan sighs. He knows this is a terrible idea, but they're not going to last out there on their own. Even if he doesn't want to partake in any more of their personal affairs, he knows what they're looking for is not what they're going to find. Him coming is nothing more than a precautionary measure—a necessary one.

"Fine," he says after much deliberation, "But not right now, let's take some time to settle in and then we'll worry about the next steps."

Caid nods, "I can get behind that, but I don't want to wait too long. I'm not expecting much, but if there's even a chance..." he bites his words and looks away, "I'd prefer to do it sooner rather than later."

All three of them make a silent pact that this plan will commence soon, but beyond that, Ryan is allowed some peace and quiet as he looks for a cafeteria.
