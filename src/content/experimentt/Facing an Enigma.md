The trek towards the military checkpoint is surprisingly easy and rather dull to say the least. They all agreed that returning to the van they came here in was suicide aftering finding a considerable number of undead guarding it. They attempt to steal a car, but quickly find that they're all either locked, or the keys are nowhere to be found. Ryan suggests breaking a window or trying to hotwire, but Bill reminds him that one of those methods are bound to produce a car alarm while the other is not a skill any of them possess. Eventually they agree that they need to start walking but they'll passively search for a vehicle on the way. 

As they walk, a few miscellaneous zombies require dealing with here and there, but Steve has sufficient knife skills to get the job done without attracting more. In fact, Ryan finds his combat ability with a knife mystifying given his prejudges against the man. He pays close attention to how Steve parries the zombie’s moment and slashes with a knife rather than stabbing. He commends the skill and imagines doing the same motions with his own body. Every time Steve looks back, however, he returns to his nonchalant demeanor to avoid giving the man any reason to stroke his own ego.

They pass countless businesses during their travels—the flashing open signs sends chills down Ryan’s spine. It’s sad to see places like the Loper’s bowling alley closed for good. The local icon used to be the center of socialization for the entire track and swim team after every competition. Or the “Game On!” arcade that had everyone from kids to full grown adults coming back for a quick retro gaming session. Each one of these local legends are now just a figment of the past with no planned date of return.

At each turn, all three men look around the the inevitable gang of zombies loitering over a corpse or gathered just for the sake of inconvenience. This concern is never met, however, as no two zombies are ever closer than the distance he could throw a rock. Their sparsity, while appreciated, calls for a considerable amount of concern that makes their travel slow.

“It’s too quiet,” Steve says, analyzing each passing car still checking doors ever know and then.

“Agreed, there should be a LOT more people here. Or zombies for that matter,” Bill adds as his walk becomes more a limp at this point.

They continue their journey a little ways further until the rain picks up. They debate pushing through but after a lightning flashes overhead, they agree to take a break inside a nearby building. 

The building they selected is tactically advantageous as it sits at the very end of main street and overlooks most of the city due to it being on a small hill which rises above the surrounding terrain. This prestigious building gained such an amazing vantage point solely due to it being the town hall. As they go up the hill, smoke becomes more visible all around the city despite the fog of rain.

They arrive at the double wooden doors and find it resting slightly ajar leaking a shallowly lit light from within. 

Bill gets to the side of the door first and turns back to them in a whisper, “We don’t want to get ambushed like last time. Ryan, you stay by the door and keep an eye out. Steve, you and I will go in and investigate.”

Each of them nod as Bill slowly swings one of the old wooden doors open. Inside a single lamp can be seen illuminating the dark room only made darker by the ominous clouds overhead. 

Steve and Bill creep in, guns drawn, and begin searching the interior. Ryan is left alone under the overhang of the front doors. The pouring rain keeps him company as his nerves make him quiver.

A couple minutes pass and Steve and Bill both leave the view of the front lobby. He waits patiently for the two to return with news of survivors or at least gun shots but none of that happens. As the minutes drag on and nothing occurs, his mind begins to wander.

Is Bill really going to die? He got bit, but he didn’t turn. With how many zombies there were in the school, the conversion rate of zombies must be insanely fast, so Bill should have turned by now. The fact that he hasn’t even shown any symptoms after an hour-long walk is astounding. Could Bill be immune? Or is he just wrong about the turning rate and Bill actually has a day left. The bigger question would be why the military isn’t allowed to help the town. He went with it before, but now that he has a second to think, it makes him wonder why the citizens of this town were left to die. Why did the military leave the school? All questions with no answer. The last three days have been a rollercoaster and they’re not even over yet. If only things could just slow down so he could have more time like this to just ponder. 

One big thing that he’s been pushing off for quite some time is what he’ll do when he finally breaks off from the Hughes. With no friends, no career, no plan, the only thing he really has right now is the Hughes and even that’s about to disappear. They are nice and all, but he longs for more in life… or maybe, he’s the problem and he’s just trying to leave to avoid worsening their lives with his presence. 

Steve said that he’s never hit Karmen and as much as he hates to admit it, he’s probably right. In the past, when Steve was pissed off, he would always have a soft spot for Karmen, but Ryan’s anger and resentment for the man prevented him from every admitting it. Charlie also listens to Steve which makes sense if he’s authoritarian, but Charlie doesn’t seem like he fears him. He’ll admit that he doesn’t hang around Steve much but every time he does see him, he’s complaining about something and drinking. Come to think of it, Steve drinks all the time, but there was never a large collection of empty bottles around him. There’s no way a drunk man will clean up after themselves, unless Karmen was granting him that service. That bastard doesn’t know how good he really has it, having a loving wife and obedient kid. He should be thankful and not complaining. Every time he fought with Steve, Steve came off as a rude, abusive father who hates when people question his authority. The last few conversations really ingrained that into his head, but there is also some doubt that exists. Ryan couldn’t possibly have misread the situation, he has to be in the right. He was just defending himself and telling it like it is. He plans on leaving them to live their “happy” life somewhere else. That’s what’s best for all of them… right?

Suddenly, a scream rings out across the landscape. He immediately looks inside but when the scream repeats itself, Ryan realizes it’s coming from outside and what’s worse—it’s Bill’s scream.

Ryan rushes out into the pouring rain, hitting the wall of water and becoming drenched immediately. He full sends it around the corner of the town hall and to the courtyard behind. There, standing across from Steve and Bill with a far more sinister form is Heimes. Scattered between the two enemies are bits and pieces of firearms which have been crudely minced into fragments with barbaric cuts. Steve stands, knife in hand, next to Bill with his gaze fixated on the monster before him. Bill, however, is hardly a contributor in the fight as he lay on his side writhing in agony.

Ryan quickly raises his shotgun and points it at Heimes.

“I wouldn’t try that, Ryan,” Heimes says without looking back, “You’ll only waste your time.”

“Ahhh!” Bill continues to scream as some jarring pain causes him to roll onto his back and bang his head against the ground.

“What are you doing to him!” Steve screams at Heimes—fiery burning in his eyes that the rain isn’t able to extinguish.

Heimes turns slightly and glances over to Ryan revealing the extent of his inhumanity. The once strong, level-headed principal is now beginning to resemble that which truly laid in deep within his heart—a demon. His arms have grown longer and larger with enough mass to rival a powerlifter's bar. His head is elongating like a stubby giraffe with two black eyes and menacing horns sprouting from the top. Lastly, his hands and feet have morphed into talons with claws that make the original zombie lady's look like butter knives.

“You’re controlling everything that’s going on! Aren’t you!?” Ryan shouts over the pounding rain and Bill’s tormented groans.

Heimes smiles: an unsightly scene that reveals cracked, shark-like teeth. The man, no, the monster's visage is something of a nightmare as its eyes are so void of emotion it might as well see this as a trifling matter.

“You don’t get it, do you?” Heimes asks slowly with a deep, demonic voice, “I am but a humble servant to something much more powerful.”

“You’re a fucking cultist?!” Steve screams now branching away from Bill slowly.

“You are annoying me,” Heimes says to Steve.

Steve takes it as instigation and charges at Heimes with his blade drawn back for a quick slice. He rears forward until within striking range and agilely slashes the blade right across Heimes’ neck. Steve lets out a deep battle cry as he attempts to swing his blade all the way through, but to no avail. The blade doesn’t even penetrate Heimes’ skin; it simply bounces off and slides by.

Heimes irritatedly glares at Steve without even bother to retaliate, “What a pathetic attempt.”

Immediately following Steve’s attack, Bill lifts himself to his feet.

“Help me!” Steve shouts to him as he attempts to slash a different part of Heimes’ alien figure.

Bill shoots Heimes a look so full of rage that Ryan believes it isn’t even his face. Bill’s face has been warped with so much pain and anger that he seems to have shifted into a different, much more pitiful person entirely. Then to make matters worse, he opens his mouth and lets out a scream that makes Ryan shiver. It’s not the scream of a full-grown man, but of someone whose lungs have been ripped out and put back in with low-quality inflatable balloons. His voice is no longer the deep masculine voice of a military man, but instead a much higher pitched voice with an airy tone. It was not Bill’s voice, it was a zombie’s.

Bill’s corpse then proceeds to charge directly at Steve, arms outstretched, and eyes full of hatred as blood begins to drip down his cheeks. Steve jumps out of the way and pushes Bill’s zombified body past.

“What are you doing!” Steve cries, “We’re supposed to subdue the target!”

Bill is relentless. He turns back to Steve and jumps at him tackling him to the ground. Steve is fortunately able to get a hand on Bill’s neck to prevent him from clamping down on his shoulder with saliva-covered jaws. Meanwhile, Bill shakes his head wildly attempting to break out of Steve’s firm grasp.

Ryan begins running towards them but Heimes shoots him a glare which paralyzes him with fear. He knows that he needs to help, but the two empties eyes that stare at him and a toothy visage to match is so mortifying, Ryan forgets everything else entirely. He lifts his shotgun again but Heimes doesn't even bother to react as the subsequent shot bounces off him like rubber balls on a brick wall.

“We were supposed to see your sister again,” Steve says so softly that Ryan can barely hear him, “You were going to teach Charlie how to dance like you did back in Egypt,” despite having rain drops splash his face with cold water, his tears create streaks on his face, “We promised that we’d avenge Justin… but I guess we can’t do that now… can we?”

Bill struggles in futility as he tries to move his head to bite down on Steve's arm. The zombie also attempts to scratch at him, but his fingers haven't mutated to anything beyond a middle-aged man's nails which provide minimal slashing value. 

Steve closes his eyes as he grits his teeth and releases a restrained sob. For a moment he almost seems to be considering giving in there and then, but then he reemerges  from that dark, isolated depth of forlorn despair with sorrow and pity consuming his demeanor. He draws his knife to the bottom of Bill's chin and proceeds to slide the  blade's edge into his jaw. Bill offers a fierce resistance, but Steve doesn't budge as he twists the weapon and offers up his best friend's life. As his comrade's blood splashes across his face and quickly drains into the puddle below him, all Steve can do is wallow in his own misery.

As Bill's body goes limp, Steve slowly and carefully sets him down beside him before closing his comrade's eyes.

“I’m sorry it had to be this way,” Heimes apologizes half-heartedly.

Steve’s eyes fly open, his once vibrant color now clouded with tears and etched with the agony of pure inner turmoil. He battles an overwhelming surge of both profound sorrow and seething hatred, each emotion vying for control of his being. It was as if he stood on the precipice of a chasm, teetering on the edge between the abyss of despair and the inferno of rage. He struggles to find words amidst the tumultuous storm raging within him, his lips quivering as he slowly, agonizingly, pushes himself up from the cold, unforgiving ground.

“Unfortunately, he’s not part of the plan,” Heimes explains completely indifferent to the strife before him. He turns and faces Ryan as Steve's entire being becomes troublesome to spectate.

“YOU BASTARD!” Steve screams, “You’ll pay!”

Steve rises and prepares for another charge at Heimes, but his rage filled attempt returns lackluster results as Heimes swipes his claw at him. Steve narrowly avoids decapitation as he dives to the side and strings back to his feet before a talon can cave his back in. Heimes releases a monstrous growl as he draws his arm back preparing for one final blow to finish the pest off. 

Ryan tries to track Heimes with his shotgun finds Steve's position hanging too quickly as he narrowly evades lethal attack after attack. He can't help but feel that firing a shot now will only get Steve killed.

Heimes slams his fist into the ground creating a shallow crater in the dirt. He then looks up slowly with a glaring stare and visage of pesky annoyance. Steve breathes slowly as the downpour pelts his back with bullets of water. The two adversaries partake in a battle of pride as Steve prepares his next skillfully reckless assault while Heimes calculates the effort necessary to settle this struggle once and for all. 

With one last bloodcurdling scream, Steve presses forward with Bill's spiritual hand on his back. He draws his knife back ready to lodge it deep with the monster's cranium. Heimes is slow to react as Steve's advance manages to reach him without so much as a chamber from the mutating principal. The knife—the weapon carrying all of Steve's anger and regret—slips past Heimes warding arm and into the monster's black eye socket. For a brief moment, Steve's body relaxes as if he's finally succeeded for once in his life. Like every struggle he's ever endured all lead up to this pinnacle moment. This grand triumph seems to be enough for him as he looks up into the stormy sky with a sort of peace.

His victory falls as a talon slams into his temple and his body rag dolls on the ground. Steve's body rolls twice before his face lands inches from a puddle deep enough to suffocate. 

“Hm… seems I missed,” Heimes musses as he leanings forward to finish off Steve’s unconscious body.

Ryan breaks the static state he was in and fires another round out of his shotgun hoping this time will return some semblance of hope. “Stop!” he yells, pulling the trigger and watching a volley of buckshot hit an immovable object once more. 

Heimes freezes and rises upright once again. He glares at Ryan and waves his hand in a “come here” gesture. For a moment, Ryan believes that Heimes' attention was on him, but that quickly turns into a fallacy when two damp, dead hands grab both of his biceps and a set of rotten teeth clamp down on his right trapezius. He isn't able to react as the crushing maw of a putrid undead digs into his flesh as if his shirt was a meaningless obstruction. The bite itself does not even sink deeply into his shoulder, but the notion that his fate is now sealed makes his body want to go limp and give out.

As he attempts to push the zombie off, the undead corpse disengaged and runs off.

“Ahhh!” Ryan cries as the pain of his shredded flesh comes to realization. 

He collapses to a knee and his left hand slaps itself onto his wound trying to hold blood in. Both the physical pain and the pain of despair eat into him, corrupting his mind, body, and soul. Any hopes for the future are now crushed under the immense weight of despair. Any ambition he may have still preserved through this futile trial is now squashed like a rancid roach underfoot and what follows is not relief but a grotesque afterlife. He bangs his head against the ground which resists his sorrows and pushes back with yet another source of pain.

"I pity you Ryan," Heimes says as he walks beside him, "I wish there were a way to help you, but you sealed your fate when you chose life. Seeing you in that bathroom with the corpse of a former student made me realize just how inevitable your fate truly is. You are the only one to blame."

The last thing that Ryan feels is the large, solid, and slightly scaly hand of Heimes taking all his pain away and putting him into a much-needed slumber.
