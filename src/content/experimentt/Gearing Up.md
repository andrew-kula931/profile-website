"What are you doing here?" Ryan struggles to say as the pain in his leg continues to rage, his vitality draining with each passing second.

"You're as reckless as ever," the man says too calmly given the situation, his raven mask only elevating Ryan's struggling heart rate.

Ryan winces in pain as he can't take it anymore, "God, it hurts."

The masked man studies the wound from afar with clinical efficiency, "Ah, yes. You're lucky it didn't sever an artery. Had that happened, you'd already be dead."

Ryan shakes his head violently hoping the rash movement would ease the pain. It doesn't. He looks around for some cloth to wipe his hands down but the only fabric he can find is on the corpse of another zombie, "What are you doing here?"

The masked man steps a bit closer to him with his hands still behind his back as if carefully inspecting a victim with the plague, "As luck would have it, I have advanced expertise in medicine... or would you prefer to use the rudimentary first aid skills Steve  taught you?"

The doesn't stop spinning as he can feel his head becoming light, "Shut up! I need... I need something to wrap the wound in..."

The ink colored doctor shakes his head dismissively, "That won't do, this is a deep laceration, you'll need to apply firm pressure to the wound and then stitch it immediately. Anything shy of that will risk reopening the wound and inducing death."

Ryan starts tries to stand up but the injury squirts more blood, and he falls back down, "What are you saying? It's hopeless?"

The man with a raven mask shakes his head, "Were you not listening? Among my collection of skills, advanced medical care happens to one of my specialties. You can be saved."

Ryan can feel himself beginning to hyperventilate as the onset of sleep begins to rear its ugly face, "W-what's the catch?"

The man laughs lightly, "Please, had you possessed something I truly wanted, I would have already taken it." 

"T-then why?" Ryan says weakly.

The masked man crouches down beside him, his being invading Ryan's personal space, "Meine Gründe..." he looks off into the distance briefly, "perhaps one day," he then reaches into a sack resting at his hip and pulls out a few miscellaneous medical tools, "when you wake up, do not look for me and do not tell anyone about me," he leans his head in closer to Ryan, "My only demand is this: you must stay alive."

The edges of his vision black out as the masked man becomes the only thing he can see. His senses erode, his thoughts fade, and everything goes dark.

A thump echoes across the room snapping Ryan’s attention to the pile of corpses. He immediately identifies the source of the noise as a body slipping off the pile and hitting the tile floor. 

His body aches like he just got hit by a truck, particularly his leg which reeks of a dull pain he's never experienced in his life. Looking down, he finds his pant leg completely tore off with a carefully stitched wound running down his leg. His own blood has been completely cleaned off his leg and the surrounding area, but a red stain trail remains originating from beside the mutant corpse to where he lay now. The mutant guts along his arms has also been cleared but only on his skin as his shirt, jacket, and pants are still drenched in a damp, putrid-smelling mixture. As he sits up, he finds additional traces of the operation below him in the form of spare string clippings and numerous droplets of blood. 

Beside him, he finds a few curious looking items that catch his attention. The seemingly simple set of items include a brand new pair of jeans, a new bland, gray shirt, and a black leather jacket. The jacket looks heavy and upon lifting it, he finds it to be quite thick and fortified. He assumes the material comprising the jacket is leather, as it's firm and visually matches his mental model, but something about it strikes him as peculiar. The material is dense and durable, yes, but he doesn't ever remember leather looking like it every fiber was layered together like the top of a pie crust. The weaving of the fiber is like nothing he can name, but every other property screams leather.

If those were the only objects to find, he would have been grateful and moved on, but he quickly notices two additional items in the orderly pile. Laying next to the jacket is a sheath made of the same fiber. Contained within that finely crafted sheath is a long, strangely shaped knife. It sits at nine or ten inches long between the handle the blade. Unlike most blades he knows, this one isn't straight as it curves backwards like some sort of awkward crescent. Furthermore, instead of a standard knife handle, he finds four finger holes as if brass knuckles were smashed together with an oddly shaped butcher's knife. He doesn't understand why it looks so distinct, but what is clear is that it can and should be used for self-defense. The blade also must be exceedingly sharp given how the blade end of the sheath has an additional layer of the strange fiber overtop the base casing. 

Lastly, below the dagger or knife or whatever it’s classified as, is a belt with a pistol holster. It only takes a bit more inspection to realize that the holster perfectly fits his current gun. 

He looks around the room for any indication of friend or foe but finds everything around him actually dead. As the man said, he's nowhere to be found, and feeling a bit indebted to him, he elects to forgo any thoughts of tracking him down. For now, he just needs to get back on his feet and get back to the same, everything else can come after that.

He manages to stand up very carefully ensuring that minimal pressure is put on his leg. That being said, when he eventually does use both legs to stand, he finds the stitching to hold firm and the wound to remain completely in place. Pain surges through his body which makes him ease up on the weight, but pain alone is the only concern that comes to his mind. With the wound fully assessed, at least in his mind, he looks down at his ragged clothes and sighs. Seeing as no one is there to spy on him, he opts to change attire there and then.

The jeans, shirt, and jacket fit him as if tailored with his exact measurements. He stretches his arms to check the wingspan and finds, that too, completely unobstructive. Upon putting the belt on, he realizes that it doesn’t fit around his waist; the belt is much larger than his hips are wide. Even the tightening strap doesn’t make it fit well enough. He fiddles around with it attempting to figure out how it's supposed to work but finds that the ‘belt’ doesn’t really match a typical belt description. Instead of a single lengthy piece of leather, the item has a peculiar connection that seems to create two separate belts—one smaller than the other. He tries placing the smaller loop around himself like he would with a traditional belt and finds it fitting perfectly through the jean's belt loops, holster on the side. The larger loop, now dangling to his side remains an enigma to him, that is, until he lifts it up and finds it naturally looping across his chest and back. A small clip resides on the loop to tighten it on his opposite shoulder. It seems this belt-like thing serves as both a belt and a diagonal loop across his chest—like a sort of bandolier. After he tightens the second strap, he notices an attachment point shaped perfectly for his sheath to sit so he attaches both the knife and pistol to the holders. As he pulls his jacket more comfortably around him, he finds both weapons naturally concealing themselves beneath it as the colors match the jacket. The pistol sits out a bit conspicuously as it—unlike his long shirt—can't quite cover the exterior holster.

He pats himself down only to find curious loops inside his jacket that he hasn't the faintest clue what they could be for. He disregards it and heads for the door—slowly.

It does take him a few steps to relearn to walk, but once again, he's remind that pain is the only thing he's concerned with. The wound may be serious, but it feels oddly stable and secure given what he had seen before. With that in mind, he pushes forward to the door and steps outside.

The environment outside that metal crypt is unnervingly silent. Birds don't chirp, cicadas don't fill the autumn air with this notorious theme, not even the wind blow a few leaves across the ground. The landscape sits as quietly as the corpses he passes with the threat of an attack becomes dangerously intriguing to speculate. The fence line is intact, the speaking zombie is dead on the fake pool, and there are no nearby obstructions where an enemy could be lurking. His intellect says he's safe, his heart contradicts this claim.

He carefully draws his knife, finding it difficult to remove at first until he notices a small lever that needs to be pressed with his palm to release the knife. Doing so, he finds it incredibly seamless to lace his fingers through the handle holes and hold the knife out—it's long, razored edge curving slightly around his forearm. He squeezes the blade instinctively and finds his forearm strained. He rolls up his sleeve and is reminded of the cut running down his arm from the fence. It seems injury is just part of survival but... how much of this is he supposed to take? How much more can he bear?

As he pushes through the gate to leave the artificial backyard, he inspects his surroundings more diligently than ever before. Every nook and cranny is carefully analyzed for threats as his heart races for seemingly no reason. He knows something could pop out and put him back in a sprint, but he's not in a position where that's a viable approach. No running, no bullets, and his swinging arm will only work properly with sufficient adrenaline—something his body probably needs a break from. One wrong move and no amount of doctor aid will save him.

He limps. Step after step grueling step towards their base. Every shift of the breeze catches his attention, every window of a house haunts him with an imaginary vase. Every car procures the false silhouette of a humanoid entity. He knows it's all in his head, but he can't stop the sweat from dripping down his forehead as he frantically searches for the enemy waiting for a single slip up. He continues to move forward, limped step after limped step. With every yard traversed, he can't shake the feeling that eyes are starring sinisterly into his back. Alas, he finds nothing upon looking back.

His body harrows over all the injures as a multilayered exhaustion causes even limping to be a strenuous activity. He doesn't understand why he finds himself so tired, he felt fine before, he only lost... a lot of blood. But at least he did get... very little sleep. The world spins a bit as his pace speeds up which forces him to grind to a halt. His knee hits the ground at the same time his hands do. This feeling—it's so... unbearable.

He looks around at his surroundings again believing something, anything to be out there. He's so close to making it to the base, he only needs to hold out a bit longer. Why does his head spin so... so... 

He shoves a hand onto his forehead, thinking the spinning might stop if he holds it still. That's when he realizes: he hasn't breathed yet. Air gasps into his lungs as breathing becomes a conscious priority. If he could just get his heart rate under control... if he could just focus a bit more clearly. He can't give up now. The base is just around the corner.

His breath comes in slowly and leaves even slower. Unwilling to let his thoughts race any further, he looks at his hands. The two trusty appendages have never failed him—they've been unbothered by the world thus far. If only he could be a tenacious as them. If only he could be so unbothered by the world, by the pain. God, his leg hurts so bad.

As time passes, his thoughts begin to clear and the pain subsides, at least a little. He looks around just to be safe.

Is this really life now? Has he really went from running away from a drunk foster father to running away from rabid undead? Is he really doomed to a life where survival is all he has to think about? Not gets to, has to.

He shakes his head. This thought process is only dragging him down. He still needs to ensure none of the hoard chased after Blake—that much he can handle.

He lifts himself back up to his good leg with his weak leg providing as much support as it can. With that, he marches forward, cripple and all.
