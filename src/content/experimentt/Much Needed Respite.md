Before he knew it, Ryan is sitting in the prison cafeteria eating dinner. He's uneasy about relaxing to a meal with barred doors virtually everywhere, but after tasting the school lunch style food, he begins to soften a bit. Despite it only being seven in evening, the sun is already beginning to set. As such, most of the survivors in the camp have made their way into it's fortified walls. Thankfully that all don't decide to eat dinner simultaneously which means the hall feels fairly empty. This sparsity works in their favor as most of the high schools fit within three separate tables and stuff their faces vigorously. Ryan finds himself surrounded by all the seniors with the addition of two junior girls.

"I think my interview went really well," Beth half-way brags, "I have a good feeling you're all looking at a future quartermaster."

"You do that," Blake says supportively as he takes a large bit of his slice of bread.

Beth holds her head up in a dignified manner, "I will."

Caid offers her a knowing smile in which she returns the favor. Max shoots them an intrigued glance before rubbing his hands together under the table with a mischievous look. Caid, being a preceptive as ever with Max's shenanigans, glares at him and shakes his head. 

Ryan, having finished most of his plate, savors the last bite of mashed potatoes. The creamy, earthy flavor with little potato chunks of the comfort food grant him a tangible sense of relief as the food gives him a sense of home. It's one of the few foods he remembers eating when he was a kid and that nostalgic taste never left the memory of his palette. 

"That good, huh?" Blake asks across from him.

Ryan stares at him blankly only realizing a few moments later that he was making a face. Upon looking around he finds most of the table staring at him with amusement. Perhaps they thought his faces were funny, but little did they know this miniscule bite was warding off a very serious hangry incident... that and starvation, but he preferred to think about the former. He recoils in embarrassment by hiding his face with his cup of water. This choice of action makes the whole table laugh—all except Jeremy who just rolls his eyes.

"So anyway," Blake starts trying to change the subject, "When are we going back for the Corvette. We went through the hassle of getting it the first time, it'd be such a waste to leave it there."

Caid and Max exchange serious glances before hiding it with a fabricated curiosity and a speculator expression. Alison, Beth, and Chloe all shrug their shoulders in sync and everyone else is too far down the table to hear the remark. The last group continues on with an unrelated conversation as Blake continues.

"I was thinking that maybe we could go tomorrow, I'd be happy to explain the situation to Drew. I had a conversation with him on the way here and think he just might let us do it."

Max and Caid have a quick conversation expressed solely in facial expression and a subtle shake of the shoulders. Then, after a timely decision is reached, they both look to Ryan as if asking for his cooperation. Exasperatedly he nods, after all, he agreed to go with them anyway.

"Yeah," Max starts, "I like that idea, maybe we could do the same group as last time: you, me, Caid, and Ryan. It worked before."

Blake throws a concerned glance, "I wouldn't call that worki–", he looks at the girls at the table, "...okay, maybe that could work."

Beth's mouth drops open briefly, "Oh I see, it's another boy's only club. What? Got to go make out in private."

Caid narrows his eyes at her.

Blake waves his hands defensively, "No–no, that's not what I meant, I was just saying th—".

Beth waves a hand dismissively as she picks up her tray, "I'm not actually offended, us girls are just going to go on our own little excursion here in the near future. Come on girls, I found a few things in storage that we can decorate our cells with."

Blake swallows silently as Beth takes her leave with Alison in tow. Chloe gets up to follow, but is a bit slower as studies Blake a bit more closely than she probably needed to. After that the rest of the girls at the table head out in alignment with Beth. Jeremy also takes the opportunity to sneak out but he's hardly headed in the same direction as the others. Ryan can't help but feel bad for the guy, after all, a few days ago he was in the same boat as him. Perhaps... perhaps it shouldn't stay that way.

“And then there were four,” Caid says without taking his eyes off Beth as she walks out.

“Quit staring at her ass man,” Max says, shoving Caid to break his focus, “Just get a room already.”

"Shut up," Caid says quickly as he gets one last glance at the girl before leaves view.

"All I'm sayin is that she's not my first choice," Max says a bit provocatively, "I think you've got a skewed perception of beauty."

Caid turns the shorter boy and makes the height discrepancy known, "You wouldn't know beauty if it slapped you in the face—because it has."

“If you want to start a fight, you could’ve just asked,” Max says rolling up his sleeve with an excited smile leaking onto his face.

“Sometimes it feels like were back in high school,” Blake says capturing the attention of all three boys, "Never thought we'd be joking around about girls again."

Max turns to him and lets his sleeve fall back down, "Well aren't you the sentimental one."

“All I’m saying is that… I’m glad we’re all recovering,” Blake’s eyes drop to his remaining carrots, which by this point are now cold.

“You know, maybe we could ask to go check out the car dealer down on Fifth Street," Caid suggests, "Since we're going for the Corvette, we might as well grab a few more vehicles while we're at it." Caid looks at Ryan intentionally. 

“Ain’t that the truth. I’d get so many bitches if I came back with two sports cars,” Max fantasizes.

“I’m sure you wouldn't,” Caid comments.

"Isn't fifth downtown," Blake asks, "Or at least near it."

Caid shrugs nonchalantly, "Maybe so, but they've got some mean ass cars down there."

Max nods, "I like this idea, a lot actually."

"Heck, why not," Ryan says begrudgingly, knowing full well that he doesn't actually like this plan one bit.

Of all the things said so far, Ryan's comment seems to take Blake by the most surprise. He brushes off the two guys' comments with relative ease, but Ryan's words cause his attention to linger a bit too long, "I... no, we shouldn't do that."

"Oh come on," Max complains, "Why do you have to be a killjoy every. Single. Time?"

Blake throws his hands out to the sides, "What do you mean? You remember what happened last time."

Max rolls his eyes but says nothing.

As per usual, Ryan agrees with Blake but doesn't want to voice his concerns. There is something different about it this time, however. Usually it's about arbitrary things that don't concern Ryan, but after Caid saying that it's a family matter, well... he just pictures Steve, Karmen, and Charlie, as much as he detested that family, he can't help but wish he could see them again. For once, he wishes he could see them as much as he wishes he could see his mother. If he had that opportunity then would he...

"I think we'll go," Ryan asserts as softly as he can.

All three boys look at him. Max, of course, looks the most excited by this, "That's what I'm talking about."

Blake glares at Ryan in such a way that it couldn't harbor less than a snippet of betrayal. The expression makes Ryan regret his decision, even despite the fact that he believed he had come to terms with this adventure.

"Fine," Blake finally gives in, "We'll go, but I get to say when it's too dangerous."

Ryan expects a witty comment out of Max, but he simply nods his head. The next guess would be Caid's agreeable response, but that too is silenced with a nod. That just leaves Ryan who follows suit and solidifies the plan.

After dinner, Blake wanders off somewhere else which gives Ryan the opportunity to head to the room he was allocated. Caid and Max follow him into the hallway at a suspiciously close distance. To respond to their proximity he looks back, "What?"

Caid nods sincerely, "Thanks for going along with us back there... It means a lot."

Ryan shrugs, "It's a family matter right, it's the least I could do."

Max nods before sagging his shoulders with an exasperated look, "It sucks that we need that killjoy to sign off, but I don't know this mythical Drew guy and I can't assume they'll just let us waltz out of here."

Caid crosses his arms and leans against the light gray wall, "Yeah, it's unpreferable, but it is what it is. If he tries to stop us from getting there, we'll just ditch him."

Ryan listens a bit more closely having hearing such a serious backup plan. His attentiveness seems to be just conspicuous enough to draw the other boys' attention.

"Oh, not really," Caid corrects, "We'll just go alone and meet back up with him if he doesn't want to come. To be honest, I don't care about the car dealership, but we're making it back whether or not that guy agrees."

"Right," Max confirms before turning to Ryan, "Tomorrow's going to be... it's going to be good."

---

An hour later, Ryan finds himself lying in a cell bed staring up at the ceiling. He is lucky enough to be the only person to get his own cell, mostly because it used to be a solitary confinement cell which Ryan didn’t mind. He likes his alone time anyway. It also may be due to the fact that the room full of cells that everyone else is staying in just got filled up and there were one to many high school students than could fit. The adults tried to convince him that they could get another bed in the communal room, but he told them not to bother. Solitary confinement was more comfortable for him than communal living.

He pulls out his knife and places each one of his fingers through the holes in the knife. It feels so comfortable and natural like it was forged specifically for him. He sits up and takes a few swings with the knife. It’s solid metal yet very light—a little too light. He swings again and again changing the angle and power behind each strike. The most natural motion is a combo of a quick slash across his body and then following it up with a stab of the knife on the way back. In total, he probably practices the different combinations more than a hundred times before resheeting the knife and leaning back. 

Not even thirty seconds later, Chloe appears at the doorway to his ‘room’ with a paper creation in her hand.

“Are you really okay sleeping in a solitary confinement cell?” she asks, inviting herself in and looking around, "It gives me the creeps."

“I like the peace and quiet,” he responds simply, “What is that?”

Chloe looks down at the little paper construction and holds it out to him. The miniature origami figure sits petitely in her hand like a dignified bird in the high class paper society. Its edges are folded sharply, and the paper looks to be made out of a material dedicated for this explicit purpose. 

“It’s a crane. Lisa showed me how to make it and said that if I make a thousand of them, I’ll be granted a wish.”

If Ryan recalls correctly Lisa was one of the 'managers' here in the prison, she also clearly has a few unconventional skills, “And what would your wish be?”

“To end the apocalypse obviously,” she says, rolling her eyes.

Chloe's eyes settle on the empty half of the bed beside Ryan. He makes a quick speculation about what she could want but struggles to find the obvious solution. Eventually she tries to push the thought out of her mind by flapping the wings of the crane, but this actually gives Ryan enough time to scooch to the side and leave a bit more room on the edge of the bed. She hesitantly takes the queue to seat herself before handing the crane over to him.

“Here, you can have this one,” she says with a prideful smile.

Ryan takes the gift and tries pulling on the tail like she just did. Just as before, the wings bend downward as he pulls the tail out like a simple machine made solely out of paper and some magical folding technique. 

“So you made this?” he asks flapping the wing again.

Chloe nods, “It took me like three tires to get that one, but Lisa is a good teacher.”

Ryan thinks back briefly, “You know, my little brother Charlie once tried to do origami. I think he went through like fifty pieces of paper and only was able to make the simple fortune-teller. It didn’t even work properly because all the folds were uneven.”

Ryan goes silent for a moment afterward as he realizes he didn't add the 'foster' part in.

Chloe smiles, “Did you ever try it out?”

Ryan snaps out of it, “Nah, I feel like it would be more work than it’s worth. I'm not talented like that.”

“You don’t know that,” Chloe counters, “You have to at least try.”

“I don’t know, I think I better stick with my strengths,” he says dismissively.

“Like what?” Chloe asks before quickly realizing, what she believes to be the proper connotations behind what she said, “Oh my gosh, I didn’t mean it like that!”

Ryan rolls his eyes, not seeing there being anything to apologize for, “It’s fine.”

Their conversation goes dull for a moment as Ryan takes the opportunity to place his first decoration on the floor in the corner of the room.

"That looks so sad," Chloe comments, "You need to get some furniture for this room."

Ryan shrugs, "Looks like every other room I've had."

Chloe goes silent. The comment clearly has a larger impact on her than he intended.

"Not really," he corrects softly, "I was just being... uh... facetious? I was just joking."

He hates that he knew the exact right word for the situation. Not because he's against sounding smart, but because the person who taught him that word also made fun of him constantly for being too 'morbid', 'condescending', and 'perpetuating intolerable proclivities'. He's not really sure what half those words mean, but that family did love to fight with words more than anyone he's met since. He just hopes Chloe doesn't think less of him from the lingering effects of that middle school year.

Chloe sighs, "Even if you were joking, you shouldn't want to live like that."

Ryan shakes his head as he sits back down on the bed, "It's what it is. Those were just the kind of families I was stuck with. They may not have bought me much, but I have a feeling they cared."

Chloe descends into a silent isolation known only to Ryan after he nudges her, and she doesn't respond. It's unlike him to pry, but it's unlike her to suddenly close off.

“You alright?” Ryan questions sympathetically, "You... don't have to say if you don't want to."

Chloe exhales slowly before turning back to Ryan, “It’s fine. I guess I’m just too easy to read… I thought my family would be here…” her eyes begin to fill with tears, “Yesterday when we were carrying all the supplies we could from the store, I saw my parents' car outside the post office. All of the windows were smashed open,” with great restraint she holds back tears, “Our little excursion today was supposed to be used to check that out…” tears begin to well up in her eyes again, but just as before she takes a deep breath and reels the emotions back in.

Ryan's not dense enough to be blind to where this conversation was headed, but he can't help but feel grateful that Chloe kept it together. Maybe she did need it, but he's not confident he'd be able to handle the situation correctly. Though, at the end of the day, talking to her did feel easier than everyone else. It's not that she understood him, or had anything hilarious to say, but... she just considered things that he didn't and most importantly, he didn't feel like he had to keep his guard up around her. For once, he felt like he could say the wrong thing and maybe... just maybe... he'd survive it.

"Sorry," she comments as she takes one last breath.

"You've got nothing to apologize for," Ryan responds sincerely. 

Chloe nods, "Alright, then..." she waits to see if Ryan has anything else to add, "I guess... good night... oh, and before you leave tomorrow, I'll give you your gun back."

Ryan shakes his head, "There's no bullets in it."

Chloe presses her fingers together nervously, "Yeah, but... maybe you'll find some out there."

Ryan's eyes soften as he nods, "Alright, I'll come pick it up tomorrow before I leave. Good night, Chloe."

She smiles politely before walking to the cell door, “It's weird, but I kind of wish we'd talked before all this and..." she hangs on the thought for a moment, "Thank you... Anyways, I’ll see you tomorrow, okay,” her smile grows a bit more genuine, she waves, and heads back to her own room.

With that, he shuts the light off in the hallway and lies back on his bed.

His mom, Steve... and... oh, nevermind. Was that... was that how he's supposed to react? Is crying supposed to be instinctual? Stop. He's been over this a million times. Just breathe. Breathe. Sleep. It's always easier to think in the morning. Always.

With his eyes shut, he drifts off before too long.
