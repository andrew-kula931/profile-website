The road downtown is full of abandon, precarious cars that could harbor an assailant at any time. The traffic jam started nearly two blocks away from where their destination really is. Despite Max only having a slight head start over the rest of them, he maintains that distance for the duration of the run preventing Blake from warning him about the cars. By the time they catch up to Max he is paused next to the side of an apartment building looking up.

“Max!” Blake yells with restraint, “This place is extremely dangerous.”

Max doesn't respond, he just stares upwards.

Caid steps up beside his friend with a grim expression, one that Ryan has never seen the nonchalant teen ever take on. Even when facing the uncertainty in the back of the store, Caid kept a tense but focused face. Even when he was enraged at Max's actions, he never showed more than mild fury, but this... this morbid gaze sent shivers down his spine.

The two desperate teens exchange glances, "Let's go."

“Wait,” Blake calls out as the two open the front door to the apartment building and step inside, “You can’t.”

Blake is about to rush after them to convince them of logic and reason, but Ryan stops him with a hand on the shoulder. He tries to break free but when he finds that Ryan's not messing around, he turns back in a moderate annoyance.

"What are you doing!" Blake exclaims a bit too forcefully, "Why do you keep siding with them."

Ryan doesn't attempt to defend himself with words—he doesn't feel that he has too. Instead, he just shakes his head silently. When Blake finally relaxes and stops trying to maintain some semblance of control, Ryan speaks slowly, "Just wait out here, let us know if something is coming our way."

Ryan proceeds inwards to find Max and Caid arguing next to a particular door. Above that door is conspicuous sign with a familiar blocky shape with an arrow pointing upwards. He approaches them from behind.

"We're doing this!" Caid asserts.

"I don't know man," Max whines, "I'm getting cold feet."

Caid glares at him with angry filling his expression, "You didn't get cold feet when you went looking for your sister. What's different this time?"

Max avoids eye contact immediately as he looks down at the blue wristband he's been carrying the whole time, "That's... uh... that's different."

Caid rolls his eyes in disappoint, "Shut the fuck up, I tired to stop you and you didn't listen, now it's my turn shut you out."

"But—", Max tries.

"But what!" Caid asserts as he stares the suddenly timid boy square in the eyes. Max never says anything back. "That's what I thought."

After that Caid looks up and notices Ryan walking up to them. He looks away as if he'd done something wrong, but Max seems a bit more willing to talk. The shorter boy releases an exasperated sigh as he waves his hand at his "friend".

"Do whatever you want," Max pinches bridge of his nose, "Just... give me a minute."

Caid studies his "friend" for a moment before opening the stairwell door and ascending it without delay. Ryan follows him up.

With every passing step, he checks every angle and readies his knife. He recalls the time where Steve, Bill, and him all cleared out a building making sure to prepare for any attack. 

"What's really up there?" Ryan inquires, mostly to clear the tension between him and the eerily silent teenager.

Caid shakes his head, "You'll see soon enough. I'm prepared for whatever happens."

The two boys arrive at the third floor and Ryan takes point by cracking the metal door open to check the hallway. About four doors down the hall a group of four zombies loiter in front of a door. Scratch marks are evident all around the door in question but it doesn't seem to have given way just yet. Inspecting the zombies, he finds one of them missing its head… or rather his head had sunken into its chest. The remaining three zombies didn't look to have any irregularities.

Ryan closes the door silently and turns back to Caid.

“How many are there?” Caid asks, his frustration quickly turning to concern. Ryan recalls the conversation he had with him the other day where Caid genuinely wondered how he was supposed to gain the courage to fight a zombie.

“Four,” Ryan confirms, "I don't like our odds."

Caid swallows silently as he looks back down the stairs. As he doesn't he nearly falls over as he finds Blake standing right behind him, Max tagging along. Blake inspects Caid's reaction before looking to Ryan, "What's the situation."

Ryan pinches his index finger and thumb together and slides them across his lips. He then holds up four fingers and points in the direction of the zombies. Blake seems to take this form of communication extremely well as he nods and mouths, "Let's go."

He starts off down the stairs, but no one follows. Once he gets to the landing of subfloor, he looks back up the flight of stairs and waves his hands to the sides. He widens his eyes and tries to gesture for them to follow him down. Max and Caid exchange glances but don't budge. 

One would think this defiance would be unjustified, at least, Blake seems to think so as he waves his fists at all three of them and points down the stairs. He quickly runs a hand through his shaggy hair and looks back with just as much vigor as he silently pleads that they follow him away from the zombies. Ryan looks to the other two boys but neither one of them motion towards the stairs, if anything, each of them become hyper-fixated on what lies beyond the stairwell door. 

Blake, growing impatient, walks up the flight of stairs and grabs onto Max's arm. He tugs at it to try and force the boy down with him, but Max immediately pulls his arm free and steps away from the stairwell. He doesn't crack a funny face, nor make a face of any kind, he simply stares at the fellow survivor blankly as he slowly turns his head towards the hallway. Caid joins in on this thought.

Suddenly, Max pulls a switchblade out of his pocket while Caid pulls out a pipe from under his flannel jacket. Ryan is more surprised by the fact that he never noticed they had these weapons, but regardless, it seems they didn't come unprepared. Ryan draws his knife to follow suit as Caid pushes the door open and catches the zombies attention.

The three zombies and zombie mutant charge at the three boys with an inhuman roar. The mutant is a bit slower than the rest, but that quickly becomes a secondary concern as Caid steps forward and slams the pipe into the skull of the first zombie. Max takes on the second while Ryan takes on the third. 

The zombie reaches out to grapple him but swings his knife upwards and finds the curved blade cleanly cutting through it's chest and then splitting it's head. The slash felt so clean... too clean. He barely felt any resistance at all as the blade created a perfect division between two parts of the zombie cranium. All he can do is stare mystified as the zombie collapses into a pile of rotting flesh. 

Looking over, he finds Caid slamming the pipe into the skull of his zombie while Max is on the floor narrowly holding a zombie back from taking a clean bite out of his shoulder. Ryan presses forward to respond by kicking the zombie on top of Max over and swinging his blade right through the zombie's head. The blade once again slides through way to cleanly and he ends up punching the wall as his mentum becomes far more than he anticipated. The brass knuckle grip once again proves it's value as it protects his fits from taking the unexpected plunge into a wooden obstacle. 

Ryan turns quickly to the mutant and finds Caid already tackling it to the ground and bashing the pipe into it's abdomen—it just so happens to be it's head. Caid lifts the pipe and slams it down over and over, each strike splashing blood across the pipe, the walls, and his arms. He lifts up, slams down, lifts up, slams down, and the cycle continues. Max, finally coming to his senses after the close encounter, watches Caid mercilessly bash the brains out of a fully deceased corpse. The two boys just watch as Caid only grows more vicious with his attack and the mangled corpse becomes less corpse and more pile of gore. Only after thirty or so strikes does Caid take a step back, his breath heaving, and drops the pipe.

"I...I think you've done enough," Max says timidly.

Ryan steps over the teen and crouches down next to him. He could hardly blame the guy. After all, he did something similar when he made his first zombie kill.

"You alright?" he asks simply.

After a few moments, Caid comes back to his senses and stands up, seemingly unphased by his previous actions, “I’m fine. Let’s—let’s go.”

Blake finally arrives into the hallway and looks down at the bodies, "What the hell?"

Max glances at Blake morbidly before pushing himself to his feet. He wipes himself off before pushing is switchblade back into his pocket and stepping up to a particularly door. Without consent of his friends he promptly knocks his hand onto the door—and waits.

When no response is given for a solid twenty seconds, Max turns the door nob and finds it sliding open unaccused. As if in a trance, he steps inside completely devoid of any protective measures, luckily nothing jumps out.

Inside the apartment is a plethora of personalized decorations cluttering the walls. A first grade painting hangs on the wall here, a piece of homemade pottery there, a second place science fair trophy beyond that. The entire room looks like one giant fair of child successes with the only unifying feature being the last name. Somewhere in the room, Ryan finds the typical household fixtures, but those too are cluttered with plushies, pillows, and a mountain of blankets. The space wasn't unorderly, per say, but it definitely felt... lived in. 

Max heads inside swiftly and begins looking around. He checks each door searching for something, anything. With each empty room he approaches, he becomes increasingly frantic in his search. By the time he reaches the final door, he's practically running. He swings open the final door at the end of the hallway and stares in at the empty bedroom in silence.

"Look at that," Max laughs, "They made it out... just as I thought."

Ryan waits silently, unsure of what to say.

Max turns to him with an awkwardly forced smile across his face, "It's honestly a relief to know they're all safe. I was really worried there for a moment, you know."

Ryan slides his knife back into its sheath and looks down at the floor. What even is he supposed to say to that?

"Nana?!" Caid exclaims from out in the hallway.

A few seconds later, Ryan and Max join Blake and Caid by the room next door. Standing in the doorway is a frail, old woman with startled look on her face. She’s probably in her late 60s or early 70's with short, white, curly hair. Not only that, but her glasses are bright red with little cat ears which screams old lady, but he overlooks that part.

“Caid?” she says in disbelief as the teenager rushes forward to embrace his grandmother.

The reunited family head into the room where Blake follows in, but Max stops Ryan just short of the door, "Caid's nana is... a bit eccentric, just bare with her".

Ryan nods.

Upon entering the house, a thick stench of feces hits Ryan’s nostrils and it requires him to pause for a moment before continuing in. This same odor hits the other boys with equal intensity, but none seem as shocked by it as Caid. While the other boys just plug their nose and proceed, Caid seems visibly stunned by the stench. Ryan quickly notices a litter box in the corner of the room which has long since overflown. At some point that cats stopped trying to make it to the box and just got in the general vicinity. 

“I’m sorry about the smell. I've been locked in here for days and haven't been able to dump the litter box,” the old woman says apologetically.

“It’s alright,” Caid sighs, "The cats were never trained well to begin with."

"Yes, the cats," the woman agrees, "My babies are starving, I'm so glad you're back Caid, You can run out to the grocery store and pick up some cat nip." The old woman’s head lowers and she hugs the empty bag of cat food before handing it over to Caid.

"Nana, that's not important," Caid says placing a hand on her shoulder and lowering the cat nip, "We need to get you somewhere safe."

"But my babies—" she attempts.

Caid shakes his head sternly only now realizing that he has a layer of blood on his arms, "I know you love Eene, Meane, Minee, Moe, and Stuwart, but you gotta leave them behind. It's not safe here anymore."

The woman steps back and holds out a defiant demeanor, "Absolutely not, not only my babies, but I can't leave Tommy."

"Tommy?" Caid asks.

The woman's mouth drops, "Caid, I raised you better than that," she smacks the teen on the shoulder as she can't quite reach his cheek, "How could you be so inconsiderate of your brother. He's been stuck in his room for days now. He's stopped answering me when I call."

Caid's eyes widen as he stares at his grandma in disbelief. His mouth hangs open as he gets locked on a single word that won't quite come out.

"Nana," Max adds, "Stop being difficult and please just come with us."

The grandma shakes her head again, "My babies and Tommy need to come out of their room first before I'll leave. I refuse to leave them behind."

"Then we can just get them out," Blake concludes, "Where are they?"

The grandma claps her hands together and points to the room at the back of the hallway, "Tommy wasn't feeling well a few days ago so he went to his room to rest. I know young boys like to sleep but I'm starting to get concerned. He's been in there for four days now. I tried knocking on the door but he won't respond. All my babies are in there so I'm sure they're keeping him company. Oh, if he'd just come out, I could make him his favorite supper again."

Blake looks back to the door, but then comes back and notices Caid. Max and Ryan watch too as Caid presses his palm against his face.

"Caidy?" the grandma asks, "What's wrong? You need to go help you brother."

Caid's jaw quivers as one eye is covered by his hand. His visible eye is as wide as it can be as he stares off into the distance completely detached from the situation. He looks to his nana slowly without changing his unsettling expression, "Nana... what are you saying?"

The grandma looks at him with a confused look, "I told you. You need to go get Tommy and my babies out of their room."

Caid shakes his head slowly, "Nana... I don't have a brother."

The room descends into a pungent silence. The vile smell, the beating of Ryan's heart, it all gets dampened by the horrified expression resting on Caid's face. He looks at Max, he looks at his grandma, he looks at the door at the end of the hallway. Something tells Ryan that the room isn't supposed to be special. Max squeezes onto his wristband as he stares at the grandma with a similarly terrified look. He shakes his head slowly trying to internally rationalize the scene.

When it becomes clear that no one is going to clarify what exactly is going on, all eyes turn to the door at the end of the hallway. Whoever... or whatever is behind that door remains as silent as it is enigmatic. Caid stares at it with an unreadable expression, Max shivers, and Blake checks to make sure their exit remains unobstructed. Amidst it all, Ryan reaches for his knife, but something prevents him from drawing it, instead he relaxes his hand and takes a step towards the door.

"Oh, wait," the grandma starts, "I don't want to wake him."

Max steps forwards and cuts her off, "Nana, I think you should wait here. You can trust us."

Caid and Max make eye contact briefly before Caid follows Ryan down the hallway. Blake takes a few steps in their direction but ultimately hangs back with Max. With the roles established, Ryan creeps down the dimly lit hallway towards the mysterious wooden door. He expects something to shake, or claws to scrap down the inside of the door, or even a tragic whimper from inside. None of that happens, however, all he is met with is silence.

"I've lived here for seven years," Caid starts quietly as he arrive in front of the closed door, "In all that time, I've never seen what's behind this door. Nana—I mean my grandma... she wouldn't tell me what's behind it nor give me a key. Whatever it is... I'm ready."

Ryan studies the guys for a moment as his gaze doesn't budge from the door. He lowers his head a bit and raises his bloody pipe ready for anything that may lay beyond that door. Without a moment of hesitation, he reaches for the handle and twists it finding that it's unlocked. He freezes, unable to take the last step. Perhaps, he needs a moment to recuperate, perhaps the unknown is preferable to reality, perhaps that door never should open... but Caid's not delusional enough accept that line of thinking. He pushes the door and and readies for an assault.

The room is still. So perpetually still that a layer of dust covers everything within the room. The plaid-colored quilt on the bed, the lamp fixtures on two separate tables, even the mirror above the dresser is foggy due to the dust. The only place evading the copious amounts of dust is the floor where five skinny cats walk around aimlessly. Upon opening the door, however, all five cats bolt out right between Caid's legs.

Caid steps inside and looks around at the perfectly preserved room. A Star Mission poster from the late eighties hangs on one wall, a wooden toy train sits on a nightstand beside the bed. His eyes wander around the room with a mixture of confusion and wonder as if everything in here was uncovering a mystery Caid didn't know existed.

"What is all this?" Ryan asks brushing off a thick layer of dust off the glass of a picture frame.

Caid turns over to notice the photo as four faces reveal themselves. An average looking guy with glasses stands beside a woman with bushy hair—a hairstyle that died decades ago. Two boys stand in front of them, both with black hair and eyes that look strangely similar to Caid's. One of the boys seems to be around ten while the younger is no more than seven. Caid stares at this picture frame, even picking it up to get a better look, for a long time.

"Is Tommy alright?" Nana calls from the other room.

Ryan looks at Caid with concern trying to decipher what the correct response should be. Caid, completely petrified in thought, can't seem to take his eyes off the photo no matter how hard he tries. Seeing as how Caid can't quite make up his mind, Ryan decides to take the initiative.

"Yeah, Nana. He's just asleep like you said," Ryan steps back towards the door only to find Nana standing there. Max and Blake stand right behind her as if they were caught off guard by how fast she can move.

Nana eyes Ryan carefully, "Didn't your mother tell you not to lie."

Ryan freezes solid as Caid finally puts the photo frame down, "Nana, what is this?"

Caid's grandmother, in slow methodical movements, steps into the room and carefully picks up a different picture from on the dresser. She wipes off the dust to reveal a photo of a single boy only eight or nine years old. She runs her fingers tenderly over the boy's face with a somber look in her eyes, "This is Tommy."

Caid shakes his head, his thoughts still racing by how slowly his words come out, "I–I don't understand. I've never had a brother."

Nana shakes her head with a hurt smile, "He's not your brother Caidy, he's your uncle."

The amount of shock that hits Caid is nothing short of overwhelming as he averts his eyes and stares at the photo in utter disbelief. His mouth opens a few times as if to procure a lingering thought, but nothing comes out. He simply stands there in silence as Ryan and the others watch from afar.

"I know you never knew your father well," Nana starts, "And I know I've never said much about our family, but that is what our family looked like forty years ago."

"So then..." Caid starts, every work taking considerable effort to articulate, "Why did you keep it a secret?"

Nana's eyes soften as if the question strikes her old heart with a dangerous amount of sorrow, "Because... you never asked."

The room grows a bit colder as Caid steps back shaking his head in disbelief, "No... I–I did ask."

Nana takes another look at the picture in her hand before slowly returning it to its spot on the dresser, "Caid, you asked me what was behind the door, not who."

Caid runs his hand through his hair and looks out the window. When his thoughts don't stop swirling, he leans over the bed and sinks his hands into the firm mattress, a cloud of dust puffs outwards. He doesn't even blink as the dust overtakes him for a brief moment. When it seems like Caid has finally regained grasp of himself, he gets off the couch and storms out of the room.

"Where are you going?" Max asks as they all follow him into the living room and to the front door.

"We're going back to the prison," Caid declares, "All of us."

Blake and Ryan exchange glances but neither of them can or are willing to argue given the situation. Not only does this feed into their interest of not pushing their luck further, it also seems like the necessary decision. The only question is whether Nana will come willingly.

"Okay," Nana says before anyone can even ask, "Why don't you boys wait outside while I get my things. My babies can come too right?"

Caid shakes his head, "I know you love your cats, but we'll have to come back for them."

Nana, feeling strangely cooperative, over to a closet and begins rummaging through a plethora of miscellaneous items. As she sorts through umbrellas, batteries, and a strange looking scarf, she looks back to the teenagers with a sincere look in her eyes, "Could you boys give Caid and I a moment alone?"

Ryan immediately checks in on the other teens to gauge their reaction but finds Blake and Max already halfway out the door by this point. Finding it to be the obvious solution, Ryan joins them out in the hall allowing the family one last moment in their home.

The three of them don't partake in any conversation as they wait. Max looks like he's cooking up some mood lifting comment, but falls short as the other two rather prefer the silence. As such, none of them make a peep as Caid and his grandma have a deep conversation on the other side of the door. 

Five or so minutes pass before Caid emerges from inside the room with a cumbersome box in hand. The cardboard box narrowly fits through the door as he tries his best no to smush his hand in between the box and the doorframe. Luckily he gets through without incident and sets the box on the floor before turning back to the door right as it slams shut.

Caid rushes up to the door and bangs on it, "Nana, what are you doing? You agreed to come with us."

"Oh Caidy," she says on the other side of the door, "I know what's out there. That world doesn't have a place for me... but this place does."

Caid slams his fist into the door as if a mere punch would break it open and grant him the opportunity to drag her out, "No! You're the only family I've ever had."

Nana's voice barely makes it through the door, but it wavers as if the person on the other side was holding back tears, "Caidy, you're a strong boy so protect your friends. I know... that's what your father would have wanted."

"Nana, no!" Caid screams pulling back and preparing to kick the door.

"Caid!" Nana shouts with much more authority than ever before, "When your mother dropped you off with me seven years ago, I outright refused her. How could I not? After my husband past away, I knew I couldn't care for another child... but..."

Caid lowers his leg and waits to hear the rest.

Nana continues with a calmer tone, but only in volume. The emotion seems to be even more heartfelt with every passing word, "Your mother didn't want what's best for you... but I do. And I still do... now, please Caidy, do what I never could and leave the dead lie. There's no place out there for an old woman with one foot in the grave."

Caid places his hand against the door, his cheeks grow red as tears begin dripping down them. He closes his fist and lightly hits the door as he looks down into the familiar red carpet floor, "Please Nana."

"You're a strong boy Caid," Nana says slowly, "Please never lose that... no matter what."

They all wait patiently in that forlorn hallway, full of corpses and empty memories. For once, no one was concerned with whether a zombie could be lurking around the corner, no one worried about where their next meal would be, not one even bothered to ask. They simply let Caid cry softly as Nana begins a distant conversation with Tommy.

A couple minutes later, Ryan and the others find themselves sitting on the curve of the parking lot behind the apartment building. None of them chatted much as they took their position in silence. Caid has since concluded his break down and now stares off into the distance with a lost gaze. They never bothered to take the box of things Nana gave him as most of it was useless garbage anyways. One thing Caid did keep with him however is a small toy car, small enough to fit in the palm of his hand. Painted across the bottom of the care in white paint is the simple name: Tommy.

Caid plays with the car in his hand as he returns from his thoughts, “I killed one of them."

"What?" Max wonders.

Caid chuckles to himself, "I actually... I actually killed one. No... I killed two."

"Oh, zombies," Ryan concludes.

“It was... so easy,” Caid says still sounding like he's talking to someone that isn't there.

"Caid?" Blake asks with a concerned look.

"It was so easy," Caid repeats as he looks back off into the distance, "If I had only known... then I could have..."

Max looks at him very closely, "Caid this doesn't sound like you."

Anger flares up in the guy's eyes as he glares at Max, "What would you know? Ryan had to save you."

Max backs off rather confused. When the insult finally lands he rubs the back of his head a bit embarrassed, "I–I just didn't have the right weapon is all."

Caid rolls his eyes, "It's not about the right weapon. You couldn't do it."

"Hey!" Max complains.

Blake steps gets up off the curb and steps in front of Caid with a stern tone, "Just calm down."

It doesn't seem like there's any other option for Caid as he immediately rises to his feet and grabs Blake's collar, "Don't tell me to calm down. I just left my only family behind."

Blake raises his hands defensively trying his best to not instigate a fight, "Caid we don't have to do this."

This seemingly de-escalatory comment has an inverse reaction as Caid shoves Blake backwards, "What the hell would you know?"

Blake doesn't back away this time, "I know what it's like to be betrayed. My father got on a helicopter and left the city without me."

Caid shakes his head as he waves his hand accusingly, "At least he can come back!"

The ever calm Blake, ordinarily would take a comment like that in stride, but something about the situation rubs him the wrong way. Within seconds, he's breathing down Caid's neck with his own hands on the guy's collar, "Don't ever say that again."

Caid narrows his eyes, "Don't compare yourself to me."

"My father was a good man," Blake warns, his hand clenching into a fist.

Caid doesn't back down in the slightest, "Yet he left... without you."

Blake draws back but Max manages to get there in time to grab his arm before he does something he'd regret. Max's actions seem to prevent Blake from starting a fight, but this completely forgets about Caid who throws a swift jab into Blake's nose and knocks him a few steps backwards. 

Shoving Max off of him, Blake charges at Caid to get him back for the cheap shot. Caid, being more proactive than he lets on, shoves Blake's momentum into the concrete to his side. He's immediately on top of the other boy drawing his own fist back with tears welling up in his eyes.

Ryan braces to intervene, but then he notices something crucial, something that stops him from joining all together. Just as before, Caid's hands won't stop shaking and his fist isn't tight enough to throw a fist.

Caid taunts the punch causing Blake to wince, but he never throws it, “Why?! If only I was strong enough before… then I wouldn't have had to leave my best friend.”

Ryan and Max exchange worried glances Blakes looks at the man on top of him in shock. No one believed this was just about the apartment anymore.

“I…I watched him die.” Caid says slowly as he falls backwards off Blake, “If I wasn’t such a lousy friend and an absolute wimp I could have killed her before she tore into my entire class… but... I couldn’t bring myself to do it... so... so I hid,” he stares intently at the concrete, “I had the perfect chance to save them. I had the scalpel and I saw her coming. I didn't have to leave.” he looks back at the boys, "Why do I have to be such a coward?"

“Caid,” Max tries.

“Shut up!” he shouts, “You weren’t there. You didn’t see Jordan's face as he laid there being eaten alive. He... he promised to get me back for our one-v-one on the court. I even called Nana to let her know I'd be home late,” he pauses as he shoulders loosen and he looks back down at his bloody hands, “I was too scared and hid behind the lab counter and just watched as my entire class was torn to shreds. Why? Why did I do nothing?"

Blake gets off the ground and walks up to Caid swiftly. A menacing scowl rests on his aggravated face as he swings his fist right into Caid’s cheek causing him to stumble back.

“Caid!” Blake yells back, not out of anger but out of desperation, “You weren’t the only one who lost friends in there,” his chin quivers. “everyone I’ve ever known was back there and now I’m stuck with a bunch of people that I didn't even know before this. I want nothing more than to switch places with one of those lucky souls who got off easy, but it's too late for that. So what if your best friend died, so what if you grandma doesn't want to live?! Haven’t you thought about living FOR your friend, FOR your grandma, instead of living in spite of them? I've asked myself a thousands of times: What if I had done this? Would that? What if? What if? And every fucking time it just hurts so... so damn much.” he pauses as a single tear rolls down his eye forcing him to turn away and wipe it off, "I don't think I'll ever get over the people I've failed... but... I just... I can't live like this! I just can't okay. I don't want to live in spite of them, I want to live because of them."

A hush falls over the boys as they each remember the horror of their last school day and for a moment, accept the outcome. Max makes a face to hold back tears all the while approaching Caid patting him on the shoulder with his head facing the ground. 

“When we escaped the school and ran through the hall, I saw so many people I knew. So many people I used to joke around with are now wandering this world as living corpses. I—I want to save them, but I can’t,” he lets go of Caid and looks back to the group, “I agree with Blake, we can’t beat ourselves up for not saving everyone. Us being alive is enough right? I'll just have to do enough dumb shit for everyone else.”

Caid chuckles a little through the tears.

Ryan takes a deep breath as he slides his hands into his pockets and looks off into the distance, "All we can do now is make our shot count."

The other boys all nod their heads in agreement—Ryan just pictures the gun that never fired.

“You know what?” Blake says looking around at the surroundings finding that there are still no zombies present, “Let’s do it.”

“Do what?” Max asks.

“Let’s go to the car dealer and get some cars, it may seem dangerous, but we’re going to live and most of all… we’re going to thrive.”
