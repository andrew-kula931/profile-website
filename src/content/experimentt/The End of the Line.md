The sun is rapidly approaching the horizon by the time they reach the military base. A line of cars leads up to the gate blocking the highway as all activity within the instillation seems to have grinded to a halt. In fact, ever since they passed that fence line, they haven’t seen a soul at all, dead or alive. It’s as if everyone and everything has been concealed by a vail of eerie ambience. As they get closer to the base, they spot to distinct pillars of smoke rising from the installation—ones likely from a campfire of some sort. They both sigh in relief upon that realization. 

“Well, the last couple of days have been a hell of a time, but we should be through the thick of it now,” Steve says with rising spirits. Ryan finds it remarkably optimistic but, for once, isn't bothered by the change in character.

“Let’s hope that’s true,” Ryan says, reducing in speed as his vision very slowly begins to fuzz over.

He can tell it's only a matter of time, but he can’t give up until he knows that Karman and Charlie are safe. Bill may have turned much more quickly than Ryan, but that might have to do with the fact that Heimes was nearby. That… creature... probably affected infection rate or something. Despite all that, he continues to push his body with every step, masking the pain and fatigue to ensure Steve won't notice. He can't show it—not yet. 

As they get closer and closer to the military base, the feeling in the air becomes more and more eerie. There are no snipers posted in guard towers, no humming of jeeps, no faint sound of chatter from within the walls, only silence.

It isn’t until they reach the gate, that everything falls into perspective. The gate, bent outwards at the hinges, indicate a powerful, blunt force from within. Blood stains and smear marks litter the area around the inside of the gate yet any sign of a corpse has yet to be determined.

Steve’s jaw drops as he views the destruction. The small brick building to the right doesn't have a single unshattered window and explosion debris speckles the walls like a ruthless splatter of paint. Further down, scorched tents shield any view of what truly went down. A few empty vehicles litter the parking lot with most having broken windows, bloodstains, and often both. Their path further in is unobstructed, yet it feels as if their is less reason to go in now than the first time.

Ryan’s eyes wander back to the broken gate and land upon a small piece of paper very intentionally taped to a broken beam. Steve notices the note too, but he consciously averts his eyes from it.

**Welcome to the end of the world.**

Ryan pulls his pistol out from under his shirt and grips it so tightly his knuckles turn white. This is now how his story is going to end. He refuses to accept an ending where his efforts were truly meaningless—he refuses. If though it's the last thing he'll do, he will get the Hughes out of here... no matter what.

“T-They must have escaped…” Steve stutters as he looks back to the military base with eyes Ryan can only describe as fear.

That’s when the soreness and fatigue really hit Ryan and his legs give out. The ground hits him harder than his fate as he's not even able to brace for it as it greets him. His stomach rages and his arms and legs begin to shake profusely. Whatever control he believed he had in his body vanishes as he feels the pathogen course through his veins like a disgusting, putrid concoction. 

“Ryan!” Steve yells dropping to a knee to aid him knowing full well that there is nothing he can do.

Ryan tries to make words but he can’t get his mouth to work. He’s a full-on human potato at this point, twitching and shaking as his body slowly fades into an ether he's unfamiliar with. It’s a bitter ending to say the least, but at least he won’t have to live in the hellscape that is forming around him. If anything, he’ll get the easy way out and not have to worry about anything else ever again. It's sickening to think it has to be this way, but he's never been the one to make the rules. 

“There should be some supplies left in the medical bay,” Steve says frantically as he tries to remain calm.

The pain and shock slowly begins to subside inside Ryan but it doesn’t go away completely. Eventually he gains enough control over his mouth to force out a single word: “No.”

Steve shakes his head, “Not yet damnit, you still need to see Karmen and Charlie one more time!”

“No…” Ryan chokes out, his body refusing to do what he wants, “Go... find your... family.”

Steve’s eyes fill with tears as his mouth quivers, “Ryan I… there’s still… there’s still one more thing I can do for you.” 

Steve unholsters his own pistol by his back pocket and points it towards Ryan’s fragile head. His arm shakes violently as tears begin to stream down his face. Of all the terrible things Steve's had to do throughout his life, why does this one feel different? Steve hated how Ryan made him feel. Ryan hated everything about Steve. This final gift should be cathartic, so why does... why does his face look like it's in so much pain? Ryan can feel his thoughts getting away from him, but it simply doesn't add up. Why, of all the performative people Ryan's ever met, does the first tear for him have to come from the person he detests the most? Why is Steve crying?

Second after excruciating second pass with the gun pointed straight at Ryan’s head. The trigger shakes as tension is added and releases in an inconsistent and frustratingly slow pace. As Ryan slowly comes to terms with reality, he finds that the trigger never gets pulled, rather Steve’s shaking grows so severe that he lowers the gun and stares down at the ground. 

“I’m sorry… I can’t do it…” two solitary tears drip down his cheeks, his breath shaking as much as his body. He never bothers to wipe the tears away as he takes a weak step back and nearly stumbles over.

Ryan, taking one last gulp of air, pushes out his four final words, “Go find your family.”

That’s the last thing he sees, feels, hears, or does before his eyes close.

--- 

He finds himself back in the kitchen with his mom humming that same familiar tune as she washes some dishes. Birds chirp quietly just outside the window over the faucet and a gentle breeze blows the curtains at a perfect angle. Ryan’s coloring page, now completely, shows a vibrant picture of a lion with an extra large mane. The soft hum of the refrigerator fills all gaps in the soothing ambience.

Suddenly, the garage door opens and a man steps in with a knowing look on his face. Wearing a business casual attire and a backpack, he looks as if he just got home from a day in the office. His build is rather impressive yet not overly stocky and looks like he could comfortably hold a baby in his rough hands. Ryan’s mom takes notice of him immediately and rushes over only to jump into his arms and be met with a romantic embrace. This was his dad.

Ryan remains seated as the couple say hello to each other in their own, mature way. Eventually, Ryan’s father heads over to Ryan and kneels down. He says something inaudible, maybe it was words at one point but Ryan can’t hear it. His dad smiles after finishing and gives him a big hug. 

Ryan's mom walks over and sits next to them at the counter as they all take their seats. Ryan's father pulls his son's drawing up to his face and smiles with a delightful laugh. He pats Ryan on the back as he points to the vibrant colors on the page. Ryan's mom says something and both her and her husband laugh at it. He doesn’t quite understand what is so cool about a badly drawn lion, but he doesn’t feel an impulse to speak up. 

Then his father turns to him and smiles for a solid ten seconds without saying anything. He opens his mouth and this time audible words come out.

“You’ve got a bright future Ryan. Don’t ever forget that.”

Ryan’s eyes open again to see the same old concrete street he’s gotten so used to recently. His body aches a bit, but it doesn’t hurt nearly as bad as before; he even is able to move his limbs on command. 

Upon looking around, everything looks just how it was when he passed out. Even the slight bit of smoke rising from the camp still remains at the same intensity. As far as he knows, he must have only been out for seconds if not at all. Steve, however, is nowhere to be found.

He picks himself off the ground and wipes himself off. Moving is still fairly taxing, but at least he’s not a zombie so that’s always a plus. He's about to walk away but his foot kicks something below him and he finds his pistol laying helplessly on the ground. He once again, can't shake the feeling that something is off about the gun but it's far too late to worry about such things so he stuffs it back in his pants in silence.

Spontaneously, a middle-aged man screams—not a scream of pain but one of agony and regret. Steve’s scream hangs in the air like a thick blanket as the emotion within pierces Ryan’s heart with a visceral sorrow.

Ryan uses everything he has to sprint deeper into the camp trying to locate the epicenter of the cry. It only takes him but a few seconds to turn a corner around a building and find Steve kneeling on the ground in front of a pile of bodies. Ryan doesn’t even have to ponder a thing as he already knows what happened.

In the front of the pile are two bodies: a boy and a woman. The boy’s right arm is torn apart with strands of flesh and muscles hanging as if violently torn apart. A trail of blood drips from both his arm, and the mouth creating a small pool underneath him. He is held tightly by a woman with charred skin and a lifeless pulse. Neither one is breathing and probably hasn’t for some time.

Ryan reaches out, his voice hoarse and his mind swirling. What does he say? What does he do? He's still alive, so there has to be hope somewhere... there has to... 

Steve shakes his head slowly, with no indication of looking back as he slams his fist into the ground. The man breathes slowly as looks back to the scene and runs his finger over a small metal object covered in soot around the woman's finger. He touches the boy's face and manages an agonized smile as if recalling the first time he ever saw that face. His body trembles as he leans forward and tears shatter against the boy's dark visage.

"Steve", Ryan mouths, finding his voice too shy to speak up enough to be heard. He manages a single step forward but is once again met with the same fear that has always held him back. Ryan clenches his fist to himself and thinks of just one thing to say. One thing that will give this man the hope he's always wanted.

As Steve sobs quietly, he leans back and looks down at his pistol. He feels the familiar surface underneath his fingertips and closes his eyes. Not even that wind dare interrupt the subsequent silence.

He hasn't lived long, but eighteen years of torture can amount to at least one small trace of wisdom. Ryan takes a deep breath, preparing one final speech—one last thing he can offer this man. 

Then, before Ryan can process anything, Steve places the barrel to his temple and joins his family. 

--- 

Ryan watches the sun sink as he leans against an old oak tree atop a nearby hill. The amber halo reaching across the sky reminds him of the morning—a time that feels eerily peaceful by comparison. The hill he rests on isn’t necessarily a large one, but is large enough to overlook the entirety of the checkpoint. Three oval-shaped patches of disturbed dirt lie before him as he sinks lower and lower on the tree. A single stone rests beside his feet with lightly scratched verbiage on it reading: "Steve, Karmen, and Charlie Hughes. Rest in Peace."

The tingling inside his body begins to grow; he can feel himself losing control once again. This time he knows, he won’t be able to get out of it. He reached the end of the line and this is where he will die. The only option now is how he wants to go out: as a zombie, or as a human. 

Steve’s death, Bill’s death, Karmen, Charlie, Mr. and Mrs. Jacobson, the girl from school, or anything else didn’t matter anymore. He wonders what the point to any of this if this is when it all ends so soon. Should he spare the world of another monster, or was this how it was always meant to be?

His life has felt so... meaningless. He hated two fathers who tried their very best to raise him even though they weren’t perfect. He gave up on so many families who stuck their necks out for him. He turned away from everyone, at school, at home, and everywhere in between only to run away and hide like a coward—a coward that who couldn't even look in the mirror. This can't be how life is supposed to be lived. Do people really spend their whole life running from their fears only to wind up at the end with nothing to show? Is this really all there is?

It's not like he didn't have an opportunity though. Back in the school, he had a chance to join those high schools and weather the storm together but, he turned them down just so he could run away and pursue some idealistic fantasy. He killed two zombies before anyone else and still ended up dead within the first three days. Is this really who he wanted to be? Is this really the man his mother wanted him to become?

His mother just wanted a son she could be proud of, not some idiot who releases zombies from a school and gets a traumatized woman infected. If he had just thought more about the people around him, maybe he could have spared them from some of the horrors. If he had just been a better person when it counted, maybe, just maybe, he could have been someone worth remembering.

Why did he have to turn away from everyone? Why couldn't he acknowledge that he needed help? Why did living feel so difficult? 

Why couldn't he just smile like everyone else? 

Ryan reaches out to the pistol at his side with tears welling up in his eyes. His cheeks feel hot and his head spin yet the rough texture of the weapon's handle feels eerily vivid against his fingertips. As his body struggles to move, even the slightest bit, he knows there's only one thing he's capable of doing now.

He's only ever thought about himself for his entire life. It's time he thinks about others, just this once, and spares the world of any more horrors he can produce.

With weak arms, he lifts the pistol against his head. It’s lighter than before, but perhaps it is his body, for once, agreeing with his mind one what needs to be done. 

He screams.

And slowly squeezes the trigger.
