The three men lead the way as they all walk down the middle of the street at an uncomfortably slow pace. At first, Ryan finds the nonchalantly meandering unbearable, but after passing multiple empty cars, he just swallows his pride and trusts that these guys' guns will protect them if their speed winds up becoming problematic.

“So how long have you kids been cooped up in that place?” the aviator guy says.

Seeing the man from a different angle, Ryan notices a machete handing off the side of his backpack with black water bottle counter balancing it. One the man's sleeve, Ryan notices a faintly grayed section of his gray shirt as if a patch had been torn out. The man doesn't seem concerned with Ryan's starring, but the second Ryan recognizes that fact he immediately turns away.

“We built that base the day this whole thing started, so we’ve been there for, what? 5 days now,” Blake responds, his demeanor feeling much smoother than it was a few minutes ago.

The man nods his head affirmingly with a slight smile, “And you kids held out against the zombies for that long, nice job.”

“Well zombie attacks are pretty mild for some reason,” Blake shrugs humbly, "To be honest, I didn't expect to get off that easy after what happened in the school?"

"The school?" the shorter man says looking to the taller one.

The aviators guy notices this exchange and shakes his head briefly before addressing the teens, “That’s because the zombies tend to stay indoors and don't come out unless they have to. If you’re lucky, you can get by without ever seeing one, but if you’re unlucky they’ll grow into something far worse than zombies.”

“How do you know all this?” Blake asserts with solidified criticism, "And how do you know about the mutants?".

The aviators guy laughs to himself, "You kids know more than you let on. Good for you. Anyways, I've already had to deal with... plenty. I have a firm grasp on how they work."

"I guess I'll just have to take you word for it," Blake says with a sigh, "I'm just glad none of the mutants have messed with our camp."

Looking off down the street, the aviators guy bites his bottom lip, "It's a good thing they're rare... for now at least."

They continue to walk a little way in silence looking at each passing building. Despite being only a few days into the apocalypse, most of the town looks like it has been abandoned for months. Broken windows, debris from damaged structures, and occasional stains of blood clutter the surroundings like echoes reminding them that their world is gone.

“So if you guys are so well off, why don’t you have a car to drive around down with?” Blake questions.

The tall guy behind them is quick to respond to this one, "Don't worry kid, I asked the exact same thing."

The most verbose guy comes up with the answer, “Streets like these are no issue to drive down, but a lot of them are blocked by traffic and roadblocks. Besides, traveling by foot allows us to be selective with our battles. A truck's engine roar will more than likely draw out all those sleepers hiding inside the buildings.”

“We actually have a rule regarding that,” the shorter guy says, “It’s not law written in stone, but everyone is supposed to remain quiet while outdoors. Drew here, actually tested it out just yesterday. He stood outside an infected building and started talking, tried to drag a few out. Nothing moved. He talked a bit louder and still nothing. He only got a reaction when he nearly screamed, which... well... caused the entire building to churn. Whatever these things are, they seem to be selective with their confrontations... which is weird considering how rabid they were a few days ago.”

With every passing word, Ryan finds it harder and harder to dislike these guys. He may feel uncomfortable deep inside, but on the surface they seem like ordinary people trying to survive a terrible situation. They even think ahead and plan things out despite it not being super convenient. For what they have been saying, he’s honestly admiring just how committed they are to staying alive. He kicks himself for not coming up with these ideas on his own a long time ago.

"So you're Drew?" Blake assumes pointing to the aviators guy.

This correct assumption causes the man's eyebrows to raise, "Did we not introduce ourself? Darn, always forget that part... Anyways, yeah, I'm Drew," he gestures to the taller guy first and then the shorter one, "This is Charles and Xavier, get this, the fourth."

"Right," Blake says laughing politely, "I'm Blake, if I haven't said so already."

"And he's Ryan," Drew points out.

Ryan nods while internally bashing himself for not introducing himself. It's such a simple and normal thing to do in a situation like this. They were going around the circle so why didn't he bother to continue the momentum. He just sighs and brushes it off, no sense in worrying about it now.

"Say, Blake," Xavier IV says honing his eyes on the teen, "What's your last name? I feel like I might know your father."

Blake presses his lips together, this clearly being the first time he's been asked about such things, "Oh, uh, it's Caldwin."

"Is your father James?" Xavier wonders immediately.

Blake nods, "Yeah that's him... is he with you?"

Xavier recoils momentarily realizing he may have dug himself into a corner but he quickly pushes through it and matches pace with the boy, "I don't actually know where he is, but I was a co-worker of his. Sorry, you look just like him... Actually now that I think about it, I remember him saying that he had a chopper coming to pick him up, you know since he was the top doctor at the hospital... you weren't on that... were you?"

Blake shakes his head as the ground becomes the only thing he cares to look at.

Xavier pats him on the back, "Don't look so gloomy kid, if I know one thing it's that James Caldwin would never leave his family behind. I'm sure he'll come back for you."

The conversation proceeds beyond that point as Xavier starts digressing into some light hearted ER stories involving himself and Blake's father. Ryan listens for a bit, but eventually finds himself dragging behind the rest of the group. He wishes he had some grand plan for why he was slowing down, but ultimately he found himself back there instinctually. Charles, the tall guy, just continues on at a steady pace, but Drew isn't so keen on letting him bring up the rear.

"What's your last name?" Drew asks, clearly trying to keep things light and personable.

Ryan shrugs, "Lancer, not that it matters, you won't know my dad."

Drew's smile twitches as if the name alone struck a nerve or long held secret, but he plays it off smoothly by nodding and throwing his arm over Ryan's shoulders, "I'm sure your dad was a great guy."

Ryan looks away, "Yeah, sure."

Drew takes the opportunity to lean in close and whisper into Ryan's ear, his breath producing goosebumps on the back of his neck, "When we get there, don't show anyone the gun."

Ryan's head snaps in his direction but by then Drew's already social distanced himself with his hands in his pockets.

"Just up here now," Charles comments as his heightened view allows him a glimpse over an obstructing bush.

The road, which has been winding itself into the forest, takes a turn around a boulder revealing a large compound. Tall chain-link fences topped with barbed wire surround the premises with guard towers stationed sparingly around the perimeter. The large concrete building in the center sits surrounded by a large grassy area with a second smaller fence dividing those fields into two halves. Within the outer circle are numerous small structures being constructed from wooden and scrap materials. The inner circle is a collection of shooting ranges, tents, and other construction projects.

The three men lead Ryan and Blake to the front gate and wave up to the watch tower above. The gate opens automatically allowing for them to enter inside.

“As you can see, we're not wasting any time,” Drew comments, “The warden plans on opening this outer region to trade with other surviving groups around the city. The inside gate will be for the group's needs. Everything is still in the works, but hopefully this place can offer a bit of normality in these trying times.”

"Don't call Michael a warden, it makes him sound... ominous," Xavier corrects with a light hearted nudge.

“Did you say that there are other groups of survivors?” Blake asks, diving in eagerly.

“Oh course, plenty of people survived all around the city, but with the internet down, we’re back to the dark ages in terms of communication,” Drew explains.

Blake and Ryan exchange glances, this is objectively fantastic news. It's sure to lift the spirits of the other teens, and not only that, offer the possibility that people are reunited with their families. Something about that starts eating away at Ryan, but he forces himself to smile.

They arrive at the doors of the prison and halt for a moment, soaking in just how ambitious this place is. Then Xavier and Charles take their exit with a quick wave. They disappear into the complex as a few curious woman starting watching them from afar.

Drew reaches for the door but promptly backs off as he takes on an embarrassed smile, "Forgot to say, but no one here is a prisoner. The actual convicts who used to be held here got shipped off right before everything went down. Suffice to say, anyone you meet is just a normal man or woman, don't stress about it."

After that, the prison door is opened and Drew shuttles them into a large room with countless artifacts of security. Metal detectors, barred doors, glass protected desks, everything reminded them that this was not meant to be an entrance people enjoy walking into. In the center of the room, just beyond the metal detectors but before the check-in desk, they find a circle of six or seven men and women talking to each other. The group quickly takes notice of the two new faces and turns to greet them. A single figure breaks from the group to greet them personally. Both boys recognize the young yet wise face of Mrs. Grail. The same history teacher that they were both learning from just a few days prior. Being shocked is hardly a word that could adequately describe how they felt at the discovery.

“Blake, Ryan! I’m surprised to see you guys here,” Mrs. Grail exclaims, “I’m so glad you made it out… I’ve only heard bad things about what happened at the school.”

“I was thinking the same thing,” Blake responds, “How did you escape?”

“Oh,” she says, studying their faces, “In truth, I wasn’t at work that day so everything I know is from people who passed by. Are there more of you?”

She glances out the window in search of a group of high school students but finds nothing.

“We have a whole camp, but we wanted to make sure this place is safe before we let them all in,” Blake explains.

Mrs. Grail’s eyes soften, “Oh look at you two being leaders. It makes me proud, and not just as your teacher.”

To be honest, if Ryan wasn’t still in shock at the sight of a familiar face, he would probably have been touched by the words of praise. Maybe that uneasy feeling before was just subconscious nervousness from his experiences with the military camp. This place seems legit if Mrs. Grail is leading it. 

“So you really became the leader of a survivor group?” Ryan asks.

She quickly shakes her head, “I’m not the leader, my husband is. He was the warden of this prison before the government relocated all of the prisoners. He practically opened the front doors for everyone when people tried to evacuate the town.”

Just as she finishes up speaking another man about the same age as Mrs. Grail approaches from a nearby hallway. He’s a charismatic fellow with the creases of a smile edged into the corners of his eyes. He approaches with a simple yet comforting stride as he projects a feeling of humble authority. Ryan doesn’t have to speculate much to identify that this man has ample experience taking charge of unwilling people with a silver tongue.

“When I heard about another survivor camp, I didn't expect it to produce such capable young men,” the man holds out his hand for Blake and Ryan, “My name is Michael Grail. I’m in charge of this operation.”

Ryan shakes his hand expressing his name and Blake follows suit. A certain wave or pride washes over him as he finally gets the opportunity to introduce himself with his own voice.

“I was informed that you two came to check this place out before bringing your friends along. I guess I can agree with your thought process but rest assured we are the safest place in the city. You have my word on that,” Michael says.

Blake and Ryan exchange glances as they both place a bit more trust in this place. At this point they were no longer searching for whether the place is safe, but whether there is some dark secret that’s hidden below the surface.

“So how exactly do you guys gather enough food?” Blake asks as he looks around at the empty repurposed processing room.

Michael smiles, “I’m glad you asked. We have been looting everywhere nearby, but that is only a temporary solution. We plan on journeying outside the city to the farms to gather livestock and seeds. From there we will begin planting in a greenhouse during the winter months and use some of this prison yard space for summer farms. A friend of mine specializes in agriculture so we should be taken care of.”

“How many soldiers do you have?” Ryan questions directly.

Michael shoots him a glance for a brief moment before returning to his usual self, “No one here is a dedicated soldier. Everyone receives training but we all take turns guarding the premises… I take it you are referring to the event of an attack? In that case we have fourteen able bodied individuals who are willing to fight tooth and nail to defend this place and that's not considering the multitude of people who would help assist indirectly.”

It's quite the impressive number compared to the high school group with only a single hand worth. Not to mention their group largely believed they didn't have any firepower, unlike this camp. Overall, this place is everyone’s best shot at surviving—it would be selfish not to bring the other kids here. 

Michael stands silently for a few moments waiting for them to ask any more questions, but when none come he begins walking towards the door. He opens it up and holds it open for them with a warm smile, “You two should head back and bring the rest of your group. Kids like you _will_ be safer here. I know what you're probably thinking: prison camp, former warden leader, this place is sketchy as all hell. I get it, but know that we won't make you do anything you don't want to. We'll send some guys over to escort you over later today,” Michael gives a knowing smile as he waits for the boys to leave the building.

"Hold on," Blake says as he gives the room and the few windows a quickly gloss over, "I'm still a bit skeptical... this feels too good to be true."

Michael finds this comment rather amusing as he smiles and waves his wife over, "I would love to keep going myself, but I think you'll trust her word a bit more than mine."

Mrs. Grail, whom Ryan didn't know the first name of, enters their small briefing session with hands folded and a radiant smile, "Please ask me anything, I simply can't wait to see everyone. How many students are with you?"

Blake thinks it over briefly, "Twenty-seven," he glances at Ryan, "Twenty-eight actually."

To their surprise and displeasure, Mrs. Grail's smile actually fades by the comment. She doesn't look disappointed, per say, but some sort of melancholy befalls her at that moment, "Only that many... surely more are elsewhere around the city?"

Blake's hope begins to dissipate as the conversation progresses, "Yeah... a few."

Mrs. Grail's eyebrows raise as her hearts raise over her heart, "My goodness, what happened back there?"

Blake closes his eyes, sorrow overtaking his composure for an instant. He quickly shakes it off and returns to his usual demeanor, "W-we'll have time to talk about it later. For now, there are a few things I want to ask."

"I'll answer anything you want to know," Mrs. Grail answers sincerely.

Blake nods, "Thank you Mrs. Grail. The first—"

"Oh, drop the honorifics," Mrs. Grail cuts off waving her hand, "Just call me Stephanie—you two have earned that much." 

The two boys exchange glances at the unexpected interjection. To be honest, Ryan already knew her first name because it was written on her classroom door, but receiving permission to forgo the usual formalities feels strangely unsettling. It's as if she had just gotten married and swapped her surname, the logistics are clear, but the name leaves and uncomfortable feeling his mouth.

"R-right," Blake says choppily, "S-stephanie".

Stephanie rolls her eyes, "No need to force yourself, you can call me Mrs. Grail if you'd prefer."

Blake turns to Ryan for support, but quickly finds that he's on his own for whether he's going along with this.

"Okay," Blake reiterates, "The first thing I want to ask is..."

The two go off into a deep conversation about who gets to eat, how much, and how long you're allowed to shower. Ryan would listen intently, but by the time they reach showers, he's already preoccupied his mind with observing the steady stream of people in and out of the front door. At first he only notices diligent looking adult men enter and exit with rifles, shovels, or boxes in hand, but after a while he starts to gain a better grasp of the diversity within these chain linked walls. The most striking thing he notices is a few children dispersed into the mixture of survivors. The first ten year old got him curious and after a few seconds of looking through the windows he notices no less than eight elementary schoolers kicking a soccer ball around in the outside courtyard. Their distant laughter echoes like a fleeting memory he thought he'd never get to hear again. Beyond that, he notices a few familiar faces every once in a while. He couldn't even begin to come up with names for any of the faces, but he swears he's seen at least four people walking by during mandatory school events. The strange part of it all was that none of them were high schoolers. 

The more he looks around the more he catches a few stray glances in his direction. None of them seem hostile, or even curious for that matter, more often than not, Ryan gets the impression that they're searching for something—or someone. They never dwell long enough for him to feel like he's the desired target, but their looks alone make him regret every looking around.

Eventually the conversation dies down and Blake turns to Ryan with a content look on his face, "I think I'm a bit more on board now."

"Right," Ryan nods, "Should we... go get everyone else."

"Absolutely," Stephanie interjects, "Bring everyone."

Blake nods his head and turns to the door waving for Ryan to follow him, "Let's go get everyone."

---

A couple minutes later, Ryan and Blake find themselves walking back alone on the street, heading to their base for the last time. Blake’s concerns about leading the group were about to be laid to rest since he would no longer have to lead anyone alone. The adults were going to take back over, which probably should be bitter sweet given the change of freedom, but Ryan can't shake the underlying feeling of relief for than anything else. Maybe he'll even get to sleep in a bed again.

“Well, I’m glad everyone will be safer now,” Blake says with a drawn out sigh as he lets his arms dangle to his sides.

“Yeah,” Ryan replies with his hands in his pockets and his eyes on the surroundings.

They pass a few houses with short metal fences. In each yard are a few garden gnomes creating a scene of fixing up a broken down bird bath. It’s a fitting narrative to what they will have to endure in the coming years—building back up what was lost. Although the apocalypse just began, it already feels like the worst is over.

He looks up to the window and notices something peculiar which draws his attention. The house's tinted windows obstruct his vision as he tries to make out what he sees on the other side. It seems to be some sort of thin, vase-like structure protruding from the base of the window up to about halfway. A plethora of thin, vine-like plants spill out of the vase creating the allusion of hair. Some of the vase’s patterns feel wrong. He keeps trying to find symmetry in them, but the designs seem to shift every time he focuses on one. At first he assumes it’s just the angle of the tinted glass and honestly finds it fascinating to see the designs creep up the vase, but then the designs solidify and the his recognition changes. Those strange decorative designs take one a more complete structure—that of a crooked smile.

Suddenly, the window of the house shatters and a figure flies out. What he once thought was a vase reveals itself to be small, only the size of a toddler, with moist skin. Its lower limbs are short and folded on top of itself like a frog's which causes it to begin hopping rather than walking. Its upper limbs are much longer with large, sharp claws—its nails jagged as if chipped away by numerous confrontations with a rock. Lastly, its head is shaped like an eagle with an elongated neck reaching out as far as the arms do. Calling it an abomination would be putting it too lightly.

“Shit!” Blakes yells as he starts off down the street, “Run!”

Without hesitation, they both break out into a sprint down the street. Without even needing to look back, Ryan can tell that the mutant follows closely behind. Somehow, that small body keeps pace with two decently athletic teenage boys. He can feel it's legs hitting the concrete as it gains on them.

As they run, the mutant behind them screeches at a volume that makes Ryan worry about more than just it's uncanny voice. He quickly looks around at all of the nearby buildings and locks eyes on a humanoid zombie sprinting out the front door of a house.
Ryan points it out with a yell but isn't fast enough to grab Blake's attention as the zombie vaults over a waist-high fence and lunges at Blake. The teen only realizes the threat when it's too late and gets tackled to the ground. He's able to quickly break free from the zombie’s grip but it grapples onto his leg and he stumbles back to the ground. Ryan doesn't bother considering the implications as he draws his pistol, slaps the safety off, and shoots the zombie through the head at near point blank. He then abruptly shifts his aim and fires a round at the stampeding mutant behind them. His attempt misses and he has to dive out of the way as the mutant flies by after a violent jump. Ryan immediately whips his gun around and fires two more shots at the mutant as it lands on the ground. The first clips its left arm, but the second just happens to pierce through its torso. It steps forwards as if to lunge again but its skimmed leg gives out and it collapses. It struggles to get back up as black ooze leaks out of it's torso, but Ryan finishes the job with a quick final round to the head. The monster twitches its finger for the last time.

"What the hell?" Blake gasps as he stares, not at Ryan, but at the gun in his hands, "How long have you had that?"

Ryan looks around for any zombies that may have heard him fire and to his demise a zombie breaks through the door of a nearby house revealing four others right behind it.

"Shit," Ryan says coldly as he helps Blake up and they take off down the street.

They approach a small parking lot surrounded by four buildings each belonging to a different business. Their base is just a block away. In a split second decision, Ryan makes, what he believes to be the necessary decision.

“I’ll draw them away, you get back to the others,” he says, pushing Blake behind the side of the nearest building, “Get the defenses ready!”

Without giving him time to protest, he turns back to the incoming zombies and runs the opposite direction yelling out at them to follow. He just needs to buy them time.

He heads past one of the businesses and through an open gate to a small gated off courtyard behind. The court yard has numerous summer time furniture fixtures scattered around a fake pool. A particular table near the gate with a hefty price tag catches his eye as he plans on vaulting over it to get to the other side. Hopefully it will provide just enough of an obstacle for him to gain some distance.

Suddenly, a zombie shatters through the glass of the building, blocking off his exit route. The mini-horde behind him catches up before he even has time to turn around. The zombies force him against the wall of the building with flesh-hungry monsters on either side. He prepares his gun to shoot them all down, but each zombie quickly slows its pace to a halt forming a circle around him. Ryan shifts his aim from zombie to zombie frantically as they all stand like statues around him—staring.

Then a single zombie begins to shake its head violently for a couple seconds before stopping as quickly as it started. It then raises its head revealing blood flowing from the eyes of the human corpse. 

“How… did you… do it?” it mumbles in a choked, horrendous sounding voice. 

Ryan doesn’t respond but rather maintains his aim on the forehead of the zombie.

“You were… supposed to be… mine,” it continues as the blood from its eyes creates a small pool atop the fake one, “Why?”

A chill runs down Ryan’s spine at the sight of a zombie speaking to him. It's not just the blood, but how… wait. Is this... is this Heimes? 

“I don’t know what you are doing Mr. Heimes, but I refuse to tell you anything. Not after you killed Bill,” Ryan defends growing angry with the thought of the past.

“I…” the zombie croaks out, barely able to talk at this point, “am not Heimes.”

Then the zombie's bleeding eyes hemorrhage so profusely that it collapses to the ground without another move, dead as a corpse is supposed to be. The static circle of zombies immediately break their formation and charge directly at Ryan. With so little time to react, he flips his gun to the right and fires at the zombie closest to the wall. The bullet lands cleanly through the shoulder allowing for just enough stun that Ryan can shove the zombie out of the way and break out of the cage. Without even thinking about it, already opening the back door and rushing inside.

The inside of the building is very similar to the outside—brand new furniture items are scattered around the building with large price tags littered over each and every one. Ryan vaults over a picnic table and hurdles a chair, stumbling briefly as his foot snags on the latter obstacle, before making it to the front door. Behind him is the pack of zombies tripping over couches and snapping plastic chairs in their merciless pursuit of a meal.

Ryan arrives at the front door pushing the handle with sloppy haste… it doesn’t budge. He tries again with increased force but to no avail. By this point his hands are shaking so profusely and he can barely get his hand on the handle. He can feel his senses rearranging their priorities and his vision tunneling. 

The fastest of the zombies reaches him very quickly and opts for a full out charge head first, mouth wide. Ryan side steps and allows the zombie to slam its face directly into the glass door with a loud clang and a crack in the transparent material. The rest of the zombies approach closely behind, blocking off the route back through the building. Ryan glances around frantically looking for another way but only spots an open door nearby probably to some kind of office. Subconsciously he knows that it’s a terrible idea, but in the heat of the moment, he doesn’t have enough self control to stop himself from running into the room and slamming the door shut. Almost immediately, zombies begin pounding on the new wooden door. He promptly twists the handle's lock and takes a strained breath.

He steps backwards not turning from the door, his leg bumping into a desk in the middle of the room. The banging on the door grows more intense as the stragglers from the mini-horde catch up. The metal hinges rattle as the wood itself creaks and groans on the verge of snapping outright.

Ryan looks around the room for something, anything to give him an escape route but not even a window is available inside this small enclosed quarters. His desperation did not pay off and his heart sinks lower and lower as he realizes he’s trapped himself in a tomb. 

Suddenly, a fist breaks through the door about head level and quickly retracts back to the other side. The hole reveals the clump of zombies pushing up against the door and that’s when another impulsive decision arises in his mind. 

Ryan steps up to the door and points his gun through the hole aiming at the head of zombies being smushed against the door. The metal hinges of the door rattle violently as they sit on the verge of breaking. With that in mind, Ryan wastes no time firing off rounds through the hole in the door. One shot misses, the next pierces through two heads, yet another hits a zombie in the torso which causes it to fall. Every time he kills one and watches as the body slowly slides to the floor under the weight of the proceeding zombies. After firing off half his remaining bullets the same bulky fist from before breaks through the door in another spot, but that doesn’t stop Ryan from expending the remainder of his magazine to kill as many as he can. By the time his gun clicks, the pile of zombies on the other side has already stopped banging on the door.. The other side of the door now only shakes from the pressure of two or three zombies pushing through the pile of real corpses. After listening for a moment he gets the impression that they may be stuck underneath the pile as their impacts with the door feels more like distant scratching than a fist slamming down on it.

He glances around the office space once more to find any kind of weapon. To his dismay, he finds nothing, not even a screw driver. With no good options and a shaking body, he opens the door and jumps back as the pile of corpses flop into the room followed shortly after by a lone zombie climbing over the pile. 

The only zombie that enters the room is the same zombie who caused all the carnage on the door. Its arm is normal sized for a human, but its fist is as wide as a dinner plate with a sharp piece of metal sticking out of various spots, namely the knuckles. Between the arm and the fist, he can only describe it as a mutated mace.

After falling forward over the pile of bodies, the zombie hops to its feet and swings its mace of a hand directly at Ryan. He responds by leaning back over the desk and counter attacks with well placed kick to the zombie's torso. Remembering what happened with the boy in the bathroom, he ensures his kick pushes the zombie at an angle rather than back into the pile. Then, using the small amount of room he has, he dives over the pile of corpses, lands on the top, and proceeds to roll off and out of the room right as the mutant slams it's fist into the pile. 

He scrambles to his feet and tries to stuff his pistol back under the strap of his pants but it slips out as a lone zombie hooks onto his foot and trips him up. His head slams against the ground as his empty pistol flies out of his hands. Pain surges through his head as the world begins to spin rapidly, his own limbs becoming difficult to maneuver amidst his disorientation. Instinctually, he looks back in spite of his dizziness and finds a zombie trapped below the pile of corpses grabbing onto his leg and tugging at him for dear life. Ryan immediately starts kicking the hand but it won't come free. His situation only grows more dire as he notices the mutant slowly climbing over the pile, struggling as limbs shift and turn beneath it.

Desperately, Ryan kicks at the hand even harder hearing the enraged grunts under the mangled pile. He slams his shoe into the hand over and over wishing for nothing more than to just loosen it's grip, but his efforts are in vain. So, realizing he has to do something quickly, he uses his free leg to press against the zombie's face, using the sole of his shoe to prevent a bite, and rips his foot free. Right as he does and rolls away, the mutant slams it's fist right into the ground where Ryan once sat—the trapped zombie becomes much more squished than it was before.

Ryan scrambles to his feet, watching the mutant lift its hand out of another zombie, but inversely trips over a chair and slams back into the ground. The world continues to spin as he pulls his knees underneath him and tries to focus backwards. The blurry figure just a few feet away draws its arm back and bursts into a dash towards him. Feeling the urge to move again, Ryan lifts to his feet but realizes he's not going to fast enough. In light of this, he immediately dives to the side narrowly dodging the blunt of mutant's hand as it cracks into the tile floor. His leg shouts in severe pain, but the mixture of a spinning surroundings and sheer adrenaline subdue the pain to the point where he mentally catalogs it as a mere distraction.  

Still on the ground, Ryan opens his eyes to find a shattered chair just beside his head. The wooden decoration was likely trampled by the horde during the pursuit which led to a half-built chair with shards of wood scattered around it. Without even realizing it, Ryan latches onto to a wooden leg with a splintered end. He then rises to his feet, finding one leg weaker than the other, and faces off with the zombie. 

Having slammed it's fist into the tile floor a bit to hard, its finds some of it's metal shards hooked into the floor which causes it's arm to get stuck. Ryan doesn't buy the idea that it'll stay stuck so he waits for a moment as the mutant gives one last tug and rips its arm free of the floor, swiping right where Ryan would have been standing had he advanced too quickly. Then, as if timing the interaction, Ryan dashes forward and tackles the zombie to the ground, being very careful not to put any part of his body near the zombie's jaw. Then, before any part of it can grapple him, he gets up onto his knees and slams the wooden steak right into the zombie's face. It screams in distress as as the wooden cracks further but doesn't break the skull, so in light of this, he raises the wooden shard again only to slam it down once more. He repeats it over and over quickly needing to pin his knee against the zombie's mace arm and left hand against the zombie's weaker arm to restraint it. He continues bashing the stake into it's skull with every free opportunity. Each strike squirts zombie blood, flesh, and brain matter all across his arm, upper body, and face. He doesn't stop striking until the wooden stake is reduced to nothing but a small splinter of the former chair.

Ryan leans off the zombie and catches his breath. Exhaustion, a spinning world, and some annoying pain in his leg cause him to nearly pass out, but he resists the urge knowing that the consequences of that would be dire. With time to finally think, he looks down at his leg and finds a deep laceration running from a few inches below his knee to right above his ankle.

He screams in both shock and pain as the realization sets in and the agonizing pain unequivocally takes over his thoughts. Immediately, his hands reach over to try and prevent some of the constant blood from draining out, but then he notices the copious amounts of zombie matter splattered across most of his right and some of his left hand. His hands recoil at the thought but the pain remains. 

He tries to move his leg to get a better view of the injury but a quick squirt of blood makes him refrain from doing it again.

The bleeding won't stop, the cut's too deep, he has no way of cleaning his hands so that he can even touch the wound. What's he supposed to do? Is he really going to die?

It's at that moment that a familiar black figure crosses his vision and stands before him with his arms behind his back.

"It's nice to see you again, Ryan," the masked man says with humored chuckle.
