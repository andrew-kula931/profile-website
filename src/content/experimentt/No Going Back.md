Once he wakes up the next morning, he immediately checks his temperature and runs a few basic physical tests. His vast knowledge of health, learned entirely from a sophomore biology class, tells him that everything is normal. The disease could have a slow burn effect, but the first day after exposure is critical. After everything checks out, his mind calms and the day sinks in. It’s Thursday, October 11th. He’s officially 18.

For some peculiar reason, he anticipated himself to be more excited about this moment yet the bland green walls and trickle of light through the curtains is the same as yesterday. His life is about to change yet nothing inside him is racing or even moving at that. It’s just yesterday but this social construct called legal rights have been altered.

Alas, he heads downstairs to grab breakfast before leaving. His current plan is to officially drop out of school and prepare for the apocalypse. If the problem truly gets contained then he’ll find a job and look for what’s next, but after yesterday, he needs to be better prepared.

Karmen is in the kitchen cooking something, thankfully with no smoke coming off, while Steve sits on the couch watching the morning news. Charlie is absent: most likely still sleeping. 

“Good morning Ryan, I’m making pancakes,” Karmen says after noticing him, she quickly follows with, “And happy birthday.”

Steve catches wind and glances over and releases an annoyingly pleasureful smile, “I heard the police came by last night. What did you do this time?”

Karmen shoots Steve a dirty glance but doesn’t say anything. 

“The neighbor did something illegal. I wasn’t a part of it,” Ryan responds, trying not to act too moody or else Steve would pounce on it.

Steve rolls his eyes as he leans back on the and clicks through a few channels, “Let me guess, you told the cops you didn’t do anything and didn’t know anything. You were probably a part of it, though.”

“Steve!” Karmen scolds timidly.

“I didn’t do anything this time,” Ryan says, fighting the urge to insult him or defend himself anymore.

“Fine, but you better not do anything else. You hear. I’m sick of lazy fucks not participating in society while actively sabotaging it.” Steve continues after no one responds, “I work eighty hours a week to provide for this family and myself. When I was in high school I worked a minimum of forty hours on top of school. You kids these days don’t know what real work is. Do you even have a job Ryan?”

Ryan sits himself down in the dining room and prepares to eat the pancakes Karmen made. He doesn’t give a response and just hopes that Steve doesn’t take it as an excuse to become provoked. Within a few seconds, Steve is standing at the end of the table staring down at him.

“I was talking to one of my buddies at work,” he says, “I’ve been easy on you but now since you are an adult, I’ll treat you like one.”

“What are you saying?” Karmen asks.

“Please be quiet and let me finish,” Steve says to Karmen which strikes Ryan in an uncomfortable way given how the man treats his foster son. Steve goes on, “Since you are an adult, you can start acting like one. Get a job and start paying rent for living here or move out. From the second you turned 18 you were no longer OUR responsibility.”

“You can’t be serious Steve!” Karmen protests, hugging the corner of the wall of the dining room. 

“This is not up for debate. This will teach the boy how the real world works.” He turns to Ryan, “Do you understand?”

Ryan returns a scowl.

“Do… you… understand?”

Karmen leaves the wall and runs to Steve pushing him back slightly, “You can’t be serious about this. Ryan is a goo–”

Her words are cut off by a backhanded slap from Steve. Ryan is hardly shocked by how careless the man is. This personality trait of his is what Ryan hates the most. The sight of it isn’t enough to prevent him from reacting.

With one swift motion, he stands up causing the chair to flip on its back. His fist swings from under the table into Steve’s sorry, ugly, completely deserving face. All the pain this family suffers because of him was put into that punch. Ryan had punched a tree so many times that Steve’s face feels surprisingly soft. Being a fighter has never been a thought in his head before, but after the clean blow, the prospect enters his radar, if only for a moment.

Steve stumbles back, barely able to maintain his balance. At first he looks stunned not by the damaging blow he took, but the strike he dealt to his wife. His eyes wide and surprised like he didn’t actually mean it even though Ryan knows that’s just a stupid facade. Only after that moment of inconsistency does his rage flare and he directs his attention to the idiot foster son.

“You little bastard, you’re going to pay for that,” he says, wiping a stream of blood that begins to fall.

“Please! Stop!” Karmen yells getting in between them.

Steve pushes her out of the way and charges at Ryan. The amount of times he’s seen a scene similar to this makes his response almost like a habit. Usually he just lets them hit him but he always, always imagines what he would do if he didn’t act defenseless. This is the solitary time when he fully intends to react. 

Steve draws back his fist, which for some reason is open rather than closed, and prepares to pummel Ryan with it. Ryan lets the strike come out using one simple move to survive: a side step and parry. The open hand flies right past and Ryan lands his own attack right to the abdomen of Steve. 

Ryan has watched Steve for the past year analyzing him. He is quick to aggression and wants things to get done as efficiently as possible but always over commits. He doesn’t know how to do things in moderation. 

Steve falls to his knees gasping for air after the violently powerful strike. He tries to spit out some curse words, but the pain from his core prevents him. 

Karmen stands wide-eyed looking at Steve, then to Ryan. She, too, is speechless. 

Ryan looks down at his hands. Within a twelve-hour period, he killed a woman and beat up an abusive father. What a hell of a way to start his adulthood. The shock he feels is not guilt of his actions but the realization that there’s no going back. Any childhood he wished to salvage is now history as everything here on out is a brand new chapterof his life.

Before Karmen can say anything, Ryan makes a dash out the front door of the house and down the street. He passes the zombie woman’s house and runs around the block. The house he was cooped up in for two years is no longer his. His impulsive rush persists until his breath grows heavy and then continues at a jog. The neighborhood houses slowly turn into restaurants and small businesses. Which slowly grow into trees. Even that massive sports stadium is passed in his crazed sprint. His constant travel doesn’t end until he reaches a bridge. The bridge runs over a small creek in town with the water only reaching a foot deep at max. Ryan takes a seat on a rock protruding out of the water.

Thoughts dance, sprint, and violently thrash through his mind. Emotions he completely forgot existed resurface at the face of an unexpected situation. His lungs inhale slowly followed by an exhale at a similar pace with a bundle of uneasiness going with it. The rave in his mind settles slowly, and he doesn't fight it until rational thought eventually becomes more predominant. 

He just said his final farewells to the seventh foster family. If his analysis of the family is correct, Steve will be too angry to ever report that he ran away and Karmen won’t have the courage to tell anyone what happened. It’s a sad reality to think about, but he’ll just use it to his advantage for now.

Throughout his life, he's dodged and weaved through foster families. Each one tried to welcome him with open arms, but those hospitalities always faded over time. One family, when he was especially young, tried to get him to see a therapist to deal with something they jokingly called "behavioral issues". In retrospect, they were right, he did act up as a kid with all the running away and familial avoidance. He remembers one instance when he even slashes his foster family's tires because they were pissing him off. He knows now that it was an incredibly stupid thing to do—it cost that family a lot of money and him a school district that actually wasn't too bad. To be fair, he was ornery as a kid since he blamed everyone and everything for his mother's death and father he never had. It wasn't until he got to his fifth foster family and high school that the bullying ramped up, and he stopped acting out. The next two foster families ditched him because he would constantly sneak out and not come back for days at a time—he became well acquainted with the police of that town. By the time he arrived as his seventh and last foster family, the foster agency told him that he would be sent to the juvenile detention center if he screwed this one up as it was the last family that would even consider to take him in. That was the only reason he stopped acting up all together. At the end of the day, each family was all the same and each family was just another obstacle to his freedom.

His only obstacle now is to drop out of school and quickly find a means of money. The school won’t allow him to just drop out so he’ll need to come up with some excuse. It would have been much easier had he not had a fight with Steve, but that’s in the past now. 

He needs to report to school or else someone will come looking for him, so that is top priority right now. Secondly, he needs to get more information on these riots breaking out all over the world. Once he gets enough information he can formulate a better plan. 

After another five or so minutes of silence, he departs to go to school, hopefully for the last time.

Stonecreek high school has never been a happy place for Ryan, but seeing it again after recent events relieves him more than it should. The daily routine he’s accustomed to gives him a sense of security that soothes his anxious nerves. That security, however, gets torn down when he enters the office and asks to see the principal. Moments later he enters the office of the most powerful man in the building. He sits silently at his desk working on the 27'' monitor before noticing someone enter his room. For a split second, the man freezes like he’d seen a ghost. It’s the kind of look you give when something so dreadful occurs that you can’t help but tell yourself that it’s fake. Principle Heimes snaps out of this state so quickly that Ryan questions whether or not he misinterpreted the reaction. The man then smiles and gestures for Ryan to sit before him only for the two of them two stare at each other with unease and about two feet separating them.

“It’s unlike you to come see me, Ryan. What’s the matter?” 

Ryan freezes at the fact that the principal knows his name despite having never shared introductions. He ultimately decides to brush it off and move forward. Mr. Heimes is a former military man, having served during the recent war in the Middle East. He definitely gives a military impression with a buzz cut and a killer stare, but he makes sure not to treat the students like privates. It’s respectable how Heimes can keep the two aspects of his life separate. 

“I’m dropping out of school,” Ryan says bluntly. He speaks bluntly intentionally to clear the air of any ambiguity. Most people don’t know it but Heimes is very manipulative. It’s way too subtle to tell, but Ryan has suspicion to believe that he was a military interrogator. He now hides that past underneath a welcoming and understanding persona. Classmates of Ryan’s have been known to try and argue with Heimes and lose every time. In fact, this school has never had any issues with how its students behave because every time something bad happens, Heimes somehow “convinces” each party to scoop it under the rug. As far as Ryan is concerned, he’s not doing anything shady, but the charisma and intelligence is there. 

“Oh, can you explain your reasoning?” Heimes responds without showing any surprise. 

Ryan knew this would be the hardest part of dropping out, but he’s here more for information than to explain his reasoning. All he wants is the requirements to drop out.

“I’ve been bullied, I can’t stand it here. The pain I have endured for so long can’t be healed, I just need to get away.” Most of what he said was bull, but to make the lie effective, he intertwined truth into everything he said.

Heimes' composure doesn’t change but his eyes grow more sympathetic. “I’m so sorry to hear that. I want to help you if you are struggling, but I can tell that’s not going to help you now.”

Ryan nods hoping he bought it.

Heimes continues, “I think that running out on your own isn’t the best idea. I once knew someone who tried that and he ended up dead. Trying to live on your own because of emotion doesn’t cut it in the real world.”

Ryan is almost taken aback by the change in tone from one of sympathy to a more passive aggressive one. The undertones of realism reminds him of Steve which only infuriates him to some small degree.

“That being said, you can’t drop out unless you can prove you can survive by yourself or else you need parent permission.”

Heimes quickly glances at the security cameras on a computer screen but rejoins eye contact immediately following. Ryan then notices that Heimes' pointer finger is tapping on the table at a pace just slightly too fast to be a tick. The last thing he sees is through the window of the office. A police cruiser pulls up and three officers get out.

“I have a question,” Ryan asks, narrowing his vision.

“I’m all ears.”

“What is going on?”

Heimes leans back a little in surprise, “Are you reconsidering your position on dropping out?”

Ryan covers his subtle meaning up with ignorance, “I’m talking about why there is a police car out there.”

Heimes turns around and glances out at the cruiser and the policemen walking in. “Don’t worry about that, nothing serious is happening. They are probably just here to talk about an incident nearby and warn the school. They tend to do that sometimes.”

The air of the room fills with silences as the two people within simply stare at the other. Any normal person would completely buy the bluff Ryan had been served, but he knows everything that has happened so far is all fake. He just doesn’t know the full truth behind it. Heimes maintains a confident and controlled composure and Ryan sticks to his “I don’t really care about what’s going on” routine. They stay that way for a solid 20 seconds.

Suddenly, the door swings open and the secretary walks in. “They’re here,” she says.

Heimes nods, “Ryan, why don’t you go to class and we’ll talk about this later, okay. I actually have something else we need to talk about.”

The secretary exits the room and Heimes stands to walk out, but before he does, Ryan gets one last comment in. “Fine, but if anyone turns, I reserve the right to defend myself.”

Heimes stops in his tracks with one hand halfway turning the doorknob, “What did you just say?” His confident voice grew dark in a heartbeat. What once was a friendly principle, now sounds like a real interrogator. 

“I’m not one for blackmail, so just tell me how long we have until chaos ensues,” Ryan responds.

Heimes shoulders relax and his principal voice returns, “I don’t know what you are talking about. Just make sure to keep your opinions to yourself. Now you better get to class or you’ll be late. Like I said, I’ll have a follow up discussion with you later.”

He was right by being suspicious. The countdown timer is running short and he’s gonna be ready for it.

Ten minutes later, Ryan finds himself sitting in the first hour staring at the board. He thought he’d never be here again, but there is nothing he can do about it now. He takes a look around the room. People are whispering back and forth seemingly about the same subject. 

“Did you hear the bullets last night,” one says.

“Yeah, it was really scary.”

Everyone had a very similar conversation, each taking place while the teacher was organizing some papers. Through the quiet chaos, Ryan spots Chloe staring at him. As soon as they make eye contact, she looks away. Yesterday, which seems like a lifetime ago, is probably still on her mind. He notices a desk right next to her being open, probably from someone being sick. He glances back up to the preoccupied teacher and walks over to the seat.

“About yesterday…”

Chloe looks at him swiftly but doesn’t respond, her face isn’t stubborn or angry, rather just simply nervousness.

“Are you and Jack a thing now?” he asks, trying to stay light.

She shakes her head, “He’s an okay guy, but he cares more about sports than a relationship. It kind of slipped that he felt like a loser not having a girlfriend. In the end he wasn’t even there for a serious relationship.” 

Ryan smiles knowing that she didn’t harbor any hard feelings. A cop car passes by in the corner of his eye, but he disregards it.

“What I really want to know is,” Chloe continues, “why did you give me a blank notebook.” She holds his notebook.

“Oh,” he says rubbing the back of his head, “You see, the thing is.”

“Did Beth tell you to go to the gym after school?”

Ryan stops, “How’d you?” He didn’t know if the girl he talked to was named Beth, but it’s safe to assume it’s the same person.

“She set us up,” Chloe says with a little laugh.

He has to admit, this is the first time he’s ever talked to a girl like this in a long time. A completely relaxed, stressless conversation with neither party being annoyed by the other. The last few months have been lonely, but this is starting to make up for it even if just a little.

“Yeah, I guess she did…”

Chloe smiles, “I… I’ll tell her not to do stupid stuff like that.”

Ryan shrugs his shoulders, “It’s fine, I’m used to that kind of stuff.”

“What kind of stuff?”

The teacher then finishes organizing her stuff and refocuses on the class as a whole. She sees the chatter and tells everyone to return to their correct seats so she can take attendance. 

The rest of class goes smoothly with a glance here and there from Chloe. If his life wasn’t shit right now, he would absolutely humor this relationship and decide what good could come from it.

A few minutes before the end of class, Ryan decides he needs to use the restroom. The teacher tells him that he can go and a minute later he’s turning the corner of the hallway and into the men’s bathroom. He immediately goes to the sink and washes his face. His emotions are going for a roller coaster right now, he can’t decide whether everything is fine or everything is wrong. He thinks back to the zombie woman and Steve and his stomach aches. Then Chloe flashes through his mind and he calms down strangely enough. Something about Chloe makes him feel like maybe the last few days haven’t been worthless. It’s strange how a connection like that can have such powerful psychological effects. Loneliness is a bitch, what can he say?

A brief, unexplainable noise echoes out through the hallway. Then a boy trudges into the bathroom with some ambiguous black object in hand. He’s slightly shorter than Ryan with a scrawny build, probably a sophomore in age. He walks into a stall and shortly after groans and grunts begin arising from within the stall. His brain goes to the thought of the kid jacking off, but it sounds too guttural to be any pleasurable experience. 

Ryan creeps over to the stall and peaks underneath. He holds back a gasp at the sight of a ripped apart rat in the corner of the stall with the boy kneeling over it. He can’t see the boy’s face, but he does see a hand reach down and pull out an eye to bring it up and out of view. Ryan doesn’t even have to think to know what waits behind this door.

He stands up as quietly as possible and inches away trying not to attract the zombie’s attention. He makes it halfway to the exit of the bathroom when the zombie spontaneously rushes out of the stall and straight towards Ryan. The strangest part of it all being that the zombie doesn’t make any loud noises and instead rushes silently.

Ryan waits until the very last moment before he jumps out of the way. The boy flies past and slams into the wall. Great, now the zombie is between him and the exit. It turns around and dashes again. He attempts the same thing. He dodges out of the way but unexpectedly the zombie pivots its foot and reangles its momentum straight at him. The zombie dives onto Ryan pushing him to the floor. Luckily, Ryan gets both of his arms in between them and positions one forearm on the upper chest.

The zombie snaps its bloody, gooey mouth at Ryan and claws at his shirt. It spits vile saliva on his cheek but thankfully his mouth is closed. Ryan uses his elbows to push the zombie’s arms back to avoid its nails from ripping through his shirt but he knows he can’t avoid it for long as the zombie’s arms continue to flail. In an act of desperation, he pulls his arms together and pushes up with all his might. The zombie's build is thankfully more book than boy and Ryan’s able to get a foot up to kick the boy back. 

Ryan pushes himself to his feet but the zombie is already on him. He feels the arms wrap around his waist like a football tackle. Ryan spins his entire body and the zombie can’t keep grasp as it spins and flies off, still in between Ryan and the exit door. 

He takes another step back realizing now that his back is against a wall. The zombie rushes forward. Ryan lets his instincts take control and drops his own shoulder, ramming the kid. The zombie lets out a groan as Ryan hits its diaphragm and it loses its footing. The zombie's arms slap the ground next to it as it lands and Ryan takes the opportunity to raise a foot and smash it right down on the head of the zombie. The impact lets out a little crack but it doesn’t break the skull. The zombie reaches for his leg and tries to advance towards him but Ryan promptly raises his foot again and stops once more. The zombie's head snaps to the side as it releases a human-like "Ouch". Ryan hesitates as tears well up in the boy's eyes and his resistance begins to diminish. His foot hangs in the air as the boy scrunches up his face and softly sobs to himself, "it hurts,". Honestly, Ryan was about to move out the way and release the boy, but that idea is shattered as fury overtakes his visage and an inhuman cackling beings erupting from his throat. Ryan's next strike lands with his heel to the temple. With a quick crunch, a section of his heel lands in the boy's brain matter. Its body goes limp and the demonic please conclude.

Ryan falls back and lands on his bottom panting heavily. Each heartbeat in his chest feels like it moves his entire body back and forth. The corpse in front of him was a normal high school boy just yesterday. He probably had hopes and dreams, probably had a stable family life, probably meant something to somebody... but now he's dead. Ryan may have been acting out of self defense but this... he can't keep doing this. 

His stomach decides it has something to say and he barely made it to the sink before release all its contents. After spitting out the last bit of acid from his throat he rises from the sink and looks into the mirror. Before him stands a killer and a loner—someone who's only alive in spite of the situation. A few drops of blood have splattered on his face and he promptly washes them off before taking his shoe off and cleaning that too. He ends up needing to clean his shoe a second time after vomiting on it during the first cleaning, but he eventually gets himself to a presentable state despite his weak knees.

With his own hygiene out of the way, he turns back to the corpse. Everything inside of him screams to run, get out, and escape, but something opportunistic part of him begs to differ. He inches towards it, preparing for it to reanimate and restart the confrontation, but with every passing bathroom floor tile, it doesn't budge a millimeter.

He slowly reaches over to the corpse and begins checking its body for bite marks or scratches. If it is a zombie virus there has to be a transmission. He scours the body and finds no visible marks. There had to be some sort of path for this circumstance to occur, otherwise the disease would need to be airborne and if that happened… they were all doomed. Despite his body shaking like a 9.0 earthquake was going off, he still manages to overlook the majority of the zombie boy making preparations in case it wakes up again. When he feels confident classifying the zombie as fully deceased, he simultaneously finishes his investigation. To his own dread, he finds no bite marks and tears running out of the boy’s halfway smashed face. His death was not without a great deal of pain.

A body quickly rushes into the bathroom and Ryan holds back a scream as he flinches backwards into the wall again. It’s Mr. Heimes.

“Ryan!” He looks down at the corpse.

“I can explain!” he shouts out as Heimes analyzes the situation, “He’s some kind of zombie.”

Heimes remains silent and looks him dead in the eyes.

“There was another person like this guy,” Ryan continues, choking on the fact that he isn’t allowing himself to get a full calm breath, “They are completely insane and tried to kill me. I did this out of self-defense—honest.”

Ryan tries to calm his shaking arms, but the scene is unapologetically a clear cut murder case. Hopefully he can convince Heimes to check out the corpse and see he’s telling the truth. He turns to the dead rat and reaches out for it.

“You should have stayed home.” 

Ryan freezes. He turns back to Heimes and sees that his face looks disappointed, but it's not directed towards Ryan, it's towards himself. It's as if the the man felt... pity? The stoic man then reaches into his suit coat jacket and pulls out a small cylindrical object with a curved top and bottom. He then flicks out a menacing blade. Ryan studies the weapon and begins to slowly back away from the man who he once believed was supposed to protect students.

Heimes holds the blade out to him, “Do yourself a favor… end your suffering before it begins.”

Ryan’s blood runs cold. Any expectation he might have at the moment doesn’t do justice to what might actually happen. The irony is that Heimes retains his stoic position like a statue waiting for Ryan to make the next move.

“I–what? No!” Ryan argues, lifting himself to his feet and bumping up against the wall, "W-what are you saying?"

Heimes sighs as he returns the blade into the handle and places it methodically into his jacket, “Everything from here on out was your choice. Don’t forget that.”

Ryan looks down at the corpse and then at the man acting contradictory to everything he knows… Actually, on second thought, that’s not true. Heimes’ face remains cold and calculated. His shoulders are relaxed, and his movements are calm, like a man who’s done truly absurd things before: this was just another day in the office to him.

Heimes pulls out a key from his pocket and tosses it onto the floor near the corpse, “Take that key, you’ll need it.”

Ryan’s breath, now frantic, slows to one controlled inhale and exhale as he searches for the words he needs, “What is going on?”

Heimes shakes his head with a slight chuckle, "That’s the second time you’ve asked me that", he smirks briefly, indulging in a moment of flat humor, "Have you finally reconsidered your position on dropping out… none the matter," he returns to an authoritative demeanor, "You are going to die... but if you take that key, you'll simply die a little bit later... the choice is yours."

Ryan shallows the heavy words that fall upon him like cinder blocks. His knees start to buckle but he prevents them from giving way as he takes another deep breath and concentrates on what is before him.

“If you choose to live,” Heimes begins with the most intimidating, monotone voice capable of a human, “My advice to you is trust no one.”

Ryan’s thoughts continue to swirl, but he digs deep and grasps onto the bit of rational thinking he can possibly find, “What is going to happen?”

Heimes adjusts the sleeve of his jacket before doing the same to his collar, “Do yourself a favor and check out the library when the opportunity presents itself. You’ll answer your own questions there.” Heimes then proceeds to the door of the bathroom only to stop just briefly with his back turned, “I’ve seen many, many people in between a rock and a hard place, Ryan. But of them all, I pity none more than you.”

With that, Ryan is left alone in the bathroom with a fresh corpse and more thoughts than rain in a thunderstorm. Though at least a thunderstorm can get rid of that water by dumping it on someone else.

He exits the morbid scene with a ghostly complexion and a golden key held tightly in his fist. A janitor with a gas mask and yellow overalls silently trades places with him as he returns to class.
