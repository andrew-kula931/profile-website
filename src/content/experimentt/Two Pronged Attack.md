When they return to the thrift store, Ryan hangs back by the door while Stephanie and her two friends promptly dive into the rustic furniture and miscellaneous home pieces. He expects to keep watch in case Jimmy decides he's not actually done with them, but that plan gets partially altered when both Chloe and Jeremy hang back as well. Jeremy preoccupies himself with some odd shelf of books in the first aisle of the shop while Chloe stands directly next to him, hands folded in front of her.

“So you really knew that guy back there?” she asks, leaning against the store window.

"Sorta..." Ryan starts, glancing out the window one more time to ensure Jimmy's not around, "Just trust him, he's... very dangerous."

His explanation feels reserved but clear to him, but this doesn't seem to strike Chloe the same way as she gives him a quizzical look, "What do you mean? Bad history?"

He shakes his head. If it were anyone else, he'd probably just shrug his shoulders and avoid answering. Sure he's a serial killer, and should probably tell everyone that, but something inside of him finds the idea of relaying that information scary. Sure it's necessary, but what if they call him crazy, after all, Jimmy just convinced every single one of them that his name is Henry. Would they believe Ryan if he told them such a striking truth that contradicts their first impression? Honestly, this line of thought would probably keep him from saying anything... but Chloe's humble demeanor catches him off guard—it's more... personable than he cares to admit. Maybe it wouldn't be so bad to test drive it with her. Maybe she won't call him crazy, heck, she didn't call him crazy when he said there were zombies in the school and look how that turned out.

"He's..." Ryan starts, finding the words far more difficult to say than he wants them to be, "He's a serial killer."

Shock strikes her face like a sledgehammer as her mouth drops a bit, "Y-you're kidding, right?"

Ryan shakes his head, "He's actually dangerous."

Suddenly, Jeremy steps into the scene with some colorful book in hand, "Ain't no way he's a real serial killer! If that were true, he would have killed you."

Both Ryan and Chloe watch the boy as he notices himself holding the vibrant book with a strange art style on the front. He glares at Ryan but when noticing Chloe staring too, he promptly places the book face down on a table nearby, 

"It's true," Ryan confirms, disregarding any suspicion that my have arisen by the boy's previous actions, "He tried to kill me a couple of weeks ago." 

Both teens stare, wide eyed, at him demanding a more thorough explanation.

He holds up his hands defensively, "I'm fine. No one got hurt."

Jeremy rolls his eyes and crosses his arms, "If you're going to make up stories, at least make them sound real. A serial killer really? In our town? There's no way."

While Jeremy's opinion is as clear as daylight, Chloe doesn't seem so dismissive. In fact, she looks at Ryan with a bit of concern as if this new information was nothing short of a great revelation and a hefty burdened finally relieved. 

"Jeremy," she eventually says, "I don't think he's lying."

Jeremy immediately shakes his head, "It's funny how Ryan always goes off on his own and ends up in situations like these. But this? What do you think that means for the rest of us if there's a serial killer in our midst? Everyone else is going to panic."

Ryan folds backwards, deciding that this is no longer a winnable battle. He doesn't have any proof and Jeremy seems gung-ho about correcting any misconceptions about Ryan's charismatic "friend". This is what he was afraid of all along, and somehow it manifested in the absolute worst way possible. Chloe, for some reason, doesn't match this sentiment.

"Even if it's true, they're still monsters out there. Disregarding a warning is not going to do us any good." Chloe's words are strikingly clear and pierces Jeremy thorough enough that he loses all momentum in his rebuttal.

In light of the counter rebuttal, Jeremy hesitates as one hand lands on his book while the other clenches into a fist. He doesn't glare at Ryan or Chloe so much as the space between them. Then, with a shake of his head and pursed lips, he releases a strained sigh.

"It's just..." Jeremy tries, "It's just one more thing. Why is it always another thing."

"It's fine," Chloe offers, "The important part is that no one got hurt... and we just have to tell everyone else to stay away from Henry."

Jeremy looks deeper into the store to avoid eye contact as he subconsciously slides his book to the edge of the table. His gaze lands on his mother who laughs with the two other ladies over some peculiar looking statue. For a moment, looks surprisingly unconcerned. Then, with a drawn out sigh, he swipes his book off the table and pushes the front door open. He stops in the doorway with the book carefully concealed against his side, "Fine, if things are really so dangerous... I-I'll keep watch. Just... don't get mad at me if he comes back."

With that, the shop door swings closed as Jeremy takes a seat against the outside wall and pops open his book while looking around the deserted street.

Ryan turns to Chloe without thinking, "Thank you."

To be honest, he's not really sure what he's thankful for. Maybe it's because she took his side, or maybe it's because the manifestation of the worst case was so easily thwarted. Either way, some level of gratitude builds up inside of him and his mouth ended up moving before his mind could.

Chloe nods her head in understanding, “It's fine, you seemed off back there so I just got suspicious. I really do hope that he doesn't come back."

Ryan nods as a lull falls over the conversation. He takes the breathing room to sit down on the floor with his back against the wall. Whether he admits it or not, his knees were trembling at some point in the last half hour. It's about time he gave his reliable legs some needed rest.

Joining him on the floor, Chloe looks around the shop, "You know, I used to love going shopping with my mom or friends. I remember one time Beth found this giant bean bag chair for fifty percent off. Naturally that bargain hunter bought it despite only having a sedan to drive it home with. Anyway, we only got maybe a few feet out of the store before the bottom tore open and all the beans fell out. Alison couldn’t stop laughing while Beth was nearly moved to tears. I probably shouldn't have laughed too, but I couldn't help it.”

She smiles at the warm memory which makes Ryan share in that warmth to some odd degree. Chloe seems to have a way of doing that which he still can't explain why. Whenever she's happy, she's talkative, or even a bit sentimental, he finds himself feeling oddly relaxed with her around. At some point he started to categorize such thoughts and his conclusion always frustrated him. It's not that said categorization is wrong, or even incomplete, it's just... he isn't sure what he's supposed to do with it.

Ryan looks down at his hands, four weeks ago, they were quite smooth—soft even. Now, thick callouses have built up on his fingertips and a few stray cuts lie in hard to notice places. He really isn't the same boy who wanted to quit school.

“You alright?” Chloe asks empathetically, with a sort of considerate smirk.

Ryan snaps out of it with a shake of his head, “Yeah, I’m fine.”

Chloe rolls her eyes, "You always say that."

He glances out the window at Jeremy quietly scanning through illustrated pages at an unprecedented speed. Everyone five seconds or so, he flips a page, glances around as a sort of formality, and continues the ritual with the next two pages. Ryan honestly finds it impressive how consistent he is at staying alert while simultaneously reading faster than Ryan ever thought possible. The only immersion breaking quality is the frown that perpetually rests on his face as he intermittently takes heavy sighs. 

"Hey Chloe," Stephanie says approaching the two of them still seated by the door, "Could I get your thoughts on a few ideas."

Chloe gets up, "Absolutely, that's what I'm here for."

Stephanie nods, "Great, I can't decide if getting Michael a wooden statue of a Greek god is too much."

Chuckling, Chloe shrugs, "Probably, but you can still show me," she then turns to Ryan, "Are you coming?"

Looking over the Stephanie, he's met with an expectant smile. At first, he feels as though he has to agree, but then he gets a lingering thought that develops just enough to change his course of action.

"Actually," he starts, rising to his feet, "I'm going to go check on Jeremy... I'll meet up with you guys in a second."

Stephanie looks out the window and immediately spots Jeremy. Concern flashes across her face briefly before she takes a deep breath and nods affirmingly. It seems the inner teacher in her hasn't fully disappeared despite the circumstances. And so, as the two women go check out some random Greek god, Ryan finds his way out the front door.

Jeremy spots him immediately, glancing at him with a side eye, "What do _you_ want?"

To be frank, Ryan's not sure if he's supposed to be offended by the snide remark or just tolerant of whatever the boy is likely going through. Either way, he's proven himself to be volatile enough that the latter is likely the safer option.

"Just hanging out," he lies, rather poorly at that.

Jeremy rolls his eyes and speaks with a blatant sarcasm, "Sure." He promptly returns to his book, making sure to angle it in such a way that Ryan can't ease drop on the story.

Being an avid recluse like the boy, Ryan finds himself leaning against the wall in silence as he looks off at the street and beyond. He takes not of the propane tanks creating a wall by the gas pumps. It's probably for the best if no one finds out what lies beyond that obvious guard. His attention then meanders to some arbitrary tree branch lying on the grass below a mighty oak tree. One of the recent storms must have taken the branch down and, with no one to clean it, it just sits there—silently. Even the grass around said branch has begun to overtake the formerly maintained yard, though not by much given the cooling weather. It's such a boring detail to notice, yet the thought of an unmaintained yard fills him with some subtle degree of sorrow—as if the idea of neglect hurts him somehow. If he's not mistaken, this is actually the second time he's looked around and felt this way... how curious.

"Are you just going to stand there?" Jeremy asks indifferently, "Someone probably needs you inside."

Ryan studies the boy causally, not wanting to draw any attention to his inspection. It's a skill he picked up when dealing with Steve—the old Steve, "You think so?"

Jeremy releases yet another sigh, closes his book, and sets it on the ground beside him. He then crosses his arms and slouches against the wall behind him, "Hey Ryan?"

"Hm?"

"...never mind," the boy immediately avoid any eye contact and elects to watch a bird fly in the opposite direction of Ryan.

Ryan's hands slide into his pockets, "Not much of a talker... neither am I."

This comment seems to take Jeremy by surprise as his vow of avoidance immediately breaks, and he stares at Ryan, "Sure, whatever you say?"

"What do you mean by that?" Ryan studies him quizzically.

Jeremy straightens up a bit but remains mostly relaxed as he throws his hands up into the air, "I don't know. I just thought we'd be more... alike I guess."

"You guess," Ryan presses.

Jeremy nods, "Yeah. You probably forgot, but we were in the same English class both Sophomore and Junior year. We never talked, but I always assumed we were kinda alike... never thought we were so different."

Ryan thinks back diligently, trying to recall the exact composition of those two classes. While he couldn't remember every face distinctly, and certainly didn't know many of their names, Jeremy does come to mind in both instances. He completely forgot about those classes to be honest, he usually spent them all minding his own business and staring out the window.

"Us... alike," Ryan starts, pondering the thought, "I guess I never put much thought to it."

Jeremy rolls his eyes as if the connection was obvious, "You know. Quiet, introverted, top of our class. The only difference is that you always seemed to be so nonchalant... It bugged me sometimes."

"uh... uh," Ryan stammers, evidently not conditioned to respond to something like that, "I'm sorry... I guess."

Seemingly disgruntled, Jeremy waves his hand, "It's whatever. I know you're not doing it intentionally, so I'm probably just reading too much into it."

Speaking of reading too much into it, Ryan finds himself hanging on Jeremy's words as if he were missing something. Sure school has never been a place he socialized or even paid attention to his surroundings, but to think someone noticed him. Despite what he told himself, four years simply doesn't go by without anyone taking interest. To think it was just ordinary boy in his English class that acknowledged his existence. It's just... it's like Jeremy said: it's another thing—another thing to ponder as he wonders how far things have changed and how much he missed.

"You know," Ryan starts, finding that the conversation had all but died while he was thinking, "Maybe we were pretty similar. Did you hate Mrs. Grayson?"

Ryan chooses this point because it was fairly mainstream amongst the students and one of the few popular opinions he actually agreed with. Mrs. Grayson was the type of old lady who yelled at students for using an online thesaurus instead of a physical one. He's pretty sure everyone in the school detested the woman to some degree.

"Did we all?" Jeremy confirms, "That hag called me a pig once."

"Oof."

To Ryan's genuine astonishment, Jeremy actually smiles as he continues, "At the end of the year, she said that only four people passed her class with an A, and I'm pretty sure one of them cheated."

Ryan laughs, "Man, I believe it too."

Jeremy shrugs finding himself a bit more comfortable in his spot, "Yeah... I will give her credit where credit is due, she's still the only teacher who's ever gotten me to use a semicolon in my writing. It felt like I was some nerd trying to program in my essay."

Ryan nods. It's quite heartening to see the boy open up with such excitement. Maybe he was stretching outside his bounds, but seeing him act all lonely made Ryan feel rather uncomfortable. He just couldn't stand there and watch it anymore—it's a good thing that's no longer a concern.

Releasing a grandioso sigh, Jeremy leans his head against the wall with a content smile across his face, "As much as I didn't like being there, I do miss it sometimes. You know?"

He looks over to Ryan expectantly searching for some gesture of confirmation, but Ryan's attention has already diverted to something off in the distance. Standing in between the edge of a brick building and an old oak tree is a solitary figure. Its tattered clothes barely cling to its body as it silently trembles in place. The immediate impulse to grab his knife causes Ryan to flinch, but then he notices a striking characteristic of the figure that petrifies him all the way through: dripping directly from the zombie's eyes are two solid streams of blood. The zombie's head shakes violently as it's cold, piercing gaze stares holes into Ryan's consciousness. Then, as quickly as he could notice it, the zombie goes limp and collapses onto the ground—it rag dolls for only a moment before it fades into the stillness of the background.

"Jeremy," Ryan says, still in a petrified trance, "Head inside."

Jeremy looks up to him, suddenly irritated, "What do you mean? We're just keeping watch out here."

Ryan takes a drawn out breath, trying to regain his bearings despite what he saw, "Something's coming."

A little startled, Jeremy springs up as quickly as his heavy body will allow, "A-are you serious... O-oh okay, let's get out of here then."

By the time Ryan notices the first sign of moment from the same corner of the brick building, he's already drawn his knife and pistol. He turns to Jeremy knowing full well that he doesn't have time to lay out a full plan—if he even could make one up—so he elects to keep his command brief, "Get inside."

Jeremy moves to rebuttal whether out of pride or a sense of personal agency, but then he catches wind of the five figures appearing down the street and his face fills with horror. The monsters presenting themselves are not simple zombies—they were mutants. Each of the five figures lack traditional human arms and instead have long blade-like appendages. Their visages have a permanent expression of rage as their former faces have long since been gnarled into an amalgamation of eyes, teeth, and pure agony. All of these mutants possess these basic, terrifying features, but one in particular is a bit more plump than the rest which only adds to the horror as it begins to bolt with the same ferocity as the lighter ones. 

"What the hell!" Jeremy exclaims with a rapid breath as he steps back into the wall, "What are those?!"

Ryan steps forward, seizes Jeremy's shoulder and shoves him towards the front door, "Get everyone through a back exit."

Jeremy's eyes, filled to the brim with fear, hesitate to make contact with Ryan's as he watches the mutants get closer and closer. Then, shaking his head, he reaches for his book, gives Ryan one more concerning look, and dashes into the thrift store. With that, Ryan is alone. With the battlefield clear, Ryan aims his pistol at the first mutant. He can feel his heart racing as if to rush right out of his chest if he's not careful, but looming above that is the overwhelming desire buy his friends just enough time to escape—he's confident he can find his way back on his own.

Forcing a steady breath, he fires off a quick burst of three rounds—a technique Drew showed him. This small burst works wonders on the first zombie as all three hit in a straight line leading from the chest to the head with the final shot entering through an eye and out the back of the mangled visage. He proceeds to try the same technique on the next mutant but results in much more lackluster results. Instead of killing or incapacitating the mutant, all but one bullets miss the mutant completely as it flails its arms wildly to either side of itself. With not more time left to spare, Ryan steps back into the thrift store and slams the door closed behind him.

Less than a second later, undead flesh slams into the mostly glass door with multiple intense cracks stretching through its brittle surface. The next mutant charges into the first with its arm slamming into the glass, piercing straight through, and narrowly impaling Ryan's head. This near-death experience is more than enough for him to stumble backwards and only remain on his feet thanks to a conveniently placed table. He focuses back on the mutants just in time for the third to add itself to the pile and shatters the door. The entire pile lurches forward and falls into the building.

Trying to act swiftly, Ryan fires a bullet at one of their heads but misses due to his inconveniently shaky grip. He tries again and manages to hit one in the shoulder. 

"What's going on?!" someone deeper inside the building shouts.

"Run to the back!" Jeremy's voice demands.

The fifth and only upright mutant, crawls over the pile of squirming flesh and dashes straight at Ryan. This last zombie is a bit chunkier than the others, and while agile and fast, is a bit slower than it's thinner counterparts. Masses of flesh and bodily fluids cover the zombie’s body making it look more like a mass of tissue rather than an actual former human. As such, it lumbers towards him, beginning to ungulate in multiple regions of his wide frame. Its body begins to swell in a pulsing motion and expands from a heavier sort to a downright obese monstrosity with deadly arms. Completely uncertain of what's to come, Ryan vaults over the table behind him trying to gain some distance between himself and the strange mutant. What comes next is not something he could have ever expected.

In one quick bang and flash, the zombie explodes sending shards of bone and guts in every direction. Between a piece of bone skimming his bicep, intestinal matter slapping against his ear, and the sheer shockwave, Ryan finds himself on the ground with his ears ringing. The world seems to fuzz out as all of his senses betray him and create a strange sensation of an indecipherable illusion. The ground in front of him is blurred and a roaring pain in his ears are only able to hear a distance echo of his thoughts amidst a deafening ringing. Knowing that he can't just lie here, he pushes himself up relying solely on muscle memory to coordinate his efforts as even his sense of touch is mismanaged and largely unreliable. With every ounce of determination he can muster, he finds himself back on his feet as his vision begins to clear at what is before him.

Sending a chill down his spine, he finds a bloodbath of zombie guts splattered around a small crater shell in the floor. Circling that crater, each staring directly at him, are the other three mutants. Each mutant stands completely still as it glares down on Ryan with what he can only describe as evil incarnate.

His heart freezes as he stumbles backwards in terror. The three statue-like mutants done even budge as the one in the middle begins to convulse in place. It's body bends over backwards as its legs tremble, its arms jerk from side to side, and it's face screams a silent cry of agony. Only after a solid ten seconds of brutal convulsion does the mutant stop and stand up straight. The mutant then glares at Ryan with different sort of expression than the other two—one not of volatile rage, but of a striking level of focus. The mutant's gaze is... deliberate.

"You are... still..." its voice is hoarse and low, closer to a growl than an actually coherent statement, "alive."

Ryan raises his gun hand and finds it empty, he can't locate it on the floor either. He must have dropped it during the explosion. In light of this, he readies his knife and prepares for the worst. If things play out like they did last time he met a speaking zombie, this isn't going to be a clean escape. He could try to run, but there's no telling how much time his friends need to properly evacuate. Not only that, but he can't quite feel his legs like normal which makes him hesitant to use them so readily. If he can just use this speaking zombie to buy himself enough time to mentally regroup, he might be able to think of something.

Bloody tears begin to well up in the mutant's offset eyes as its malformed mouth continues to speak, "You join... me..." it coughs up a mixture of blood and phlegm, "no more... I will... save... you."

Ryan takes a step back, the feeling in his legs has since returned, though he's still a bit dizzy and hearing is only moderately functional. He instinctively tries to deduce what the zombie could mean, but then his thoughts get lost in his disorientation and he's back to the present sooner than usual.

A greenish, red blood begins to drip out of the mutant's eyes as each subsequent word spits a sparse cone of blood, "You must... stop..." it struggles with the last and truly haunting word, "Living."

With that, the mutant collapses into the bloody crater as the two remaining mutants charge forward with their arm blades chambered for a lethal strike. 

Immediately, Ryan dives over a couch next to him and promptly pushes himself to his feet. The closer mutant dashes around the couch and swings its arm blade towards his head. As quickly as he can, Ryan thrusts his blade upwards intercepting the arm only to cut clean through it. He proceeds to shove the mutant backwards and duck right as the other mutant attempt to dive over the couch at him. Said couch reveals itself to be excellent cover as the mutant flies right overtop of him and lands on the ground—hard. This buys Ryan just enough time to evade out of the first mutant's way as it swings its remaining arm wildly. He then elects to jump back over the couch to put additional distance between himself and the two mutants. 

The first mutant, which never lost its footing, climbs over the obstacle and swings its arm. Just as before, Ryan intercepts the strike at the elbow with his blade and cuts off the final lethal limb. He nearly over-swings as the knife cuts the limb off a little too clean and his momentum caries his arm high up into the air. Thankfully, he's practiced swiping it enough times that he's able to bring it back down loosing his coordination. Lastly, he draws back and cuts halfway through the mutant's mangled head as it falls forward. 

As the mutant falls, it opens up the opportunity for the second mutant to dive back over the couch—this time Ryan can't dodge fully. He manages to evade the arms enough to avoid a serious puncture, but the mutant does scrap his shoulder as its body falls onto his. His arms reflectively draw to himself to brace for impact, but this has the unintended side effect of slamming the metal grip into his diaphragm. He gasps as any air in his lungs suddenly exits, and the impacted muscle begins to spasm. Out of desperation, he shoves the mutant body off of him before it can decapitate him, bite him, or whatever else. The body flops off of him after considerable effort, yet without resistance. When he's eventually separated from the mutant, he discovers why. 

On the mutant carcass is a rather wide wound on its upper chest. The wound, the exact shape and proportions of his knife, extends all the way through the creature until an exit point can be spotted on the other side. To be frank, he's not sure why the thing went limp so quickly, but he'd be stupid to complain about it. Then he looks over the monsters monstrous visage and finds its two offset eyes glaring at him as the mouth opens and closes slowly. Ryan immediately pushes it away and slides back only for the mutant's mouth to click its teeth together as a blind rage fills its gaze. It continues to gnaw at air for a few moments before the mouth slowly stops swinging, and the eyes glaze over. The mutant goes completely still.

Ryan wheezes as the adrenaline begins to subside and the sheer magnitude of the previous impact begins to take the forefront of his mind. He tries to inhale, but it doesn't work, he tries again and only succeeds to a very moderate degree. Leaning over on his side, he tries in vain to get a full breath in as his diaphragm aggressively resists a coordinated effort. He ends up lying on the ground, partially deaf, a bit blurred, and hard of breathing for a solid minute before he's able to think clearly again. When he does, he notices the butt of his pistol hiding under the edge of the couch. He carefully grabs it and stands up.

He looks to the scene before him and finds yet another disheartening sight. The blast crater, the haphazard painting of mutant blood, and three mutant corpses lying on the ground. It feels as though—no matter what he does—he never gets away from things like this. 

A stinging sensation arises on his shoulder and he finds the most peculiar situation. His black, leather-like jacket is completely untouched yet underneath, blood starts seeping into his shirt from a shallow wound. As crazy as the undamaged jacket seems, he promptly pulls it off and inspects the wound. To his relief, it only looks like a graze so he doesn't feel the need to employ any first aid skills. First aid... he actually knows how to treat a wound thanks to Steve...

He shakes his head, disregarding the thought, and starting off towards the back door. The job's not over until he ensures everyone made it out safe and sound.

Stumbling to the back door, he finds no sign of his friends which actually comes as a huge relief. It takes him a moment to navigate through an aisle of handmade statues and furnishings but he gets to the back exit in due time. As he pushes it open, he created with the blinding light of the sun, and the disturbing sight of Jeremy on the ground. The boy is hardly unconscious but his left eyebrow is swollen and blood drips out of his nose. His arms lay weakly at his side as he slouches against the side of the building.

"What happened?!" Ryan exclaims looking around for any sign of the women.

Jeremy looks over at him slowly, his eyes welling up with tears—only some sort of masculine pride holds them back.

"How pathetic can I be?" he tries as he sucks in a deep breath, "I—I just stood there."

"Jeremy, what happened!" Ryan reiterates, searching again for any indication of what happened but finding only a small trace of blood on the pavement right in front of Jeremy—as subtle smear of red on his cheek tells Ryan all he needs to know.

The boy hangs his head, a few drops of blood dripping out of his nose and onto his shirt, "I couldn't do anything."

Ryan drops to a knee and ceases the boys shoulder, forcing him to look up, "What. Happened?"

Jeremy shakes his head as his lips quiver, "We just left the building and then some gang appeared out of nowhere and took everyone. They... they didn't want me..." Jeremy immediately covers his ears and squeezes his eyes shut, "My mom tried to scream through the gag... but... she..."

Searching the scene once more, Ryan stops looking for a struggle and starts looking for an escape route. To his dismay, he finds countless escape routes as the back of the building opens up into a parking lot with no fencing. They could be anywhere—in any direction.

"Where... where do you think they went?" Ryan says, suddenly realizing his own disbelief.

Jeremy shakes his head in his hands as he sniffles up a dubious mixture of snot and blood. He immediately coughs afterwards, probably from multiple causes as he nurses his side, "I don't know."

Ryan shakes the boy rather forcefully, "Come on! What'd you see? How many people? What'd they look like? There's... there's got to be some way to track them down."

Whether it be the stress of it all or maybe something far deeper, Jeremy finally snaps and pushes Ryan off of him, "Shut up! Don't you think I know that?!"

In light of the outburst, Ryan backs off as the boy continues.

"Don't you think I would have tried to figure out who they were!? Don't you think I wanted to help—to be a hero!", Jeremy rises to his feet slowly and turns his back towards Ryan, "It didn't matter. They didn't tell me anything and they sure as hell didn't pull their punches. What was I even supposed to do?"

Ryan struggles to find words, not just emotionally, but logistically too—that's the part that scared him the most. What were they to do? When Karmen got taken, at they knew where she was. But this? Who knows how many thugs there and now they're supposed to track down a specific bunch of them... track down? Well it's not much of a plan, and it kills his pride to admit it, but there's only one person would could help them at this point.
