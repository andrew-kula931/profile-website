The baseball stadium is a bit further away than Ryan anticipated which leads to a rather annoying game of cat and mouse between him and the lunatic driver behind him. His motorcycle is unmatched at accelerating really fast which allows him to lightly shake Jeremy off after every turn seeing as the van can hardly keep up. That being said, Jeremy is anything but afraid of slamming on the gas given the opportunity and takes turns that would make a lesser driver queasy.  This recklessness, paired with the fact that Ryan isn't comfortable taking fast turns, allows Jeremy to keep up with him for the most part. After a few minutes of this dangerous game of chase, they arrive at the stadium, exactly where Ryan remembered it... to think Steve's house is just around the corner.

Ryan parks the motorcycle in the parking lot beside a striking number of other vehicles. Jeremy swiftly rolls into the spot next to him, nearly crashing into a light pole while doing it. They both dismount their vehicles and find themselves standing just outside the massive stadium—the echoes of voices can be heard inside.

"Why are we here?" Jeremy asks, looking up at the massive building which is easily five stories tall, if not more.

He shoots the boy a side eye, still a bit annoyed that he came along, "I'm here to meet somebody, just... don't do anything stupid."

Jeremy shrugs with a roll of his eyes, "Spare me, I'm not a child. We're here to get our people back."

With reluctance, Ryan presses forward through an unlocked side gate. He doesn't know if people are going to try and check him for a ticket, but he doesn't plan on drawing any attention to himself regardless. The two of them carefully head inside as the plethora of voices only continue to rise in volume.

To be honest, Ryan has never actually been inside a sports stadium, ring, facility, or any kind of place. He went onto the high school football field once for PE but beyond that, this is his first time every visiting such a popular, normal place. That being said, he never expected the massive stadium to have such a spacious interior with plenty of room for posters, signs, closed concession stands, and a bunch of other things that he isn't quite certain of their purpose. The absurdity doesn't stop there, however, as Ryan quickly finds additional facilities outdoors and that's before even getting to the stands. 

Speaking of the stands, when Jeremy and him pass through the interior area, they find a wide, half circle of stands surrounding perfectly green grass. Within the stands, in sparse clusters, are a multitude of ordinary looking people. The stadium as a whole is probably only at five or ten percent capacity, yet it feels like a massive crowd for the apocalypse. Well, on second thought, they're not quite ordinary looking as he hones in one a few and finds tattoos on practically every single person. Not only that, but half of them have a cigarette between their fingers which makes the entire stadium smell quite rancid. He's not one to judge, but they don't seem like welcoming families and groups of adult friends.

"This place stinks," Jeremy complains which draws a dirty look from a goth girl in the back row.

"I think it'd be best if we both stay quiet," Ryan comments quietly, so only Jeremy can hear him.

"Why?" Jeremy argues, a bit quieter but still too loud for comfort, "For all we know any one of these people could be the part of the culprits. They might know where my mom is."

Ryan shakes his head, "But getting on their bad side won't get us anywhere."

He watches as a few people meander behind them with unrelated conversation. One such group starts heading roughly in their direction, though they are standing pretty close to the stairs. Ryan respectfully pushes Jeremy to the side so they can get by but then a familiar face flashes past. At first, he thinks he's seeing things, but then the man stops briefly in front of him with his head down.

"Sit in the front row," he says softly, practically in Ryan's ear. The man's breath sends a chill down his spine despite no cigarette stench reeking out from it.

Ryan remains frozen and silent as the group of suspicious looking people descend the stairs.

Evidently Ryan must be displaying some level of shock on his face because Jeremy takes notice of it and inspects the bunch that just passed by. He looks at each one of them but doesn't give any indication of familiarity, that is, until he spots the one person he does recognize, "Wait a minute, isn't that—"

To prevent the boy from saying a potentially dangerous name, Ryan holds out his hand which thankfully cuts the sentence short. 

There's no mistaking it, that was Drew's informant. The coincidences have stacked up just enough that it only makes sense. Sure he still has his doubts, in fact, he has serious doubts, but the chances of it not being the case are quickly waning. Of all the people that it could possibly be, whey couldn't it have been someone he's never met—a mysterious shadow that's been watching the city from behind the scenes. This 'informant' is anything but a shadow, however. After all, under-the-radar informants aren't well known serial killers.

"We're sitting in the front row," Ryan proclaims as he starts walking down the stairs, completely indifferent to whether Jeremy follows. Luckily, he does.

He takes a spot as close to the grass as possible, exactly as he was told and promptly takes a look around. Despite just crossing paths, he can't spot the informant anywhere. Not in the clusters of people behind him, and not anywhere further across the stadium. It's as if he vanished out of thin air, or at least moseyed his way into someplace he probably shouldn't be.

This stale search does raise his curiosity to look around for the dragon tattoo guy and his slut side piece. It's a rather vulgar way of describing it, but those thugs who attacked him back in the car dealership are likely here. Trying not to stare, he glosses over every single clump trying to spot them but doesn't find a single familiar face. He can't even locate any of the other random thugs that aided that little scuffle.

"What is everyone doing here?" Jeremy asks looking around at the crowd like a conspicuous child.

"I don't know," Ryan answers honestly, "I just know that we have to be here."

Jeremy looks back, "Do you know when this 'game' is going to start?"

Ryan shakes his head.

Relieving some boredom, Jeremy leans back in his spot and stretches his arms across the seats behind him, "You know, it's been a hot second since I've been here."

Seeing as he has nothing better to do, Ryan begrudgingly partakes in the small talk, "Oh yeah, you've been here before?"

Jeremy looks at him incredulously, "Have you seriously never been here before?"

Once again, Ryan chooses honesty by shaking his head.

Shock hits Jeremy's face, "Wow, everyone I know's been here. Heck, we came here on a fifth grade field trip, do you not remember that?"

"I wasn't in this school district in fifth grade," Ryan admits.

Jeremy sighs, clearly accepting that answer, "I guess that makes sense. You missed out though. I'm not even into baseball and I have to admit the games here were electric."

"Is that so?"

With a bit of excitement, Jeremy nods his head and leans forward. He then outstretches his arm and points at a powered off billboard-looking thing hanging above the stands. The charcoal colored screen is only made darker by the setting sun and shadows creeping up on it, "That screen over there usually plays hilarious ads in between innings. My mom would always complain about how silly they were, but that only made it more funny," he shifts his finger towards home plate on the field, "Oh, and the batters always tap their bats on home plate before swinging. Apparently it's a thing or something here. It never made sense to me, but everyone did it and the crowd would complain if a batter forgot."

"Wow," Ryan says, not really sure how he's supposed to respond to that.

Jeremy shrugs, "Those are just some... cool things to know I guess. Not that it's important."

Looking over, Ryan expects to see Jeremy performatively looking nonchalant. What he finds isn't far off from the assumption, but something catches his eye that shatters that narrative. While he was speaking and even now, Jeremy's hands won't stop shaking subtly. 

"We're going to get them back," Ryan consoles, feeling a more assertive than usual, "I won't leave without them."

Jeremy sighs and takes interest in something on the other side of the stadium, "I'm not—I'm not worried. I'm just feeling a bit nostalgic is all. After all, you said that getting on everyone's bad side won't help us, so why dwell on it."

Ryan can't put his finger on it, but it doesn't seem like the conversation has been fully resolved. Normally he'd be content just leaving it there, but something deep inside eggs him on—something doesn't want him to leave it on that note.

"You know, my foster father liked baseball too," he says, "He used to turn the volume up way too loud on the TV whenever his team was playing. He'd scream his lungs out whenever his favorite player hit a home run."

Turning back to him, Jeremy takes on a much more collected demeanor—far less performative, "Is that right... wait, you were a foster child?"

Waving his hand dismissively, Ryan looks down, "Yeah, but it's all in the past now."

"Shoot," Jeremy says a bit more empathetically, "I didn't realize... but hey, I didn't have a father."

Completely out of nowhere, Ryan laughs. His reaction is brief and the energy dissipates as spontaneously as it arose, but laughed nonetheless, "Neither did I... well until I did."

Feeling that he may have said too much, Ryan averts his gaze and shifts uncomfortably in his seat. It's not like him to say something like that. What could be going through his head? Perhaps it's just nerves after encountering Jimmy again... yeah, that's probably it. Hopefully Jeremy doesn't get offended by his awkwardness.

When he finally looks back, he finds Jeremy casually looking at the field. Without moving his head he begins to speak slowly, "I get it man. It sucks that I get it... I didn't want a mother... until I did," he looks back to Ryan a bit alarmed, "Sorry, that must have sounded super mushy."

Ryan shakes his head respectfully, "Nah, you're good."

Jeremy lets out a sigh of relief, "You know what Ryan, you're easier to talk to than I expected."

"You think so?"

Jeremy nods as he rubs the back of his head rather timidly, "Yeah... and... sorry about being so forward and demanding earlier. I just... I wasn't gonna sit by and do nothing again. I've stayed behind just to play video games far too many times... it's about time I actually, as my mom always says, 'get off my ass'... It's funny really, that phrase used to piss me off to no end... weird,".

Ryan can't help but notice Jeremy's chin quivering just slightly as he finishes off his comment. He intentionally acts as though he doesn't notice. It seems there's more to Jeremy than he initially let on... it reminds him of someone else whom he's misunderstood. The connection makes his own chin quiver, though briefly enough that no one notices—no one.

Feeling that he might be losing focus, he takes another look around him, only partially to avoid eye contact. That's about the time that someone beings walking onto the field. Ryan doesn't catch where he came from, but he does notice two very important details: one of the man's hands is stained with blood, and the man in question is none other than Jimmy Lashing himself. He proceeds to the center of the field and turns around to look over the sparse crowd.

"Wait a minute," Jeremy starts with a serious concerned look, "Henry something? The serial killer?"

Ryan offers a grim expression to confirm the boy's suspicions, "Yeah."

"Ladies and gentlemen, boys and girls," Jimmy starts with a flamboyant tone and gestures—a demeanor Ryan is unfortunately all too familiar with, "I'm so glad you all came here tonight in these trying times. Truthfully, I had hoped that there would be more of you, but beggars can't be choosers."

"I don't like this," Ryan comments assessing what his escape routes are. 

First and foremost is the way he came in, but that is at the top of the stairs and through three different groups of people who he's not certain are even friendly. He could jump over the guard rail and run across the field, but that would put him in prime position to get directly involved with Jimmy, perhaps that is the intended route given that Jimmy is likely the informant, but he still detests that idea all together. The last option is to run along the bottom of the stands and escape via a side gate, which might not be a terrible idea, but that side gate is almost certainly locked so he'll have to climb a ten-foot tall chain link fence.

Jimmy continues with a showman-like presentation as the crowd begins to grow restless, "I'm sure most of you are wondering about where the food, the weapons, and/or the information that you were promised. Rest assured that I will be getting to that very shortly. Please allow me to finish off the introductions, and we shall get to the main event shortly."

Despite the simplicity of it, this explanation seems to calm any outstanding worries in the crowd. Some people appear dissatisfied seeing as their patience will be tested further, but everyone takes a seat and the side chatter dies down to near nothingness.

"Jeremy," Ryan whispers, "If things—"

"—go south, run. Yeah, I was thinking the same thing. Heck, I may not look it, but I've got some speed if the need be." Jeremy promptly smiles, whether to indicate that he was joking or to show off some unexpected pride. Regardless of the truthiness of his statement, Ryan decides to let Jeremy handle himself for the time being.

"Now, for those of you who don't know, this... unfortunate circumstance began one month ago. Many of you came directly from years of incarceration, so you likely didn't expect to find a broken world waiting for you," some unrest breaks out while Jimmy speaks, "But!" the crowd simmers down briefly, "I am so happy to announce that this burden stall be elevated from all of your tired shoulders. No longer will any of you have to fear horrific monsters breaking in while you sleep, or battling for food like animals, no. All of those concerns stall be resolved, hence why I promised many of you such extravagant things. I have found a solution to all of your problems!"

Jimmy's words perk the interest of the crowd, so much so, that some people actually start cheering when the speech reaches a natural pause. Of course, many people were skeptical and kept quiet, but more than a few were already starting to buy into the deception. Perhaps, deception might be a little harsh, but Ryan doesn't trust Jimmy for a second, even if he's working with Drew. Then again, he doesn't trust Drew either.

"Get to the good part!" someone in the crowd eggs on.

Jimmy releases a little laugh as he steps back onto the pitcher's mound, "First, I must introduce you all to someone. We have become quick friends as of late and only plan on growing closer in the future. We are truly kin at heart."

Ryan gets a foreboding feeling as he shifts uneasily in his seat. He looks at Jeremy and finds that he too, doesn't seem comfortable in the slightest.

"Many of you don't know this, but the outbreak originated from the public high school, not too far from here. My friend was there that day and single-handedly saved dozens of students from being slaughtered. He proceeded to escape himself and battle a multitude of mutants, each with valor and poise. His successes are truly, truly a sight to behold. Now without further ado, please welcome to the field: Ryan Lancer!"

Ryan's blood runs cold as Jimmy gestures directly towards him. If only he could fain ignorance and blend into the crowd, but the front row is not only the closest to the field, but no other group of people sit in their immediate vicinity. As all eyes turn to him, and some people instinctively start clapping, it becomes apparent that he either needs to acknowledge the attention or tell Jeremy to do it. Though, he isn't going to do the latter.

"Come on down!" Jimmy encourages, "Don't be shy, no one can receive anything until you get down here."

Ryan looks around, his face growing red and his heart racing. Jeremy seems to mimic this confusion as he looks around at all the eyes directed in his direction.

If there was a time to run, now would be it. Though, for whatever reason, he finds himself taking a deep breath and reaching for the railing in front of him. Right before he goes, he turns back to Jeremy.

"If things go south, don't wait for me," he explains.

Jeremy shakes his head, "No, I won't leave you behind... but I won't be stupid."

The comment catches Ryan off guard, but he pushes himself over the railing and falls to the fake grass before anyone can notice his hesitation. No one has ever said that to him before yet now is not the time to get sentimental.

He begrudgingly walks out onto the field and towards the pitcher's mound. Jimmy stands patiently with a smirk across his face as he holds his hands behind his back. Only when Ryan is a few feet away from the man does he stop and look back at the crowd all staring down at him. Right as he does, the stadium lights snap on, and the stadium is filled with bright light that temporarily blinds him. When he finally does get his vision back, Jimmy is standing right beside him.

"Play along for a moment," Jimmy whispers into his ear "you'll understand very soon."

Jimmy then steps aside and addresses the crowd who's clapping ceased with the entrance of the blinding lights, "Ryan is truly amazing in countless ways. He has grit, perseverance, dare I say empathy. In fact, I'm sure many of you know someone like him."

His heart pounds in his chest, but not wanting to argue with him in front of a crowd, he forces himself to nod as if agreeing with the blasphemy he's hearing. In the far background he hears a strange noise that he's not familiar with. It's almost like some distant rumbling of an engine.

Jimmy steps closer to Ryan and lays a hand on his opposite shoulder. This position makes Ryan highly uncomfortable, so he carefully reaches into his jacket and grips his knife. He's sure to keep it concealed, but if things go south, he can and will finish this serial killer off once and for all.

"Like—not many—all of you, Ryan has been wronged. Some of you have been falsely accused of crimes you didn't commit, some have been unjustly mistreated by society, still others never had a chance in the first place."

Ryan notices Jeremy nodding as if buying into the speech. He wouldn't call it ignorance, but he's beginning to become involved in this speech, more so than he probably should.

Jimmy looks at Ryan briefly before looking back to the crowd, "Ryan was wronged a very long time ago by two unfortunate parents. That wrong quickly lead to his one and only character flaw..." he allows silence as the crowd actually quiets down to hear everything that's wrong with some random kid. In the midst of the silence, the crowd begins to take notice of the low engine rumbling which is growing louder with each passing second, "he's..." Jimmy struggles to get the words out, whether that be part of the performance or otherwise, the stadium begins to shake, "...he's still human."

Suddenly, Jimmy slides his arm around Ryan's neck and pulls him into a headlock with an inhuman amount of strength. He tries to draw his knife, but Jimmy uses his other hand to strike the back of Ryan's elbow which causes him to lose grasp of his knife as his arm writhes in a temporary agony. As the knife hits the grass below, Ryan looks up and discovers what this was really all about.

From the stadium doors—the main one—the side ones—all of them—zombies begin to flood in. At first, many of the people in the crowd jump to react in a defensive manner... but the tsunami doesn't stop, the horde keeps flooding in. What follows are screams—many of them.

For whatever reason, Jimmy isn't actively choking Ryan which allows him to reach for his gun and attempt to use that. Jimmy seemingly disagrees with this idea as he grabs the barrel of the gun and tugs at it. Ryan is able to hold on for a brief moment, but then Jimmy's overwhelming strength comes in and the weapon is forcibly torn from his hand. Jimmy then does something with the pistol that Ryan can't see before waving the same hand—now empty—at the stadium before them.

"Watch it Ryan!" Jimmy laughs in the at same maniacal tone that he's always been concealing, "Is it not beautiful. The freedom that they are receiving."

After each disarm attempt, Jimmy immediately lets go of Ryan's other hand. This time, Ryan raises his arm and attempts to slam his elbow into the man. Jimmy, however, intercepts it halfway and stops the momentum altogether.

"You're missing it!" Jimmy demands, "Do you not see you friend?"

Ryan's head snaps up to find Jeremy actively being grappled by a zombie. His face full of fear as he takes a last second effort to shove the zombie over the front railing. The zombie falls off allowing Jeremy to stand up and start running for the side door Ryan spotted earlier.

"Let go of me!" Ryan demands, thrashing his body around to no avail. 

"Just wait," Jimmy says with a excited smile, "We're almost there."

As Jeremy dashes for the side door, Ryan notices the rest of the scene unfolding. Dozens of people offer futile resistances against the horde of zombies. One guy slams a knife into a zombie's head only for a second to jump on him from a few row up and tackle him. The man's head smashes into the concrete a few rows down—he likely doesn't even feel the zombie tear his throat out. In another section, two women stand back to back as they fire off two pistol in opposite directions. They accuracy is quite good, but the bodies don't go down as quickly as they fire which leads to three zombies diving forward and tackling one of the women to the ground while dragging the other with. Both women scream as a mob piles up on top of them within a single second. 

Ryan's entire body freezes as he realizes that not everyone is fighting, some people are dropping everything and running. A group of four people make it to the front of the stadium and try climbing down, heck, one of them even beats the flood of zombies and falls to the turf while his friends scream as teeth sink into their shoulders or arms. This one, lone guy glances up in horror before bee-lining it in the other direction—tears beginning to run down his face as he doesn't look back. This solitary man seems to be on his way to escaping the horror and Ryan can't help but root for him. Maybe just one person can escape... then Jimmy lifts his arm.

*Bang*

The man collapses face first into the turf as Jimmy redirects Ryan's pistol towards Jeremy, "Look!"

Ryan turns back, still squirming pathetically. What he finds is Jeremy tugging at the side gate and finding that it won't open. The chain link fence was supposed to be a climbed exit, or at least that's what Ryan planned on, but Jeremy doesn't have the physique to get over that obstacle in any timely manner. So, with sheer horror on his face, he turns around and nearly falls over as someone appears behind him. Luckily, it's only the goth girl from before who immediately pushes past him and starts climbing the gate. 

Jeremy looks around, then to the girl, and finally to Ryan. A dangerous amount of hesitation strikes him as his mouth opens but no words come out.

"Get out of here!" Ryan screams, waving his hand to try and signal for the boy to follow the girl's example.

With shaking arms, Jeremy turns to the woman and says something Ryan can't quite hear. The woman slips and falls most of the way down the fence before looking back and nodding her head. Then, with far more strength than Ryan ever knew he had, Jeremy grabs the woman's hips and tosses her up the fence like a beanbag. This effort allows the woman to get over and hop down on the other side. Then, Jeremy grabs onto the gate and yells something to her while pointing at the locking mechanism. The woman, while hyperventilating, looks at the locking mechanism and then at the zombies quickly approaching from Jeremy's flank. She stammers something which causes Jeremy to scream once more. Then the woman turns tail and dashes away. It seems as though she might actually make it out—

*Bang*

Jimmy sighs as he lowers the pistol without a word, his eyes still focused on Jeremy.

Ryan's friend screams something unintelligible at the top of his lungs as he slams his fists into the chain link fence. Only when his scream subsides does he turn around just in time to catch a zombie from sinking its teeth into his shoulder. His hands get to a stable position but so too do the zombie's creating a mutual grapple. Jeremy screams as an attempt to throw the zombie over the railing fails and nearly gets his neck opened up.

"Ah!" Ryan shouts, putting all his strength into one last attempt to ripping the man's arm off of him. To his surprise, the arm actually gives and he stumbles over as the he gets turned around in the processes.

"Ryan," Jimmy says, far too calm for a man witnessing a massacre, "You can still save you friend."

Ryan looks up to find Jimmy pulling the magazine out of the pistol, inspecting the final bullet left in the chamber, and tossing what remains onto his lap.

"You got one shot," Jimmy explains, "You can grant him relief before it's too late."

"What the hell!" Ryan exclaims as he fumbles his hand onto the weapon before pointing the gun at Jimmy—the serial killer only smiles smugly.

In desperation, Jeremy screams Ryan's name as his arms shake, on the verge of giving out. He tries throwing the zombie again, but it fails miserably and Jeremy ends up on his knee with the zombie saliva dripping onto his face. The boy grits his teeth the weight of the zombie is too much to bare.

Jimmy. The magazine. But he could dodge. The zombie? Are there more? He could. He could. Jeremy? No. No. He could miss.

"Damn it!" Ryan cries.

He can't think. He has to. Now. He has to. Move. Move!

On top of all of this, he finds Jimmy staring at him with the most gut-wrenching, terror-inducing, malicious grin one could ever possibly conceive. To penetrating eyes pierce his heart as they seemingly glow a fain tint of red. This is not the face of mere man—it's the visage of a monster. It's... hopeless... 

Then his hand lays down on the back of his knife. A plan doesn't formulate—his body reacts.

He fires the last chambered bullet at Jimmy and proceeds to chuck the empty shell of a gun right at the monster's head as he ducks to the left. The weapon makes contact perfectly and Jimmy stumbles backwards. Ryan then grabs his knife and sprints off towards Jeremy at a speed he's never reached before. 

"Jeremy!!" he clears the air in his lungs hoping that maybe, just maybe, his words will motivate his friend to hold out for just a measly second longer. 

Fighting with everything he's got just to live for mere moments, Jeremy lets out a muffled battle cry and falls backwards. Only then does Ryan reach the edge of the field, jumps up, climbs over and falls into a slash the severs the zombie's head from its body. He quickly stumbles to his feet and tugs the zombie corpse off his friend and prays that he made it in time. When the corpse finally rolls off, Jeremy pushes the decapitated head off of him and wipes the copious amount of blood off his face. He sits up slowly as Ryan notices a clear gash on his lower neck. Zombie blood covers the scene while additional blood slowly drains out. The boy's face scrunches together as the pain finally sets in.

"Ah," Jeremy cries out as he sits up slowly with a bloody hand on his neck, "T-this can't be—"

Ryan doesn't let him finish as he grabs the boy's arm and starts lifting him up. Unfortunately, he's not strong enough to lift the boy's heavy body but he does tug just enough to get him to understand the directive. He points to the fence, "Climb over! I'll buy you some time."

Jeremy's lip quivers as he stares up at Ryan in disbelief, then his eyes look further ahead and the horror returns. Ryan, however, doesn't care for a second and tugs even harder until the boy stands up on his own. Then, as quickly as he can, he shoves Jeremy towards the chain-linked gate and flips around with his knife readied.

As he expected, the first zombie is nearly on top of him by the time he catches sight of it. So, with no time to properly react, he thrusts his blade towards it haphazardly and is delighted to watch it slice through an arm and then impale the chest. This action doesn't kill the zombie right away, however, so Ryan promptly shoves it over the front railing and watches it fall onto another zombie already down there. He then hears a near clanking above him which he quickly finds to be a short zombie running down the stands—two more behind it.

With half a second to spare, Ryan looks over and finds Jeremy struggling to reach the top of the gate. At present he's only slightly beyond halfway up the twelve foot climb which means Ryan will have to ward the zombies off for at least another few seconds. 

In light of the zombies charging down the stands at him, he preps by bending his knees and lowering his center of gravity. Then, right as the first zombie dives off the third row of stands, he evades to the side and lets it fly past him onto the field. The second zombie—possessing the body of a former athlete—doesn't make as costly of a mistake and drops onto the bottom row of stands before snarling loudly and making it's attack. Ryan jumps backwards to avoid the third zombie scratching at him only to hit his back against the gate. This blockage allows the athletic zombie to dash forward and grab onto his shoulders. Everything would have ended there, had Ryan not gotten a foot up and kicked the zombie backwards before drawing back and stabbing the infinitely sharp blade right through the zombie's skull with ease. Finally, he pushes it back and slices the leg of the third zombie about the make another scratch attempt at him with an oversized claw for a hand. The third zombie falls to its side allowing Ryan to finish it off without a second thought.

He doesn't even need to look around to know that he's a mere second and a half away from being overtaken by the flood of zombies. Two or three he can deal with, but a stampede of two dozen zombies is enough that it'd overwhelm anyone. So, without further deliberation, he pushes his knife into its sheath and throws himself up the gate finding Jeremy carefully crawling over the top. Ryan's affinity for climbing is nothing more than a wet towel being thrown at a wall, but that doesn't stop him from using all his strength to scramble up the metal links. Right as he reaches the top, a wall of zombies slam into the base of the gate and shake it fiercely. This shaking is enough to throw him off and land right on top of Jeremy who cries out in both pain and startlement. 

Getting up as quickly as he can, Ryan notices the gate wobbling violently and tugs at Jeremy to get back up. The boy groans as he rubs a fresh bruise on his face to compliment all the blood but ultimately complies as they scramble towards the open exit. 

Jeremy follows as quickly as he can to keep up with Ryan as they make their way back to the parking lot on the other side of the stadium. Ryan had prepared to encounter a few stragglers by the vehicles but is pleasantly surprised to find the parking lot completely devoid of threats. With no zombies tailing them just yet, he feels as though they might be home free. That is, until Jeremy collapses. 

Ryan pivots and rushes back to him as the boy lands on his hands and knees with sweat dripping through the blood and tears. He grits his teeth as his fists clench and the water works struggle to be resisted.

"I'm going to die!" Jeremy yells as he tries to get up but his leg gives out and he falls over again.

Ryan knees down and grabs the boy's arm, intending to support him just enough to walk to his mother's van just a few yards away. Jeremy immediately swats the hand away.

"No!" he cries, his face pain and regret, "Leave me."

"I can't do that," Ryan answers swiftly, "We're all getting out of here."

Jeremy shakes his head as he hugs himself and curls up into a ball on the ground, "It bit me... I'm done."

Not liking the idea of wasting time, Ryan reaches down to grab the boy again, "Don't say that, you can still make it."

Jeremy is begrudgingly assisted back to his feet but upon standing, he shoves Ryan away with that same level of force Ryan never expects, "Now do you know!? You were at the school. You know. I'm going to—to turn any moment now."

"That's not true," Ryan rebuttals, "Now lets go, we don't have time."

Just to be sure, he checks on the exit the came from but thankfully doesn't find any zombies chasing them. The interior of the stadium still echoes of a pandemonium but they seem to be clear for the moment.

Jeremy takes a slow step back as he shakes his head, "no... go without me."

Ryan rakes his brain for something, anything to convince this boy to listen, to just—just follow him. There has to be something he can say to get him moving, even if the situation looks as dire as it is. Then, as much as he hates to admit it, there is one thing he can do.

Still in a panicked motion, Ryan pulls his jacket back and stretches the collar of his shirt to the side revealing the scar on his shoulder. The dark, dotted pattern of teeth lays in stark contrast to his lighter skin which makes it very obvious for Jeremy to see. 

The boy's eyes widen as he realizes what he's seeing, "W—what is that?!" 

Ryan looks him straight in the eyes, "I was bitten before... and survived. You can too, now let's go. You still have to see your mother again."

Jeremy stutters an unintelligible thought before stumbling forward. Ryan catches him and wraps his arm around the shoulders.

"There's just no way," Jeremy mutters as he limps towards the van.

"There is a way," Ryan half-lies, not really knowing the extent of what he's saying. At the end of the day, he just needs to get himself and Jeremy out of the situation and then they can brainstorm where they go from there. When Bill got bitten, he lasted several hours. If that trend holds, all Ryan has to do is track down the masked man and beg him for the cure again... if he can do that... then...

"Ryan," Jeremy starts as they get closer to the van, "Do you really think I'll see my mom again?"

"Of course," Ryan comforts with as much sincerity as he can muster.

Jeremy purses his lips together as they quiver, his eyes looking at the car as if he were watching a beloved pet pass away. He then looks over at Ryan with wavering eyes, "We'll all have a good laugh about this one day, right?"

"Right," Ryan confirms as he reaches for the handle of the door.

Jeremy chuckles dryly, "I'll prove to her and everyone else that I ca—"

Ryan pulls the door open and drags Jeremy over to it so that he can sit down, but the boy's weight becomes strikingly heavy as he practically falls forward into the seat, his head slumping forward. With the deafening chaos behind them, Ryan didn't even register that the boy cut himself off. So when Jeremy falls forward, his first worry is that he passed out—not that he had turned—not anything else. Wanting to prevent an unconscious driver, he shakes him only to reveal the boy's already bloody head... or lack thereof.

What Ryan finds when he rolls the boy over is not the quivering lip of a fearful boy, no. He finds a gapping hole in between the boy's eyes, his mouth wide open with his own blood splattered across his tongue and teeth. Nothing remains of the boy's natural face except a mangled mess of flesh.

At that moment, the word seems to freeze solid. The wind stops. The scream stops. His movements stop. His breath stops. The world collapses into a static void where nothing dare cause motion. Even a distant moth hangs in the air amidst the downward stroke of a wing flap. Ryan finds himself incapable of moving, only his thoughts proceed unaccosted while everything else succumbs to the unadulterated stillness.

One would think his heart would start racing at the feeling of not being able to move, but for whatever reason, he can't feel his heart beating at all. He feels absolutely nothing, yet he can still see the static frame in front of him and he's allotted the generous capacity to observe this phenomenon. 

Then, before he can question things further, a familiar, foreboding sight enters his vision like a shadow manifesting into a material form. Standing on the other side of the parking lot is the dark figure of a cloaked man wearing the mask of a raven. He says nothing, he simply stands still as if the frozen world had taken him over as well. But then he moves. With a single, simple raising of the arm, he snaps his fingers and the world blinks into a different sight.

Ryan finds Jeremy's arm around his shoulder as they trudge towards the van. Jeremy pursing his lips together as they quiver, his eyes looking at the car as if he were watching a beloved pet pass away. He then looks over at Ryan with wavering eyes, "We'll all have a good laugh about this one day, right?"

Instinctively, Ryan responds with, "Right", but then a deep seated dread fills his heart. He doesn't reach for the handle this time and instead looks over.

Jeremy chuckles dryly, "I'll prove to her and everyone else," Ryan glances backwards and spots the true enemy: Jimmy stands a few yards back with a pistol pointed, not at him, but at Jeremy, "that I ca—"

*Bang*

The first time around, Ryan was so over-stimulated that he didn't register the sound of the gunshot. The second time, however, he is left completely powerless as the bullet traverses the air effortlessly before passing right through the back of Jeremy's head. Blood splatters across Ryan's face as he stares at the boy in his arms—his emotions unable to catch up.

As Jeremy's body goes completely limp, Ryan looses the ability to hold him. Jeremy's corpse slams mangled head first into the door of the van, splattering blood on the window as he drags downwards until he's just another corpse on the ground. All the while, Ryan stares blankly.

Eventually his gaze turns to Jimmy, utter disbelief still monopolizing his expression. He doesn't even know what to think, what to feel, or even what happened. All he finds is Jimmy, with a sinister smirk across his face, watching the scene unfold.

"And that's that," Jimmy tosses the pistol onto the ground in front of Ryan. Ryan just looks down at it blankly.

Jimmy turns around and starts walking away—not towards the stadium, but rather in some arbitrary direction. He looks over his shoulder as he walks, "The hostages are in the old warehouse down the street. I'd hustle over, they won't last long."

Ryan looks up, his mouth moving but not words coming out—only a pathetic groan of uncertainty. What is he even supposed to say?

He's not even able to process a thought as Jimmy disappears into the city. The looming night envelopes his tracks. 

With no real actionable idea, Ryan retrieves his pistol before staring at Jeremy's corpse. What should he do? Bury him? Take him back? ...cry?

His questions are answered by the crashing sound of a metal gate hitting the ground. Shortly after a stampede of zombies appear around the corner of the stadium and barrel towards him.

His brain freezes. His body moves. He's on his motorcycle in seconds—leaving Jeremy behind.
