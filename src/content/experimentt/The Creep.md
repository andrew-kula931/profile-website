After a rather short, silent drive, he pulls up to the location in question, or rather, one of three. Three blocky, dark gray buildings sit behind a chain link fence and a single sliding gate. Of course, it has to be another chain link fence, he's beginning to grow sick at the sight of them. Luckily there are no zombies loitering outside both in front of, and behind the fence.

He parks the motorcycle directly in front of the gate and positions it in such as way that he will be able to make a quick escape. Assuming he doesn't stall it right away, he should be able to bail if things go south—or at least, that's what he tells himself.

Approaching the gate, he finds that his hands won't stop trembling, even when he holds it down against his side, it still shakes. Not just that, but he feels light-headed for some reason. He tries to reassure himself by clutching his knife but ends up drawing it unintentionally and looking around. Nothing stands around him yet the hair on the back of his neck stands on end. His back hits the fence line, and he jumps thinking the metal perimeter is some kind of threat. Only when he touches the cold surface of the metal does he relax. He wonders why... what... what was he thinking about? 

Sitting between the gate and a fence pole is a small little combination lock. Ordinarily he'd just climb over but an unhealthy amount of barbed wire tops the entire fence-line with the gate having simple spikes instead. Pair that with that face that multiple people will need to exit here shortly, and it becomes apparent that he'll have to open the gate... but how? 

Out of curiosity he sticks the sharp end of his blade in the middle of the lock loop and tries to pry it open. The lock doesn't give, but then he slides the blade out and finds a deep indention in one end of the loop as if some power tool had drilled into it. Without even thinking, he sticks the knife back in, shoves the blade into the side of the metal loop and, wow-and-behold, it goes right through the metal. He doesn't even question it as he cuts the other side and rips the lock off. 

Perhaps he should be amazed at the sheer utility of this impossibly sharp knife, but for some reason he can't bring himself to care. Instead, he just opens the gate without a passing thought and immediately starts looking for where the hostages could be.

The warehouse lot is frighteningly dark as there are no streetlights or otherwise powered nearby, this makes for dim atmosphere that he can barely see through. One thing does catch his eye though. In one of the warehouses, he finds a faint light illuminating through a door window. With no other leads, he opts to check that one out first. 

The old metal and concrete walls reek of mold and neglect. Rust presents itself all across the metal shell of the building. On the side door, a powered-off sign sits reading: “Employees only”. Only then does he catch it: a whiff of decay. The smell of a rotting corpse is not a very familiar smell to him, but it's a pungent odor that's so recognizable that he doesn't need to know it to identify it. The distinct smell hits you like a truck and lingers with you, even if you try and leave. Now that he thinks about it, he has smelled it before—twice even. The first was at the zombie woman's house, in her basement. The second was the woman in the freezer. Both times, this pungent stench make his nostrils burn. Then, ever so faintly, he hears something. It’s a groan and a light sob at first, but then it forms into words. “Help me…” It is the voice of a woman, though weak. 

Ryan looks back briefly, some thought enters his head, but he can't quite comprehend what it means at the moment. Whether it was fear, anxiety, caution, or whatever else, it simply hits the invisible wall around his current mind and fades into nothingness. Only the shadow of an emotion is left to make him stop and think. And so, he goes inside.

Upon entering into the cursed building he is met with the stench of death so strong it makes his eyes water. He pulls back and sticks his foot back out the door, but after a moment to process the smell, he elects to keep going. His body feels oddly heavy as he starts inside but upon noticing it, the stiffness disappears, and he's back to his usual self.

Inside the large warehouse is countless tables cluttered with lab equipment. Half filled beakers, empty test tubes, little pipettes, and a litany of other instruments cover more table than there is table space. On the floor underneath each table are various boxes, each labeled in a foreign language he can't understand. On the other side of the room are three scattered electric lamps which cast a dim light across the room. From the ceiling are a multitude of thin wires holding strange looking objects. Each object appears to be some sort of tattered cloth, but he can't truly be sure. Even as something drips from one of them, he ignores it and walks right on past as his eyes look solely for where the woman's voice could have originated. 

In his peripheral vision, he does notice countless spills across the tables and even on the floors but the dim lighting prevent him from knowing the color or composition of the spills. He just categorizes it as an arbitrary dangerous chemical and avoids stepping in any of the spills.

From further in the room, he hears a faint whimper and immediately pushes forward. Disregarding everything around him, he visually interrogates the other end of the room in search of the sound's origin, despite the copious amounts of clutter all around him. Then he spots something. Beside one of the three electric lanterns is a large cloth creating a makeshift room. On the floor, sticking out from the corner of the hanging cloth, is a human arm as if someone were laying on their back and reaching out for help.

Ryan rushes across the room, almost tripping over some squishy ball on the floor, and arrives to the cloth wall. He immediately reaches out and grabs the cloth just above the hand in an attempt at seeing how could be reaching out. Almost too quickly, he lifts up the cloth and finds the person owning the hand—or lack thereof. There is no body attached to this hand. Nothing exists beyond the elbow, only a surgically precise cut severing the arm from whatever body it came from.

Ryan falls backwards in disbelief, but as his hands hit the ground behind him, he immediately slips and falls into some of the chemicals he so desperately tried to avoid. Scrambling out of them, he finds his hand oddly warm... and sticky. The chemical doesn't burn or itch or any other sort of unpleasant stimulus, instead it's just... almost metallic. A little confused, he looks down at his hand with much more clear lighting and is able to decipher what substance it is. In fact, he doesn't even need to be a scientist to label this chemical. He's seen it far too many times to not know it. From the tips of his fingers all the way up to his shoulder is the deep crimson of blood.

His eyes widen and his heart skips a beat as he finally glances around at scientific equipment he's engulfed in—this time with a bit more adequate lighting. 

Heads. Hands. Eyes. Blood. Death. Decay. Everything that he was resisting suddenly hits him so hard his head spins. The world blurs out for a moment, so he shakes his head and blinks his eyes to adjust but that doesn't save him from what he sees on the other side of the mental fog.

Hanging from the ceiling is indeed tattered cloth, but inside that cloth are loose human appendages. Severed arms, legs, hearts, even eyes are pinned to bulletin boards and splattered across the walls in a terrifyingly organized manner. Ryan's stomach tightens. 

Each appendage is labeled with a small notecard containing the words “Experiment” followed by a different number. Below each arbitrary label is a short description. One says, “Experiment #102: Implosion” another describes, “Experiment #95 Excessive Mutation”. Many of the dismembered body parts have precise cuts along the ends, but more than a few seem to have been violently torn from the host body. Many of these body parts are still fresh with blood. Out of all the body parts, one catches his eyes more than others. Sitting on the ground, right where that "squishy ball" was supposed to be is a head with its eyes rolled back. It’s mouth wide open as if it is still screaming in agonizing pain. The label: “Experiment #71: Suicide”. 

Before he knows it, his breath has already grown thin. His chest pulses rapidly as both his heart and lungs beat against it, all the while he slowly slides away shaking his head. Then, right before he fully looses himself, he slams his fist into the ground and stops—stops everything. This isn't... he can't... the woman!

Ryan pushes himself to his feet and scrambles to the other side of the fabric wall. On the other side of that wall he finds a single surgeon's table with what can only be described as a woman laying atop it. Her arms, legs, and head are strapped down to the table not allowing her to shift anything but her eyes and mouth. She wears only a patient's gown with multiple holes around the lower abdomen, bloody rags are thrown across her body next to open wounds, many of which are held open by surgical clamps. Her body is frail and weak yet it doesn’t even seem like she’s been here long. At first he just sees another body—another corpse—but then her eyes move and spots Ryan. The realization make's Ryan's knees weak. The woman laying there is Amber: Jeremy's mother.

She tries to whisper something weakly but it comes out as a pitiful groan more than anything else. Her face looks like she’s about to cry, yet tears are unable to form in her strapped-open eyes. Her flush cheeks tell him the rest of the story.

"A-amber?" Ryan tries, his own voice choking him up.

She groans again, "R-r-ryan... is... J-jeremy o-okay?"

Ryan's heart sinks as his blood runs cold. Not Jeremy. Anything Jeremy. Not now. His hand begins to shake profusely—thankfully she can't move her eyes down far enough to see. 

"He's," Ryan starts, every answer feeling like the worst possible answer, "He's..."

Amber shifts abruptly as if a sharp pain had spiked through her body, "It's... o-okay..."

Ryan immediately shakes his head without thinking, "No."

What is he supposed to say now? 

His body begins to take over as his mind runs a blank, "He's fine, you'll see him soon."

This ill-sighted comment seems to alleviate some part of Amber's fear, yet it also cause her chin to quiver. Her eyes, while clamped open, seem to look as though they're about to cry again. The rest of her body trembles in place.

"M-my b-b-baby boy..." she practically whispers, her voice drenched in a somber sorrow, "T-tell h-him th-that I l-love him. S-so much."

"No," Ryan says in disbelief he rushes over to the table and analyzes the scene. He's no doctor but there has to be something that can saver. He not going to lose her too!

Amber's body, frail and feeble, has countless incisions across her body, particularly across her arms and abdomen. Many of the incisions are skillfully patched with stitches, but just as many are left unattended or still propped open. This is some sadistic surgery that Ryan doesn't even begin to have the skills to understand. Maybe... maybe he can just wrap her body in gauze and try and get her back to the prison. No, she's bleeding too much. Could he go get help first? Bring them here? Make them see this horrible lab and risk the sadistic doctor coming back? No, he can't do that. Then what?

He hangs his head in despair, his mind slowing down to such a degree that he can barely process what he's seeing. Then, to make matters worse, Amber starts convulsing. With the tight straps she doesn't move much, but her head does shake violently as something in her abdomen begins to twist and turn. It's a sight he's only ever seen in a poor dog. He searches for some way to help, but he can't find a single clue as to what to do. Then, just as it began, she stops convulsing and goes utterly silent.

"Amber?" he questions wistfully. 

Her eyes move slowly to match his. Her subsequent words nearly forcing Ryan to a knee, "P-p-p-p-plea-se. K-kill m-m-me."

Ryan steps back slowly as he shakes from side to side even slower. His eyes wide, drilling holes into Amber's pleading expression. His back hits a wall of some kind as he slowly crumbles to the floor. This can't be real. It just can't. He spontaneously starts choking on the odorous air full of death and decay. The very nature of breathing becomes foreign to him. Only when the coughing subsides, and he finds Amber still lying on the table do thoughts re-enter his mind.

He blinks and suddenly finds himself standing over Amber, his knife shaking in his hand. 

He blinks again, and the knife is raised up into the air, blade pointing down. Amber struggles but manages to offer him one last parting gift—a smile. 

He blinks again, and he's on the floor, the knife sitting beside him... Amber still alive.

He blinks, and he's back on his feet, his knife at his side, Amber still smiling poorly.

He blinks. Everything becomes blurry as his cheeks grow hot.

He blinks. Amber begins to seize. 

He blinks. She stops.

Silence. Stillness. Not even a fly dares to move its wings to break the static void within the confines of this room... Within the confines of this small, makeshift surgery room from hell. Silence. Stillness. Despair.

A second outstretches it's vile hand towards a minute. A minute stumbles upon two. 

In the solitude of the sick room, he steps out of the cloth-covered room and leans over one of the tables. His stomach tightens. Then something moves.

He turns around slowly with his eyes still covered in his own shock and disgust. Behind him stands a figure short in stature yet even more grotesque than the human arm he bumps into as he steps back.  

A humanoid figure stands with a face dripping in bright green ooze. Its eyes are the same neon green color but solid and full of unreadable emotion; a vortex of knowledge and curiosity while retaining no hint of humanity. It’s mouth sagging at the corners with the green ooze bleeding out and white teeth slyly sticking out around the corners of its sagging mouth. The skin on its inhuman visage is white as snow yet radiates the neon green aura from its blood. It stands slightly hunched over with two hands sticking out of its white lab coat that match the image of its face. Once it speaks, Ryan’s blood runs cold—he recognizes that voice.

"And who might you be?" the creep says with its vile and deformed voice. Flashbacks of the library make his avert his eyes temporarily.

He shouldn't even entertain this monster with conversation, he should just kill him... or it. He should just raise his knife one more time and take... one... more... life. What is he thinking?

The creep's sagging mouth begins to raise into a terrifying smile, bioluminescent material dripping from the corners of its mouth, "Wait a moment, One point seven... eight meters. Seventy...eight kilograms. Seventeen... no, eighteens years old... You're that boy! What was your name again..." the creep proceeds to pace back and forth as it visually interrogates its thoughts by waving its hands back and forth and flapping it's deformed lips in a manner that can only be described as silent words. It spontaneously stops in its tracks and points a single, gnarled finger at him, "Your birth name is Ryan!?"

His head begins nodding immediately, an ingrained behavior he never knew he had up until now. Upon realizing it, he shakes his head and steps back, his knife raising in case he may have to... to.... to use it.

The creep chuckles to itself smugly, a noise that reverberates through the room as more of a guttural choking sound. It then glances into the makeshift room and notices the scene of a canceled experiment. Its eyes widen, not in shock, but with intrigue as it folds its hand together in amusement. The monster's gaze then shifts from the failed creation to a very living, breathing one. Ryan steps back in horror as the two green, glowing eyes interrogate him silently.

"May I ask you a question?" it begins with a civil nature that's unbecoming of its monstrous form.

Ryan hesitates, his body wants to move, wants to scream, wants to fight, wants to run, but he can't seem to control it—not even a little. Instead, his head begins to nod once more, without even being prompted. 

The creep chuckles once more and reaches for a translucent vile on a nearby table, "I remember you within that roster of students in that school. Tell me, how did you escape? All the exits should have been sealed."

His mouth opens but nothing comes out. Being unable to speak is a terrifyingly feeling of futility and strikes him like a sledgehammer as his hand goes to his throat. This is not the time to struggle. He has to... for everyone that died... he has to...

"Timid proclivities... no, stoic predisposition. Very curious... Emotional turmoil, shock, disbelief: you reek of humanity," the creep highlights so fluently that Ryan isn't sure of what he's listening to anymore.

His hand clenches down on the grip of his knife as he takes a deep breath. He just has to kill another monster. So... why can't he move?!

The creep raises its mangled hand and lowers its head slightly, "One moment, before you indulge in aggressive temperaments, I have a proposition I would like to extend."

Ryan still finds himself still unable to move.

On a separate table, the creep reaches for a syringe and promptly sticks it into the vial of translucent fluid. It then holds the syringe into the air and pumps out an excess air until a small droplet of the liquid exits the needle, "I have attempted to administer this serum through a plentiful number of trials but have had little success. Often it is a strict lack of constitution that results in immediate desecration of the internal organs, but... I have a feeling that you may be the counterexample I've been searching for."

Ryan's body doesn't dare move but upon the creep taking a single step forward, he finds himself stumbling backwards as if a static world has been miraculously shattered. He manages to catch himself on the corner of a table and stands up slowly.

Taking an additional step forward, the creep flicks the side of the syringe with a broken fingernail, "In truth this serum doesn't perform much, but it proceeds much more potent injections that have truly magnificent results. I only ask that you receive this initial, prerequisite injection. You're survival would be an extraordinary advancement in my research."

Given the situation, Ryan doesn't trust that his knife will be long enough so he instead draws his pistol and points it at the monster before him.

The creep stops in its tracks, "Oh, it seems I have forgone the formality of offering you something in return," it turns around briefly to swipe a key off the table and holds it up for Ryan to see, "I am more than happy to exchange all of my future subjects for the opportunity to see this deal to fruition. It's very rare that I ask for consent, but that was one of two of the Director's demands."

Unable to make a clear thought, Ryan grits his teeth and tightens his finger on the trigger. This isn't a deal, it's a death sentence, and he's not about to... he'll just have to... he can't take it anymore! 

He squeezes his finger on the trigger and the bullet pierces straight through the creep's forehead. Its head snaps back after the impact, but it remains on its feet. Then, as if to spite the bullet, the creep bends back to a standing position. The hole in its head begins to bubble up in green, luminant bubbles before they each pop revealing a completely reformed head. 

The creep smiles widely, "As expected, you harbor some deep-rooted hatred for me. None the matter, I anticipate that it should be out of your system now, no? Therefore... have you reached a deliberation on my proposition?"

As irrational as it may seem, Ryan finds himself pulling the trigger once more only for the exact same result to unfold. It seems he really is going insane. Regardless, he just doesn't understand what's going through his head. Everything feels so surreal. 

"N-no, I won't help you," Ryan answers slowly, ensuring his words are actually his—a feeling he never thought would cross his mind.

The creep lowers the syringe in disappointment but some subtle degree of fascination arises shortly after, "Fascinating, I see why The Director said to ask for consent. You reached a timely resolution without asking a single question, and you even seem to have let go of your former rage. Talking to you alone has proven itself to be worth my time."

Ryan's mouth opens instinctively probably about to ask some sort of follow up question, but nothing reaches a full thought.

The creep stops itself mid-thought—whatever that delusional thought may be—and gestures to Ryan, "Go ahead, ask you question. I want to hear it."

Automatically Ryan begins to contemplate what he's supposed to say, but as he dives into the depths of his mind space, he finds it a cluttered disaster. He tries to decipher something in order to make sense of literally anything, but attempt only seems to exhaust him further. With literally nothing in mind, he defaults to the only thing he can think of.

"Why?"

The creeps stares blankly at him for a brief moment before sighs contently which ejects some of the glowing, green liquid out of its mouth, "Most people seek certainty before understanding—you didn't. And of all the words you could have used, you so happened to have used my favorite. Why. I believe it to the pinnacle of all questions. Even a human child knows of its magnificent nature. In any body of science, it embodies an open ended pathway to further understanding. It's purely and objectively a superior alternative to knowledge roadblocks such as what, where, and who. I implore you to never lose that lust for knowledge."

"I—" Ryan stammers.

The creeps laughs at his half-hearted response, "I'm an well aware you were not articulating your thoughts as intricately as I make is seem, but I resolved to end this discussion had you asked who or what I am. I find those trivial questions repulsive because it shows utter ignorance. I am clearly not human and what could be gained from a name? What is the purpose of a category if you do not understand what it denotes? Why waste your time pursuing something if you seek only an answer and not understanding itself?"

Ryan clenches his fist before slowly putting his gun away—it doesn't seem like he'll have any use for it.

"Oh," the creep exclaims, "I never even answered your question. How foolish of me, and after I had the audacity to digress to such an extend," it holds up the syringe once more, "I required your assistance for nothing more than my unquenchable curiosity. After all..." it gives Ryan a sinister look as its two glowing orbs for eyes seem to narrow, "it's the only thing alleviating me from the paradox of the Experiment."

Ryan's eyes wonder to the countless note cards all around him, each labeled with that same foreboding word. He can't help but shake at the thought of what this paradox could be. Any further contemplation feels beyond his reach so he just looks back to the creep with a somber look—not because he's sad, but because he doesn't know what he's supposed to do. He can't kill this creep with a bullet to the head... is he really this powerless.

The creep seems to notice Ryan's uncharacteristic expression and promptly snickers, "Sorrow. No, emotional fatigue. This'll do," the creep turns and begins hobbling away. Ryan takes a step in its direction but can't muster the courage to pursue. After a few steps the creep turns around and looks back at Ryan, shaking the syringe in the air, "You have a new deal. Either I inject this into you, or I inject it into that girl—the only other one who survived the trial in the school."

Suddenly, the fog inside his head is lifted. What remains is not a swirling storm of rampant emotions and tumultuous thoughts, but rather a simple, very instinctual desire. As his heart begins to race and his blood surges, he finds himself drawing his knife and preparing for a fight despite the odds. He doesn't think he can win, heck he'd be damned if he stands a chance, but all logic seems to be irrelevant for some reason. The thing that kept him alive for so long now feels like a distant memory as his sole objective becomes something so simply, so tangible, it's the only thing he can think. 

The creep sighs, "You're making this harder than it needs to be. I'd much rather you take it as you're much more likely to survive the endeavor, but I'm not about to let this serum to to waste. Just think, I'll gladly give you the key if you simply allow the injection."

Seemingly losing all sense, Ryan rushes forward with his knife at the ready. So long as he does let the needle touch him, he can still cut this monster into so many pieces that it is incapable of regenerating. And so, with as much grace as a stampeding rhino, he vaults over a table and slashes his blade down on the creep... only he misses.

Contrary to the bullet, the creep actually dodges the attack of his knife by swiftly dodging backwards. Ryan follows up with a secondary slash but finds the insane monster dodging once more by climbing into a table and jumping backwards. He expects the thing to start monologuing about aggression in the animal kingdom, but finds it utterly silent as if analyzing his every move. Ryan jumps onto the new table and attempts to cut the creep again only to miss once more as he falls over and rolls onto the floor—it bounces back to its feet before Ryan can follow up, the syringe still untouched. He advances with another series of slashes but the creep continues to dodge, weave, and use the environment to its advantage without once attempting to stab the syringe into Ryan's arm.

With the nimble little bastard evading his every move, Ryan gives up on his training and begins more reckless tactics. He tries throwing a vile of acid looking stuff at it and then slashing. He tries flipping an entire table and then rushing the monster. When neither of those strategies work, he begins swing his blade even faster and harder than before. Using as much strength as he can, he slams the knife down on the creep only to land on the table and wind up cutting straight through it. Turning his attention back to the nimble monster, he pushes forward with another volley of slashes.

As he swings his knife around wildly, he hears the creep mutter a simple phrase: "Curious. You do change when someone else is threatened."

Immediately following its statement, Ryan's knife sinks into the monster's neck. The creep's head quickly rolls on the floor with its glowing eyes losing its luster. This time, the bubbles don't come back and the corpse stays on the floor. 

Ryan steps back with some sort of dampened relief. It's at that moment that a overwhelming pain erupts in his leg. Upon looking down, he finds an empty syringe sticking through his jeans.

He pulls it out immediately but by then he knows it's already to late. What follows is nothing short of unadulterated hell itself. His leg completely gives out, his arms tense up, his chest pounds, his head throbs. Every part of his body simultaneously losses control while writhing in agony. Falling to the floor, he tries to smash his head against the ground to knock him out—save him from the unimaginable torture—but his muscles don't comply. He squirms in place trying to scream but unable to open his mouth to do so. His body convulses on the floor silently as he pleads for someone, anyone just put him out of his misery. 

The agony goes on for an unfathomable amount of time. Perhaps it isn't actually an eternity, but it might as well be as the dimly lit ceiling because his entire existence for that time. Eventually there isn't even pain anymore. There isn't a warehouse. There isn't a body. There isn't a Ryan. There is only the vague certainty that something once existed—and even that certainty eventually disappears.

Then, once everything has been completely and utterly flushed out and erased, it all stops. And like a massive tsunami, the tide gradually begins to wash back into the ocean leaving behind the memories and identity he lost. As it floods back in, at some point, he almost wishes it wouldn't have come back.

When all is said and done, the pain completely stops and he's back to normal—on the floor of a warehouse surrounded by blood that isn't his.

He sits up and looks around finding everything to be exactly as he left it. A cut table, the corpse of a creep, a cloth wall concealing a horrific sight. Reaching to his heart, he expects to find it beating so fast he can't control it—but it's not. He looks to the blood on his hands expecting it to terrify him—it doesn't. He looks to the creep expecting to feel a deeply rooted hatred and resentment—he feels nothing. He thinks... he thinks of Jeremy... and of Steve—nothing. He feels as though that thought alone should scare him—it doesn't. Then, getting up slowly, he finds his hand shaking and his heart pounding. He doesn't remember when that started. He lifts himself to his feet but his knees buckle—why? Nothing is making sense. What was in that syringe? What part of himself is he missing?

Taking a deep breath, he rises to his feet for real. His body feels unsettlingly jittery yet he can only sense it intellectually—nothing more. Even the grotesque organs splattered across the tables seem to be nothing more than a hard-on-the-eyes sight and nauseating smell. He glances over at the cloth wall one more time knowing exactly what's behind it. He recalls all the memories so vividly yet... his brain doesn't stutter like it did before, he just thinks. 

Then his eyes land on the keys and suddenly his body starts moving. He grabs the keys and heads for the door. Stepping past experiment after experiment and chemical spill after chemical spill requires great care and coordination. Ordinarily it would be a trivial matter, but for some reason his legs feel heavy and his knees weak. He manages to circumvent any accidental slip but he doesn't remember having to try so hard for something so simple. It just feels... foreign.

Eventually he reaches the door he came in and promptly outstretches his hand for the knob—it misses. Instead of grabbing the handle, his hand winds up a few inches to the side, shaking profusely. He uses his other hand to stabilize the first, but finds the second shaking just as much. Completely confused, he forces both hands onto the handle and proceeds to swing the door open. Beyond the trembling, he still seems to maintain full control over his body... at least there's a silver lining. 

As he steps out of the warehouse and is met with the refreshing air of the outdoors, he finds his breathe a bit faster than normal, as if the lack of pungent corpse smell is some sort of potent drug he needs more of. It doesn't impede on his movements or thinking, so he just let's it be and hopes it will calm down on its own. He takes one step away and suddenly every muscle in his body loosens up without warning. Strangely enough, they don't quite give out, so he manages to stay on his feet and lean against the wall, but it feels rather strange that his muscles would just... loosen like that. With a small bit of effort he regains control and everything returns to normal. Though his hands still tremble slightly—he hasn't figured out how to stop that one yet.

As he continues to the next warehouse, he realizes that he left Amber back there in the lab. Well, he can't really carry her, but it feels as though it should make him pause. After all, he left Jeremy behind too so shouldn't he do something? He immediately turns around and starts walking back, but then he stops again. The key in his hand. The hostages! That has to come first. He turns back. Then stops. But... no. 

With that he proceeds to check out the next warehouse over.
