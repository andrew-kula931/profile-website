“Mom, why are we packing so much? Are we going on vacation?” Charlie nags as his visibly distraught mother vigorously shoves clothes into a suitcase. 

Karmen takes a deep breath to hide her panic before turning to the boy with a forced smile, “You’re right, we’re just going to go on a little trip. So, don’t worry and go grab your favorite toys to take with us.”

Ryan often forgets that Charlie is only 9 years old. He’s at the age where he can understand some adult things, but he’s still a kid at the end of the day. Ryan readjusts his backpack and heads over to the kitchen. His current plan is to fill a second backpack with food and supplies just in case things go south and Steve is unable to hold up his end of the bargain.

As he turns the corner, he finds Steve already in the kitchen rummaging through the canned foods and other non perishables. He takes notice of Ryan right away with a knowing look, “You ready?” 

Ryan nods, “How far do I need to take you guys.”

Steve sighs and looks back to the food, “Once we get outside city limits and to open roads, it should be far enough. Military blockades are going to be our biggest issue.”

“Alright,” Ryan nods, "One thing though."

Steve doesn't bother to look up as he reads the label of a can of chicken stock.

"Why you trying to get out of the city in such a hurry? Wouldn't it be better to sit tight and let things settle down?" Ryan wonders.

He's not against this plan by any stretch of the imagination, but intuitively it would make sense that other people would have the same idea. He just gets the feeling that running out of town right now might be a careless choice of action. Again, he doesn't care one way or the other, but his curiosity and skepticism will not rest until he inquires.

Steve shoots him a confusing side eye as he takes a deep breath, "Let's just say I... I have a complicated past with the military. The emergency broadcast said that martial law was in order which is something I'd like to avoid. Besides, I don't trust... I mean I think... Karmen will be better off on a farm in the middle of the Midwest than whatever's going to happen here, okay?"

Ryan accepts that answer as he kneels down and begins filling his bag. He makes sure to make the most of the small storage capacity by keeping similarly shaped objects together and opting for lightweight, high calorie options over snack foods—every calorie will count in the long run. The lack of variety between canned corn and canned olives makes him recoil at the thought of living of this stuff but he knows that beggars can’t be choosers. Ryan reaches all the way to the back of the cupboard and pulls out the last can: a can of chicken noodle soup. The can is of a brand he’s never seen before, but it looks edible so he doesn’t pay it much attention. Steve however notices it immediately.

“Give that to me,” he commands.

Ryan gives him a puzzled expression, “Why? Your bag is already full.”

“It doesn’t matter, give it to me now,” Steve asserts with no room for arguing.

Ryan shakes his head. Why would he care about a single can, after all, he’s helping relocate the family so he should be entitled to first dibs anyways, “No, this is the last one. It’s mine.”

"It's Charlie's favorite," Steve asserts with his nostrils flaring at what he probably deems insubordination.

Ryan pushes the can disorderly into his own back just to spite the man, "I like it too."

“This isn’t up for negotiation. Give me the can now, damnit!” Steve’s voice begins to raise as his eyes fill with that same authoritarian anger he loves to dish out in spades.

Suddenly, Charlie appears in the doorway of the kitchen, “Daddy, have you seen my drone?”

Steve’s expression changes, “Sorry, Charlie,” he snickers a brief moment. Ryan can tell that it’s forced and out of character, but Charlie doesn’t catch on, “Haven’t seen it.”

Ryan rolls his eyes and tosses the can onto Steve’s pile knocking over a few, “Suck up.” He then proceeds to zip up his bag and walk out of the kitchen.

“We’re leaving in ten!” Steve calls out for the whole house to hear.

---

By the time school would normally be out, Ryan and the foster family were on the road heading away from home: for good. It was a bit of a struggle to get Charlie passed the dead body on the front lawn, but he only had to hug Karmen for a minute or two before he was ready to get in the car. Karmen on the other hand, would not let go of Steve for as long as the body was in view. It’s a good thing Steve could operate a car one handed.

As they pass each block, Ryan notices that some houses have broken windows while others have forcefully opened doors. There are plenty of untouched homes, but the ratio is concerning enough to raise his heart rate. Initially, Ryan finds himself analyzing every bit of the scenes but after he finds the third trail of blood leading to a front door, he decides its better to keep his head down. Eventually, the neighborhoods end and businesses begin. Packed parking lots occupy the storefront of virtually every business. Despite only being about an hour after the initial alert, it seems people were already anxious to prepare for the long haul. It really makes him wonder just how much carnage these zombies have already done. The passing scenes aren't anarchy yet, but the prerequisites are stacking up. 

“Why are we headed deeper into the city?” Ryan asks, “We should be going the other way.”

Steve doesn’t look away from the road, “The emergency alert didn’t go off that long ago. I have to take a stop before we can leave the city for good.”

“Leave for good?! What about my baseball game next weekend?” Charlie complains.

Karmen looks back from the front passenger’s seat, “It’s okay honey, don’t worry about that right now, okay.” She isn’t very good at hiding her fear.

“Where are we going?” Ryan asks a bit more forcefully this time.

Steve’s hand on the wheel tightens but he remains relaxed otherwise, “I have a buddy we’re meeting up with. It’ll be better with him around. Besides, I'm pretty sure he would have prepared for something like this.”

“Then why do you need me?” Ryan mutters under his breath.

“What was that?” Steve asks genuinely not hearing what he said.

“Nothing,” Ryan closes with.

The conversation then drops as Ryan returns to watching the scenery go by. His hand rests on the pistol back on his hip. The elastic belt on his athletic pants are beginning to voice their complaints, but it would have to do for now. 

Traffic is a big issue in the town, thankfully the town isn’t large, but that doesn’t stop a full fledged traffic jam from occurring on every single street remotely close to a grocery store or gas station. Despite that set back, Steve powers through the slow moving cars and they get through the downtown area without a single living or undead conflict. By the time they reach the neighborhood they were headed to, the sun is on the verge of setting. Ryan’s head becomes sweaty every time someone runs by but he tries it best to look nonchalant—for Charlies sake. In spite of his outwardly demeanor, he never lets his land leave his hip.

Steve pulls to a stop in the driveway of a tan house. The neglected lawn has been overgrown with weeds and the like with a bush taking over the path to the porch. Beyond that surface level observation, the suburban house presents no other criteria for suspicion. 

Steve turns around to the passengers in the car, “You guys stay here, I’ll go talk to him.”

Ryan is about to protest but decides against it. If something were to happen to Karmen or Charlie, it would be best to stay nearby to protect them. 

Steve exits the car and briskly advances to the front door. He does his best to conceal it, but Ryan notices that the man looks over his shoulder more times than necessary.

“Mom, do you know where we are?” Charlie slouches over the back of Karmen’s seat.

Karmen watches Steve intently as she responds, “I know your dad’s spoken about Bill before, but I can’t remember ever meeting him.”

Ryan’s eyes drift off to the window. The street is quiet and long, intrusive shadows stretch their intimidating forms further across the landscape with each passing minute. A figure off in the distance stares at them with an ominous look. For a moment the thought of a zombie crosses his mind, but that quickly gets ruled out when it walks in a civilized and normal fashion in his direction. He decides to brush them off as an ordinary local, but that turns out to be a fallacy.

The stranger gets to the end of the sidewalk connecting to the driveway before swiftly rushing the car. He quickly tries to open Karmen’s passenger door but finds it locked. After a brief moment of contemplation he pulls a revolver out and points it right at Karmen.

“Open the damn door right now or I’ll open it for you!” he yells.

Karmen freezes—arms shaking—and finds herself unable to move at the sight of both a hostile stranger and a gun. She twitches towards the handle but can’t force herself to move fast enough to get the door open. Charlie curls up into a ball and covers his eyes with his knees.

Ryan forces himself into action by turning the safety off the pistol and opening his door. He’s not going to make the same mistake twice and get an innocent person killed due to his own incompetence. Being right behind the driver's seat, he has to look over the car to see the intruder. As quickly as he can, he stands up, points the gun, and proceeds  to hesitate despite his initial judgement. The barrel is clearly pointed at the hostile’s head but he can’t bring himself to pull the trigger. So what if he’s killed zombies before, they were already dead. Killing a monster and killing a person were two completely incomparable situations. But it’s him or Karmen and only one of those people is someone he remotely cares about.

He's about to muster up the courage to pull the trigger, but the man ducks out of the way before that can happen and uses the car as a shield. Just as quickly as he took cover, he puts his gun on the roof of the car continuing to use the car as cover and aims straight at Ryan. 

“Bill! Hold your fire!” Steve shouts running over to the car.

The man flicks the gun to point at Steve in which he responds by raising his hands in the air. 

“Steve?” the man says, clearly startled by his entrance.

Ryan’s shaky hand sags to his side as the other guy disengages. He almost killed someone–a real person, not a zombie, but a live human being breathing the same air as him. What if he was the man? Yes, maybe he was attacking an innocent family, but to die for it before getting to explain. Is this what the new world was going to be like? Is this what it meant to hold a gun against another. His simple life from before seems so easy now.

The man glances at Ryan once more to be sure he’s not going to get a bullet slung at him before walking over to Steve. The two men exchange a handshake and brotherly hug, probably the same action but he can’t tell in the dim lighting. 

“Sorry, I thought you guys were trying to break into my house, I promise I wasn’t going to hurt anyone,” the man explains with Steve nodding, a slight smile across his face.

“It’s okay as long as everyone is alright,” Steve looks over at his family, stopping briefly on Ryan but then returns his attention to the man named Bill.

As they exchange some sort of pleasantries, Ryan’s eyes don’t leave the pistol in his hand. He was really about to kill someone. Death is one thing, but killing… these intrusive thoughts won’t spare his mind for even a moment.

“Let’s head inside,” Bill says, “it’s dangerous out here right now.”

---

At first glance, Bill’s house is no different from his foster parents’. A couch in the living room overlooks the TV which, by the look of the cushions, has gotten its fair share of use over the years. A faint scent of vodka floats around but Ryan’s only able to sense it because he’s familiar with the smell. Beyond the obvious similarities, there aren’t any toys laying around and most of the house is fairly bland in comparison to the few decorations that the Hughes have. It seems that Bill neither has a family nor cares to show off his home beyond the bare necessities.

“Have a seat on the couch,” Bill directs, “I’ll grab some drinks and then we can talk.”

Steve, Karmen, and Charlie all find a seat together on the small couch in the living room. Ryan takes the lazy-boy next to it and proceeds to indulge in its soft cushions which relax his stressed mind. The events of the day really took its toll and now he can put his mind at rest—even if for a few moments. 

Bill returns holding a couple water bottles and two beers. Naturally, he gives the beers to himself and Steve and the rest get water. Bill then walks over to the TV and turns it on. It starts with the local news agency reporting on mass chaos going down in the city but quickly details that the origins seems to be the high school based on eye witnesses. They repeatedly convey to the viewer that the authorities are taking care of the situation and anyone watching this should remain at home until the situation can be handled.

“I take it you’re here because of this,” Bill asks rhetorically.

“Yeah, it's pretty bad out there and is only getting worse,” Steve responds.

“Do ya know what is causing it? News won’t say much,” Bill wonders as he pops open the cap of his beer with the corner of the table in the center of the room.

Steve looks over to Ryan and nods, “He does.”

Everyone’s attention turns to Ryan. He glances over to Charlie and Karmen and debates whether to explain the whole situation, but then it dawns on him that it could hurt them more if they don’t know.

Ryan swallows his pride and resolves to keep a straight face, “Zombies.”

“Funny guy I see,” Bill laughs, “Now what is it really?”

“Zombies,” Ryan repeats as seriously as the first time.

Bill’s smile fades, “Oh, you’re serious.”

“Are you saying we’re in some kind of zombie apocalypse now?! Are we gonna get cool weapons and start a group of survivors?” Charlie asks, growing excited in his own childish way.

“What are you talking about?” Karmen asks, trying to keep him and herself calm.

“I saw this TV show about a group of people in a zombie apocalypse. They were so cool,” Charlie explains with too much enthusiasm for the given situation, “They drove super cool cars and got to go anywhere they wanted.”

“You weren’t supposed to be watching stuff like that,” Karmen says with a concerned frown.

Bill shifts away from the TV and over to the doorway leading to the kitchen. He places his hand on the doorframe and leans against it. “Are you sure of this, son?”

Ryan nods confidently, “We’ve all seen one. I’ve seen many.”

Bill shakes his head as he thinks. He nods his head back and forth as if having an entire court trial inside his head. Eventually the jury is dismiss and the judge makes a verdict, “I hesitate to agree, but I can tell you're not trying to deceive me," he takes a deep breath and a drink of his beer as he gives himself another moment of contemplation, "Alright. In that case, we need to remain quiet and not draw any attention. Going into the city is likely suicide so we’ll lay low here until morning.”

Steve pipes in, “So you’ll help us get out of the city?”

“Shouldn’t we leave right now?” Ryan argues as he leans forward in his seat, "You know, with the military blockade or whatever you said."

Bill shakes his head, “If we left right now, we’d have to deal with panicked drivers and threats we won’t see until they’re on top of us. Tomorrow won’t be much better but at least we'll have a sun to spot the threats before its too late. Now, Steve's told me about all of you..." he turns to Ryan, "Except for you. Who are you?"

Karmen starts but can't finish her thought which forces Ryan's hand at the introduction. Ryan sighs, accepting the aforementioned logic to be more sound than whatever he was thinking, “I'm Ryan. Steve and Karmen have been taking care of me for the last two years?”

Bill studies him quizzically, "Are you a relative or...".

"Orphan," Ryan corrects, "Though not anymore as of today."

Bill stands straight and eyes Steve, "That's what you were talking about..." he shakes his head, “Okay, why don’t Karmen and Charlie, come with me and I’ll set you up in the spare room. I’ll be back in a few to discuss things with the rest of you.”

The three of them depart the room and continue on down the hallway. That leaves Steve and Ryan alone in the living room with the quiet background audio of the TV and the dying rays of light coming through the curtains.

“So I take it I’m an adult now,” Ryan says sarcastically, crossing his arms and leaning back.

Steve rolls his eyes, “You can think whatever you want, the fact of the matter is that you know more about the current predicament than me or Bill. We’ll need you to formulate a strategy.”

“Okay,” Ryan agrees. At first he’s content with ending the conversation there but his curiosity quickly perks up and he decides this might be an opportunity to get some answers, “By the way, about earlier.”

“Which part?” Steve says blandly as he finishes off his beer.

“When did you learn to use a gun?”

Steve’s eyes narrow, not in a hostile way, more of a pondering thought process sort of way. After a moment of debate on how much he wants to say, he sets his beer down and stares right at Ryan, “You know, I’ve lived a life before, kid. I haven’t always been stuck working shady construction.”

“But–” Ryan tries.

Steve immediately cuts him off, “Tell me Ryan, how much do you even know about me?” Steve squares up and faces directly at Ryan.

“Enough to know what kind of person you are.” Ryan matches Steve’s body language, almost as if to challenge his authority.

“And how much is that?”

Ryan nearly stands up out of the chair. Every time he talks to Steve it fires him up. Maybe it is that typical voice inside every teenager’s mind telling them to establish their boundaries or maybe it was the last two years worth of an engrained grudge against this man. “Why are you questioning me? You’re the one who shot a zombie through the brain.”

Steve shrugs as he speaks. “And it saved Karmen’s life. Your point?”

“As far as I’m concerned, that beer is the only thing you know how to do. The amount of times I had to come back from school only to see you drunk.”

Steve hesitates and his eyes drift to the floor, pain and regret filter into this voice if only for a moment, “I’ll admit it happened a lot, but did you ever stop to talk to me instead of retreating to your damn room. I didn't even know what you looked like for the first three months of your stay.”

“I didn’t want to get in the crossfire of your anger,” Ryan says aggressively and snarkily, "I've seen it before. You're just like the rest". 

Steve’s tone begins to escalate to match Ryan’s, “My anger? I may have said a few shallow things while you passed, but that doesn’t mean I was angry!”

Ryan rolls his eyes and crosses his arms, “I’m sure your long work day takes a toll on you. But you weren’t the only one that was struggling. Sometimes I wish you could learn not to make everything about you.”

Steve takes a deep breath before altering the tone of the conversation, “Is this about how you snuck out every night?” Ryan is taken aback as Steve continues, “Yeah, I knew. I knew for a long time that you weren’t just going to your room and staying put. But I never told Karmen because I knew she would worry to no end. If anything I was doing you a favor despite my… fuckin habits.”

Ryan is speechless. What is he supposed to say to that? He could backtrack to all the times he heard Steve yelling from downstairs, but the shock from his current realization is too great to ignore. 

Bill suddenly appears in the doorway of the living room, “Am I interrupting something?”

Both pairs of eyes match Bill’s as they both relax into their seats once again.

“No, we’re ready to discuss strategy,” Steve establishes with a calm demeanor.

Bill hesitates while he analyzes the two of them but brushes it off and moves on, “So what exactly are we up against, Ryan.”

Ryan leans forward and presses his hands together.

“They’re like enraged animals. They’re fast, they’re aggressive, and worst of all, they adapt. I had the misfortune of running into one and it altered how it attacked me after I dodged it just one time... I don't want to get close to another one," Ryan runs a hand through his hair as he thinks back to the boy in the bathroom and the deranged woman before that, "They also seem to mutate to some degree. One of them had fingernails that could easily cut through steel. Just—just do yourself a favor and keep your distance.”

Steve and Bill both listen to his analysis and nod. He’d be delusional to say that they didn’t look at least a little bit skeptical of his claims but neither of them rebuttal. They simply listen intently and formulate some kind of plan in their heads.

Bill nods, “Alright, follow me. I’ll show you two what we got.”

Bill leads the two ‘men’ to his bedroom. He doesn’t waste any time with a tour and instead goes straight to the closet turning the light on once the doors open. The walk-in closet is filled with clothes, but behind each rack of polo shirts or dress pants, he spots the dark shade of metal. Bill pushes a line of sweaters out of the way and reveals an impressive collection of guns and ammunition. Weapons ranging from AR-style to shotguns to sidearms are all present. Ryan counts at least 5 of each variety. 

“Some of these are only for hunting small game, so we’ll probably have to stick to a higher caliber,” Bill explains as he points out a few guns.

“Caliber?” Ryan interrupts glancing between them in an attempt to gain some context on his own. He only receives confused looks as if the word's connotation should be blatantly obvious.

Steve rolls his eyes, though Bill takes him seriously, “Son, have you ever shot a gun before?”

Ryan shakes his head.

Bill sighs, “Alright, you’ve got a lot to learn if you wanna be able to shoot properly, but for now I think a 12 gauge shotgun with buckshot will do best: low barrier of entry and and intuitive reloading system.” 

He picks up a shotgun from the rack and hands it over to Ryan. The weight of the weapon surprises him at first, but he adjusts to it quickly. 

Bill continues, “I’ll show you how to use it later, but for now, just know that you need to point and shoot. There’ll be some kick back but you’re big enough to handle it.”

“And turn the safety off,” Ryan adds, that’s an important one.

Bill gives him a mildly confused look but ultimately disregards it.

“What about silencers?” Steve asks.

“Right. I have one for my 1911 and a few for my ARs but we shouldn’t rely on them too heavily. It muffles the sound a bit, but we don’t know how well these… zombies can hear.”

“What about a silencer for this shotgun?” Ryan asks.

“Doesn’t exist,” Bill asserts, “But if CQC…” he looks at Ryan, “Close-quarters combat isn’t a viable option then confrontations with these zombies should be avoided at all costs. If we get into a firefight, there is no way to de-escalate the situation.”

“I agree,” Steve says, “The next question is: where do we go? Let’s say we escape the city, where is the next place to go.”

“My sister and her husband live on a farm in the rural midwest, if we can make it that far, we should be able to ride out the storm.”

It’s an intriguing idea to go somewhere safe like a farm in the middle of nowhere, but there is one problem with that plan—he’d be stuck with Steve. Even if this plan is a good one, he’d rather take his chances on his own than get stuck with this annoying family. He’s ready to move on and find his own place in the world even if that world turns out to be a post-apocalyptic wasteland. 

“Alright, we’ll be up early tomorrow so Ryan you better go get some sleep. You can sleep on the couch for now. Steve and I have a few more things to discuss.”

Steve nods in agreement. 

He may be 18 but he still feels like he’s getting the child treatment. Things are said without him in earshot and everyone thinks it's only normal that he should be excluded from the adult conversation. It's not a huge concern, but it bugs him that it has to happen at this moment. Though he doesn’t care, they can do whatever they want, he’ll be gone soon enough. Or at least that’s what he keeps telling himself.

Ryan lies down on the couch and stares blankly at the ceiling fan. Was it really just a normal day this morning? Did he really hit Steve this very same morning? This entire zombie world only became active in his life yesterday. How times have changed. 

He allows his eyes to drift close as memories flash through his mind, so potently vivid his heart starts racing at the mere thought. His pulse returns to normal slowly, however, but only after he forces his memory to focus on clouds. The pretty shapes, their tranquil nature, and all the joyful times he used to spend looking up at them. Those clouds reminds him of people he cared about, people that were kind to him... Kind to him... Hopefully, the students in the school were able to escape with their lives. After all he did, it would be shame if none of them could make it out... if Blake's gratitude was wasted... if Chloe's smile... was...
