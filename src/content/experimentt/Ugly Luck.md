The military camp consists of three zones, excluding the parking lot and gate area. There is the civilian zone for uninfected individuals, the military only zone, and the treatment zone or as Ryan calls it, the dead zone. Everyone in the third zone is supposedly undergoing treatment, but Ryan already knows half of them are dead—he just hopes it's not at the hands of that creep. The civilian zone is outside with tents set up all around for people to sleep and stay in. Everyone in this zone is able to leave, but they all stay for the same reason: they have a loved one in the dead zone. 

Ryan, Steve, and Charlie all sit together in a small tent with two cots. Bill is currently out talking to some of the military officers, so they have to wait to get any word back from him. Steve taps his foot quickly against the concrete ground and presses his hands tightly together. Charlie lies down on one of the cots and pretends to sleep, they all know he’s awake. Ryan sits on the floor leaning against one of the beams holding the tent up. No one speaks, but rather waits for Bill to return with some good news.

Eventually, the waiting becomes tedious and Ryan finds the tension to be more unbearable than conversation, “This isn’t the best situation to be in.” He chuckles awkwardly while realizing that he’s being facetious.

Steve glares at him, “No shit Sherlock.”

Ryan rubs the back of his head, “I get that Karmen has social anxiety, but that—”

Steve cuts him off with considerable irritation, “Social anxiety?! That’s what you think this is?”

“Well yeah,” Ryan concludes a bit timidly. 

He can’t say that he knew Karmen well, but this aspect of her personality is pretty obvious. Maybe a little extreme, but what else could it be? She was never on any medication that he knew of.

Steve pinches the bridge of his nose as he leans forward, “That’s not social anxiety, that’s trauma.”

“Trauma?” Ryan repeats.

Steve lets out a long deep sigh and contemplates saying something. He shakes his head a bit in thought before readjusting to face Ryan head on.

“When she was a little girl, she had abusive parents. Parents, like mine, who only had a kid by mistake. Between the two of us, her upbringing was far worse. Like..." he struggles to connect the whole narrative, or more specifically, struggles to digest that he's actually articulating it, "When she was 14 years old she was sent to the hospital with multiple STDs. The worse part was that the hospital wasn’t a good one. They practiced… whatever it’s fucking called… malpractice? At the end of the day, they treated her terribly, and she developed a phobia for doctors and all medical personnel. I thought she would just go in like the rest of us, and then we could move on, but that was just a short-sighted farce. I should have known!”

Ryan remains stationary not knowing how to respond.

“Once she finally got out of that horrid place, she tried to make a life for herself. Just as most kids do, they follow in the footsteps of their parents while thinking they are ‘being themselves’," Ryan can't say for certain, but Steve sounds like he resonates with that reasoning for more than he lets on, "She's been abused, neglected, raped for fuck's sake..." he takes a deep breath, "I met her about 15 years ago while passing through a rough part of town. She ran up to my car after being chased down by a group of, what I assume, were gangbangers or some other shit like that. Before they got their hands on her, she pleaded for help through my window… What was I supposed to fuckin do… of course I got out and helped her.”

“Against the whole group?” Ryan wonders.

Steve nods, “They were just a few punks, by that point I had already left the military.”

Military. Ryan repeats the word multiple times inside his head. Steve was in the military. Yet another thing he just didn’t know about him.

Steve continues, “There were only three or four of them, so I knocked a few on their asses and broke an arm. Easy enough to say they scattered after that… I didn’t know what to do with a sickly girl who had been through shit. I guess… I had a soft spot for people who have the world beating them down. So, I invited to take her somewhere safer and just drop her off. Simple enough. I drove her out of that part of town and we got talking. One thing led to the next, and we were dating soon after that. Married after that. I promised her that I would never let people take advantage of her again. I would protect her…”

Steve trails off.

“But…” Ryan attempts.

Steve returns to focus and shifts to a more comfortable position.

“I know what you're thinking...but I care... about Karmen... and Charlie. I may screw up... a lot, but you've gotta believe me, I'm trying my best. She’s been through so much so the least I could do is be there for her. It took three years to get her to accept the idea of wanting a child… I’ve never hit Karmen once the entire time I’ve known her.”

“What about—” Ryan starts.

“Not. Once.” Steve interjects, “I felt horrible when I pushed her the other day. I wasn’t thinking straight… yeah there were other reasons, but I was never angry with Karmen in any way. I just slipped up. And now I’ve slipped up again and Karmen is on the other side of that fucking gate… I failed to protect her. I might as well be a failure outright.”

So he admits to being a failure. Ryan had always believed that those words would be so satisfying to hear, but after that story, Ryan can’t bring himself to be happy about it in the slightest. Honestly, he feels bad for both Steve and Karmen. Ryan swears to himself that he’ll be nicer to Karmen from now on. Never in his entire life has Ryan felt this bad for someone other than himself. To think all it took was to have someone else's struggles laid out before him. He's discussed by the world and by the things it does to people.

Suddenly, Bill enters the tent and looks around the room before locking eyes with Steve. The two men exchange a few words silently, but it is clearly not quite enough. 

“So they’re not letting her out?” Steve says clenching her fists. 

Bill shakes his head, “Apparently, the estimated wait time until they are healthy again is a week if not longer. The only thing that doesn’t make sense, is that she wasn’t showing signs of symptoms.”

Charlie sits up on the cot and looks over with his eyes half closed, “Is mom going to be stuck here for that long?”

Steve hops over to his knee right next to Charlie’s cot, “Don’t worry, we’ll get mom back safe and sound and be right out of here.”

“What is going on?” Charlie asks, “We have to leave home for a long time and now the army men have us stuck in these tents.”

Bill crouches down as well, “Everything will be just dandy. Are you hungry?”

Charlie shakes his head as Bill continues, “You’re not hurting anywhere, right? You have a bed and a tent. Life isn’t great, but it’s not terrible… right?”

Charlie nods his head again with childish skepticism while Steve gives him a hug. 

Ryan picks his hand up and looks down on it. He closes it slightly a few times before relaxing it. He wonders what a hug must feel like. Ever since his mom died, he’s never felt the soothing touch of another. It sounds really edgy to say that in his head, but that’s just how he views it—nobody ever touches him. Perhaps its the inverse of what Karmen must feel about touching. A deep somber, sentimental feeling rises in his heart, but that disappears as a military officer opens the flap of the tent.

“Steve Hughes, Willian Becker, you two please come with me.” The officer doesn’t wait for a response, he just turns around and walks off. With no other option, Bill and Steve follow behind. Ryan and Charlie remain staring at each other in the dead silence that follows.

---

Nearly an hour passes before the two men return and by that time Ryan and Charlie had already gone and got lunch from a nice lady distributing rations. They arrived back at the tent to find Steve and Bill discussing something in the tent.

“Dad, you’re back!” Charlie exclaims running into the tent.

Steve forces a smile for his son, “I am buddy, did you really miss me that much.”

Steve gives Charlie a quick hug and then returns to a more serious demeanor. 

“So, what happened?” Ryan asks.

Bill waves his hand indicating that he’ll go first, “The officer who came and got us said that he pulled a few strings. He and I were friends back when I was in the military. Once he saw Steve and I’s records of past military experience, he agreed to help us out.”

“And?” Ryan gestures for them to go on.

Steve picks up where Bill left off saying, “There’s a man back in town who the military needs. They are under strict orders not to send any ground troops into the city, but they never said that civilians can’t go back.”

Ryan studies the man’s face with fierce skepticism, “So they need you guys to capture a person? For what in return?”

“They’ll let Karmen go early. The procedure says she has to stay a week, but she’s not even showing symptoms yet. The quarantined zone over there is a very dangerous place. People are turning into zombies on a daily basis and the head officer wasn't sure they had the manpower to contain it much longer. We need to get Karmen out as fast as we can.”

“Yes, that place over there is going to get her killed,” Bill adds, “But Steve, are you sure you want to work for the military again? Especially after what happened back then.”

Ryan takes notice of the last few words, “back then?”

Steve waves his hand, “Don’t worry about it, for now we have a way to save Karmen. That’s what’s important.”

Ryan studies the two ex-soldiers. The course of action seems pretty straightforward now, but the question remains of how he’ll break from the group. He still plans on going off on his own. If Steve and Bill both go off and find this man, he’ll need to stay here with Charlie to watch over him. In essence, he’ll be stuck here until they get back. He’s not about to take the risk of waiting for them, especially given the proximity to the military. With his luck, he’ll have to break out of a zombie infested camp if he doesn’t make a break for it now.

“What was his name again?” Steve asks, looking over to Bill.

Bill thinks for a second before responding, “I remember his face, but I think they said his name was Seth Heimes or something. Must be German to have a last name like that.”

Ryan freezes. Of all the people that the military could be targeting, why does it have to be a man he’s already roped in with. Though maybe his connection with the guy can be his saving grace to get out.

“I’ll go with you,” Ryan expresses calmly and confidently.

Steve shakes his head slightly while Bill shoots him a confused but surprised look.

“Do you have any idea what you are saying?” Bill counters, “This isn’t some simple job, we’re going to become dogs of the military for a day. You don’t want to live that life. Besides, someone has to stay here with Charlie, that’s non-negotiable.”

“It has nothing to do with the military,” Ryan argues, opening his stance and broadening his shoulders.

“This isn’t something you can do kid, I’m not trying to be a jerk,” Bill opens his hands revealing his palms to try and deescalate the situation.

Ryan doesn’t back down, and glances out the tent to check if the coast is clear before returning to the task at hand. 

“Mr. Heimes was my principle,” Ryan explains while receiving unconvinced looks from the adults, “Before the school was locked down he talked to me…” Ryan reaches into his pocket and thankfully finds the key he left there, “and he gave me this key. It’s only because of this master key that I was able to escape the school. It’s clear that he’s not working with the military… and… and I’m special to him or something.”

“Hm… that does change things,” Bill speculates, placing his hand on his scruffy chin, "You'd be able to recognize him better than us."

“Shut up!” Steve announces as if he didn’t hear anything Ryan just said, “You’re not going and that’s final.”

Ryan scowls at Steve. To think that he believed there was something good inside that man, but he’ll just shoot you down the second you go against him. Every bit of compassion he had for him is gone now. He thinks that Ryan is just some punky kid who wants to be an adult, maybe even making up stories to sound official, but he doesn’t know anything. There’s more to life than just living, sometimes you need to take a risk and get out there. This is one of those times when Ryan’s dreams are being pushed against an opposing force. 

Then, in accordance with his dreadfully bad luck, Charlie begins to cough violently. The once energetic boy is now on the floor coughing his lungs out completely overtaken by the spontaneous outburst.

Steve is quick to react, picking him up and charging out of the tent. He needs medical attention immediately. Ryan only prays that he isn’t infected like Karmen; those two were the only good parts of this family.

“This is really bad,” Bill says under his breath while wiping his hand across his forehead. 

“At least now I don’t have to stay back with him,” Ryan admits, trying to suppress any flaring emotion with pure logistics.

Bill snaps to him, “Is that really your first thought, boy?!”

Ryan is taken aback by the sudden change of character from the middle-aged military vet. His usual calm and civilized personality is now replaced by one much more hostile and authoritative. It’s just like back in the woods only this time he’s the target of the shifting personality.

“Karmen is sick and now Charlie may be. How can you be thinking about yourself at a time like this,” Bill scolds Ryan's shoulders, “You don’t have to like them, but they’re your family. Hell, you don’t even look fazed by the fact that your little brother may be on the brink of death.”

Ryan pushes Bill away from him. He definitely does feel something, but crying about some acquaintances of his would not make the situation better. He’ll just grin and bear it—exactly like he did in the school, “I get it! Don’t patronize me. I never wanted to be here anyway so get off my back.”

“So what?! They took care of you for two years and this is how you treat them in return… Maybe I gave you too much credit… to think I had high hopes for you.”

Bill pushes the tent flap out of the way and storms off in the same direction as the medical tent. The tent returns to a state of silence with only the ambient background noise to give him any comfort. 

Ryan sits down on the cot and puts his head in his hands. 

Is he really just a selfish teenager? What about the time he helped open the school and give the students inside a fight chance? But that led to the death of Mr. and Mrs. Jacobson. What about the time he agreed to help the Hughes out of the city? That leads to his current situation. Why does he have to be the way that he is? All he wants is to leave and be on his own—so he won’t be judged by the people around him for being a failure. Having to deal with all of these other people is so incredibly exhausting. He doesn’t want to have to put up with them anymore, they only criticize him. Every time he attempts to explain his point of view or express his emotions any further than surface level, he gets in a fight or gets yelled at. He’s never had a conversation with anyone about anything that truly matters to him. Maybe he wasn’t all in on the idea of leaving the Hughes family for selfish reasons, he just liked the idea of finally being able to make his own decision. 

Back when he was about 14, he was under the care of another low income foster family. They weren’t poor, but they just barely made it past the requirements to foster a child. Honestly, they only fostered him because it looked good on their record, and they would be able to send their other six children to college easier. They already had six, another one in the mix wouldn’t change much. Alas, he lived with the family for about a year. In that time all of his ‘siblings’ would constantly make fun of him for being introverted. They were raised by parents who were really out there, and who valued communication skills above all else. His inherent quietness made it difficult for him to express how he felt all the time and that ultimately led to the family degrading him as a human. When he had a bad day and punched a wall, they all teased him and asked why he was so angry all the time. When he was feeling playful and energetic, he would say something he found funny, though a little awkward. Those ideas were met with a cold wall of intolerance. Every thought he had was not allowed to leave his mouth unless it was refined and made clear sense. It didn’t even need to be a logical thought, but he had to be ready to back up what he said with another witty comment or some rationale. One time two of his ‘siblings’ were fighting over which kid their teacher liked the most. Neither one had any reasonable arguments, but their parents took interest in the conversation because it was loud, and they accentuated their words. Maybe, he didn’t get along with that family because they knew how to steal attention and that attention was the thing that caused Ryan hardships. Because of them, he never felt comfortable speaking his mind or letting anyone see it. Now those days are coming back to him as he attempts to explain that he doesn’t want to be chained down anymore. He needs a sense of freedom, not because he deserves it, but because he’s tired of being hurt by those around him. He wants to be alone.

Ryan finds himself lying on the cot for almost an hour before Steve walks back into the tent… without Charlie.

Steve glares down at him, “You coming or not?”

Ryan sits up and lets his feet fall to the floor. He’s still a bit shaken up internally, but he suppresses those thoughts as he looks up to Steve, “What do you mean?”

Steve shakes his head and looks back out of the tent. He has to force the words out of his mouth as Ryan can feel the rage and self loathing emanating from each word, “Charlie is on the other side now. Are you coming to help capture Seth Heimes or not?”

Ryan feels the gun still on his hip and stands up putting his shoes on his messy socks. As he rises, he finds that his body is physically shaking—a visible aftereffect of his mental battle. He clears his throat in an attempt to draw attention away from that weakness, “I’m right behind you.”

Once he finds Heimes, he can break away from Bill and Steve. He knows he's screwed up, but at the very least, he can do this much to make amends. Every negative emotion he feels towards them can be quenched once he's gone so he'll only have to bear it a little longer. After that, he’ll finally be able to escape this dumpster fire of a childhood.
