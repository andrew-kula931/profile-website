When Ryan comes too, he's met with the dull glow of peaking sun. His body acks with bitter soreness and his solitary desire is for just a bit more sleep. It's upsetting that it has to be a weekday, he'd much prefer it to finally be the lazy weekend already. Alas, he’ll just call out for the first hour of school and go in late, he’s done it before so why can’t he do it again. Besides, Karmen will understand given how much his body hurts—especially his shoulder. What did he do the day before for such a furious pain to wreak havoc on his body?

That’s when his eyes burst open as he springs to life. His eyes rush around in a daze as his hands fumble around to find something to grab onto. He eventually calls down when he finds himself sitting in the lobby of the town hall staring out at a warm amber sun. Clouds hover over the horizon reflecting the soothing rays all around forming the distant horizon into a mosaic of color. 

Ryan looks around further and finds Steve sitting with his back against the wall staring out to the sunrise. He takes notice of Ryan’s awakening but hesitates before saying anything.

“So… you feeling all right?” he asks unconfidently. 

Ryan places a hand on his shoulder and finds an unfamiliar shirt on. He doesn't question it as he drops his gaze to the floor. He has no room to talk. Bill is dead and now he’s going to die soon. The constant burning in his shoulder is a perpetual reminder of his immanent doom. 

Ryan takes a deep breath, “I’m not a zombie yet… Maybe it’s a good sign.”

No response is given.

Ryan looks back to the sunrise once again trying to force himself to be content with this being the last thing he ever sees. Sure it's just some sun on the distant horizon with a few pretty clouds, but it makes his heart ache to think this was it. He can't... he can't accept this... does he really have to?

“I’m sorry,” Steve finally says, causing Ryan to snap back to reality, “I’ve failed yet again.”

Ryan gives him a weakly twisted look, “I have no room to talk.”

Steve throws his hands up into the air haphazardly and slumps down even more, “I couldn’t save my wife or son. My best friend is dead. Now my stupid foster son is headed to an early grave too. All the while I'm sitting here having to endure it all.”

The part about Ryan makes his heart to sink. It’s not like he didn’t already know, but just the fact that it was said out loud really stings.

“I just,” Steve continues, “I’m a god-damn screw up.”

Steve pushes himself to his feet and walks over to a desk in one corner of the lobby and picks up a piece of blank paper from it, “Back when I was in the military, I used to be a hotshot. I thought that I could do anything if I believed it was the right thing to do. Looking back, I was just a fool... a naive fool… you know, Bill and I were like brothers back then... it's the closest we ever were. Whenever I would do something irrational, he would make a quick joke and knock me straight. The whole barracks respected him like hell I got to leach off that. Despite how reckless my sorry ass was, Bill vouched for me... every damn time.”

He smiles at the paper in his hands like he a document solidifying his experiences, “Eventually, we were released from boot camp and sent to the field. That was the start of a two year tour through the Middle East and some surrounding areas. I can’t say it was fun, but I did really learn to trust my brothers who fought alongside me. But shit like that doesn't last…”

He sets the paper down and transitions away from the desk. He eventually ends up leaning against the wall and starring out at the sunrise, “We were tasked with a very important mission: one of the enemy leaders was crossing through a valley near where we were stationed. My squad was the closest available unit so we were tasked with capturing him… alive… The plan went perfectly all the way until we were ready to extract…”

Steve glances up at the ceiling as if imagining a scene as large as life itself, “There we were—sitting on the roof of a tall building waiting for the helicopter and then… and then that bastard starts trash-talking me. ‘Who do you think you are?’,” Steve tries to impersonate a middle eastern accent but doesn’t perform very well with only makes his voice crack and a pause while he relives the painful memory, “he started with petty insults, like sassy tolder, but soon he was insulting the squad, our families, and everything in between. I already wanted to beat him alive for commanding his troops to kill so many of my brothers, but Bill stopped me… just like he always did back then. That’s when he said something to me that I’ll never forget. He said, ‘Your father probably beat you and told you to follow orders. I’m sure he’s glad that his parenting worked’... That’s when I lost it… I lost control and shot the guy right through the leg. I intended to just cause him considerable pain, but his damn artery got in the way... he bled out a few minutes later. It’s the most impulsive mistake I have ever made… and I swore to myself that it was the last time… I wanted that to be the last day of my hotshot lifestyle.”

Steve’s body slowly slumps down until he’s returned to a seated position against the wall with two dead eyes starring off into an amber blaze, “It’s safe to say that the military didn’t like that one bit. Dishonorable discharge was the best they were willing to offer me. I took it and left… I left Bill and the rest of my squad… it was the second time I walked away from everything... and damnit it wasn't the last."

Steve takes a deep breath, “After the military, I had nowhere to go. My family had disowned me years prior and I had no money to get anything. I ended up landing the lowest job and virtually nothing else to my name. About a year after that I saved up for a good ole '96 Subaru. I thought my life was finally on track once I could pay my own bills and not rely on old friends to drive me around, but nothing really changed. My life still sucked. At one point I lost my job and got swindled into accepting a shady loan. Really I only took the loan because my car needed repairs I couldn’t afford, but of course my dumb ass took it and got even deeper in debt. I just wanted to end it all… you know. And—and I wasn't just want it, I was planning on it."

Ryan’s blood runs cold at the thought of something he’s been offered in the past.

“When the day came, I was driving home for the last time just sight-seeing before it was all over and that’s when I met Karmen. I don’t know why I talked to her for so long, I’m not sure why she listened to me talk, but something about her… something about her gave me a new reason to live. You already know what happened after that. A couple years later we moved out here and away from the big city. Then I found out that Bill lived here so I went to meet him and… and…” 

Steve starts to tear up.

“Bill greeted me with open arms. He didn’t blame me for what happened and was even willing to continue being my friend. Even though everyone else turned their backs on me, he still cared and wouldn’t abandon me. Guy should've hated me... but he didn't… rest in peace brother…”

“I’m sorry,” is all Ryan can respond with as he uncomfortably shifts his weight. For some reason, this situation feels more foreign to him than walking through a military checkpoint.

“But don’t you get it?” Steve continues, “Bill was always that way. He is just a better man than me. I failed him back then and I failed him now... and all he got was an early death. It should've been me, not him."

Ryan stands up in protest, “You still have a family to take care of!”

Steve shakes his head pathetically, “So what… I don’t make their lives better no matter how much I try.”

“Don’t say that,” Ryan tries to protest with no ground to support the comment.

Steve looks up at him spitefully, “You know what Ryan! I’ve always hated you.”

Ryan sighs unwilling to go down this route but finding it to be the most truthful thing he's heard, “Yeah, I know.”

Steve looks up slightly with a glare as he crosses his arms, “Bill told me what you said back there in the tent. How you don’t care about any of us. You only stuck around because of our deal.”

“Like you ever cared about me,” Ryan antagonizes, debating walking out and never coming back after the man’s words strike a nerve, though he wouldn’t make it far at this point, “You never batted an eye when I was struggling. It was always about your problems and I became an afterthought. Do you even understand how it feels to lose everything you ever loved before you were even able to understand what was going on!”

“You know what pisses me off the most,” Steve grows increasingly irritated, seemingly disregarding Ryan’s point of view yet again.

“What?” Ryan asks, annoyed with both the man before him and the idea that he’s changed.

Steve takes a long, slow breath, “I was the exact same way.”

Ryan stops cold as he stares intently at Steve as he proceeds to explain in a loud, rash voice.

“When I was kid, I was just like you. Bullied, neglected, nobody cared for me, not even my worthless parents. I began to resent the world and wish I could… just be free. Maybe that’s why I went to the military, maybe it's because my parents disowned me, maybe it's because I just didn't know what else to do. Either way, I hated everything and everyone and all I had to show for it was lousy a gambling addiction."

Steve slowly stands up and shifts around to stand behind the desk continuing to make his monologue dramatic yet sincere, “When I was 14 years old, I finally did it. I stood up to my abusive father and alcoholic mother. Do you know what happened? Huh?! They starved me for three days… Sure, it was illegal, but I was so scared out of my god-damn mind that I never even thought about telling the cops. Hell, my father was the cop. I knew that if word got out about my parents, I'd be the one taking the fall..." he pinches the bridge of his nose before staring at Ryan with wet eyes, "Imagine living in a world where you're afraid to go home... My childhood was a shit show and I thought that becoming an adult would free me of that… Now every time I see you, all I see is my younger, stupider self staring back. It fills me with so much rage I just want to strangle you, because you're screwing the same ways I did and… I…” he struggles to get the word out. 

“I can't stand it!” he slams his fist onto the desk. 

“There you have it… I can't stand the sight of you. The only reason you live with us is because somebody offered me twenty thousand to take you under our wing for two years. I didn’t question it because I still had to pay off my gambling debts. To this day I still work 80 hours a week not for myself but to pay off the debts so lown-sharks don’t come and take my kid, rape my wife, and… take another family away from you,” a few tears start to roll down his face, “I just wish I could do it all over and apologize for my mistakes. I love my family, but they deserve better than a bastard like me…”

Steve collapses to the ground and sobs quietly into his hands. To be frank, this is the first time Ryan has ever seen a man cry, much less Steve. 

Ryan’s head falls until he’s staring back down at himself, the same person who also failed others. Steve was right, he just wanted to be free and wasn’t even thinking about what that could mean for himself or for those around him—it was a selfish endeavor. Maybe he doesn’t even have a right to stand here, alive, when so many others have died meaningless deaths. Why should he get to breathe air when all he cares about is himself. Maybe this is just plain ole karma. But… he can’t leave a legacy like that. He needs… he needs to do something right for once.

“I’m sorry too,” Ryan says sympathetically, "I could have done better."

Steve raises his eyes revealing the puffy red cheeks he concealed.

“We're pretty pathetic, huh,” Ryan continues needing to take time between each sentence to ensure he’s capable of compiling the next, “But how would that make Bill look… or Karmen or Charlie,” Ryan lifts his hand up slightly before hesitating. He knows he’s feeling some inclination to do something, but his entire life has honed his skills of restraint which reveals itself to be a much larger barrier than he ever expected. His hand begins to fall back to his side, but then all those years of starring up at the bedroom ceiling in silence begin to flood back into the foreground of his min, and he decides nothing he does now can be worse than all that time wasted. And so he steps up to Steve and holds a hand out to him with new found motivation, “We can’t claim to be good people, but let's go do something right for a change… Let’s—Let’s go save our family.”

Steve stares at his hand for a moment contemplating life, his eyes waver as his own arm shakes in disagreement of whether to continue, but in the end, he accepts.

Luck continued to frown upon them that day, so their efforts for finding a car returned futile results. After almost an hour searching the nearby area, only keyless cars turn up so they make the decision to begin walking towards the military camp. Another hour passes, and they find themselves strolling down a freeway—still on foot. Every car is either stuck in miscellaneous traffic jams or sitting on the side of the road. Every few hundred yards a zombie will slap its face against a window and attempt to break out but never to any success. A couple of times they have to take out a zombie standing in the way, but Steve usually does the hard work. 

Each time a zombie dies Steve will casually explain how he killed it. One such time he stabs a zombie in the temple with his knife and curses at how hard the skull is. He tells Ryan that people’s bodies don’t fall apart like in movies. Dispatching a zombie can easily go wrong if the soft, vulnerable kill shots aren’t hit outright. Ryan can’t say he understands everything he heard but the simple idea to make shots count really sinks in quickly. This lesson is reinforced as the next zombie Steve encounters tackles him to the ground after he misses the kills hot. Ryan tries to help, but the former soldier is able to stab the knife through the bottom of the zombie's jaw and throw it off. After a quick inspection, Steve finds himself to be unharmed so they journey on as if nothing happened.

“So you’ve never shot a gun before yesterday?” Steve asks as they pace past a few overturned traffic cones.

“No, but for some reason it feels natural,” Ryan explains, gesturing with his shotgun, "I can't explain it, but I just feel comfortable with it... I guess."

Steve offers him a sort of smirk as he shrugs his shoulders. He scans the area ahead and quickly spots something in the distance as his eyebrow raises with a concerning idea dawning. He turns to Ryan and holds out his AR.

“Take my gun and shoot that bird up ahead,” he says far more casually than Ryan is used to.

Ryan stares at the gun with a dumbfounded look. The gun isn’t the problem but the nonchalant demeanor of a man who has never trusted him before. Sure things seem to be changing, but target practice, right now?

“What?” Steve asks shaking the weapon, “Take it.”

“Shouldn’t I just use my pistol,” Ryan argues, finding that he’s more likely to use his own weapon in combat.

“Come on,” Steve persists shaking the gun a bit more vigorously this time, “Take it.”

Ryan inspects the man once more but ultimately complies and sticks the butt of the gun into his shoulder. He then closes one eye and leaning over like he believes he's supposed to. He focuses on the scope and immediately notices that he can’t see a thing.

“You see it don’t you?” Steve asks, “Not every sight is as simple as ridicule placement and shoot. This one changes depending on where you position your head.”

Ryan looks over to him confused about the contents of his explanation.

“Try pulling your eye further away from the scope,” he says.

As soon as the directions are followed, the fog of confusion is lifted. The environment ahead comes into view as soon as he scoots his eye further away, but if he travels too far that narrow tunnel of vision collapses in on itself. Moving his eye side to side also changes the field of view by bouncing the circular tunnel to the corners of the scope.

“Are all scopes like this?” Ryan asks, continuing to play with his view.

Steve crosses his arms smugly, “No, some are much easier to use, but I like this one. It’s traditionally used for range shooting, but I just don’t use the scope if anything gets too close.”

Ryan aims in once more and places the newly found plus-sign onto the bird about a hundred yards away. The pitch black crow sits on the hood of a van simply admiring its surroundings; a simple yet peaceful lifestyle. Ryan puts his finger on the trigger and takes a deep breath in.

“Wait wait wait!” Steve exclaims right before Ryan can finish activating his firearm, “You can’t hold your left hand that close otherwise the recoil is going to kill you.”

Steve marches forward and pulls Ryan’s hand away from the back of the gun and deeper down to the area next to the magazine. Then he kicks Ryan’s back leg into a deeper stance and bends his knees slightly.

After adjusting Ryan's leg, Steve stands up and steps back, “If your stance is bad, you won’t fare so well either. You'll probably topple over.”

Ryan nods his head and aims once more intending to hit his target this time. The rifle’s plus lands directly on the head of the bird and he builds up his courage enough to finally pull the trigger. 

The bird gets to fly away alive after Ryan officially misses his shot. At the sound of the gun shot he snaps his head over to Steve, “This is going to draw attention to us.”

Steve chuckles, “You’re not wrong, but this is important. If we get back to camp and find it in a bad state, I need to know I'm not going to get shot in the back. Now, next time you shoot, make sure to squeeze the trigger slowly. Pulling it too fast can throw off your aim.”

Steve promptly marches forward past a few more cars with Ryan in tow. His small boost of happiness slowly fades back to its usually sorrowful composure. As his instructorly pride fades, so does his facade and they walk on.

They continue walking down the freeway dodging around piles of dead bodies—the ones lucky enough to not become zombies. A single car they pass catches Ryan’s attention. The unimpressive sedan would ordinarily struggle to warrant any attention unless it were driving backwards on the road. The thing that made this car so special is a collection of marks around the car. The driver’s side window is completely absent with shattered glass scattered both within and outside the car. On the exterior of the door are streaks of blood originating near the handle and trailing all the way to the hood. Resting on the hood of the vehicle is a stiff corpse filling the air with the dreadfully pungent odor of rot and decay. Ryan pinches his nose to avoid the putrid stench but can't help but notice the litany of gashes enveloping the dead body. Across their arms are deep lacerations from which the blood has long run cold. The corpse's head is bent upward with an agonized scream petrified into its visage. Last, and disturbing enough to make Ryan feel wheezy, is a flat torso, no thicker than one or two inches. An impact crater presses so deeply through their chest that the metal of the hood can be seen, their organs create wave-like impressions around the circumference of the impact zone. Whatever hit the poor bastard was no car, and no small force either. Ryan stalls as he finds his body viscerally mortified by the sight, but Steve knocks him out of it by walking right on by as stoicly as one could. 

It takes a couple of minutes before Ryan's able to perish the thought and focus on the task at hand. He's returns to reality just in time to spot two zombies standing up from behind a five car pileup. Steve preps two knives and throws the smaller, more versatile one to him. 

“I’ll get the one on the right, you take left,” he commands as he rushes forward to confront his target.

Ryan hesitates as he juggles the knife given to him. Eventually he grasps his barrings and grips the knife tightly before narrowing his eyes on the zombie beginning to run towards him. His breathing has suddenly found itself in a frantic state but he tries to slow his racing thoughts. He recalls the time he killed two zombies at the start of this whole ordeal. He hones in on the fear he felt, the shock, the grit he displayed, but most importantly the courage and perseverance. The zombie stretches its mangled, bloody arms with flesh barely hanging on. Underneath its rotting flesh is irregularly shaped bones creating a sort of incompletely shell running down both arms and around its chest. The armor fragmented at best, but its presence is sparse enough that haphazard placement of an attack with likely be useless. One vulnerability that he spots, however, is the zombie’s neck where the shell has yet to form.

The zombie approaches to an arm's length away and attempts to grab Ryan’s head. Ryan avoids this by leaning to the side, sliding forward quickly, and landing his knife directly into the front of its neck.

Ryan pulls the knife out quickly and kicks the zombie back watching it twitch and gargle its own blood as its false second life fades from existence. Killing others isn’t something he ever saw himself doing, but some sort of numbness arises in his body as the corpse stops moving. Maybe it is some killer instinct or desensitization or maybe something else, though he the moral dilemma he's always faced feels strangely less burdensome all of the sudden. It tries not to question it as the burning in his shoulder reminds him of where he stands. 

That’s when fatigue, or rather sickness and stress hits him. His body forces him to collapse to the hard, unwavering concrete. Steve arrives at his side asking him the basic questions, “Are you okay?” and “What happened?” but he’s unable to properly respond. Then Ryan’s stomach roars to life and he is forced to comply. The bite mark on his shoulder continues to burn up as if set on fire while his body starts to shake uncontrollably. Could this be what a seizure? Is this what it feels like? Is he supposed to be able to think clearly while this is happening? He just hopes this isn't it... it can't be.

Steve pulls Ryan over to his side and allows him to spit out the rest of his stomach’s contents. It takes a minute or so for his body to calm down, but eventually he returns to his senses and regains control over his body.

Ryan looks up at Steve to see his eyes closed and eyebrows lowered. His fist clenched and everything else just as tight. He wears a look of painful defeat even though Ryan is the one experiencing the seizure. 

“I think I’m okay now,” Ryan begins as he coughs through strained breaths, “I just need a second.”

Steve nods his head a few times in silence before grabbing one of Ryan’s arms and propping it around his shoulder. He then assists Ryan as they carefully make their way to the shoulder of the road. A small wind break of trees lies just beyond the pavement which functions as great cover from the strengthening sun.

They sit down and grab some water as Steve rummages through his bag for some snacks. They don’t have much, but they weren’t far from the military base so perhaps things are looking up. The blockade should be just up ahead as is. Steve remains silent as he finishes off a granola bar and takes a large gulp of his water bottle, emptying the container.

“We shouldn’t be far now,” Ryan says practically.

Steve remains silent.

“I think we can make it in time,” Ryan says, trying not to sound as defeated as Steve looks. 

“I’m sorry,” Steve says yet again.

Ryan rolls his eyes getting sick of Steve’s apologizes, “For what? It’s not your fault that I got bitten.”

Steve throws his water bottle onto his bag and stands up. He throws his hands into his cargo pants' pockets and begins to pace back and forth, “It doesn't make me feel any less guilty. I knew bring you along was a mistake,” he runs a hand through his short but graying hair and then through his short beard. 

Ryan sighs, “Not everything is your fault, we’ve all made mistakes.”

“No…” Steve starts, “When I accepted you as my foster son, I took the responsibility of taking care of you. This isn’t how it’s supposed to work.”

“It’s fine, really. I don’t think zombie apocalypses were covered in the contract. Besides, if anything, I deserve a fate like this…” Ryan trails off thinking about how little he’s done with his life.

At the very least, Steve did things with his life. He overcame a difficult upbringing and even fought in a war. He then settled down, started a family, and tried his very best to overcome his past failure. Even today, he struggles with countless things but at least is trying to make things right. In the meantime, Ryan just blames everything on others while trying to run away from his pain like it’s the solitary cure. Ryan was wrong before, Steve wasn’t the only problem in the family.

“Stop!” Steve asserts, “I may not be your real dad and you might not be my real son, but I still care about you.”

Ryan is taken aback, “I thought you couldn't stand the sight of me? Brought back childhood trauma.”

Steve shakes his head and kneels down closer to him, “No that's—I hated..." he searches for the right words with a quick glance around, "I hated myself okay. It wasn't you... it was me. I hated how you reminded me of my mirror... and how... how I was repeating the same path that my father took when he beat me to the dirt… To be honest, you're a good kid. I knew when you snuck out you weren’t doing anything bad and the complaints from school were all bullshit. You were doing your best and I just watched.”

For once, Ryan recognizes the truth of the matter: they two of a kind. They aren’t enemies, they’re just at different stages of life. He loathed himself with was probably the reason he loathed Steve. He snuck out, Steve drank, honestly they were both running from something they were too afraid to face. Ryan's walls are tall which means crying doesn't come easily but for the first time he honestly doesn't think it is such a bad idea. The concept is strictly foreign to him, but maybe if there's still time when the job's done. He just might indulge this attitude.

“I… I guess…” Ryan tries, “Nah, never mind.”

Steve returns to his spot next to his bag and pulls out an old altoid can. The painting on the outside of the can has long since warn off leaving only the metal shell in its place. When Steve opens it, an assortment of black, chalky substances poof out. He then grabs a few of them and places them on the ground before him. 

“The grass is wet enough that this shouldn’t be a big issue,” Steve says under his breath before returning focus to Ryan, “Let me show you how to start a fire.”

Ryan exchanges a confused glance.

“I know it’s kind of a bad time, but you need a break anyway don’t you?” Steve asks, “...just… let me do this for you. I don’t know what else I can offer you.”

From there, Steve begins to show Ryan how to light a fire the tried and true way. He explains how there are multiple stages to starting the fire. First, you need something to start the fire, then you need kindling to grab that fire and begin to burn. Then tinder, small breaches or whatnot, will light a longer-lasting fire. Finally, the fuel will burn the longest and brightest and be the primary fuel source for the fire. Steve makes it look really easy, but Ryan can tell that the process can be screwed up easily. Before long the entire fire is both started and put out safely without anything unintended combusting.

“You ready to go now?” Steve asks after packing all of his stuff back in his bag. A strange satisfaction has spontaneously edged itself into his tone as he gets up.

Ryan pushes himself to his feet, finding it much harder than ever before, “Yes, I think we better get going.”

They head back to the freeway and continue on their journey passing empty car after empty car. By the time the sun passes its zenith, they make it to the military blockade.

Steve pulls Ryan behind a car and ducks down, only being able to view ahead through the back windshield of the car. 

“What is going on?” Ryan questions trying to get a better look at what lies ahead.

“Stay down,” Steve whispers, pulling his gun from his back stiffly and uncomfortably as if in shock, “There’s something ahead.”

Ryan peaks around the car ever so slightly to sneak a peak at what has Steve spooked. Then the monstrosity shows its ugly face to their position. A ten-foot bony behemoth towers on top of an Abraham tank. Its arms, while not massively thick, bristle with spikes protruding out of various spots along the back of each arm. Its feet talon-like feet wrap around the tank’s barrel creating a small indent in the metal. Its grotesque torso looks to be an assortment of old organs thrown around and forcibly mangled together with bone sutures. The hands of the thing are thick heavy claws, a stark contrast to the much thinner arms. Of all facets of the behemoth, the thing that truly shakes Ryan to the bone is the monster's ghostly visage. Its face has no definable features whatsoever save two wide, bloodshot eyes looking independently of one another. This is no mere zombie—this is an abomination running the line between nightmare and reality.

“Let’s quietly backtrack our steps and take the long way through the forest,” Steve explains, keeping his voice as quiet and controlled as possible. 

Ryan nods his head nervously and begins to crawl away from the car they crouched behind. He only makes it a few feet before his worn-out body takes a turn for the worst and he slips against a car knocking the side of his shotgun into its metal body. The subsequent clank it makes causes Ryan’s heart stop for quite a while as he prays the monster doesn’t hear it.

Three gunshots ring out right next to him as Steve stands up. Already engaged in combat, the monster returns a demonic, blood-curling shriek. Loud stomps converge on their location while Steve continues to fire off a few rounds. The commotion offers Ryan enough adrenaline to sit up and look back through the car's window.

The monster charges towards them at a speed much faster than either of them could hope to travel on foot. In a quick, last-ditch effort, Ryan grabs his shotgun off the ground and points it at the monstrosity. He places the butt of the gun onto his shoulder and makes his best effort to get the gun aimed at the correct spot. He knows he doesn’t have much experience but pulls the trigger regardless and hopes for the best. The buckshot pellets fly right into the face of the monster and primarily miss, but one such pellet lands directly in the left eye of the monster. In response to the sudden loss of sight, it rears back in pain covering its eyes with its clawed hands.

Steve doesn’t hesitate as he rushes over and pulls Ryan to his feet pointing at the blockade ahead. Tall metal pillars lie further down the road with a barbed wire fence filling the gaps to prevent traffic access, not to mention that line of spikes before then. A single car had driven itself past the road spikes, slamming into the sandbags and sturdy fence line creating a small hole only big enough to fit a person if they squeezed. Steve focuses on that as he practically drags Ryan behind him.

The behemoth scratches at its face creating bloody wounds on it's expressionless face before refocusing on them. It lowers its center of gravity, stretches its arms to the sides and restarts its charge in their direction.

The loud stomping of the beast accelerates in their direction as Ryan and Steve look to their goal—the fence line. Even if they make it there in time, trying to crawl through would leave them vulnerable to impalement by claw which means one of them will have to hold the beast off. 

Just as the monster reaches a single pace away, Ryan shoves Steve off to the right and he dives to the left. This small action is quickly followed by the monster flying past, stopping just soon enough to avoid colliding with the fence.

Ryan lifts his shotgun, “Get through the wall!” he shouts as he fires two rounds at the face of the mutant. 

The monster takes great offense to these futile rounds and begins rushing towards him with both claws open wide. Ryan turns and heads to the tank noticing something small and round in his peripheral vision cut in front of the monster. As soon as Ryan rounds the corner to take cover the small round object explodes with a mighty blast that knocks the monster off balance. It collapses to the ground and slides directly into the opposite side of the tank with a loud crash.

Without wasting any more time, Ryan's back on his feet sprinting towards the fence. By the time he reaches it, Steve is already on the other side aiming over Ryan’s head at the approaching zombie. He can hear the thumping of each footstep radiating through his bones. Each one slightly closer to him and slightly closer to his own demise. It’s hard to say if the mutant even breathes but he swears he feels its breath down the back of his neck. Then with no time left, he ditches his backpack and dives through the hole. A sharp wire catches his arm and cuts down the length of it, but he makes it through. The monster on the other side, however, slams into the fence and gets a leg tangled in some toppled over barbed wire. 

Ryan lands on the ground and grabs his forearm in pure agonizing pain. Steve doesn’t give him time to grief as he pulls him away from the barricade and down the street. They don’t slow beyond a sprint until the military blockade is behind a line of trees from the forest. The roars of the monster echoing across the landscape as it tries in vain to free it’s leg from the entanglement.

“We…made it,” Steve tries as he huffs in gulps of air, “We… actually… did it!”

Ryan slows down and presses his hand against his bleeding arm as he breaths heavily, “Yeah… did you see… I actually hit it!”

Steve slowly regains a normal pace of breathing, “I did… you did great.”

“I’m so glad Bill smooth-talked his way into acquiring a grenade,” Steve says with a massive smile on his face.

“I don’t even care how you got it,” Ryan laughs despite his wheezing of breath, “We needed that break.”

“You got that right!” Steve expresses in the most upbeat voice he’s ever heard.

“After that bitch, I don’t think the military looks so tough, do they?!” Ryan mocks bending over in both humor and sheer pain.

“They’re little tommy-guns are just toys anyway,” Steve pretends to be shooting a machine gun at his hip, “I could take the whole military with my own two hands. The bastards won’t even know what hit ‘em–not after dealing with that mother fucker.”

“I’ll just put a can on their heads and get some target practice,” Ryan laughs.

“That’s what I’m talking about!” Steve cheers.

“Oh boy, Charlie is going to be so jealous of everything I’ve learned,” Ryan encourages as he stands back up disregarding the throbbing sensation in his arm and shoulder.

“I’m proud of you son,” Steve says with softening eyes.

Ryan stops his amused chuckling and stares wide eyed at Steve. He’s never heard that phrase used before, not just by Steve but from anyone… ever. He doesn’t even know what to say. Sometimes… he misses not having a dad, but maybe, Steve isn’t so bad after all.

He looks down at the stinging cut down his arm. Luckily, it isn’t deep and the bleeding has mostly stopped but it still will leave a mark for the next couple of days. Though, yet again, the pain in his shoulder reminds him that a little scratch is the least of his worries. 

Steve takes his bag off and rummages through it finding a small first aid kit.

“Let’s get the wound cleaned,” he says, pulling out a few pieces of equipment, “You first need to disinfect the wound before bandaging it. A bad infection will kill you just as easily as the blood loss.”

He reaches over and grabs Ryan’s arm being careful not to disturb the wound. From there he pours a clear liquid out of a small bottle which stings a bit on contact. Bubbles quickly form on the edges of the wound from the liquid itself. Right after that, Steve wraps the cut with gause, tying it at the end. 

“It’s as simple as that,” Steve says with a satisfied expression.

“Awesome! Now do this one,” Ryan says, pulling the neck of his shirt down to reveal his swollen bite mark.

Steve’s smile drops expressing the same despair that a student who forgot to do their homework feels. He bites his bottom lip and clenches his fist before putting his first aid kit back in his bag. It’s only then that Ryan may have been a little too facetious.

“I already cleaned it,” he says timidly, "There's nothing more I can do."

Ryan smiles, “It’s okay, I’d rather laugh about it than cry. We’ll worry about it after we save Karmen and Charlie.”

“I–” Steve starts before getting cut off by the dull thumping of the street they so recently ran down.

Their eyes fill with horror as the monster reveals itself above the collection of cars on the road. Its hideous visage now morphed into a cryptic smile as a claw mark stretches parallel to it's eye line.

“Fuck!” Ryan yells looking for his shotgun only to realize it isn’t there.

Steve picks up his gun and fires a few rounds at the behemoth as he strafes to the side of the street and behind a van.

Ryan looks around for something to use but finds no good weapon nearby. He’s practically useless.

The monster approaches at a thundering speed, reaching up to Steve’s van within seconds. While running, it covers its face with its thick hand to block the incoming fire. Using its dexterous talons it vaults over the van and lands on the car behind.

Steve yells something before diving to avoid the slash of the monster’s claw. The strike misses Steve but leaves a large scratch mark in the van.

Ryan looks around for something to fight with. Since his bag is left on the other side of the fence, he has no equipment to fight back with. He’s completely useless and there’s nothing he can… he leans over and feels a sort of pressure on his hip. He vaguely recalls knowing a pressure on the front of his waistband but this object presents itself on the side. His recollection of the past floods back into his mind as the monster pounces on top of Steve and pins him to the ground. Ryan pulls out his pistol and aims down at the monster, he’ll hit this time unlike back on the road. The gun feels awfully light for some reason, but he doesn’t heed it much thought as his focus goes straight to the weak spot of the monster.

“Hey!” he screams at the mutant drawing its attention temporarily.

The mutant lifts its distorted face and receives a bullet straight into the other eye. Ryan is so focused he doesn’t even feel the recoil of the gun or hear the pop of it shooting but alas the monster rears back in agony and that is what matters.

The monster steps back, releasing Steve, and letting out a loud screech. In fact, the screech is so loud, that the windshield of the car next to it cracks. It then proceeds to swing its arms wildly around hitting the van, the car, and every air particle in its wingspan. Luckily, Steve had already crawled away by that point.

After a few moments of hysteria, the monster turns tail and sprints out into the forest running into multiple trees on its way out. That leaves Steve and Ryan alone once again on the highway. 

Ryan stares at the gun in his hands. Something about shooting it feels so natural, he felt it last time he used it, but it just doesn’t add up. This gun has no real relevance to him, yet he feels as if he’s meant to wield a weapon such as this. With very little training he’s been able to hit very difficult shots in high-pressure situations. He’d even go so far as to call himself a prodigy. Plus, this whole recoil thing that Bill mentioned doesn’t seem to affect him in the slightest.

Steve gets up and walks directly to Ryan.

“I… uh,” Ryan begins.

Steve doesn’t say anything as he holsters his own pistol and wraps Ryan up in a warm embrace. Affection is a prevalent weakness of theirs yet something about this hug offers the weakness in Ryan's a short moment of repose. His shoulder may writhe in the background and his head might be feeling lighter by the second but, for a split second, all of that pain disappears as someone finally welcomes him.

“We're gonna make it,” Steve whispers.

It only took eighteen years but for the first time ever, he wonders if this is what having a father is like.
