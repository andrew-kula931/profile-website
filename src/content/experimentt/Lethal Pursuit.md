Six… no seven zombies trail behind him before he makes it around the first corner of the school hallway. To be honest, he planned on being quiet until he got closer to the cafeteria, but some freshman girl screamed for help in a nearby classroom and incidentally led a group of zombies right into his path. With no other option, he quickly found himself in a dead sprint with an accumulating mass of zombies behind him.

By the time he reaches the cafeteria, the mob behind him is nearly 20 zombies strong. Most of them leak out of neighboring classrooms resulting in a steady stream of tributary undead. As he rapidly approaches the cafeteria, he finds it empty at the cost of dead soldiers littering the floor. In the heat of his sprint, vaulting over the occasional body was no issue, the issue is that the entryway doors are completely barricaded shut. He didn't anticipate being able to get out that way, but it still sent a chill down his spine to think those sheets of metal he saw earlier were actually being used to seal the tomb.

He transitions his momentum towards the kitchen where the maintenance shaft was, but nearly topples over as a zombie soldier begins scrambling to its feet right in front of him. He nearly opts to vault the zombie but then another soldier stands up behind the first leading to a cascading effect of zombies rising all around the cafeteria with his projected escape route completely inaccessible. With the horde behind him rapidly gaining ground, he pivots and rushes down a different hallway on the other side of the cafeteria.

He gets to the end of the new hallway without incident and even gains a bit of ground as the zombie soldiers trip up the incoming stampede, but then he turns the corner only to be greeted by several zombified students. A few of their eyes wander over in Ryan’s direction but a few of them are completely oblivious and are instead feasting on the corpses of those students who didn’t turn in time. With no time to look around and process he bolts right into the midst of the crowd. 

The first zombie kneels over a corpse eating so Ryan quickly vaults it like a track hurdle but unfortunately nicks his foot on the zombie's head and has to stumble back to his feet. The next zombie takes one step towards him with arms outstretched, but he uses his forearm to push both of the arms up and slides right past. The next begins to stand up at the noise of the horde behind him so he steps around it only to be tripped up by the next one who was leaning against a locker. As he stumbles forward, the zombie attempts to grapple him but he dives forward through its arms and somersaults. He’s back on his feet in less than a second and barely steps out of the way of another zombie who dashes out of a classroom as his feet clumsily rebalance himself. He pushes off the back of an oblivious zombie’s back before turning his attention to the final zombie in this section of the hallway which is now completely aware of his presence. It leans forward and swings its arms back, clearly about to pounce. As it jumps, Ryan and the floor become acquainted once again as he slides right under it. He pushes himself to his feet once more while having to use the locker as a brace as his legs begin to cramp up. He then feels a cold dead hand on his back as he begins sprinting completely disregarding any fuzziness in his vision. The touch only lasts for a brief moment, but he knows the horde behind him is mere inches away. 

Ryan advances further and further down the hall only to catch a dangerous sight out of the corner of his eye. He could swear he saw a person standing in a classroom and watching through the crack in a curtain. As sad as the thought was, he didn’t have time to worry about them right now. 

He turns the final corner back to the cafeteria and looks directly to the kitchen. There’s not a lot he can do for anyone now, but if he can get out it’ll be a massive help. The barricades at the entrance of the school will get anyone trying to escape killed. He needs to get outside and remove the barricades. Everything after that is up to the students left in the building.

The kitchen, or as he calls it, the child’s bedroom runs adjacent to the cafeteria to allow for easy access during lunch time, but the door to get inside requires a sharp turn to enter due to it being flush with a hallway leading out of the cafeteria. With so many footsteps behind him, he can’t take the risk of slowing down so getting in without being taken over by the mob will require much more precision. To accomplish such an ordinary task during such a stressful situation, he gets as close to the opposite wall of the hallway and right before passing the door he dives through the doorway. His legs hit the door frame but his entire body makes it inside. He curses under his breath as he can feel the bruising boiling up in his legs which causes a few limped steps as he gets back up. The horde behind him tramples over each other preventing any zombies from getting through the doorway immediately. 

Still filled with adrenaline, he rushes to the maintenance door, swings it open and dives through. By the time he lands on the other side he can already hear a zombie smashing into the wall behind him. 

The first zombie up attempts to crawl through the opening but gets trapped against the bodies behind it and is unable to move its lower body through the hole. It’s only a matter of time before even that changes, so Ryan has to keep moving. 

As he maneuvers through the shaft, he realizes just how exhausted he truly is. His dead sprint takes a toll which he wasn’t prepared for. He’s a fairly athletic guy having unintentionally eaten properly and going for runs when his previous foster parents would argue about him, but a dead sprint is a dead sprint especially when he has to do some parkour on the way. 

By the time he reaches the air conditioning room—or whatever it is—he can already hear echoes of zombie screams through the darkness. It's ironic seeing as only little children fear the dark, but staring into that black maintenance shaft makes him feel like a child all over again.

The door leading to the torture chamber is thankfully closed, and he has no intent of wasting time to check it out, but for now he wants to see if his hunch—that he risked his life for—is correct. He pulls out his key with his now sweaty hands and fumbles it a little as his hands refuse to stop shaking. Being no small effort, he eventually gets the key into the lock and promptly twists it. With a trembling body he pulls the lock off and removes the chain from between the handles of the door. He then grabs the handle, glances back into the darkness of the damned, and opens the door. 

The intense light of the afternoon sun beats down on his exhausted body. It’s almost enough for him to start an inner monologue, but then the screams echo behind him and he slams the door shut in the least graceful way possible. The outside of the door blends in perfectly with the brick walls of the school making any attempt to get back in near impossible. Whatever room that was, it’s not supposed to be well known by the government or the staff.

The city around him remains dormant and eerily quiet. Despite the apocalypse starting, no gunshots can be heard in the distance. There’s no smoke rising from the center of town or police sirens wailing away.

The door behind him thumps from the inside as he takes it as his cue to leave. 

As he walks around the perimeter of the school, he finds that his breath refuses to calm down for some time. His legs wobble after the aggressive display of physicality and his heart races like no other. He even leans on the wall of the school to take a few deep breaths and fully catch his breath.

No zombies stand around the outskirts of the school, not even any military equipment. Despite all the soldiers in the school, there’s no sign of any military presence whatsoever. No large humvees or tanks occupied the bus lanes or mounted turrets in the parking lots. It’s as if the soldiers had arrived at the school in private, commercial vehicles just like any ordinary person.

The front doors have slabs of sheet metal blocking them as he noticed earlier, but seeing them from the outside reveals that they each have been bolted to the wall in an unorganized fashion. It seems that whoever put the barrier up was in a hurry and didn’t do a very presentable job. Beyond that, every external window of the school was boarded over with plywood or a sheet of metal. There were no exits left.

As Ryan slowly begins to catch his breath, his eyes being to wander. The first thing to catch his attention are the trees of the neighborhood across the street which gracefully sway in the wind. Furthermore, the clouds above take various unrecognizable yet familiar shapes. His mom comes to mind, particularly her soothing voice and compassionate smile. She used to sit at the dinner table and just stare at him in silence. At first it was an uncomfortable ordeal of putting up with the awkwardness of silence, but after a while he got used to it. Every time she would smile and say, “You’re just like your father.” One particular time he built up the courage to ask, “What do you mean.” In which she responded with a bigger smile. “Your father was always the quiet and strong type and I see so much of that in you. I hope that you grow up to be just as strong willed and courageous as he is.”

Ryan never really knew his dad, but his mom used to talk about him all the time. He would show up once or twice a year if that even. When that happened, he just spent time with Ryan’s mom and gave Ryan a toy or something. Despite that, he felt like his father abandoned them and left his poor mother to raise him alone.

“Your father may not seem like he cares, but he does. More than you know. Don't ever forget that.”

Care about people.

Why was this coming to him now? His entire plan in life was to ditch this school and live his own life away from everyone who would drag him down. He didn’t want to help people who just made his life miserable. He wanted to fight his own battles and not suffer under the weight of battles others place on him. He wanted to be alone.

“Do you wanna be my friend?” 

Ryan snaps back to reality and shakes his head. Friends? He almost forgot what it’s like to have someone who willingly wants to talk to you. He can’t claim that anyone in there is a friend, but at the very least he can’t say he has any reason to have them killed. 

“I’ll give them a chance and then leave,” he says aloud, “Does that sound fair mom? I’ll help them but not risk my life anymore to do it.”

Whatever the cause of this spontaneous self reflection, he doesn’t care. Maybe he's projecting, maybe he's not, either way... it makes him wonder why he did the things that he did. He had no reason to stick his neck out and be the distraction for everyone else, even if he told himself it was the "tactical" thing to do. Maybe he did it because... oh, who cares, it's not like anyone would listen if said it aloud. Chloe's attention and Blake's gratitude: it's all lost on him.

He gets closer to the front door and places his hand on the metal sheet blocking the zombies in. He notices a particular screw in one of the metal sheets that seemed lazily twisted in and he casually begins rotating it, just while he thinks up a solution. The other side of this barrier was the entryway and cafeteria which harbored no less than a dozen zombies by this point. Maybe there was a way to give the students inside a sign to find the maintenance shaft. The problem would be just getting back into the school in the first place. There had to be something he could do even if small and insignificant.

At some point in this thought process, the screw comes loose and falls out, immediately the entire sheet swings on the single screw holding it up and clangs its corner against the ground. A loud, metallic clashing sound echoes across the front of the building as he backs up instinctively. With a quick glance around, he checks the barricade once more and finds a small peep hole has revealed itself. Hesitation is the first thing to strike him as he looks around once more but ultimately his curiosity wins out and he crouches down to look through.

The other side of the metal barricade is completely black which feels anti-climatic to say the least, but then the obstructing object shifts and a zombie grown escapes the small hole. He falls backwards as a bloodshot eye with completely dilated pupils looks back at him through the peep hole. Immediately, the banging begins all across the barricade as the zombie moves and it's black pant leg covers the hole once more. Banging erupts across the metal surface, each from a loud, solid strike. The bang continues with each passing second only leading to a more ferocious struggle. The banging on the doors escalates until the sheets of metal themselves begin to rattle. The bolts barricading the doors shut begin to shake as a bolt begins to slip loose. Ryan takes a step back worrying that he might be in for another chase.

He can hear the pitter patter of footsteps of the undead, but one pair really stands out. It sounds so close and unmuffled. Then the rotting corpse of a zombie pounces on him, pushing him to the unforgiving cement. His body rolls twice before the attacker can regain the distance and reach out for him. Its head swings forward. Ryan manages to get a hand on the shoulder of the zombie and hold it at bay.

Two more figures appear from around the corner of the school, each breaking into a run once they spot him.

The zombie on top of him doesn’t simply continue attempting to bite, it begins to pry Ryan's hold on its shoulder with its hand. Then with its other hand it digs into his free arm with short daggers growing in place of the old finger nails. On top of it all, the zombie seems to smile at him as a red liquid with the consistency of maple syrup drips from its lips. Ryan lets out a groan of pain at the feeling of his flesh being cut open. The wound isn’t large, but it stung worse than anything he’s felt in a long time.

Using all his strength, Ryan rips his hand free of the zombie's grasp and results in additional tearing of his flesh. Then, to follow up, he swings his fist into the zombie's cheek which causes his head to snap to the side just enough for him to push the zombie back with both arms and slide completely free. He immediately rolls away before the zombie can grapple him again. Ryan pushes himself to his feet as his own blood drips on the concrete, and he breaks away into another sprint. Behind him three zombies continue their pursuit while the large metal barrier verges on a breaking point.

It’s quite amazing how adrenaline can take away all pain and make fatigue seem insignificant, but that boost only lasts so long before the body can’t handle anymore. 

After the chase in the school and now this mad dash he quickly finds the air in his lungs slowly running out of stock. No matter how long he runs, the zombies behind him keep pace. One of the undead was a freshman and not the most athletic type either and here it is running as fast as him. His stride gets smaller and smaller with each step but he doesn’t allow any hesitation as he runs down the sidewalk in the nearby neighborhood. 

He makes it a few grueling blocks before he comes upon two passerbyers further down the street: Mr. and Mrs. Jacobson. They're an elderly couple in their 80s who love to go on long walks regardless of the time of day. They were one of the rare couples who still loved each other despite 50 years of marriage and six kids. Ryan recalls vividly how he's see them as he walked home from school and every time they would wave at him despite not knowing his name. He only knew there's because they insisted on telling him about what an amazing dance team his school had and how their grandson was expecting a child soon.

His eyes match their gaze and they both quickly move to the side of the sidewalk to get out of the way—Mrs. Jacobson even smiles as he nears. Ryan attempts to yell out to them but he can’t muster enough excess air to create more than another gasp for oxygen. He waves his hand for them to run, but as each step draws him closer and closer he can’t get them to move. Besides, they were an elderly couple, how could they outrun these zombies who had him on his toes? He couldn’t just pass them, they would surely die. Sure he doesn’t know them, but he can’t just go around getting people killed. He has to help them.

Once he reaches two paces from the couple he spins around, draws his gun, and aims right at the head of the zombie. Everyone knows that the weakness of a zombie is the head—he just has to land one shot. His restless breath causes his arms to waver, making this new aiming action exceedingly hard to maintain. The first savage zombie is only one pace away when he draws forth the strength to pull the trigger.

Nothing happens. Literally nothing. The trigger doesn’t move and no bullet escapes the barrel—the safety is still on. 

The zombie drops low to the ground and lunges forward allowing Ryan only enough time to dive into the nearby grass to avoid another assault. The problem is not that he was in danger of the lunge, but that collateral damage exists. Mr. Jacobson stands right in the line of fire from the attack. 

The bright, open smile of the lovely couple used to be perpetual regardless of who they met, but for the first time, it turns to horror as Mr. Jacobson got his neck ripped open by the teeth of the zombie. He tries to scream but nothing comes out so Mrs. Jacobson does it for him. The following zombie rushes at Ryan attempting to grab and bite, but he manages to throw it off of him using its excessive momentum. The aggravated, rotting corpse flies past and redirects its attack to Mrs. Jacobson instead. She too goes down quickly with a volley of screams. 

The final zombie is a bit bigger than the first two which caused it to be slower. Despite this drawback it still makes its way to the conflict at an impossibly quick pace given its size and joins the feast completely disregarding Ryan.

Before him now stands five dead bodies with only three able to move. The grotesque scene reminds him of his neighbor’s basement. The difference now is that these corpses are not nameless faces–they’re people he’s actually seen before. The tips of his fingers begin to twitch and his breathing escalates on top of his exhaustion. The paralysis of fear wouldn’t allow him to move or even look away. 

Why did they choose the helpless people? He was their target yet they gave up once an easier one took rise. He isn’t responsible for deaths right? He tried his best, right? He didn’t kill them!

By the time one of the zombies looks up from the butchered bodies, it finds an empty grass yard where Ryan once sat.

A few grueling minutes pass with Ryan taking multiple breaks to catch his breath before he finds himself staring down a place he hoped to never see again: his foster home. He pushes the unlocked front door open and collapses onto the floor of the living room. His pistol slides out of his hand and a few feet in front of him while his face takes a resting position straight into the carpet.

His chest heaves up and down as his breath attempts to steady from the demanding run. He honestly has no idea why he chose this place to return to after escaping the school, but after two years of walking home it just kind of happened. He mentally kicks himself for making such a mistake, but after a moment of reflection, he realizes that he doesn’t really have anywhere else to go. At the very least, he might be able to snag a few granola bars before he leaves town.

His greatly strained body groans at him to stop moving, but he forces himself to persist. Upon lifting his head up, he is greeted by the penetrating eyes of a corrupted man. Steve looms over Ryan’s tired body, pistol in hand, simply looking down at the dangerous weapon.

“A 1911. Good choice. Must have come from a cop,” Steve unloads the magazine and checks to find it full and ready. He then reinserts the magazine and turns the safety off.

Ryan gets up slowly holding his hands out to try and stop Steve from doing anything rash.

“I don’t know why you’re home so soon, but I’m not here for you,” Ryan explains.

Steve snickers, “I know that, you little shit. I’m more surprised that you actually got your hands on a gun. Now tell me, why you came back?”

“I just need supplies, then I’m gone for good,” Ryan pleads with his palms completely visible to the volatile man.

Steve’s fixed gaze creates a certain kind of tension which prevents Ryan from speaking freely. Anything he says could have serious consequences especially because it's Steve who's holding the gun.

“A few minutes ago, an emergency broadcast went off telling everyone to stay indoors. You were just at school right? Why are you here?”

It bugged him the whole time, but Ryan finally realizes something that is off about this place; it doesn’t reek of alcohol. In fact, Steve looks completely sober. 

“Even if I told you, you’d never believe me,” Ryan says, averting his eyes.

“Don’t talk to me like that, boy. Be straight with me.” Steve’s voice is direct and assertive yet for some reason it doesn’t feel as hostile as Ryan expected it to be.

Ryan grits his teeth and looks out the door in the direction of Mr. and Mrs. Robinson, “Okay fine,” he sighs, “It’s zombies.”

Steve’s head turns and he begins to stroke his short, black beard in thought. He’s not a very clever guy, but hopefully he’s not just dense. Ryan fully expects him to completely refuse the idea.

“I see.”

Ryan takes a step back, changing his expression to match the surprise he feels. They should hate each other, when did trust start building.

“You believe me?” Ryan gasps, “I said zombies.”

“I heard you,” Steve reiterates, “but zombies don’t exist.”

Ryan sighs and drops his shoulders, of course he doesn’t believe him, but Steve keeps going.

“That being said, I’d be stupid if I didn’t hear you out first.”

Ryan shoots him a concerned look, “When did you start trusting others?”

Steve scowls briefly, “Don’t misunderstand me boy, I still don’t like you. I just knew a guy who didn’t believe me when I told him that there was a cobra on the construction site. We’re nowhere near a place where cobra’s live. Regardless, he didn’t heed my word and ended up in the hospital. The being said, you’re here, covered to the teeth in sweat, a bloody arm, and bringing a gun with you. I’d say the worst thing I could do right now is jump to a conclusion and start pointing fingers. Even if you did get into a fight with a cop, I'll at least grant you the dignity of confessing your crimes yourself.”

Ryan narrows his eyes, unsure as to whether he could be grateful for the man's consideration, or spiteful of the fact that he's only listening as a short-sighted curtesy.

Steve turns the gun and points it at Ryan; It’s barrel threatening to end him if he makes any sudden movements. Ryan’s heart skips a beat, his hair stands up, adrenaline surges. This is a moment where he’ll have to decide whether to fight or flight. He has no idea how good of a shot Steve is, but he’s not willing to risk his entire life on one such point. 

“You know Ryan?!” Steve asserts, “When you hit me earlier, I wanted nothing more than to just beat you within an inch of your life.”

Ryan narrows his eyes, choosing to look intimidating rather than cowardly, “Why didn’t you?”

“Shut it! If I really wanted to, I would have won that fight… but…”

Ryan thinks about speaking again, but every word he says is just taunting him to pull the trigger. It’s best to just get through this and not push his luck any further.

Steve shakes his head as he holds back some sort of resentment, though not for Ryan, “But… I couldn’t do it.”

Ryan studies the man trying to get a read on his true intention, but he only draws blanks. Steve has always been a hothead with no filter… so why does he sound like he’s actually being self conscious for once?

“I know what I am! It's just... I can't," he pushes his lips together as the next words struggle to come out, "I can't be like him.”

Ryan can’t quite recall a time that Steve has ever reflected on his own decisions. In fact, over the past two years he’s spent with Steve and Karmen he has only really had a handful of conversations with Steve and they were all surface deep. The bulk of what Ryan knows about him is the hostile comments he makes whenever he enters the house or passes through the living room.

“What are you trying to say?” Ryan finally says after Steve’s head drops into his palm.

This would be the ideal time to attack given that Steve isn’t looking, but something is telling him not to fight this battle—even if he really wants to.

Steve returns to focus and something about his face makes it seem like he’s contemplating saying something. Maybe he is trying to say something Ryan doesn’t understand.

“Forget about it…” Steve lowers the pistol and sighs deeply. Deep enough to know that he too was just as stressed as Ryan. “You just want supplies right?”

Ryan nods skeptically.

“I’ll make you a deal. If that emergency broadcast has any level of seriousness and something really is going on in town, I'll need help getting Karmen and Charlie out of the city. If you help me keep them safe for just a few days... I’ll give you anything you want: money, guns, food, you name it, I’ll give it to you,” Steve looks down at the gun and then at Ryan's arm, "And I'll overlook whatever you did."

Ryan’s jaw drops faster than his words can get out, “What, why would you need my help with that? For all I know, I’m dead weight to you.”

“I can tell the world is about to go to shit, not fuckin zombies, but something serious enough to get the military involved and emergency broadcasts sent… I’ve seen it before,” Steve hesitates but Ryan allows him to finish his thoughts. “I can protect my family, but I’m afraid that Karmen won’t be able to take it if you left us at a time like this. We may not see eye to eye, but that woman loves you like her own child.”

“Wait,” Ryan says, “When I left this morning, I was planning on never coming back. Why wouldn’t she have realized that by now?”

Steve shakes his head and moves over to the wall. His rough hand flows over the brown paint which he put up himself.

“Karmen won't say it, but she knows it. Her entire life has been just as fucked as mine. I don’t think she will be able to bear it if she loses her entire way of life and one of her sons on the same day.”

“So now you’re thinking about your wife. What about this morning when you pushed her to the floor?” Ryan counters with a hint of malice.

“You don’t understand!” Steve yells with a new found intensity, something he’s quite good at finding, “There’s more to it than that!” He calms himself, “You know, Karmen really likes you. You and Charlie are her foundation right now; I’m just someone who provides for her... or at least that's what I've become.”

“I’ve got my own problems, why do I need to associate myself with yours. Sure, I could help Charlie and Karmen, but you’ve been the problem Steve. Why don’t you take care of _your_ family? It’s always about you and your eighty hours. It’s about your alcohol and how you want your family to run.”

Steve leans back slightly, visibly hurt by the little shit’s words, “Maybe I was wrong then, but… that’s not… that’s not who I’m trying to be. That’s not who I am.”

Ryan doubles down, “You’re just saying that because you’re scared you’re not strong enough to protect them.”

A small blue car pulls into the driveway. The sight of Karmen pulling Charlie out of the car while looking around with fear in her eyes makes Steve freeze. Ryan can’t bring himself to admit it, but Karmen was always nice to him. If he just left without at least giving some form of repayment, then what kind of person would he be. The Jacobsons died because of him, he honestly doesn’t want to witness the same thing happening to Karmen and Charlie. Despite his own personal grievances, helping the Hughes family out and taking the supplies he will be owed might be his best bet right now. After all, he has nowhere to go after this and no way to get supplies so this might be his only way.

Ryan’s train of thought is interrupted by Steve rushing out the door to greet his wife and biological son.

“Steve! The roads to the high school are blocked off. How are we going to get Ryan out?!” Karmen panics while Charlie runs inside and up the stairs.

“It’s okay,” Steve responds reassuring Karmen by rubbing the side of her arm.

“But we can’t just leave with him!” she protests with genuine worry and concern in her eyes.

That is the point where Ryan steps out the door and into view. Karmen’s panic settles as spontaneously as it started and she rushes to give him a hug. Her outstretched arms have a certain level of nostalgia to it, but then he remembers that it's Karmen and not his real mom. As she takes the first step away from Steve something quickly approaches from the opposite side of the car. It rushes directly at Karmen.

Ryan jumps off the patio intending to intercept the crazed man with blood dripping from his mouth. He attempts to close the distance as quickly as he can but the zombie’s surprise attack seems to have given it a considerable lead over him. He’s a mere pace away when the zombie reaches Karmen, in which Karmen responds with a blood curling scream. 

Ryan presses forward as quickly as his fatigued body can muster. He promised himself that he wouldn’t let anyone else get killed right in front of him, he can’t break that promise a mere ten minutes after making it.

The zombie rears its head back and opens its mouth revealing its gooey, bloody teeth. Then those teeth get even bloodier when its own guts leak into its mouth from a fresh hole in its head. The fresh corpse collapses to the ground and reveals Steve pointing the pistol right where the bullet flew. Once the zombie finishes twitching, Steve steps forward and kneels down next to the hyperventilating Karmen. 

“It’s alright, you’re safe,” he reassures.

Karmen glances at Steve and then at the corpse switching between them and is too frightened to let out even a single remark. She is pulled into an embrace with Steve in which she doesn’t reciprocate the sentiment. Steve then turns the safety back on the pistol behind Karmen’s back and tosses it over to Ryan.

“W-w-what is going on,” Karmen stutters as her arms instinctively raise to embrace her husband, “You just said to go pick up the kids and come straight home. W-what is that?”

Steve doesn’t respond while he picks Karmen up and brings her back to her feet. 

“Let’s go inside,” he says, pushing Karmen to the house.

As they pass Ryan, her eyes never leave the corpse on their lawn. She was never one for horror movies, but now she’s gonna have to live one. 

Steve pushes Karmen through the front door and looks back to Ryan. “Well fuck me, I guess you were telling the truth... I’ll ask you one last time. Are you with us or not.”

Steve is a good shot. That is the take away in Ryan’s mind from the whole encounter. Karmen almost died, but that was to be expected with her social anxiety, Steve on the other hand shouldn’t possess any abilities except drinking. A certain level of curiosity and danger emanate from that man.

Ryan glances down the street for any other threats. Nothing catches his eye so he leaves the dead high school boy, the same one who chased him earlier, and goes back inside. He will stick around for now and earn a head start to his future, but he has no intention of spending a single day longer with this family beyond that. Karmen needs to learn that.
