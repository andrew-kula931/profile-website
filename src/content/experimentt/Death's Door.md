The sweet release of death leaves him in suspense as he waits for its icy embrace. The expectation that all of his pain would seamlessly melt away is never realized, however, as he lies in the brutal silence of the darkening skies. The barrel never sparks to life and that small chunk of lead never flies through his temple. He’s just left in silence as the gun taunts his boldness with a gruesome silence.

Ryan keeps the barrel on his head believing that with enough time it’ll all be over. He resists the urge to move despite the soreness of his muscles nagging at his consciousness. He tells himself that he just needs to wait a bit longer... just a bit longer.

Eventually, his fears are realized and he's forced to inspect his gun. The safety is off and the trigger pulls just fine, so what could be so wrong? That’s when he notices the magazine... or lack thereof. The barrel is barren of a bullet, in fact, the entire magazine is vacant leaving just the worthless shell of a gun. 

How and when did this happen? He just used the gun not long ago to shoot the mutant on the freeway. His disbelief nearly draws him to throw the weapon, but then the truth dawns on him, and he’s left in a static disbelief.

Ever since the fight with Heimes, his gun has felt lighter. With a zombie bite on his shoulder and one last mission to save his foster family on the forefront of his mind, he didn’t even think twice about the weapon being any different from usual. When he tried to use his gun for shooting practice, Steve turned the idea down and handed him an assault rifle. When he resolved himself to take the mutant down, he didn’t feel a single ounce of recoil from that shot. It wasn’t just adrenaline then… the gun truly never fired. If it started after the fight then… there really is only one possibility left.

But why him?

Ryan recalls how Steve helped him in the past few days: he tried to run, but Steve wouldn't let him. He thought he was alone, yet Steve believed him. He thought life was over, yet Steve taught him to shoot and start a fire. Even when he was collapsed on the ground, Steve refused to put him to rest once and for all. Perhaps, that stubborn man held out hope that Ryan would survive despite what he'd seen. Perhaps... Steve suspected what would happen and didn't want Ryan to end up like him—like his father.

Why him?

The sun slowly fades beyond the horizon and the bright fluorescent orange and reds fade to dark blues and purples. A once vibrant sky fades to black with small twinkles of light bleeding through the solid darkness. He reaches out his hand towards the blooming stars with fingers that tremble under their own weight.

Why him?

The pain across his body resurfaces, and he’s too weak to resist it. The nerves flare across his body like a violent eruption contained in his pathetic, fragile form, and he takes it like a hollow husk of a man. He can feel his tear ducts beginning to activate as his eyes struggle to stay open. His body is too weak to move, and maybe it's time he accepts it.

"Is that really it?"

Ryan’s body is too fatigued and despondent to contextualize the disembodied voice, so instead he uses his eyes to glance around only to see a mysterious, cloaked figure staring at him. Some sort of black mask covers his enigmatic visage. Regardless of who the man is, maybe they’ll be merciful and put him out of his misery before it’s too late. His senses begin to fade, and every worldly feeling leaves him, probably for the last time.

"Even now, you still believe you are a burden?"

The voice almost seems like his conscience trying to tell him something, but he doesn’t listen to it—it speaks as if it already knows his thoughts and regrets. By this point everything is so dark, light becomes a memory, and so he doesn’t resist any further—he just doesn’t want to hurt anymore.

"Is this how you want it to end?"

He can’t feel himself anymore, words don’t come out of his mouth and neither do thoughts.

"Your story doesn't have to be over."

---

Ryan no longer knew whether he feared dying or feared surviving. The feeling drifted somewhere beyond thought—an instinctive dread hanging over him like a weight tied to his soul. If death truly awaited him, then fine. At least it would finally be over.

The clouds are a beautiful pearl white upon the early blue sky. One is shaped like a penguin, which he spots immediately. Another takes a strange shape which kind of resembles an octopus, but not quite. He knows his mother would have laughed at that one. Further away in his peripheral vision, he spots a very circular one with a hole in the middle. It’s a donut for sure. The entire sky glows so vibrantly that he wonders how it could all be a dream. Even the nearby tree's shadow seems so surreal as it cuts through the halo of the blazing sun.

Could this be heaven? He doesn’t deserve such an honor, yet he’s staring at something so beautiful… something so nostalgic. 

Then, as he begins to settle into the new situation, his body gets up. He doesn’t command it—he doesn't command anything. He has absolutely no control over what is occurring, only the feeling that it is in fact his own body moving, but he’s not telling it anything. 

His head turns down to see the three graves before him and he knows this is no simple dream.

His body then forces him to begin walking down the small, grassy hill back to the military camp. His stride is uncomfortable and uneven, and he struggles to traverse the land. Although he can’t control his head, he can feel a peculiar feeling aroused in his arms. It's as if bone-like structures are pressing up against the top of his forearms threatening to pierce through his skin at any moment. A deep rooted sense of horror overtakes his consciousness yet his body coldly refrains from listening to such thoughts.

The small plumes of smoke that once arose from the military base are no longer present, now only the silent pleas of rotting corpses remain. The smell of decay hits Ryan, but for some reason it doesn’t bother him—he only notices it intellectually.

His body passes the gate of the base without a second thought and trudges on by without a shred of empathy for the dead. Its destination is not back at the base but somewhere further, somewhere off the main road and into the precarious forest.

A tall ceiling of leaves and branches create a dim environment with shadows lurking around every corner. A mixture of trees, bushes, and tall weeds make it difficult to navigate through denser regions, but Ryan’s body disregards these issues and tramples right through accepting any cuts and scraps along the way. His head sways from side to side though not due to momentum, but out of curiosity as his body searches for… something. This rhythm of motions continues for quite some time allowing him to lose track of where he is in the forest. Every turn his body takes confuses him even more and makes it nearly impossible to find the way back. Though, his body hardly seems concerned.

Suddenly a bush nearby shakes causing Ryan’s body to snap towards it and growl. The subsequent noise he makes is not one he ever thought possible. He finds his voice forced and uncontrolled like a dying dog’s whimper yet far more sinister—its the same growl the zombie woman inspired terror with. This growl foreshadows his intent as he peers around a tree and spots his target: an innocent little bunny. 

Without a second thought he charges at the rabbit at an unprecedented speed. The harmless bunny doesn’t even have time to react as Ryan’s boney fingers pierces through the animal's neck killing it instantly. 

Sure, watching a cute bunny die is heartbreaking enough, but there is a different level of disgust when he then proceeds to rip it limb from limb… and eat it raw. Ryan tries with all his might to stop his actions or at least look away, but he has absolutely no control and is forced to witness in horror as the poor bunny gets eaten by a living corpse. The warm meat has a stringy, wet consistency as it goes down his throat and he can feel his mouth getting covered with the messy, red blood of the meat. He worries about futile issues like food poisoning, yet his body treats such things as trifling matters. 

Amidst the feast, Ryan wonders if this is how it's supposed to be. Is his fate truly to watch the destruction his undead corpse is capable of?

When all is said and done, the bunny is nothing but a bit of fur and bones. Ryan’s body stands up and growls a malicious, demonic growl. 

"Do you truly want to continue living like this?"

The disembodied voice returns as if it were standing right next to him. Its voice sounds human, but subconsciously he knows that voice is anything but.

"Has your life not taught you the cost of running away?"

Of course it has, running away solves nothing, but at least for him, that's not a concern he'll be allowed to confront. He’s done. Even as a zombie, he knows he doesn't want to fight anymore. 

He’s done.

"What if you had another chance?"

Chance? What right does he have to believe something like that? If nothing else, perhaps this new life can serve as a punishment.

"You now know what it feels like to lose everything, why not fight, so others do not feel this same pain?"

If only it were that simple. If only he were capable of doing something like that.

"What if you could change?"

Change? What does that word even mean? He tried so many times to change himself but every single time it only ended in people getting hurt. He doesn’t have that kind of power.

“What if?”

What if he could, one day, when all was said and done, smile just as the people around him could? Well, maybe he'd want that day to come to fruition. He’d protect others from hurting and in the process find the purpose he's always desired. If he had the power to do that, just that, then maybe he'd feel like he has a right to live.

"It's time you prove who you are. Who you really are."

Suddenly the presence of the figure vanishes and Ryan’s body completely blacks out. 

When he comes too, he finds himself standing idly in the dense forest. At first, he believes that the zombie is waiting for something, but then he imagines looking at his hand and, to his surprise, it actually moves. His limbs begin operating like they used to, his head turns to look at things he wants to see, and his voice is once again his own. He looks down at his arms expecting to find a protruding bone, but he only finds his usual forearm.

He wipes the blood off of his face and looks around at the unknown forest landscape. Every shrub on the ground, every piece of moss on the trees, and every bug that flies by is so similar yet so foreign. In the stillness of the forest, a doe appears from through the bushes. Its innocent form makes the entire landscape seem even more majestic. Ryan watches in awe as the doe crosses his path and continues on its journey somewhere else in the world. He then pears back into the sky above looking at the beautiful turquoise blue filtering itself through the leaves above. Small rays of sun shine through those gaps in the overhang and brighten the surrounding scenery. Mushrooms at the base of trees become more pronounced than ever before. A small colony of ants climbing a nearby tree becomes visible as they march in their orderly fashion. A bird flaps its wings a few times over its nest dropping a worm to its babies underneath. Ryan finds the surrounding beauty dulling the irking pain in his heart, though only for a moment.

Ryan releases a drawn out exhale as his eyes close—a reluctant smile emerges from the ashes of his face. The experience of death is no gift, rather it's a reckoning that commands him to move forward. Death has rejected him… it seems he has not yet given up.
