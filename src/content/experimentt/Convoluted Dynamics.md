When things die down, Ryan half expects himself to start socializing as if his life really had change. This ambition is quickly met with reality, however, as he finds himself once again by the sidelines of the group. A few people offer him passing glances, but beyond the gruelingly thick tension throughout the camp, the group dynamic seems to fall back to what it was a week prior. The sophomore girls still talk to the sophomore girls, Jeremy and Ryan still sit by their lonesome, and everyone else just minds their own business. It's not that he finds it offensive in the slightest, not at all, he simply finds it a tad bit anti-climatic.

So there he is, sitting on roof of a van composed within the makeshift wall. A sheet of metal, screwed into the side of the vehicle, offers him a shallow arm rest to lean on as he looks out to the streets beyond. 

A week ago, this street would have looked so abysmally bland and forgettable that he wouldn't be able to remember its name even if he tried. Before the road curves out of view, three buildings rest on either side of the deserted street. They offer little indication of their purpose and the street sign itself is but a single number and suffix. These traits are so incredibly generic that he wonders why a city planner would even consider such boring aesthetics... yet looking at it now, he can't seem to view it that way. The clean, empty street feels starkly forlorn as if neglected by its maintainer with no date for return. The simple buildings feel like ruins towering out of a grassy rubble of a society no longer existent. He can't help but morn that world he used to hate.

"What's up?" an energetic voice says as they climb up the ramp-like windshield of the van and sits down on the other end.

Ryan glances over Max who sits contently with a smirking grin across his face, "Hello."

Max looks up into the air in thought as he bites his lip for a moment, "You know, I've got to say, I did not believe you when you said there were zombies."

Ryan studies the boy before nodding silently. 

"And you know what else," Max continues, "It pissed me off to no end when I found out you were right," he laughs to himself as if something he said was funny.

Ryan remains silent as he looks off into the distance once again.

"Hey Ryan," Max adds, finally getting to the meat and potatoes of his approach, "I wanted to ask you what you thought about food."

Ryan looks back with a quizzical expression as he shrugs his shoulders, "It's food, I guess."

Max rolls his eyes, "You sound like my brother. No. I'm talking about quality cuisine. Everyone here seems to be content with halfway stale granola bars and whatever else they scavenged in the food pantry," he waves his finger towards himself, "But not me, I think we're stumbling over an opportunity here," he waves his hand as if reading off a headline or neon sign, "Apocalypse apothecary."

Ryan shakes his head, "I don't think you used that word right."

With a wave of his hand, Max rolls his eyes, "T-The name's still in the works, okay," he starts waving his hands passionately as he speaks, "All I'm saying is that we can't become complacent. I'm not going to sit here and snack on _moldy_ bread when I can just as easily make the bread myself. You feel me?" 

Intellectually, the boy's mentally makes sense, not particularly necessary or warranted, but... functionally coherent. That being said, Ryan can't help but feel like there's more to this than meets the eye. There's no way a guy who sat through hell with him just spontaneously elects to pursue culinary endeavors. That's the part he doesn't get emotionally... he has a sinking suspicion that what comes next is likely going to be further out there then 'Apocalyptic apothecary'. 

"So... I was thinking," Max continues as predictably as a child, "Would you come with me to the grocery store—just for a few things."

Ryan leans back against the metal wall and contemplates the idea. It's not that he's afraid of leaving the confines of the walls, unlike everyone else Max is probably implicitly referring to. He's also not opposed to gaining more resources. What's holding him back is that Max hasn't already done this. He doubts Blake would argue with a plan like this, yet why is Max coming to him and not to literally anyone else before Ryan even got here. 

"What's the catch?" Ryan specifies as he narrows his eyes on the overly eager boy.

Max shakes his head, "No catch... you can pick up some things too."

Ryan narrows his eyes further, "Then why haven't you gone already?"

"It's because, before you got here, Blake was too busy to go with him."

Both teens look off the van to spot Caid standing beside them looking up. He smiles knowingly at Max who crosses his arms and rolls his eyes.

"And what about you?" Ryan adds remembering how Caid managed to hold Jeremy back during the innocent a few minutes before. 

Caid chuckles to himself as he leans up against the side of the van, "Help that pip-squeak out?"

"Alright, flat foot giant," Max counters.

Caid, being a taller guy who previously played basketball for the varsity team, steps back defensively, "Hey now, that was one time."

Max starts opening and closing his hands by pressing his upper four fingers against his thumb, "Yap, yap, yap. You always have an excuse, don't you."

Caid rolls up his sleeve, "Why don't you get down here, five-six know it all. I'll break your ankles without a court."

Max leans over to the edge of the van and looks down at the much taller boy. He narrows his eyes and releases his next words very slowly and methodically, "I'm five-seven thank you very much."

Caid immediately reaches up and attempts to latch on to the boy but misses as he jumps back and crawls backwards to Ryan, "Help me, he's gonna smash my face in."

"I don't know, Max, you might deserve it," Blake says as he enters the scene which incidentally causes Caid to settle down, "What are you guys talking about?"

A smirk immediately grows across Caid's face as Max desperately tries to hush him by placing a finger across his lips, "Max wants to make a shopping run."

Blake's content demeanor fades as he glares at Max. The previously energetic boy looks away as he crosses his arms, "I...don't know what you're talking about."

"That grocery store is not safe," Blake asserts.

Max immediately turns back to him and crawls over to the edge of the van, "But that was three days ago. By now it's probably fine. Besides, we don't know how long our rations will last."

Blake shakes his head as he crosses his arms, "The pantry will hold us over for quite a while."

"Oh, come on," Max complains, "I don't want to go by myself."

Blake scoffs at his please, "Good, then don't go at all."

Max falls backwards and lets his feet hang off the side of the van as he whines unintelligibly. Ryan can't help but hide a smirk at the boy's theatrics—something that caught him far more off-guard than he expected. The disgruntled boy groans some more as he rolls over to his stomach and crawls over to the edge of the wall and hangs his arms off the edge. After a few more moments of mopping around he turns back to Ryan with a hesitant determination.

"Please Ryan," he says poorly interpreting a puppy his eyes, "Go with me?"

"Don't let him guilt trip you," Caid offers nonchalantly, "Just tell him to fuck off."

Ryan takes a deep breath as he looks off to the forlorn street beyond the wall. Once upon a time, leaving captivity was his sole purpose in life, now he can't help but feel as though that fact is... far less appealing. Some part of his still wants to go—to run freely in the world—but another part is afraid that he'll just continue being who he used to be if he does.

In front of him, Max still waits eagerly with a growing smile with every passing second that Ryan doesn't respond. It's as if his hesitation was become a sort of confirmation. This is only reinforced by Caid's disapproving look and Blake subtly shaking his head.

"I–" Ryan starts. 

"Wanna go?!" Max finishes.

Ryan sighs, "We'll have to eventually, might as well get it out of the way now."

Max pumps his fists into the air with a brief 'yahoo' while Blake glares at the display irritatedly. Caid, however, just shrugs his shoulders, "Alright, I guess I'll come too."

Max leans over the edge of van and glares at the taller boy, "I never invited you."

Caid chuckles incredulously, "Okay? I invited myself."

Max shakes his head, "Well I don't want you to come."

Caid studies the boy for a moment before disregarding the comment once again with a simple wave, "Fat chance, I already made up my mind."

Max leans back a bit disheartened by Caid's insistence to come with, but can't find the right words to stand his ground. His head leans side to side as if he considers a few things mentally, but nothing concrete comes out. Instead, he just punches the top of the van lightly and leans back further.

From the outskirts of the conversation, Ryan notices Beth ease dropping as she multitasks between her discussion and theirs. No one but Ryan seems to notice so he just disregards it, after all, Max wasn't exactly being quiet about his intentions.

"Guys, you can't all go," Blake counters, "Some people have to stay here to protect the base."

Caid and Max collectively roll their eyes as if the interaction were practiced, "There you go again."

Blake looks between them with offense before turning to Ryan, "Back me up here."

That's the point when Beth enters from stage right and nudges Blake's shoulder playfully, "Stop being such a worry-wart. I know what you're thinking, but everyone here can handle the base alone for two hours. You four can go on a supply run, and we'll be _fine_".

Blake does a double take before shooting everyone a confused look, "I never said I was going."

Caid sighs, "You know what, I changed my mind. I like this idea now. Mini-Max can get his peasant food, and you can get outside your comfort zone. This will be good for both of you."

Max's mouth drops at the hurtful comment, but he doesn't retaliate. Ryan initially believes it's because Caid is objectively larger than the small boy, but something causes him to wonder whether there's more to their dynamic than meets the eye. He thinks brothers, but they don't look anything alike and as far as he knows, he's never seen them together before today. 

"See," Beth adds resting and arm on Caid's shoulder which incidentally is almost at her head height, "You need to cool off and this is a great opportunity to do that."

"I'm all for this," Max adds only to be shut out by a stern look from Blake. 

"So," Beth continues, "I'm going to go document our inventory and, in the meantime, you four will go make my life harder by changing the numbers. Sound good? Perfect. See you boys in a few hours."

Blake tries to rebuttal, but Beth immediately takes off towards the food pantry without bothering to look back. The reluctant leader hangs his head in frustration before looking at the three boys whom he's 'forced' to tag along with. 

"Fine," he says rather begrudgingly, "Let's get suited up."

A couple of minutes later, Ryan finds himself walking down the same street he pondered on. The simple road with a simple turn should probably terrify him as a zombie could jump out at any moment, yet he feels at peace around it. That is, until Max starts talking again.

"Dude, why do you always walk like you're Him," Max points out as he gives Caid multiple once overs honing primarily on his shoulders and feet.

Caid rolls his eyes and shoves Max which nearly topples the boy over, but he manages to stay on feet. He then lifts his hand and starts opening and closing it like the mouth of an alligator, "Yap, yap, yap."

Max's mouth drops open, "You did NOT just do that to me."

Blake takes the opportunity to pick up his walking speed as to stand in between the two boys. His mere presence seems to deescalate the situation enough to prevent a one-sided beat down—Caid didn't seem like he was even going to try.

"You two seem close," Ryan says aloud, not really intending for it to be.

Caid glances over, "Oh us? Yeah, we're neighbors... or, were neighbors, you know, before," he gestures to the empty street, his eyes hang on a house for a bit too long.

"So family friends," Ryan labels internally which once again gets verbalized.

"Boyfriends actually," Max corrects matter-of-factly as he leans forward ahead of the horizontal line of boys.

Caid rolls his eyes as he directs his attention mostly towards Ryan, partially beyond him, "Don't listen to him, I'm as straight as they come, and he is too... probably."

Max's mouth drops again, taking offense to the comment, "Please, unlike you, I've proven myself with a girlfriend."

Caid runs a hand through his hair, his eyes still dwelling on a nearby house, "Bro, that lasted for two weeks. That doesn't count." he shoves his hands into his pockets, "I'm telling you, sit by that kid for your three years of middle school, and you'll be corrupted too."

They round a corner of the street and start heading down a new one with a few deserted vehicles parked on the side of the road. A few more buildings pick up but no signs of movement appear anywhere. Ryan rests easy as he keeps a hand firmly positioned near his concealed weapon. Caid's attention finally breaks from the house as he raises his chin and walks on. 

Blake's gaze hangs on Max for a moment before he whispers, "Girlfriend, huh?"

Max looks over, "What'd you say?"

Blake shakes his head, "Nothing. Let's focus in, we're getting close."

They walk a bit further with each of them studying the surroundings. Ryan checks high and low for any sign of movement, whether dead or alive. Blake does the same as he mentally formulates a bread crumb trail of their journey. Except for Ryan, all of these boys had licenses so they likely knew the streets fairly well, but Blake likely just wants to keep track of a clear route. Ryan lets him do that and pays extra attention to the path ahead as Blake looks back. Max's attention meanders to a few places with his favorites being any store, outlet, or attraction that would remotely interest a child. Caid, being the least inclined to look around, occasionally glances at clinging of a wind chime or the flutter of a leaf to the ground, but beyond that, keeps his gaze fixated forward as if looking around were dangerous.

They turn the final corner on their route and wind up standing in front of old mom and pop grocery store. Max swallows as he glosses over the sign and takes a deep breath. Caid, having kept his awareness narrow, only notices the sign once they stop. He reads it blandly before his eyelids begin to raise slowly. By the time he finishes reading the store sign, he turns to Max with a quietly upset face.

"Max, I thought we were going to the store on Maple? You know the _big_ one."

Max chuckles under his breath, "Whoops, looks like we got lost... thought this one might still have stuff in it." 

Although the group's attention was primarily on the sign above the door and the store windows, Ryan takes the time to notice Max's hand. He fiddles with his fingers like a kid with ADHD, but Ryan notices the subtle shaking masked behind the subconscious movement. 

"Don't fucking lie to me," Caid spits out, rage building on his face as quickly as his hands clench into fists, "Why are we _here_?"

Max shrugs his shoulders holding back an awkward smile, "Seriously... I got lost." 

"What's wrong with this place?" Blake comments as he looks back and forth down the street to ensure the coast is clear, "Shouldn't we head inside?"

Caid snaps his head at Blake and then back to Max. Finally, he turns and starts walking back the way they came, "You know what, I'm done with this. If you're so insistent to go in there then be my guest. I'm heading back."

"Come on Caid," Max complains to no avail. The guy doesn't even seem to hear the plea.

"Caid," Blake calls out.

Unlike the previous command, this one does cause Caid to slow to a halt and begrudgingly look back, "I'm not going in there," he points to Max, "And he shouldn't either."

Blake glances between the two friends in confusion as he inspects the store once more, "What's going on here?"

Caid points his finger at Max accusingly, "Why don't you ask him. The idiot who led us here."

All eyes turn to Max.

Max, the boy in question, glances around at the attention with a nervous smile. He waves his hands in front of his body as he slowly backs away, "I swear I know this place. Like, I was confident that we, uhm, would find something—honest," he gestures through the window at lightly stocked shelves, "look there's still stuff here."

Caid storms back into the circle of teens, "Cut the bullshit, tell them why we're really here."

Max and Caid have a visual standoff as Max silently pleads for mercy and Caid restrains his last nerve from breaking lest he actually pummel the guy. Tensions only seem to rise the longer the situation goes on, but before Blake or Ryan could step in, Caid reaches out and grabs onto the collar of Max's shirt.

"Tell them what's in that store," Caid announces, his free hand twitches upwards but doesn't fully chamber.

"I—I—" Max stammers as he frantically looks around for something to give him resolution—something he's clearly not willing to do himself.

"Tell. them." Caid threatens.

Blake steps forward and raises his palms, "Guys, let's not do this here."

Caid looks away from his "friend" and answer Blake but the second his attention is diverted, Max pries himself free of Caid's grasp. Caid tries to grapple him again, but the boy slips free a second time and rushes to the door of the store. He manages to get it open and slide through before Caid can catch up. In mere seconds, both boys disappear into the store with Blake and Ryan barely able to exchange glances before following in.

Ryan's hardly far behind them as he enters into the small grocery store and finds the two guys near the checkout counter in the back of the store. Max is on the ground with his hands up in a futile position while Caid towers over him with a menacing stare.

"What did I tell you?" Caid asserts as Max starts slowly dragging himself away.

Blake enters the scene swiftly and breaks up the confrontation by standing in between them—exactly as he did with Jeremy and the girls. Looking between both of them, he tries to grapple with the reality of the situation, but his hesitation speaks for itself. Caid doesn't bother pushing past Blake as he glares down at Max.

"Why do you never listen?" Caid says much more a lot softer than before. On second thought, Ryan can't quite call it soft, to be honest, Caid comes off sort of... emotionally—a way Ryan has never seen before.

Blake turns down to the guy on the floor, "Max, just tell us why we're here. There's clearly more to this than food."

Max, having crawled his way across the floor and now sitting beside a closed, employee only door, looks back with a morbidly serious face. His gaze doesn't follow on Blake or Caid, instead it falls on Ryan, more specifically, it falls on his hip. With a quick investigation Ryan confirms that his hip looks as ordinary as it should. That being said, Ryan gets an uncanny feeling that Max knows more than he lets on.

Max doesn't stare for long as he rises to his feet slowly. His hand rests conspicuously on the door behind him, not reaching for the handle strangely, instead just resting his palm across its metallic face.

"I've already told you, we're just here because I knew there would be food in stock..." he steps away from the door as his head locks onto the ground, "the foods in there."

Ryan studies Max once more to find his hand trembling even though he tries to hide it behind his back. Meanwhile, a bead of sweat drips down Caid brow despite the temperature being on the colder side. Blake hesitates before looking to Ryan, but by then Ryan's already made up his mind. It's serious enough to make the visibly uncomfortable, even a bit irrational, but they're comfortable enough to turn their back to it. He had a sinking suspicions about what was behind that door and it might be better to just get it out of the way now.

He steps by Blake and winds around Max who watch him with a worried curiosity, but evidently no one expected him to be so forward as he slides his hand onto the door handle and opens it without a second thought. Max releases a yelp and Caid outstretches his hand but none of them can react fast enough to prevent Ryan from taking the initiative. Just to be safe, he reaches for his pistol and prepares to kick if something jumps out. To his delight and everyone else's surprise the back room is completely empty. Ryan sighs, as he suspected, it was too quiet for anything to be behind the door.

Caid and Max look at each other in complete disbelief as Blake makes the first move, "Was something supposed to be behind that door?"

Max's face is as pale as a piece of paper as he talks very cautiously, "Uh... no... just storage... as I said, it's just storage."

Caid takes a deep breath as he runs a hand through his hair. He bites down on his lip and shoots Max a glance but ultimately just elects to remain silent. When things go quiet for too long, he starts pacing in the background without ever letting the back door leave his sight.

Seeing as how no one is going to come clean, Ryan steps into the back room which Max flinch.

"What?" Ryan asks, Blake seconds the confusion.

Max rubs the back of his head and avoids eye contact, "N-nothing, just watch your step, the lighting can be bad back there."

Blake steps up to the door and turns back to the teens waiting awkwardly outside it, "Caid and Max, grab whatever you can find out here, Ryan and I will check in the back."

"Right," Caid says as he takes the opportunity to meander over to the mostly empty frozen treats section.

Max steps up to the door but gets blocked off by Blake, "Hey, I know my way around back there the best. My older sister used to work here so, I guess, I don't want you to miss something. I think I should take a look first, you know. Just trying to do my part."

Blake doesn't budge, "We'll be thorough, don't worry," he waves to Caid, "Now settle whatever beef you have with him."

Max swallows hard as he looks deeper into the back room with a plethora of boxes and hidden spaces. He struggles to comes to terms with the direction, but is forced to comply as Ryan and Blake start walking deeper in. Max just watches them exit view around a stocked metal shelf in silence.

The back room is eerily quiet, not just because it was musty, dark, and cramped, but because it lacked that familiar boiler room hymn of a concrete storage room. Half full boxes of miscellaneous objects clutter the metals shelves containing backup non-perishables and cleaning supplies alike. As he visually browses through the unopened cereal boxes, he simultaneously ensures nothing is lurking in the dark of the room. The room itself was not impressively large, but with no active lighting, the back of the room seemed more shadow than room.

Suddenly a light flashes and three fluorescent lights flash on along the ceiling bringing the room back from the brink. Ryan snaps around to find Blake standing by the light switch starring at him blankly.

"Were you not going to turn that on?" he wonders, mostly confused.

"Oh, yeah... right," Ryan admits. He can feel himself loosing his cool... but why?

With the room far less foreboding, he walks down the remainder of the storage room with a bit swifter of a pace. By the time he reaches the end he finds the entire room completely void of any commotion, blood, or zombies. He releases an audible sigh of relief. 

"That tense huh?" Blake laughs as he looks across the room at all the boxes, "I'll go let the other guys know that the coast is clear, why don't you take a look around."

Ryan nods as Blake makes a speedy departure.

He's never actually been in a storage room before, at least not that he can recall. The dull gray, concrete walls seem far less molded over than he envisioned, there even seemed to be fresh-ish paint in some areas. The metals shelves also looked neatly order, the boxes were handled with care, and the HVAC stuff, or what he assumes to be HVAC stuff, seems orderly and in it's own separate region of the room. Overall, he can't shake the feeling that he got inside his own head on this one. 

That's when his eyes fall on the last unexplored region of the room—the walk-in freezer. Curiosity washes over him more than anything else at the sight of it, but then he notices a box next to the door. The brown cardboard has a large impression across the top as if someone stomped on it—in a hurry. Beside the door is a lightly colored smear, like the color snot turns after resting on a surface for too long. All of this leads up to the handle which harbors faint white stains across its surface, the same texture as salt accumulated from dried sweat.

Ryan's eyes narrow as he checks to make sure Blake isn't in the room. He then proceeds carefully towards the door with his hand reaching under his shirt to his gun. The handle is strikingly lukewarm for a freezer handle, but upon tugging he finds the insulation quite burdensome to move. It doesn't budge on his first attempt so he tries again with a bit more effort and luckily gets it to crack open. To be safe, he lightly kicks the door so that a clanking sound would echo inside. He waits for a moment—nothing returns. He glances around one last time at the box, at the smear, and at the salt, before slowly swinging the door open and stepping inside.

The dark freezer clutches on to its secrets greedily for as long as the shadows allow, but then the light fully enters and Ryan's eyes land on a frozen visage. Two eyes, wide and frightened, stare directly at him so still he thought it a statue. Crimson trails leak from those cold eyes until their journey ends in a solid puddle on the floor. Frozen, blocky strands of hair run down the scalp as if being torn out and clumped together while falling off only to freeze back to the skin haphazardly. A mouth hangs open with frozen saliva dripping down the cheeks—green mucus mixed in. Two arms dangle to the sides now frozen into the form as nothing more than an extension of the ghost. One of the arms has a blue wristband with some sort of ancient tongue inscribed into it. Two legs exist, each with a pink tennis shoe wrapped around the frozen toes. Those pink sneakers, once used for its money's worth, now float six inches above the floor. The entire form levitating as if supernaturally, if not for the stiffness of a solitary rope.

Ryan freezes colder than the ice around him, the door slowly drifting closes as he becomes trapped in here with... it. He stumbles backwards, his hands shaking uncontrollable in spite of the cold as he nearly knocks over a box of frozen food. Breath becomes sparse causing him to crave it and gasp for air increasingly quickly—the frigged air cutting at his lungs. His knees begin to tremble as he instinctively draws his gun but it slips out of his shaking hand and crashes against the ground. He looks around at the metal box and finds them closing in on him like oppressive hands crushing him.

He falls to his knees and slaps the ground to find his pistol and upon touching it, Steve flashes before his eyes. He pushes the gun away and crawls backwards in disbelief. Steve kneeling there, gun in hand. His eyes fall back on the gun, the trigger, the pressure, the barrel, the scream, the terror, the zombies, the uncertainty. He can't breathe, he can't breathe, he can't breathe. His stomach begins to churn. He's going to...

Acid splashes across the ground as he leans over it. He wipes his lips with his sleeve and looks back to the gun slowly, it's small form resting quietly beside a block of frozen swine. He looks up and then looks down. Amidst all of this, his hands never truly stop shaking. 

Begrudgingly he crawls over to his gun and holds it to his chest. He closes his eyes and just breathes... that's all he knows how to do. 

Eventually he faintly hears his name from outside the freezer and promptly slides his feet underneath him. He pauses as he stands up and nearly runs into the body. Glancing around, he finds an box cutting knife lying on top of a large beef box and quickly grabs it, cuts the rope, cuts the rope of the corpse's neck and tosses the rope behind a box in the corner of the room. He then backs up and steps to the door. As he does, he notices the blue wristband wrapped around the corpse's arm, the warn out latin phase feeling cryptic in his mind. He pauses once more upon reaching the door and forces himself to take one last, slow breath. Finally, he opens the door and steps out.

"What's in there?" Blake asks so incredibly naive that Ryan wants to vomit all over again.

Ryan shakes his head as Blake leans over and catches a glance at the corpse. His eyes widen, "Is that?"

"Zombie froze in here," Ryan lies, "There was no fight."

Blake nods understandingly as he rubs his chin and looks away, a mixture of confusion and speculation clashing across his demeanor. In the meantime, Ryan realizes he's been holding his pistol and promptly stuffs it back underneath his shirt before Blake can turn back around. When the guy ultimately does turn back around, Ryan has already preoccupied himself with grabbing a nearby box of cereal he saw earlier.

"So we're all good?" Blake wonders looking at him sincerely.

Ryan nods, "Yeah, everything's fine."

Blake dwells beside him a bit too long for comfort, "You sure we're good?"

Ryan nods once more, "Yeah... we're good."

Lifting the box with trembling hands, he keeps his back to Blake as he carries it out into the front of the store. Max and Caid wait in the store expectantly, rushing up to him as he makes an appearance.

"There was nothing back there right?" Max asks quickly, "It was empty? Right?"

Ryan bites his tongue, what should he say? Caid begins looking Ryan over starting at the end and proceeding downwards. By the time he reaches Ryan's hands, he's already hid them underneath the box and away from view.

"There was a woman," Ryan admits carefully, "She froze... no fight... must have been painless."

Max looks at Caid before closing his eyes and turning away, "It's alright," he eventually says, "glad we got that over with. Now we have plenty of food and no one got hurt. Everything's fine, everything's great."

Caid clearly doesn't buy it, but elects to withhold judgment once more. The subject matter, for some reason, doesn't strike Caid as foreign as it should. Ryan too, conceals his lingering thoughts, and moves past them with his head down. The remainder of their time collecting resources is spent with trivial small talk. Small talk that Ryan detests more than Steve's drunkenness.
