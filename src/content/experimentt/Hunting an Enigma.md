A rather large cold front rolls in later that afternoon. A once vibrant autumn day slowly changes its tone into a dull, brewing storm. The wind picks up and rain looms over them threatening to fall at any moment. Trees bow towards the streets before waving their branches to and fro. The clouds cast a wide shadow across the landscape.

Steve, Bill, and Ryan all find themselves in their small blue car driving down the road in the town they expect to never see again. They take the trail through the forest to make it back without running into any other military installations and, quickly enough, find themselves driving through neighborhoods again. The new scenery of cookie-cutter neighborhoods is exactly as before, multiple houses are broken into while others were left untouched. The saddening part of it all is that most of the house damages were far too precise to be made by a zombie. Looting after only two days feels far too abrupt. As luck would have it, the streets are mostly completely barren other than a few parked cars that were never touched. They pass a few houses where Ryan gets the feeling that people reside inside but he doesn’t see any humanoid figures—zombie or human. The only intel they received on Heimes’ whereabouts was his house address which is why they're still in the suburbs to begin with. 

The small dark brown house they roll up to is quite the anticlimactic sight. Instead of a garage, the house just has a small gravel driveway and the yard is smaller than most around it, being occupied with unkept bushes and trees. To the sides of the house, and connecting each house of the neighborhood, is a tall wooden privacy fence. For the way that Heimes presented himself, he really didn’t play the part when he got home. Not only is the house rather small, but it’s as plain as can be. If it wasn’t for the sprawling bushes and trees, you would think the house is for sale. It’s oddly reminiscent of Bill’s generic house which makes him think that Heimes might also have picked up his taste for decoration from the military.

“I’ll go around back, you two go straight in. Don’t bother knocking,” Bill explains as he quickly scrambles out of the car and into the thick vegetation. 

Steve steps out of the driver’s seat and readies his gun, “You have your shotgun, right?”

Ryan nods as he reaches over to the seat next to him gripping the powerful weapon before exiting the car. He glances around to look for any possible threats that might be lurking nearby or happen to hear the car and decided to investigate. Thankfully, he finds an empty street and begins advancing towards the front door along with Steve. He steps onto the porch and analyses the door, but Steve cuts him off before he can do anything.

“Let’s go through the window,” he whispers, stepping over to the only window on the front side of the house.

“What do you mean? It’s probably locked,” Ryan argues.

Steve doesn’t wait for a response, he smashes the butt of the gun straight into the window opening a small hole with a loud crash. Ryan aims his gun immediately and looks around, with a loud noise there’s no telling if a zombie will run out of nowhere to attack them. The surroundings remain stationary; not even the stormy sky moves for a brief moment. The coast seems to be clear.

Steve reaches through the hole in the window and quickly unlocks it. Without so much as a scratch, he’s able to safely open the window and crawl through—gun raised. 

Ryan follows him into an office space, having to climb over a desk with miscellaneous documents dispersed across it. He jumps over it to land on a fuzzy, gray carpet littered with fallen bulletin board pins and string. Large books shelves line the walls with every variety of books you could think of yet somehow fit within the confines of a small office space. Several books were missing amidst the wide variety, but somehow it seemed intentional and rushed given that many of the books surrounding the gaps were pulled out to various degrees. Overall, the room felt like the bat cave of an overworked office worker, but Heimes was a principal, what would he need with this kind of room?

Ryan takes notice of Steve waving his hand at him and nodding his head to follow. He has to force himself to comply while evading further curiosity.

The office exits into a living room which harbors the most generic setup in the American family dictionary—one that puts Bill’s living room to shame. A couch with a table next to it all facing a TV. That’s it. A clock also hangs above the TV, but otherwise the living room is completely plain. 

The plan is for Ryan to search the kitchen while Steve and Bill search the bedrooms and garage respectively. They’ll meet back in the living room as soon as they conclude their searches. 

Ryan quietly transitions over to the kitchen connected to the living room and takes a look around. He opens every cupboard, fridge door, appliance, and box only to find absolutely nothing out of the ordinary. Then, as he shifts some cereal boxes out of the way, he notices a little box in the back of the cupboard. It’s only about 10 inches wide and four inches tall—about the size of a jewelry box. A small key hole is placed on a lock in the front of the box holding the top lid shut. Ryan pulls the box out to inspect it further.

He places it on the table taking note that it’s fairly light, in fact, it’s too light to have any jewelry in it at all. Something small, but valuable must be behind that lock, but how to unlock it. Then the realization dawns on him about the key hole and the key Heimes gave him. He reaches into his pocket and removes the tiny golden key. Without a second thought, he pushes the rigid peace of metal into the slot. The key fits perfectly  and results in a soft, satisfying click after the key turns 90 degrees. The lock pops open as Ryan carefully lifts the hatch to view what lies inside.

Inside is a small piece of paper, clearly ripped from a notebook without regarding how neatly it was done. Bold, black ink covers the surface of the paper forming into poorly constructed words. It’s as if a toddler was given a sharpie and told to write a poem in a notebook without clarifying that the paper doesn't need to be ripped out from the bindings. The only issue with that idea, is that the words are sloppy but the grammar and content is far too mature for a toddler. Ryan’s eyes are glued to the strange font as he reads:

**Ryan. You made a mistake by not accepting my offer and, as such, will have to suffer the consequences. Just remember: Don’t trust him.**

Ryan drops the paper back onto the kitchen table and stares off into the distance. Consequences? What could he mean by that? Why won’t Mr. Heimes ever give him a straight answer. Sure he gave him the possibility for survival with the key, but that doesn’t explain why he’s now ditching him with some lackluster advice. Don’t trust him? Who is ‘him’ and why is he not trustworthy. So many questions and so few answers.

His stream of consciousness is suddenly interrupted by the forceful opening of the back door: the door directly next to the kitchen in the laundry room. As quickly as the door opens, a figure turns the corner of the laundry room and faces Ryan head on.

Heimes’ confident demeanor towers within the kitchen doorway as he notices the note and box next to Ryan, “I didn’t think you’d make it here so quickly?”

Ryan debates pointing his gun but notices that Heimes’ eyes are twitching along with his hands. On top of that, his skin seems grayer than before, as if the humanity was slowly being drained from him. Ultimately, Ryan settles for a nod. He wants to hear what Heimes has to say.

“I take it you want an explanation,” Heimes’ voice lowers but his not so subtle twitching continues.

“What is this note?” Ryan asks, picking the paper back up.

“Exactly as it says,” Heimes responds with a shaky voice that still retains most of his interrogator charisma, “You made a mistake by refusing my offer.”

Ryan takes a few steps around the table and closer to Heimes, “And who’s him?!”

Bill enters the kitchen and glances at Ryan before realizing just who he is talking to. He raises his gun at Heimes.

“Put your hands up now!” he shouts walking closer to him, “Ryan tie him up.”

Bill throws a nylon rope over to him and continues to close the distance between himself and Heimes. Steve, from down the hall, exits out and scopes in on Heimes keeping his distance.

“Oh, please. I’m not here to fight you,” Heimes explains, putting his hands in the air and not resisting when Ryan ties them together behind his back. 

“You’re coming with us,” Bill says sternly as soon as Ryan finishes with his knot. 

“And where do you want to take me?” Heimes asks, startlingly calm and composed.

Bill doesn’t respond and instead grabs a handkerchief and begins rolling it into a strip, probably to gag Heimes’ mouth. 

“So you don’t want to talk? That’s fine, but I’m busy so I must be leaving,” Heimes says as he snaps the rope behind his back with raw strength. 

Immediately following, Steve fires a round at Heimes hitting him in the right leg. Heimes almost doesn’t react to the shock of getting shot and instead just continues his slight twitching before he turns to leave. 

“I’ll see you again soon, Ryan,” he announces before walking out the back door, tanking a few bullets from both Bill and Steve.

“What the hell, is that man on?!” Bill exclaims under his breath before making a gesture to Steve. The two men both dash out together in pursuit of their target. However, their plan falls apart quickly when they get outside and find absolutely no sign of Heimes. No footprints, no disturbed grass, not even a noise of him running off. The tall fence surrounding the backyard looks completely untouched, a stark difference between the front yard which is mostly overgrowing. It’s as if he completely disappeared. 

While aiming down sight and scanning the area, they exchange a few words.

“Any idea where he could have gone?” Steve questions commandingly.

Bill raises his gun to check the roof but doesn’t find anything up there either, “He clearly has some mode of transportation we’re unaware of. Besides, how the hell did he just walk off so many shots?” he looks at the grass, “There’s no blood trail at all!”

“There has to be some kind of armor, right?” Steve turns back to the house and faces Ryan standing at the doorway, “What did he say to you before we got out there?”

Ryan recalls the note and has to force the questions from overtaking the foreground of his mind. 

“He just handed me this note,” Ryan says waving the piece of paper in the air.

Bill nods his head a few times, “That doesn’t give us much but he did say that he’d see you again soon. That should give us a bit of hope that we can find him again.”

Steve lets out a deep, tense sigh, “I don’t know, but we better figure it out quickly or else—”

The rattling of the fence cuts Steve off. The wooden beams and metal hinges groan as they rock back and forth as if something were banging on it from the other side. Shortly after, the rattling begins from another point of origin on the other side of the fence. Within seconds, loud banging noises are ringing out from dozens of spots all around the yard. The fence itself can’t handle the stress and begins to bend inward like malleable metal. A dull roar of a dead mob protrudes through the cracks that are slowly forming all across the wooden surface.

Bill turns back to the house, “Get inside! NOW!”

No arguments are made as the whole squad scrambles back inside the house and slams the door shut behind them. 

“Get back to the car!” Steve calls out as they continue to rush through the house nearly tripping on furniture.

It is only after they are all successfully out the front door that their current situation is fully realized. Over a dozen zombies stand in the front yard and the subsequent street. To make matters worse, a single rain drop falls onto the concrete sidewalk. It is quickly followed up by a second, a third, a fourth, until those drops of water on the pavement are stomped on by a mass of running human corpses. The rain grows stronger as Bill and Steve open fire.

Ryan shakily raises his shotgun, he’s not used to the weight of the weapon and the current situation sends a rush of adrenaline through his body—a feeling he unfortunately associated with the undead freaks. He can’t seem to keep his body still to aim, but he attempts it anyway.

Bill and Steve are both pretty accurate taking down a zombie with every three shots or so, but Ryan misses his first shot. Pumps the gun like Bill showed him and misses the second. Then he notices how Steve and Bill are both putting their cheeks on their weapons to aim better. Ryan quickly shoves his right cheek into the gun right behind the weird looking iron sight and immediately understands how it works. He pumps the gun again and this time points the gun right at the torso of a zombie only a few feet away. He pulls the trigger and simply watches as the zombie gets hit with the barrage of buckshot pellets and falls to its back with holes littering its body. The kick of the gun is quite strong at first, but he finds himself adjusting to it quickly. At the very least, he has some affinity or talent for shooting this weapon—one that he plans on using to its fullest capacity.

Steve stops firing for a moment and yells, “Reload!”

Then as if their luck wasn’t bad enough, the closed front door bursts open and zombies rush out through it. The whole group takes notice of the collapsing door and have the collective thought to run towards the fence line perpendicular with the street.. 

Ryan reaches the fence and throws his shotgun over before jumping up and grabbing onto the top. Upon looking back at his group he finds Steve right on his trail, but Bill is on the ground being grappled by a zombie. Without even thinking, Ryan pulls the pistol from his pants and aims it right at the zombie’s head. Ensuring the safety is off, he fires a shot praying that he doesn’t miss like always, but this time was different. The bullet lands perfectly in the temple of the zombie allowing Bill to throw it off and just narrowly evade the cold arms of zombies behind him. Immediately after that, Ryan finishes hopping the fence and waits for his team.

Steve gets over first soon followed by Bill which is also followed by banging on the fence from the outside. Bill and Steve run right past Ryan and head towards the back side of the yard. Ryan follows but he could have sworn he saw hands grabbing the top of the fence behind him and one of them was less of a hand and more of a bear claw. He doesn’t have time to ponder the thought, however, so he continues to trail closely behind the two men. 

They each hop the back fence together and fortunately find nothing beyond that. Bill leads the way as they dead sprint through the small field behind the yards.

About three minutes later, they catch their breaths together next to a convenient store a couple blocks away. The rain never picked up so they thankfully only have to contest their heaving breaths and a light drizzle. No zombies loiter nearby so they can each relax a bit.

“What was that?!” Steve exclaims in between breaths.

“Something must have drawn them,” Bill gulps some more air in a very strained manner, “to us.”

“At least we're all alive,” Ryan admits, finding that he’s the least out of breath between the three of them.

Bill, who is leaning against the wall of the convenience store, slips down to his bottom with his back against the wall. His head sags a little before giving a sideways glance over to Ryan. He releases a pitiful cough as he takes another forced breath. One of his arms shifts over to nurse his abdomen.

“Thanks for shooting that bastard,” he says softly, “but I think you were too late.”

Bill raises his arm revealing a fresh, bloody bite mark tattooed onto his forearm. This is further accentuated by multiple gashes on his torso, seemingly from the zombie prying at him with bony fingers. His body shakes while his movements feel weak and drawn out, yet none of it shows on his face. After a brief moment, his arm falls to his side and he slumps down even more. 

“What the hell,” Steve spits out as he crouches down next to Bill inspecting the extent of the wounds. Fear boils up in the man's eyes as he traces the edges of each gash frantically with his eyes, "There has to be something we can do."

Bill refuses to move, “I’m fine, we don’t know how long I have, though.”

Steve looks at him in disbelief, “What? N-no, we're not leaving you.” Steve shouts, his eyes growing empathetic and emotional, “We don’t even know how this disease is transmitted. If they can treat my family, they can treat you.”

Bill stands up slowly as he reaches into his back pocket and pulls out a handkerchief. He unfolds the cloth and slowly places it against his wounded arm as the tender region of his body forces out a grimace. The makeshift bandage is immediately soaked in blood as he carefully readjusts himself to put less pressure on the gashes.

“Come on Steve, don't lie to yourself,” Bill concludes with absolute certainty as he winces away any pain, “I’d love to complain about it… but we both know what's going to happen,” he turns to Ryan, "How long do you think I got kid?"

Ryan takes a step back in disbelief. The same guy who scolded him for being heartless is now succumbing to thing Ryan discredited. His life aside, Ryan can't help but feel like this is some cruel joke.

"I-I", Ryan stutters, "I don't know. Some people got sick and lasted a few hours but others... turned really fast I guess," he moves his hand to his shirt cradling the pistol underneath, "I-I never really counted."

“I'm in a bit of a pickle, aren't I?” Bill sighs as he uses his good arm to reach for a holster strapped to his leg, "It's a shame this has to be the way I go out."

“I won’t accept that,” Steve asserts as he grabs the man's hand and doesn't entertain the idea further, “There has to be a way out of this. We’ll get you back to the camp in time for treatment–and–and then we’ll find Heimes while we–”

“Steve!” Bill shouts as he holds back the pain caused by his injuries while wrapping some gauze around his abdomen, “Don’t be irrational. Your best bet is to head back to camp and explain what we found out. We could wait for Seth Heimes on our own, but we don't know how long that's going to take. For all we know, he might be planning to come back in two weeks."

Steve stares intently as his lifelong friend before running a hand through his hair while pacing around the area. His free arm twitches nervously while he looks around at the surrounding environment.

Ryan debates offering his point of view but sharply shuts that line of thought down after remembering what always happens when he speaks up. Besides, he has nothing to add to this situation. He may want to leave this family behind, but this is not the way he wanted to do it. Nobody deserves to be put into a situation like this—not even Steve.

Bill finishes his bandage work before using the wall to slowly rise off the ground. He then draws his pistol and reaches it out to Steve, “I know you well enough to know that I can't get through to you. Therefore, I'll come along and pray I last long enough to make it back to the checkpoint. If that doesn't happen, I trust you know what to do,” he stares at Steve insistently.

Steve looks back but with the most remorseful face Ryan's ever seen on a man. His eyes are filled with disbelief as he slowly shakes his head unconsciously. His lips whisper 'no' as his fists argue on whether they should close or remain twitching. 

"You can't ask me to do that," he finally says.

Bill narrows his eyes and tosses the gun over which forces Steve to catch it, "That's an order."

Steve clearly wants to retaliate more than anything else, but in his mind, an order is an order. Begrudgingly, he accepts the directive and the team trudges forward into the broken world.
