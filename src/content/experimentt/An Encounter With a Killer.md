The apartment stands on 26th street; the car dealer is on 5th. The walk down would normally take about a half hour but due to severe traffic it takes much longer than usual. For some reason cars lined the streets in a packed manner and even when the traffic lights turn green, the cars don’t budge. Every now and then the group of boys have to back track a block or two due to seeing an unwanted group of people all crowding the streets ahead. Usually they are surrounding some dead animal or something but they don’t want to bother them so they stuck to the shadows. Along the way they have a serious conversation about how they would drive back due to the terrible traffic, but every time they come across a side road that’s empty they gain enough hope to continue on. At one point they pass a clothing store and discover almost a hundred people standing around inside looking at the floor instead of the clothes all around them. Most of their clothes are tattered and ragged so it's no wonder they're so desperate for a good deal.

Eventually they arrive just two blocks from the car dealer and get stopped by a mob loitering on the road.  

“How do we get around this one?” Caid asks as they crouch behind one of the cars stuck in the traffic jam.

Right as he finishes the horde of zombies begins to collectively shuffle through an alleyway—their attention diverted to something out of view. They watch as the large horde slowly diminishes in size until it completely disappears between the two buildings. Then, not even one minute later, smoke begins to rise up from one of the buildings. The wails of corpses dying a second time echoes about the town as the inferno grows large enough for anyone in a mile radius to see. 

As the fire continues to rage, a group of humans rush out from the front of the building and hurry across the street. Ryan and the others stay hidden and watch as the group of seven hustle over to the cannabis factory and head inside eagerly. 

“Let’s use the opening to get past, but stay low and try not to be seen,” Blake directs as he takes the initiative to start moving.

They each take turns crouching behind anything and everything in the street to avoid being in view of the looters smoking a join. Puffs of smoke leave many of their mouths as they continue to dig through every form of weed in the store. Before too long, all four of them make it past the cannabis store without being spotted.

“Let’s hurry, we’re almost there,” Max says as he hustles past Ryan towards the car dealership directly in front of them. 

By the time Caid finishes sneaking across the street, Max is already at the door trying to pry it open.

“Of course its locked,” Max comments as they all catch back up with him. He stops pulling at the door and turns to face them, “So how are we going to get in? Break the window?”

The car dealership is a medium sized one story building with almost entirely glass walls. A variety of cars sit around the parking lot with a unique price tag that exceeds Ryan's former budge by multiple orders of magnitude. Inside the car dealership are all of the brand new and exciting cars including two Mustangs and a red Corvette. The biggest challenge now is not causing a scene as they break in.

"I'd like to keep breaking the glass as a last resort," Blake decides as he looks around the lot. Finding nothing promising in the front he proceeds to start walking around the side of the building, “I’ll go check the back to see if there is another way in.”

As Blake leaves, Caid takes the opportunity to check out the cars in the outside parking lot. The first one he looks at is a Suburban.

"Say Max, doesn't your mom drive one of these?" he says running his hand over the hood of the car.

Max, being caught off guard by the mention of his family, doesn't respond right away as he inspects the care with a sort of somber nostalgia. Caid takes notice of this reaction and motions to apologize, but Max beats him to the punch, “Yeah... you're right, this is the same care. My family isn’t huge, but my mom used to run a small daycare and needed the seating capacity to carry a bunch of kids.”

“Oh, I see,” Caid says with a sigh of relief, "I knew I recognized it. This is the same car that used to drive us home from soccer practice, you know, back in middle school."

Max rolls his eyes with a smile, "Don't get me started, we were the dynamic duo..." his face grows long in a dramatically sarcastic manner, "Until someone ditched the squad to go play basket ball."

Caid shrugs as he steps back from the car, "What? I hit a growth spirt. You should have too."

Max steps up to his friend and places a hand on his head. Then, keeping his hand parallel with the ground, he pushes it forward until it hits the top of Caid's chest, "Yeah... I'm still waiting mister giant."

The two boys laugh, not because the joke was inherently funny, but Ryan get the feeling that they both really wanted to laugh. To say he missed it is probably an understatement, he truly had no idea how much their stupid banter lightened the mood. Ryan welcomes the opportunity to divert his attention from everything that's happened today.

Suddenly, Blake appears around the corner of the building and calls out, "Hey guys, you should come see this."

The boys quickly divert their attention from the grid of new vehicles and rush around the building with Blake. On the other side, they find two corpses bound together with a chain at the base of a light pull. No bite marks or injuries are visible on either person’s body but it is very clear that they haven't been breathing for a while.

"What the hell?" Caid closes his eyes and places a hand on his forehead.

Blake gets a bit closer to the corpse which emanate an awful odor that puts feces to shame. He crouches down next to one of the corpses and points to the face “They must have died of dehydration. You can see that their lips are parched.” 

“Is this all you wanted to show us?” Ryan asks a little confused. Sure the scene is sad, but Blake isn't the kind of guy to drag down the mood, especially after their little moment.

“No,” Blake counters, “I just didn't want you guys to get caught off guard... What I actually wanted to say was that the garage door is unlocked, but I’m not strong enough to open it alone.”

Without having to say anything more, all four boys walk over to the garage door and grab the bottom. With a quick count to three, their collective strength is more than enough to bring the door up. To all of their surprise, the door is considerably heavier than a one car door probably should be. They actually lose ground as Blake and Ryan have to transition from an overhand to an underhand grip, but alas, they succeed at pushing it just high enough for them to get through. 

On the other side sits a pile of boxes collapsed to the floor. They must have been leaning against the door, making it harder to open. Ryan looks around the room but Max has other ideas as he rushes straight in.

“You’re going to get yourself killed if you keep rushing in,” Blake criticizes as he follows. 

"It's a tactical advancement," Max defends as he nearly trips over a wheel.

Luckily, all the interior doors are unlocked so getting around the building is a breeze. Caid goes straight to the Mustangs and begins checking them out. Blake searches the offices for the keys while Max searches everywhere else. Ryan voluntarily stays back by the garage door to keep watch. He’s not going to make the same mistake he did at Heimes’ house.

Speaking of, it makes him wonder where is Heimes these days. It's rather astonishing how long it's been since he's thought about it, but Heimes was the one who gave him a key back in the high school to save everyone's life. All of the teens he's been hanging out with are only alive because of that action. Then again, Heimes is also the one who killed Bill, got him infected, and ultimately led to Steve's... It seems life has been so hectic the last few days, he's forgotten about what happened before all this. It pains him to admit it, but maybe distancing himself from those memories is the right thing to do. Living in his head won't solve any problems and, most of all, it'd be living in _spite_ of them.

Then as his thoughts continue to chug along, he notices movement on the street. A group of people, the same ones from earlier, all walk around the block and before he can move to evade their line of sight, he’s spotted. One of the group members point directly at him and that justifies a steady march in his direction. The group consists of seven thug-looking individuals with most wearing hoodies, leather jackets, or light coats. Many of them have tattoos on their faces and walk with a certain narcissistic stride that keeps Ryan on edge. Each one has a weapon of some sorts, no guns, but basic melee equipment such as bats and pipes. One even has a machete and proceeds to flaunt that fact as he waves it around as they walk. With no point in hiding from the thugs he walks over to greet them in the street rather than against the building.

“Hey, I saw you guys taking out that horde of zombies,” Ryan starts as they get within conversational range.

“Oh perfect,” one of them with a hoodie on and their hood up says, “I remember seeing you and some other boys sneaking by.”

Ryan curses himself for being spotted before. They were being courteous for not saying anything after spotting their poor execution of stealth.

The thug rubs his chin with a mischievous glint in his eyes, “What could you guys be hiding I wonder?”

Ryan takes a look back at the car dealer unsure if he can trust these people, “We’re just here to buy a car, you know how it is,” Ryan laughs awkwardly as his heart begins to race, "Prices are pretty reasonable these days."

One of the group members is a woman dressed in skimpy clothing with her cleavage distractingly visible. She turns to the man with a hood and seductively grabs his arm, pressing her body against his side, “Hey babe, I really want that Corvette in there. Can you get it for me?”

The man chuckles as his eyes inform everyone what he's thinking, “Alright babe,” he turns to the rest of the group, “You heard her, lets go get the car. Grab a few more while you're at it.”

“Wait!” Ryan interjects, “We got here first, we should get to pick. Isn’t that fair?”

Something snaps inside the man’s head which causes the rest of the group to begin flaunting their weapons such as baseball bats and batons. One moment they're smirking at him and the next they all lift their weapons in perfect sync—like a messed up dance team. Ryan takes a step back as he can't mentally catalog so many weapons in such a short time. All five goons tap their weapons in their hands in nearly identical fashions which only creeps him out further. Even their sadistic smiles seem to be inherited despite each of them looking vastly different from one another.

“You don’t get it, do you?” the man says menacingly, a dragon tattoo revealing itself on his face that Ryan didn't notice before, "We don't have to ask kids for permission."

Ryan holds his hands up, “We don’t need to start anything, we can come up with a compromise.” 

The woman steps away from the hooded man and cross her arms while pushing her hips to the side, her demeanor habitually accentuating her breast which causes Ryan to instinctively avert his eyes. She studies Ryan with a disapproving look. “I think he’s in that prison group. The same one that rejected us.”

“Is that true?” the man asks as if taking turns to speak, “Are you with the prison?”

It’s pretty evident by this point that yes is the wrong response. He needs to come up with a convincing lie. Even if he has a weapon, there is no way he can take on five adults at the same time.

“No, we’re just a group of survivors getting by like the rest of you,” Ryan tries find it to be a good balance of truth and remaining vague.

"Is that so?" one of the five guys says with a twisted smile finally breaking away from the conformity of the gang, "Just survivors?"

"Y-yeah," Ryan stutters, kicking himself for showing weakness in a time like this.

"I like this guy," the man says, "I'll play with him, the rest of you can head inside."

“Alright, Jimmy, you take care of this shrimp," the hooded figure says as he and the rest of the group start walking by.

"It's a shame," the woman says sadly, he pace slowing momentarily, "I like em young. But Jimmy's prey aren't my type."

Ryan tries to run off first and warn his friends but Jimmy's far faster than he expected and manages to him over before he can get even five steps away. The rest of the mob heads to the garage while Ryan stumbles back to his feet.

Jimmy pulls out a knife and walks straight at Ryan with a crocked grin on his face “Alright kid, I don’t like killing people… oh wait, yes I do.”

Jimmy slashes horizontally at Ryan, only missing by an inch as he evades backwards. Without a moment of hesitation he steps forward with another strike directed towards Ryan’s head. As quickly as he can, Ryan reaches into his jacket and pulls out his knife to parry the strike. 

Ryan doesn’t have any experience with knife on knife combat but one parry isn’t that hard to do right. He quickly finds out that the small blades are not like swords and block easily. He manages to make contact with the attacker’s knife but it only shifts the blade to run down the side of his arm instead of his face.

“Ah,” Ryan gasps as the sheering pain exploding through his nerves.

He falls to his back, throwing a foot into Jimmy's abdomen before getting to his feet in a uncalculated jumble of motions. His concentration is fleeting just like the blood gushing out of his arm. The slash isn’t super deep, but it's also the same arm that got cut on the fence days ago which means the former injury has just been unsealed. He switches the hand holding the knife and finds the grip to be very uncomfortable in his left hand.

“So you’ve got some fight in you?” Jimmy says with a insane grin and excited eyes, “I love it when they fight back. It makes it so much sweeter when they die.”

“Who are you?” Ryan forces out through the pain in his arm.

The man named Jimmy slows his pursuit as he performatively places a hand to his head and flicks his hair, “Oh, little ole me. I’m Jimmy the terrible, Jimmy the ripper, but really my title is former serial killer, Jimmy Lashing.”

Ryan remembers hearing that name on the news. He got caught just a few weeks ago and was holding up in the prison. When all of the inmates got transferred out of the prison, Jimmy must have escaped and wound up here. He's fighting a professional in the art of murder. This is no longer a gang beat down, it's a fight to the death.

“That’s an intimidating knife you got there,” Jimmy points out raising an eyebrow at how impressed he is, “I can’t wait to start slashing throats with it.”

Ryan takes another step back, “How do you live with all the killing you do?”

Jimmy's visage is overflowing with insane theatrics as his eyes widen in asynchronous patterns and his smile grows larger and more crooked, his hands wave with each word as if a unique gesture is mandatory, and his knees stay bent so that a quick dash is never out of the question. The thing that throws Ryan off is not the perverse mentality and malicious intent, it's that Jimmy feels... to comical to be real. No serial killer he's ever seen in documentaries ever acted this way. It feels far too... performative. It makes Ryan's skin crawl to think his life is being threatened by such a comical villain. Of all the things he's had to overcome. 

Jimmy throws his hands up in the air and overdramatizes his speaking, “I don’t know, how do you deal with waking up in the morning? It’s part of life, you wake up, eat breakfast, and kill someone. It’s my _profession_," he hangs onto the last word a bit too for it to feel natural, "Now… without further ado,” he bows to Ryan and his voice grows far less dramatic and much more serious and menacing, "Let's see what you're really made of."

Using his lowered stance, Jimmy is able to dash forward at a strikingly fast speed and swings his knife over and over. Each strike is intentionally placed to be nonlethal—yet so precise that Ryan can hardly see it coming. Ryan evades a deeper cut by trying his best to dodge or parry with this knife, but he knows he can't last long. Eventually, Jimmy gets tired of the simple slashing, grunts in annoyance, and stabs Ryan right through the hand. Despite the overwhelming onset of pain, Ryan uses his brass knuckles to punch the side of Jimmy’s face. Then as he pulls the knife away, the blade slices the edge of Jimmy’s cheek, only cutting enough to result in a slight trickle of disturbingly dark blood.

Ryan steps back hitting the hood of a parked car behind him as Jimmy slowly touches his cheek. He chuckles for a moment which quickly leads to a sadistic laugh. He continues to giggle as his eyes grow to max wideness and he tilts his head while starring right at Ryan. No anger is left in the man’s face, only the insanity and joy remain, yet behind that Ryan gets a subtle hint of relief. For some reason, Ryan can't shake the feeling that this fight is becoming more enlightening for the serial killer with each passing slash.

Sweat trickles down the side of Ryan’s head as his arm continues to rage in pain. As it hangs limp at his side he scours his surroundings for some escape route, some course of action. The situation can't be hopeless, not yet. He still needs to live. He needs to get back to the prison and… he needs to tell Chloe that her family is still alive.

As Jimmy leaps forward with a finishing strike, Ryan takes his knife and angles it straight for the wrist of the incoming arm and fully intends on taking it off. He doesn't need superior skill to win, he just needs to be more audacious than a killer, more ruthless than a monster.

The two blades grow closer and closer, one towards the heart while the other towards a wrist, until something unexpected happens. Jimmy’s knife which was pointing straight out perpendicular to his wrist, quickly in the blink of an eye, flicks down and bangs against the side of Ryan’s knife protecting his arm from injury as he uses to instability to move his hand away. Jimmy jumps back eyeing Ryan after his attempt at a counter offensive.

His madness induced face only grows even brighter as he realizes what Ryan just attempted. Somehow this serial killer was not joking when he said that he loved to kill people and was a professional. His knife skill is no joke. He flicked his knife faster than Ryan could physically see. The reaction felt... uncanny.

Jimmy lets his arms dangle and he cocks his head back starring Ryan down with the same disgusting grin he was born with. He chuckles and opens his mouth to speak but stops before words come out. He recloses his mouth and frows lifting his gaze to something behind Ryan, "Seems play time is up."

Ryan flips his head around preparing for a zombie to be behind him but nothing is there. He fell for the oldest trick in the book and now he is going to get stabbed in the back for it. Jimmy’s presence bleeds into his personal space and he can almost feel the knife jutting into his back. He attempts to spin around in time, but he knows its too late. This is why you never take your eyes off your opponent.

Despite imminent doom, the slightest tension builds up in his cheeks and the corners of his lips. His mouth, if only a little, raises into the faintest smile. Maybe it's the insanity infecting his persona, or maybe it's just Ryan's inner self no longer filtering itself. The only thing he feels is not fear—but relief.

“It seems I made a slight miscalculation.”

The knife never stabs into his back, he waits despite the words hoping for the anticipated pain that never arrives. When he looks back at Jimmy he doesn't find a sadistic grin like usual, he finds cold, calculating eyes glaring into soul. That gaze does not belong to the criminally insane, it belongs to a tired man who's watching disappointment all over again. It's as if Ryan loosing was the worst outcome that could have transpired. The most shocking part of it all is that Jimmy isn't moving an inch, not his eyes, not his breath. Not even the wind blew as the surroundings mimicked the sentiment and became impossibly still. The only thing that doesn't stop, doesn't succumb to an absolute petrification, is the pain in his arm and hand. He looks down at it and finds the blood still splashing across the ground.

"If I recall correctly, my solitary condition for saving you was that you did not squander your life again," Ryan spins around until his eyes land on the speaker. His blood runs cold as his gaze befalls a familiar raven mask.

“What is going on?” Ryan commands as he glances back to the frozen Jimmy and nursing his arm, luckily not bleeding as profusely as it was the first time.

“It’s just as it looks,” the man explains, "Time has stopped, save you and I that is."

“You can do that?!” he exclaims biting the inside of his lip to see if he’s dead or asleep.

The man shakes his head carefully, his beak of his mask making the motion much more pronounced, “No, unfortunately not. Things would be much simpler if I could. Instead I must tediously rely on others for this bizarre phenomenon,” the man walks over and climbs onto the roof of the car before taking a seat.

“So what now? Are you going to save my life again?” Ryan asks looking down at the double wound on his arm.

The man doesn’t respond right away but instead offers Ryan a piercing stare of two eyes clouded by black glass. Only after Ryan begins to shift uncomfortable does he respond.

“No,” he says leaning back slightly, “I’m not going to save you. If I did, we would just be back here again at some point."

“So why are you even here then?!” Ryan commands with greater emotion, "Why do you care so much about keeping me alive?"

The man taps the side of his beak before chuckling to himself for a moment, "Why are you so keen on dying?"

Ryan takes a step back. Something about his words struck him in a place he didn't realize he was vulnerable to. The comment itself fills him with some sort of rage that he narrowly suppresses in fear that overreacting might prove the man right. 

"It's—it's not my fault," Ryan tries, searching for something to change the subject to.

The man’s mask doesn’t shift, not even a little, but Ryan can tell a smile cracks out of his face.

"You're right," the man says slowly, "Your mother's death was not your fault," Ryan's blood runs cold. How could he know about that? He's never told anyone. "But everything after that was."

Ryan's mouth whispers something unintelligible about being confused, but his mind refuses to accept that as his only response. He glares at the man, knowing exactly what he's getting at and hating him for doing, "You don't know anything about me."

The man lowers his head slightly as he lifts a finger and points it at Ryan. While index finger sticks straight out, the thumb rises perpendicular to the hand imitating something Ryan has become intimately familiar with. Then, as he raises his mask but slightly, he jerks his hand upwards, not with a bang, but with a _click_. Ryan is left with his mouth gapping open as the man crosses his legs on the windshield of the vehicle and leans back slightly.

Why would he bring that up? He was saved back then, he knows he's in the man's debt but why?

"I know you saved me," Ryan says reluctantly.

The man shakes his head, "I was not the one who saved you then."

Ryan stutters out something—he's not sure what—and proceeds to shakes his head in disbelief. He knows what the man is trying to say, he's always known the truth, but being confronted with it is far scarier than reliving it in his head. 

The man leans forward crossing his arms onto his knee with professional grace, “When you attempted to end your life, you discovered that Steve had taken your magazine out. But do you know why he did that?”

Ryan struggles to get a word out, “H-he was worried that I might... that I might be dangerous with a gun. He didn't trust me."

“No, not at all,” the man says, “Steve was not a distrusting person, not like his friend. He took it out for another reason." 

Ryan scavenges his thoughts for anything to say but can only listen.

"Even without your magazine, he still taught you how to shoot a gun, use a first aid kit, and start a fire as a way to make amends for being a terrible father. He held out hope that you would be immune to the disease and survive the bite. His hope was... rather misplaced, but he'd be pleased to see that he was right. You're only alive become of him.”  

The frozen world seems to gray out as Ryan collapses to a knee, "It's not fair, why would he do that?"

When Ryan looks back up from the ground, he finds the masked man much closer than he was before, “Do you want to know why you keep suffering?”

Ryan looks back at him with tears welling up in his eyes, the traumatic memories being the only thing holding them back.

The man crouches down in front of him, “In all his life, Steve never forgave himself for his own sins. Perhaps what he wanted to tell you, in his own way, was… that you need to forgive yourself.”

The man watches in silence as a tear rolls down Ryan’s face. Followed closely behind by a single other. He never allowed himself to truly accept the deaths of anyone, not Steve, not the Jacobsons, nor his mother. He’s been running from them trying to give him the excuse that he’s already reached the acceptance stage of grief. However, that was all a farce, the only thing he ever reached was denial. He's been trying to hide from the fact that the Hughes are gone and he's alone—alone in this damning world. Even with new friends, nothing changed, he just talked a bit more. Even when Chloe was tearing up beside him, he just stood there and told himself that he wants others to be happy before even considering the fact that he was hurting too. 

This is the first time he’s ever cried for someone else.

It’s a strange feeling to have a blurred vision, not out of drunkenness, but from tears filling his eyes. To have a runny nose even though he's not sick. To have puffy checks even when he hasn't been hit. It’s quite the unique feeling indeed, but despite how he feels, all he can think about is Steve and how he argued with him so many times and neglected to ever talk with him. He recalls Steve showing him how to shoot even though he sucked at it and he recalls how Steve acted cool under pressure. Steve even heard him out when he came home with a gun and said zombies were real. Steve was never a bad guy, Ryan just didn’t give himself the opportunity to get to know him. He just wishes he could have one more day with him and apologize for being a stupid brat of a son.

Maybe that was the opening Ryan needed to truly let it all out, but reality is never so forgiving. The pain in his arm reminds him that his battle is not yet over.

“Why are you telling me all of this?” Ryan asks with a soft voice as he stands back to his feet.

The man chuckles while slowly and methodically climbing back onto the car. His black tunic and black pants seemed to match the darkening environment, “It seems our time here is reaching its conclusion. Therefore, I will leave you with one last remark.”

Ryan waits patiently to hear the last thing before whatever comes next.

"My aid is not infallible, I advice that you treat this as your last chance."

After his final remark the environment fades completely into black and he finds himself standing before the group of thugs from before.

“Alright, Jimmy, you take care of this shrimp. The rest of us can deal with the ones inside.”

The six thugs, not counting Jimmy, branch off in a uniform march and head to the car dealership. This is the exact same scene from before, perfectly matching what his memories dictate. After the group disappears, Jimmy begins marching straight towards Ryan with a sinister grin across his face. His wide eyes display the depths of his insanity while also masking the bloodlust he radiates. Ryan remembers that he tried to rush off the first time and Jimmy pounced on him but this time he didn’t run remains on his feet. He's not sure what's going on, but he's not going to die this time.

Jimmy raises his knife, “Alright kid, I don’t like killing people… oh wait, yes I do.”

“Jimmy Lashing?!” Ryan exclaims, causing the serial killer to pause in his tracks. 

He cocks his head to the side, “So you do recognize me? Are you a fan perhaps…” his voice grows darker and full of hatred, “or one of those bastards who imprisoned me?”  

Ryan pulls out his knife as quickly as he can and remembers the fight from before. He knows he can’t beat Jimmy in knife to knife combat so he’ll have to outthink him if he hopes to survive.

Jimmy lowers his stance, “Let’s see if your blood—”

Ryan cuts him off, “Runs as red as the others. I know.”

Jimmy’s face expresses an emotion he’s never seen before. Shock. For a moment Jimmy is speechless as how Ryan is guessing what he’s going to say. It’s not perfect but its certainly strange how he’s doing it. Then as if the moments of lip reading were not enough, Jimmy gives Ryan a knowing look. Shock is one thing, but Jimmy looks as if he knows more than his insanity is letting on.

Eventually, the maelstrom of emotions fade and his maniacal laugh sets in, giggling at the turn of events. The situation is granting him some form of sadistic pleasure before he gets his true intention of being able to kill him. Jimmy’s laugh quickly fades too and he lowers his head lifting his eyes to maintain focus on Ryan. He does all of this quickly before dashing forward bringing his knife out ready to strike.

Ryan, knowing he needs a new plan, draws his pistol and aims it at Jimmy before he gets within slashing distance. At the sight of the gun, Jimmy dramatically gasps and jumps to the side, flinging his leg out and kicking the gun out of Ryan’s hand. Then as if he is an acrobat to begin with, he does a sort of sideways hand-spring and lands directly overtop the gun. He picks up the weapon and aims it back at Ryan.

“Valiant effort!” he yells, “You tried your best and this is how far you came. Most people don’t survive my first strike unless I’m toying with them.”

Ryan opens his mouth and widens his eyes is shock as he tightens his grip on his knife. This is where he will make his decisive strike.

“May I ask you your name, young man?” he says in the most sane voice he’s ever used.

Ryan knows that whatever he's planning is a trap, but going alone with it might buy him some time to prepare for what comes next. Ultimately, he gives the serial killer his name.

“Ryan Lancer...” Jimmy shakes his head a bit in thought, “I like to memorize the names of my victims so if I come in contact with their family, I can relay in excruciating detail exactly what their loved one's collage of excrements looked like."

“That’s too bad,” Ryan counters, hoping his dark humor will come off as witty, “My entire family is dead. There’s no one left to talk to”

Jimmy's smiles doesn't fade as he looks down at his hand and lifts one finger, "Family dead?" 

He lifts a second finger and glances back at Ryan with a knowing gaze, "Is that so?"

Jimmy’s eyes narrow with an aura of finality to it, Ryan takes it as an indication that now is the time to act. Without delay, Ryan rushes forward and chambers his knife, ready to strike.

“What a shame, I thought this would be more fun,” the lunatic says, pulling the trigger—only a click follows.

Ryan may have fallen for his cheap trick the first time, but now it's time to flip the script. It seems Chloe's intuition and carefulness came back to save him. He would have forgotten about it too, had the masked man not reminded him of Steve and what he did for him. This plan only worked because people put forth enough conscious effort to help him—it's something he doesn't take lightly.

He swings his knife straight up at Jimmy but then the serial killer, using his impossible speed, somehow gets his knife into place to intercept the weapon. However, Ryan expected this very situation and uses his brass knuckles to punch Jimmy’s blade out of the way and follows up with his own blade right to the man’s torso cutting cleanly across his body.

Immediately after the strike, Jimmy kicks Ryan back and does two back hand-springs to put distance between them. He then drops the gun and places the hand on his open wound. A dark blood oozes out, almost appearing gray under the shadow of a nearby building. He doesn't twitch at the pain but instead begins to smile and laugh.

"You're just as I expected," Jimmy says ominously as he turns around, "We'll see each other again Ryan Lancer, you can count on it."

Just as soon as he finishes he sprints down an alley nearby. A sparse trail of his dark blood follows him as he runs, the distant echoes of insanity and laughter echo through the streets. Ryan is left alone huffing and calming himself after the rush of adrenaline.

Once he regains his bearings, inspects himself briefly, and ensures the surroundings are safe. He turns his attention to the car dealership.
