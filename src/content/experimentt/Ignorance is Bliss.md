The class finishes off, but the bell never rings. An uncomfortable chaos allowly creeps through the room as the tardy bell becomes more apparent throughout the class. The teacher is about to let the class leave, but is interrupted by the intercom. 

“Teachers, please keep your students inside their classrooms for the time being. We are in a soft lockdown mode. This is not a drill.”

Chatter breaks out between each student on what could possibly be happening. Ryan leans back in his chair, the unease he feels is echoes instinctively by the rest of the class despite them all being ignorant. He presses his palms together as he imagines what might happen. The heavy burden placed upon him in that bathroom makes him briefly reflect on the past eighteen years of his life and wish he didn’t hate it so much. His arms tremble as he imagines what could possible be about to happen.

A boy next to him leans over and whispers softly under the dull roar of chatter in the classroom, “You were the last person to leave, was something happening outside?” 

Ryan shakes his head forcing his arms to remain still and his voice to sound controlled, “I didn’t see anything out of the ordinary, it must have just happened.”

He remembers the last lie he told and how he based it in truth. This one is no different as the boy to his side doesn’t question him any further. He lightly taps the top of his leg to check for the key still in his pocket before returning to reality and looking out the window into the school courtyard.

A few minutes after the initial shock, the teacher gets an email on her computer and she reads it silently, her eyes widen as she gets to the meat of the email. Heimes must have told the teachers that the place is being locked down.

Eventually, the teacher stands up and addresses the class, “So apparently, there is no danger in the building but the lockdown is occurring all around the city… It might take a while before we are released so get comfortable.”

Suddenly, the lights flicker as if there was a spontaneous power surge, but everything goes back to normal quickly after that.

One girl from across the room blurts out louder than everyone else, “I have no signal!”

Everyone’s eyes dart to their phones and check to see if the claim is true. Each person comes to the same conclusion very quickly when they too realize the worst has come to pass.

Ryan still doesn’t have a phone so he doesn’t care much, but the thought of cutting off communication does cross his mind. He remembers how the emergency line didn't work the day before which only adds to the absurdity of the situation. That being said, the situation does beg the question of who could be behind it. Is the military trying to quarantine the town? Are the zombies already running rampant outside and took the tower down? No matter what it is, Ryan feels the looming threat creep closer and closer like a panther about to pounce.

The class remains in their desks for another ten minutes, but after that they slowly start breaking off and sitting around the room. The floor presents an enticing surface to sit on rather than the uncomfortable desks, not to mention that two people fall asleep pretty quickly, though they were the type of boys to have stayed up the entire night gaming.

Ryan finds himself sitting alone in the corner of the room with a book he found on the teacher’s shelf. He doesn’t care much for reading but it's better than staring into the worrying faces of those around him. He may know more than them about the situation but that doesn’t change how he gets annoyed by all the redundant questions about what’s going on. At some point he might tell someone to shut up with the complaining and join the boys napping.

After another five minutes passes. Chloe gets up and approaches Ryan, taking a seat next to his corner.

“Do you think we’ll get service soon? I’m sure all of our parents are worried,” she says with a genuine level of concern. 

Ryan restrains himself from talking about his family and instead redirects the conversation, “Don’t you remember, the lockdown is all over the city. Our parents are all probably in the same boat as us. There’s nothing we can do.”

“You’re probably right.”

“I get it, we’re all worried, but that doesn’t change our situation. We should remain calm—trust the authorities,” the hypocracy in his voice make him gag silently.

Chloe purses her lips as she curls her knees to her chest and hugs them, “Yeah, but there’s just nothing to do.”

Ryan crawls over and grabs a whiteboard and marker from a cubby next to the book shelf. When he returns, he draws two vertical lines and two horizontal lines on the whiteboard making a grid.

“Wanna play tic tac toe?”

Chloe gives the board a quizzical look like the sentiment is a bit childish, but she eventually shrugs her shoulders and nods her head. It's not like tic-tac-toe was his first choice of time passer but he's not very creative and this felt like a sufficient excuse to avoid any awkwardness between them.

Ryan marks an x in the top right corner and hands the board over to Chloe, “Do you have any friends in the class or am I just that intriguing?”

It’s hard to tell but he could almost swear he saw Chloe blush by the comment but he shrugged it off and doesn’t get too invested.

“I don’t really have any friends in this class period, but it looks like I’m going to have to make one or two.”

Ryan raises an eyebrow, “Okay, when you do, give me some tips.”

Chloe marks a circle in the center square and hands the board over a little forcefully, “You can make friends too. You were the one that said that we were all in the same boat.”

“That’s not what—”

Chloe rolls her eyes, “Come on, you can’t sit here in the corner all by yourself.”

Ryan crosses his arms, “Watch me.” 

Chloe rolls her eyes, “I didn’t realize you were so stubborn.”

He could continue on with another passive aggressive comments but in the end, having another person here somehow makes him feel calmer than before. 

“Do you wanna be my friend?” he asks innocently while avoiding eye contact. Instead he fixates his gaze on the simple two dimensional game in his hands.

Chloe is stopped mid thought by the unexpected comment, “What?”

Ryan forces himself to glance over at her, “You said I should make some friends, so here we are. Do you wanna be my friend?”

There is a kind of face everyone gives a smart alec and Ryan receives that exact look, but there is something oddly comforting about it. In fact, he'd be so bold as to say he quite enjoyed receiving that expression.

“You know what Ryan, I—”, Chloe is cut out by the opening of the door and the sharp looks of the entire class upon a fully equipped military soldier with an AR on his chest and a stern but not quite angry look on his face. He scans the classroom and quickly steps up to the center of the room.

“Hello, my name is Lieutenant Roger Kroner. I just want to assure all of you that you are completely safe, but some events in the city have caused some mass panic. Internet connection was shut off because of an overload in the power grid, but we're working on fixing it right now.”

Ryan can feel the deception in the soldier's voice but he doesn't voice a concern. The whole class sighs in relief and continues to listen to the words of the Lieutenant with a lingering tension.

“For now, stay put in the classroom and we’ll give further instructions as soon as possible,” he turns to the teacher, “Ma’am, are you the teacher of this class?”

She nods with a slight, “Yes.”

“If students need to use the restroom, allow them to leave one at a time and come straight back. Stand at the door to make sure they don’t wander. Also, if anyone has any flu-like symptoms, send them to the library immediately. There is a disease going around that can be treated, but we need to keep the sick separated from the healthy to avoid the epidemic from spreading out of control. Military soldiers will be patrolling the halls, but no one is in trouble so don’t panic, it’s just standard procedure.”

Lieutenant Roger finishes up the order and quickly exits the room, probably intending to repeat the same thing to the next class. With his departure, the room is left with an eerie silence, one so thick not even a knife could cut through.

While most of the students are probably thinking about what to say to the nearest working ear, Ryan focuses on a single word uttered out of the soldier’s mouth: library. What Heimes told him echoes through his mind as he debates what is going on. The library is for the sick, but the sick turn into zombies. Why doesn’t he need to go there to get answers? None of it makes sense which only adds to his growing headache.

Suddenly, a girl still seated at her desk begins to cough uncontrollably. All eyes point to her and the teacher is put on the spot. Will she follow instructions and send her to the library or what? It’s the question on everyone’s mind.

“Umm…” she hesitates with shock covering her face like a mask.

The fear of infection is an instinctual feeling; one like the fight or flight response. Without even having to contemplate the choices anyone would want to avoid someone with a potentially deadly illness. Ryan battles this instinctual desire to live with the logic and curiosity of an explanation. Either he attempts to take this girl to the library and risk getting infected for the third time, or he stays here and hopes that Heimes was lying about dying a brutal death. When he puts it that way, the choice is fairly obvious.

Ryan inhales slowly and stands up and begins to fake a cough as well. “I think we both need to *cough* go to the library.”

He walks over to the girl and pats her on the back. God help him that this disease is not super contagious.

“They said they could help us. We should at least check it out.”

The girl nods and gets out of her chair, a little bit unsteady. The slight wobbling of her body makes it evident that this disease is laughing matter, though he already knows that. How long she has left until she turns is something he’ll need to watch closely for.

The teacher doesn’t stop them from leaving, but the piercing eyes of everyone in the class does make him hesitate as they walk. The two ‘sick’ students leave without a single supportive word passed to them.

The library used to be somewhere that students only went when absolutely necessary or if they were a bookworm, but two fully armed guards really changed the aesthetic of the space. One could no longer walk into the library and greet the old librarian eagerly rereading a book; the new librarian was a middle aged man with a holstered pistol and a gas mask.

“Step over into line,” he says sternly pointing at the breadline of coughing students.

Ryan transitions over to the line with the sick girl from his class. The group of ill teens reek of uncertainty and fear like a foul smell of old, wet clothes. He places a hand on his forehead certain that he should be showing symptoms by this point. He made first contact with the disease yesterday, the fact that he still feels fine is a really good sign.

“I was *cough* just fine during first hour,” the girl next to him says, “Hey, do you think they *cough* they *cough* actually have a treatment.”

Ryan holds his breath for a few seconds as she coughs. With so many sick people here, he’s bound to catch something but the thought of breathing coughed air is still a bit disgusting, though he doesn’t have much room to talk given the last few days.

“I sure hope so.”

The girl continues, “My mom promised to take me out to eat at my favorite restaurant tomorrow night. It’ll be my birthday and I’ve been really looking forward to *cough* it," her gaze falters,  "though, I don’t think that’s going to happen anymore.”

Ryan bites his bottom lip as he tries his best not to think about what the girl just said. It's his birthday today and all he wanted was to ditch everyone. The reminder only recalls the harsh reality he's found himself in.

The officer with the gas mask steps around the table, arms behind him like he’s about to give a briefing of a very important mission. Three doctor-looking individuals in full biohazard walk out from the back room of the library and fall in behind the first officer.

“Listen up!” he shouts. Every trace of whisper dies out immediately. “Everyone form a single file line. These doctors behind me will come and check each and every one of you. If you are labeled as green, please head back to class, everyone else go through that back door there.” 

Normally getting a bunch of high schoolers to line up is a process that takes an unnecessary amount of time, but today and today only that line was formed almost immediately, though that is mostly because they were already lined up and many were too sick to move out of the breadline.

The hazmat doctors came by and began to inspect every individual; they checked eyes, mouth, neck, and a few other spots. After roughly twenty seconds of inspection, the doctor would then call out green or red. Green was told to leave as the instructions implied, but red was escorted by gas mask guards who Ryan hadn’t seen come in.

As the line began to dwindle, some kind of anxiety began to form inside his mind. It’s not as if anxiety was a new thing, rather that this unnerving uncertainty of what was to come behind that door was a bit unsettling. The line dwindled further and further with each passing student being sent into the back room or out the front door. Eventually, it was Ryan’s turn.

The doctors checked him like the rest, whispered a little something to each other and then spoke the anticipated word: “Green.”

He was promptly shoved out of the library and back into the hallway. One guard stands outside the door with a menacing M4 in hand.

“Head back to your class, son, you’re safe.” 

Without a second thought, he made his way down the hallway and turned the corner into an even more forlorn corridor. He racks his brain searching for what ‘answers’ he could have found. The military is within the school, but he already knows that. What could he be searching for? What did Heimes want him to see?

With all the thoughts dancing around a concrete conclusion, he notices a door leading to the janitor’s closest. Some devilish, impulsive thought intrudes into his mind as his intuition tells him that something important is in there.

Ryan checks the hallway one last time for any military personnel who might be wandering by and see him, but luckily there was nobody. He tries the handle and almost laughs as it shakes but remains locked. His rational thought kicks in as the impulsive behavior bears no fruits. He’s about to leave but remembers something important. The key in his pocket doesn’t seem that special at first glance, but when he puts it into the lock and it turns, he realizes that this impulsive decision might be fate.

He steps inside, closes the door gently, and turns around to be greeted with the butt pockets of a pair of worn, yellow overalls.

Ryan steps back into the door causing the man to stand up and take notice. He’s an older man with a large balding spot and a slightly crooked face. His hair is unkempt and a scar runs through his left eyebrow. His back also arches slightly over, not like a hunchback but rather a man who has had bad posture for an extended period of his life. 

“Who are you?” the man says pulling out a wet mop. His deep accent is one of a poor background. The words are English but not every word is easy to understand.

Ryan kicks himself, he just wanted to try out the key; he didn’t intend to get noticed.

“I’m sorry, I didn’t realize anyone was in here…” Ryan says preparing to get scolded by the janitor. Instead he inspects the the man closer and recognizes the worn, yellow overalls to be owned by the same janitor that cleaned up the corpse from earlier.

“Wait, you’re da kid,” he says, putting the mop down.

“You know about the zombies don’t you,” Ryan responds, getting a bit excited.

The janitor’s eyes narrow on him. He isn’t a very intimidating man but he definitely has seen his fair share of absurd things throughout his life. It’s at that moment that Ryan notices a truly terrifying sight. Laying on the ground below them is a stocky man dressed in all camouflage with a large red welt on his temple. Unlike the previous soldier, he doesn’t have an AR, but a pistol is holstered at his hip which makes Ryan nervous as the janitor reaches for it.

“What are you doing?” Ryan asks cautiously as he slowly rests his hand on the doorknob.

“Calm down boy,” the janitor commands as he waves a hand and straightens his hunchback with the pistol in hand. He then promptly extends the weapon handle first out to Ryan: the dumbfounded teen glances between the two things, hesitating to take action.

“Take it,” the janitor persists, “You’ll need dis.”

Ryan slowly reaches for the gun and swipes it out of the old man’s hand before stuffing it between his hip and the waistband of his pants. He then pulls his shirt down over it to conceal it as best he can. He doesn’t particularly want the weapon, but he feels even less safe letting this strange man hold on to it. Normally he would have said nope, not today, and dipped, but given everything that’s happened, he knows that turning this offer down will only get him killed even faster.

“Who are you?” Ryan asks with his back practically part of the wall now.

“I’m jut a wise old man,” the janitor shakes his head, “dat’s not important. You need to know more bout da libary, right?”

Ryan nods instinctively but doesn’t quite feel comfortable with his decision.

“There is a secret spot by der. If you go into da kitchen der will be a door in the back. You’ll want to see dat.”

“Why are you telling me this?”

“Get does answers boy,” the janitor asserts as he motions for Ryan to get going.

Ryan quickly pushes down on the handle and pulls the wooden door open before stepping out without turning his back to the old man. The door is forced closed right as he clears the frame not allowing even a second more of nerveracking eye contact. 

So this janitor must be working with Heimes or something. It’s a peculiar situation, but that word has really lost some of its meaning by this point. Everything is strange.

No guards wander the halls as he stealthily creeps through corridor after corridor arriving at the cafeteria and eventually the kitchen. 

The door to the kitchen unlocks with his key revealing what looks to be a toddler's bedroom: the stoves were covered in papers and books. The tables in the center of the room have beakers scattered unorderly around. A shattered beaker lies on the floor next to the table. The assortment of counters, fridges, and other cooking furniture are all completely taken over by the disaster of scientific equipment. This is no longer a kitchen, it's a makeshift laboratory. 

The messy tables make it difficult to spot, but in the back corner of the kitchen sits a small square door. It could easily be confused with a cabinet door, but it's sitting on the wall between the walk in freezer and a fridge. A sign with the words: “Maintenance” label on it. Why a school kitchen needs a maintenance shaft is beyond him, but alas its present exactly where the janitor said it would be.

He walks over being careful not to step on any broken glass or knock something off. Then he grabs the handle of the door and pulls. Nothing. Again. Still nothing. Inspecting the door closer, he discovers a small keyhole hidden underneath the maintenance sign. To the right of the keyhole is a small logo he's never seen before of a snake-like creature. Shrugging of the curious glyph, he promptly sticks the golden key in with a quick twist. After the resistance of the lock gives way, he untwists the key and places it back in his pocket. After another tug at the door he finds it still jammed, so with one last attempt, he puts his foot on the wall and pulls as hard as he can. In an instant the door gives way and swings open sending Ryan sprawling onto his back. 

Upon returning to his feet, the musty air of the old shaft greets his lungs. Cobwebs liter the 6' by 2' space. His body would barely be able to fit through, but he had to at least try.

Ryan felt the pistol at his waist and key in his pocket one last time before squeezing his way through the door and into the cramped, claustrophobic gap in the walls. Spiders have never really been a big fear of his but the thought of countless spiders being able to get onto him made him shiver. He looks back at the door to the kitchen and doubt begins to fill him. There has to be another way to do this. Only an idiot would give up a certainty for an uncertainty and he had no idea where this walkway went.

Then he heard it, muffled and faint, but present, the scream of a teenager deeper down inside. His level of anxiety and fear sky rockets but so too does his thirst for knowledge. Countless trifling thoughts argue with him to stop and run away, but the overarching desire to live reminds him that he needs to get these answers or Heimes warning about a brutal death might come to fruition.

The tight, darkening shaft led around what he deemed to be the perimeter of the library. He continued to push down through the darkness with only small patches of light seeping through small cracks in the wall. Eventually he arrived at the end of the shaft. It opened up into a room full of machinery. Air conditioning and heating units are what he guesses they were, but he’s no expert. A metal door with a chain and lock wrapped around the handles lies on one wall—a faint red exit sign sits atop it reminding him of what's possible. He only catches sight of it briefly but he notices the same curious snake-like logo on the lock of the door which prompts him to pat his pocket one more time. On the opposite side of the dark, musty room is a wooden door ajar just enough to see a sliver of light seeping through.

Then he heard it again: the muffled sound of a scream only this time it was easier to hear. Tracing the sound of the voice, his heart skips a beat as he deduces that the origin is on the other side of the wooden door.

Crouching down and tip-toeing over to the door, he tries to listen through it while staying as silent as physically possible. Talking can be heard from the other side but he couldn’t make out what they were saying. With a deep breath he reaches his fingers onto the end of the door and slowly slides it open a bit more so he can peer inside.

The other side is another room completely empty of anything except a table and a chair. The table had various tools on it with no purpose Ryan could comprehend and the chair had a high school student strapped to it. A man with an AR and a full hazmat suit stands next to a short man hunched over the student. The kid looks dazed and exhausted not from torture but like they’re sick. Then it hits him, that kid is the girl from his class, the one he walked down with.

“Can we just put this one out of her misery? It’s over for her,” the hazmat suit guy says, pointing the gun at her hanging head. 

“No!” the short man says with a deep and deformed voice. Deformed is a weird word to use but nothing else quite captures the extent of how strange this guy's voice is. It’s english and has traces of humanity but its like the voice box was put through a grinder then filled with acid. Not of someone who’s smoked their whole life but of one who just simply sounds like a demon. “I still have more tests to run.”

“Don’t forget creep, our orders are to assist you, but that doesn’t mean we have to support needless torture. You may not be human but I am.”

The creep steps back and shakes its head. “Calm yourself lieutenant, the organization sent me because I’m the best of the best, these things take time.”

The lieutenant paces in place, “How could you be the best of the best?”

The creep lets out a little chuck, “You grunts don’t know anything about science. To put it into words you would understand, in my field, only novelty leads to progress. Every new and unique result leads to a new discover, and in turn, real, unadulterated progress. That process takes time and many, many trials to sufficiently complete. No fact left behind.”

“This is the eighth student so far, the other seven have died. You might be blind, so I’ll explain this to you: these are KIDS!”

The creep picks up an instrument from the table and hobbles over to the girl picking her sagging head up so it is looking right at her. 

The girl's face is bright red with tears streaming down her face. He thought she was unconscious before but now he realizes that she heard the whole thing.

“Ha…ppy… Bir...th…day” she sobs quietly, “To…you…”

The creep brings the instrument closer to the girl's face, “As you said yourself, she’s already a goner, with her sacrifice we could save so many more. Something you humans just don’t understand.”

Ryan closes the door right before the blood curdling scream of the girl rings out.
