“How could you let this happen!?” Michael demands as he slams his hands onto the table of the meeting room.

As their bitter luck was, Stephanie still had the car keys with her when she was abducted. As such, Ryan and Jeremy had to return to the prison on foot giving them just enough time to wallow in their own misery and build suspicion around their delayed arrival. The guards by the gate already suspected the details of their situation by the time they made it back.

Now, after an emergency meeting was called, Ryan and Jeremy sit at the end of a meeting table surrounded by all the prison camp leaders. Blake, Caid, Max, Beth, Alison, and some other teens showed up to sit in on the conversation, but none of them could get a word with Ryan before he and Jeremy were thrust into the chairs they now find themselves in.

"This is why... all unscheduled departures have to go through me," Michael finally says, cooling his rage and finding some degree of level-headedness.

"She was just trying to get you an anniversary gift," Jeremy comments coldly as he avoids eye contact. Ryan actually comments Jeremy for his memory as he didn't seem to be listen the whole car ride with loud music playing.

Michael shakes his head, "It doesn't matter, this is still unacceptable."

Ryan can’t bring himself to say anything. He was suspicious of the whole thing from the get go. Sure he's been downtown a few times and made it back each time, but taking people who can't hold their own always implies unique risks. It was foolish to think nothing bad would happen, and that's not even considering the whole talking mutant ordeal—something he intentionally refrained from recounting. It would only cause more problems to lay that one out at a time like this.

“You know its not all the boys fault,” Drew says defending him, “As Ryan explained, they were attacked by zombies. As far as I'm concerned, this gang could have been waiting for a time to take some of our scavengers hostage.”

Michael gives Drew a suspicious look, "I take it you have an opinion?"

Drew nods, "I do."

“Then I want your thoughts. How are we supposed to resolve this?”

Drew studies every individual at the table, rather meticulously. Then, when he's inspected every member, he glosses over the crowd that stands in the background. He concludes his investigation by staring at Ryan with a thoughtful expression.

"I have an idea," Drew starts, "But there's a few details I need to clarify with the boy before I can enact any plans. Allow me a moment to discuss with him first... I'd prefer to keep it private."

Michael looks at him incredulously, "Why can't you just ask him here? It'd be beneficial for everyone to know."

Drew shakes his head, "These aren't the sort of questions that can be relinquished to prying ears."

Despite Drew's calm, collected, and confident reply, Michael doesn't seem to be on board with his request for confidentiality, "No, I think you should ask him here. We all have a right to know."

Shaking his head once again and crossing his arms casually, Drew leans back, "No."

Annoyance and resentment flash across the man's face as his eyebrow twitches. Whatever this interaction is, it's definitely not the first time Drew has stood his ground, "Drew Wilbur this is not the time to be arguing. Members of our group have been kidnapped—including my wife—ask you questions here."

Drew once again shakes his head without so much as an ounce of fear or regret, "No, information such as this cannot be unheard. It's not my place to put that burden on anyone here."

Ryan can't help but roll his eyes. He didn't think that way when he placed that burden on Steve, Bill, and him. Wonder what's changed?

Michael studies Drew very carefully, his lips pressed together in a restrained anger, "Drew. What are you getting at?"

Drew takes a deep breath, "Let's just say my time here is waning. I don't have to talk to the boy now, but not helping anyone until I do."

The two prideful men have an unnerving stare-off, tense enough that not a soul in the room dare move to disturb the moment. Then, with a disappointed sigh, Michael waves his hand, "Alright fine, step outside and ask the boy whatever you need. You better come back with a damn good plan afterwards."

Drew nods as he stands up and gestures with his head for Ryan to follow. Then, with all eyes glued to them, the two men step outside the meeting room and reconvene one hallway over. 

"So you really have a plan?" Ryan asks as they stop at the corner of the hallway.

Drew shakes his head with a smirk, "I have a lead, that's all I need," Ryan tries to rebuttal but Drew cuts him off, "—in truth, my informant has the plan. He just contacted me."

This informant of his has been mentioned before, but Ryan has never truly inquired about him. From what he's gathered through sheer happenstance, it appears as though Drew and his informant are simultaneously doing recon in search of Heimes. The "training missions" that Drew's been taking Ryan on have all been a cover to follow subsequent leads. Some have run dry, but others provide Drew with just enough information to strike his curiosity. He'd always nod slowly and hum to himself with a little "I see" at the end. Perhaps, things might actually be changing on his end.

"You have a lead on Heimes, don't you," Ryan surmises, "A real one."

Drew points up to the corner of the room where a small security camera sits looking down at them, “I disabled the camera for the next twenty minutes so we’re free to talk without anyone knowing what we're saying.”

"So I was right."

Drew nods, "My informant believes he knows where Heimes is, but there's one catch."

"What?" Ryan asks with a sinking suspicion on what it could be.

Leaning against the wall, Drew chuckles under his breath, "Let's just say my hunch was right. Three weeks of training so snot-nosed kid are about to pay off."

A bit startled, Ryan takes a step back and holds a hand up, "Wait, what are you getting me into?"

His reaction seems to be humorous to Drew as he laughs a bit and pushes Ryan's hand down, "Let me put it this way, if you do as I say, you'll get the hostages back—and a bunch of other people—what I stand to gain is a real, tangible step towards Heimes. If all goes well, I won't be trailing him anymore, I'll be at his doorstep."

Ryan takes a deep breath. These sorts of ideas are always problematic and every time it ends up with Ryan risking his neck for seemingly no reason... that being said, if he has a chance to repay Chloe for her kindness, he's going to take it.

"What... what do you need me to do?"

Drew smiles contently, evidently hearing exactly what he wanted, "Years ago, I was part of a group called Allegiance One. We were the best of the best, it's no stretch of the imagination to say that we could have brought down a small country ourselves."

Checking to make sure no one is watching—not because he doesn't trust Drew—but for his own sanities' sake, Ryan gives the man a quizzical look, "What does this have to do with anything?"

The man nods, "I tell you this, because Allegiance One knew about the apocalypse long before it came to fruition. Now... I'm responsible with ending it. That being said, it is imperative that I find Heimes, so I need you to complete this final mission."

Mentally backtracking, Ryan waves his hands to shut the man up, even for a brief moment, "Wait... wait, you still haven't told me why you need _me_. Is it because of my association with Heimes in the past?"

Drew gives a certain nod, "It is. You met a cultist and got away, I guarantee he'll react if we're this close to him, and you show up. You'll finally fulfil your usefulness."

"Right," Ryan comments incredulously.

Drew sighs, restraining some seemingly unnecessary comment, "Alas, I'll be handing you off to my informant soon and all you have to do is stick close to him—and survive. Though if my training meant anything to you, you should be fine."

"So," Ryan begins, still trying to keep up, "Who's your informant?"

The look Drew gives him is a serious one, though not without the shadow of a smirk, "You'll know him when you see him. All you need to do is go to the baseball stadium after the meeting. My informant will be there... and he'll find you."

After that, Drew turns and starts walking away, but Ryan catches him by the arm and tugs him back, "Is that really all you're going to tell me?"

Drew looks back with irritation, "What do you want? This is a win-win situation. You get to play hero and I get to Heimes. Unless you have some problem with that, I advise you do as I say."

Ryan lets go with a hurt expression. It's not that his feelings are volatile, but that he really can't argue with him. Sure, Drew set him up in the past, but the last three weeks have proven that he knows what he's doing. Even if the missions nearly got him killed, they were never suicidal—they always served some sort of purpose. They may not have fulfilled Ryan in any way, shape, or form, but that experience cannot be understated. Then again, he could outright refuse these orders. Walk away. Forget about Drew and his informant. But then... Stephanie. Amber. Janelle. Chloe. Whether he likes it or not, he doesn't know where to start and this is his only option. It's a shame though, he doesn't want to be the hero, he just wants to right his wrong.

They return to the meeting room in silence. Afterwards, Drew presents some—rather believable—story about how he's been keeping track of unnamed survivor camps and intends to find the one containing these malicious thugs. A few people ask questions but Drew effortlessly answers with this bogus narrative that everyone eventually buys. Only Ryan, sitting quietly in his chair, knows that there's more to this story than meets the eye. Michael especially pushes for specifics but that too only reinforces the story and eventually draws the meeting to the close. The only actions items being: Drew going out alone and everyone else on standby in case he spots them. As one bonus item, Drew lets Michael know that he needs Ryan to check out another location so he's to leave camp as well. After the perfectly cultivated narrative, Michael begrudgingly agrees—not even asking what the private conversation was about. 

With the meeting adjourned, Ryan leaves the room and heads for the parking lot. He's not able to make it there as Blake cuts him off halfway.

"Tell me the truth", Blake starts as Caid, Max, and Beth approach from behind, "Are you really checking something out for Drew?"

Ryan glances around at the small group of teens around him, "Strangely enough, it's true."

Blake crosses his arms, "I take it you trust him."

With a sigh, Ryan reluctantly nods his head, "Not a lot, but enough."

Max steps up and wraps his arm around Ryan's shoulder, in order to do so, the boy has to get on his tippy-toes, "Sound like we've got another Boys trip in the making."

Making her presence known, Beth clears her throat, "Chloe's my best friend, I'm coming too."

Caid shrugs his shoulders, "It's a group effort now."

With a little smile, Blake starts turning around, "Alright, I was thinking we could take my truck. It's got four-wheel drive and plenty—"

"No!" Ryan asserts, causing Blake to turn around and drawing everyone else's undivided attention, "This isn't a group expedition."

Caid steps forward and lays a hand on Ryan's shoulder, "Calm down. We're not trying to press anything, but you remember how it went last time—five heads are better than one."

Ryan can't help but take a deep breath after that. Risking his own life at Drew's whims, sure, nothing new, but to risk his friends' lives. That's not a line he's willing to cross. He's confident that this isn't a suicide mission, but he's not going to risk getting anyone else hurt by a bad call. He's survived alone long enough—what's one more mission?

"Do you all remember the start of this apocalypse," Ryan starts, "...back in the school."

Max gives him a weird look as he shrugs his shoulders as if the comment was obvious, "Of course we do, how could we forget?"

Ryan stares directly at Max but intends for everyone to hear it, "Do you know why that happened?"

Silence. Not even Max dare make a terrible joke amidst that question. Everyone else just stares with a serious look and the faintest hint of dread.

"It's because of Mr. Heimes—our principal. He intercepted me in the bathroom and... offered me an out... I refused," Ryan looks around and finds utter shock shared among every teen in attendance, "In order to get Chloe back, I'll likely meet Heimes again."

Disbelief keeps the entire group in a static shock for a long moment after Ryan concludes. Blake looks as if he's doing some mental calculations, but it's Caid who breaks through that thick silence first.

"Then... this is something we're all a part of," Caid decides unconfidently as he looks around the room for any reinforcement.

Of all the things that could dwell in his mind at this very moment, it has to be the most foreboding thing of them all. Drew's words stick to the foreground of his mind like an impossibly sticky glue that only infects everything it touches. "When you're dealing with a cultist only two things can happen: either you kill them... or live long enough to become one." He hates to even imagine that the latter might ever transpire, but that's not a risk he's ever willing to take.

"No," Ryan reasserts, "I've... I've seen things—I know things. This..." he suddenly finds himself at a loss for words, "I'm sorry... it's just... I have to..." he grits his teeth. The entire group takes on a worried aura around him. He closes his eyes and looks down, "I'm going alone."

Before anyone can speak, he pushes past Blake and makes an exit. He prays that no one follows, and to his relief, no one does. They simply let him walk away in silence.

He makes it to his room, grabs his keys, and then makes his way to his motorcycle. He still doesn't trust himself to drive it, but it's the only vehicle he's ever driven before, and he'll take a certainty over an uncertainty any day. When he actually arrives to the bike, he finds a familiar, heavier guy leaning against the vehicle. At the sight of him, Jeremy stands up.

"I'm coming with you," he asserts, with more conviction that Ryan has ever seen on the boy before.

Immediately, Ryan shakes his head, "No you're not. This is something I have to do alone."

Jeremy puts one foot in front of the bike, "This isn't up for negotiation. I'm helping."

Some sort a fatigue washes over Ryan at that moment. The strangest part is that his muscles feel completely fine—energetic even. Yet something inside of him feels so, tragically weak, like he just can't deal with it anymore. In light of this, he elects to push past Jeremy and reach out for his motorcycle.

Seeing as that was not the outcome he wanted, Jeremy grabs Ryan by the arm and pulls him off with a surprising amount of strength, "I'm not done talking yet."

Ryan looks back with an irritated gaze, "I told you that you're not coming."

Jeremy purses his lips as his chin quivers, "I'm responsible for this. I was there when they got taken, so it's my responsibility to help get them back. I know I'm not a hero, but I want to help—I have to."

Strangely enough, Ryan finds himself looking down after that confession. His mind just... doesn't commute it for a moment and some level of shame arises because of that realization. Jeremy can't come, but how can he shut down a conviction he aligns himself with—sometimes... maybe too much.

"N-no," Ryan stammers, "you can't come."

Jeremy sighs, "I thought you might say that. I'll just follow you then. You won't be taking me, I'll just happen to be in the same place."

"No!" Ryan demands, "Stop this Jeremy, you can't come."

Rage builds in his face as if a massive band-aid had just been viciously ripped from his sensitive skin. He steps forward and pushes Ryan with more strength than Ryan thought possible for an overweight boy. Ryan stumbles backwards and barely catches himself on the motorcycle.

"Stop trying to be a hero," Jeremy asserts as he stands up straight, "I am giving you my help whether you like it or not... and if I slow you down... then just leave me behind—I'm coming."

Hesitantly, Ryan regains proper footing after finding that the boy has no intention of continuing this engagement in a physical manner. Instead, Jeremy seems to calm down a bit and take a step closer to the motorcycle. This leaves Ryan with the heavy burden of a decision. Jeremy is completely ignorant of what he's about to face off against, for all he knows, Drew's informant could be an elite assassin that's hellbent on killing mutants. Yet, on the other hand, Jeremy has made it abundantly clear that he's not going to accept no for an answer. Ryan could try to reason—tell him the truth—but if he's anything like Ryan, no rational advice will clear his conscience. If that were possible, Ryan probably wouldn't be here.

"Jeremy," Ryan offers as one final attempt to convince him that his resolve is misguided, "Do you have any idea what you're trying to do?"

Without a moment of hesitation, Jeremy responds, "She may annoy the crap out of me and I might spend plenty of time wishing she'd disappear, but she's still my mother. Those bastards took the one person who still cares about me. I'll be damned if I'm going to sit idly by and disgrace her care—not again."

Ryan takes a careful seat on the bike and hangs his head a bit. Why does it feel like every time he really needs to say something, that's the moment words escape him? He didn't know what to say to Steve and, yet again, he finds himself wishing he knew how to say more.

Words continue to evade him, so Ryan decides to slide the keys into the ignition and let the roar of the engine drown out his thoughts. This action, while primarily used for himself, seems to give Jeremy the go ahead to try and pry his way onto the seat. His lack of grace and dexterity ultimately leads to him pushing Ryan all the way forward and twisting the handlebars.

"Get off!" Ryan demands as Jeremy nearly falls over trying to climb on.

"I'm coming," Jeremy reiterates taking finally taking a step back after Ryan doesn't offer enough space to slide on to the one-person seat.

Ryan pinches the bridge of his nose and takes a deep breath, "Stop saying that. This isn't a joke."

Jeremy narrows his eyes as he throws his hand into his pocket and pulls something out that clanks as sunlight hits it. A small ring of three keys, a pink, fluffy rabbit's foot, and some sort of small bobble head shakes in the boy's hand as he waves it in the air, "I'll follow in my car. Maybe my mom will be more comfortable if her own van picks her up."

At this point, the decision has begun to clear up a bit. Either Ryan takes him with willingly or he comes along at despite the warning. One choice might be more convenient for him and maybe even preferable, but that's also the method that is most likely to lead to another dead body. Whether he likes it or not, Ryan only has one option here if he wants to ensure everyone gets to come home.

Ryan glares at the boy as he promptly jogs over to a light gray minivan and hops into the driver's seat. Of course the boy can drive, it's probably trivial to him. This provides for an excellent reason to look away as Ryan carefully switches gears and pulls forward. His motorcycle is still quite awkward to ride, but it's the only thing that he knows will get him there. And getting there is the most important thing right now.
